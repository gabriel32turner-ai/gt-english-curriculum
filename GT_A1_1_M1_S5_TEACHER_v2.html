Module 1 Review
→

No photo, no prompt, no timer — just you and the language. Talk freely about Module 1. Answer in as many sentences as you can.

1
What is the most useful thing you learned in Module 1?
Answer in English, in full sentences. Take your time.
2
What was the most difficult session — S1, S2, S3, or S4? Why?
Try: "Session ___ was difficult because ___."
3
Have you used any English from this module at work this week?
Be specific: "I said 'Nice to meet you' to ___." or "I wrote my job title in English."
4
Introduce yourself completely — everything from Module 1 in one go.
Name · job · company · city · nationality · colleague · email. No notes.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Free warm-up is diagnostic — listen carefully. Note: which structures are automatic? Which still require effort?
  • Q4 (full introduction from memory) gives you the clearest picture before the formal review. Don't correct — just observe.
  • List errors quietly: use them to prioritise which grammar point to stress in §3.
  • Don't fill silences — let the student work through them. Productive struggle.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Today there's no photo, no scenario — just you and English. This is what it's like in a real meeting."
  • If student answers Q3 with a real work example: celebrate specifically. "You used English at work. That's the whole point of this programme."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "After today's session, Module 1 is complete. Module 2 opens your world — daily routines, schedules, and talking about your company."
  • Testimonial trigger: "How would you describe your English before Module 1 vs now, to a colleague who's thinking about starting?"

§ 03
Grammar Review
12–15 min
To Be — All Forms
I am / I'm notAm I…?
You are / aren'tAre you…?
He/She is / isn'tIs he/she…?
We/They are / aren'tAre they…?
A / AN + Possessives
A + consonanta manager, a doctor
AN + vowelan engineer, an accountant
My / YourMy name · Your email
His / HerHis job · Her office
Wh- Questions
WhatWhat is your job / email?
WhereWhere are you from / is your office?
WhoWho is your manager?
HowHow are you?
Short Answers
Are you Mexican?Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.
Is she a doctor?Yes, she is. / No, she isn't.
Are they here?Yes, they are. / No, they aren't.
⚠️ Never: "Yes, I'm." or "Yes, she's."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Use the reference cards as a study guide — student can refer to them during Activity 1 if needed. By Activity 2, encourage no reference.
  • Activity 2 (error correction) is the most diagnostic. Errors students miss here = concepts not yet internalised.
  • Activity 3 (matching): clickable logic is built in. If student asks to do it verbally instead, allow it — the production is the same.
  • After §3: summarise in 30 seconds which grammar point was strongest and which needs more practice in Module 2.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You now have 4 grammar tools: to be, a/an, possessives, and Wh- questions. That's your complete A1.1 toolkit."
  • If student gets Activity 2 fully correct: "You can now catch your own errors — that's advanced self-awareness."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In A1.2 you'll add simple present, 'can', and 'there is/are'. The grammar builds on everything you know."
§ 04
Reading
8–10 min
Read · ~120 words · A1.1 — Full Module 1 integration

My name is Ana García. I am a coordinator at Grupo Industrial Torres in Celaya, Mexico. I am Mexican. My company is an industrial manufacturing firm.

My manager is Carlos Ramírez. He is a director. He is not from Celaya — his city is Monterrey. His office is on the third floor.

This week, we have a visitor: David Chen. He is an engineer from Canada. He is Canadian. His email is david@engcorp.ca. David and Carlos are in a meeting today.

"What is your job, Ana?" — "I am a coordinator." — "Where are you from?" — "I am from Guadalajara, but my office is here in Celaya." — "Nice to meet you, Ana!" — "Nice to meet you too, David!"

Comprehension — Full Module 1
Answer from the text.
1. Ana is a director at Grupo Industrial Torres.
2. Carlos is from Monterrey.
3. David is American.
What is David's job?
Where is Ana's office?
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This text (~120 words) is the longest of the module — intentional for S5. Student should read it more fluently than S1's 80-word text.
  • Ask student to find and label in text: 3 nationalities, 3 job titles, 2 possessives, 2 Wh- questions. Full M1 grammar scan.
  • Q8 (where is Ana's office?): text says "I am from Guadalajara, but my office is here in Celaya" — students who skim will answer Guadalajara. A careful reading test.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Compare this to S1's reading — you just read 50% more text at the same pace. You've grown."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Module 2 texts will be 150+ words. Same structure, more content. You're ready."

GT Originals — Module 1 Highlights
🔴 False Friend: "I'm embarrassed" ≠ "Estoy embarazada" — still the #1 error at A1.1
🤝 In North American business: use first names from the first meeting. "Licenciado" doesn't translate
🔊 "colleague" /ˈkɒliːɡ/ · "engineer" /ˌendʒɪˈnɪər/ · "accountant" /əˈkaʊntənt/ — the pronunciation trio
💬 Survival phrase: "Bear with me — I'm still learning!" — works in any meeting
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Exit Task: mark checkboxes only when you observe each skill clearly. If an item is not demonstrated, note it and revisit in Module 2, Session 1.
  • Storytelling: listen without interrupting. After the story: give 2 specific pieces of positive feedback, then 1 correction.
  • If student cannot complete the story independently: scaffold with "And then? / Who is ___? / Where were you?" — Socratic questions only.
  • All 5 exit task items should be checked for module completion. If 3 or fewer are checked, recommend an extra session before Module 2.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You just told a story in English. Not a list of words — a story. That's the definition of communication."
  • "5 sessions. ~5 hours. You went from zero to professional English. Module 2 opens a completely new world."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Testimonial trigger: "If you had to recommend this programme to a colleague right now — what would you say? Say it in English."
  • Referral prompt: "Do you know anyone at your company who needs English for their international work? I'd love to help them."
  • Upsell — Module 2 preview: "Next module: daily routines, work verbs, and simple present. You'll describe your entire workday in English."
  • Celebrate explicitly: mark Module 1 as COMPLETE together. Make it a moment.
