🔥 Hot Take — Cold Start

5 min
No prep. Someone needs your advice — give it NOW.
Cold start: El alumno da consejos inmediatos. Si se bloquea: "Should she stay or leave? — Start there." Push para should + shouldn't + could. Corrige "I suggest you to" al final, no mid-utterance.

Andrea just texted you: "My manager gave me terrible feedback in my performance review. I feel bad and I don't know what to do." You have 60 seconds. Give her your best advice. GO!

§2

🎯 Pitch — Assigned Sides

10 min
Defend your side — even if you personally disagree
Topic: "You should always ask for what you want at work — a raise, a promotion, a day off." Teacher asigna lados. Push para should/shouldn't/could con razones en ambos. 90 seg por lado, luego 60 seg de debate.
🟣 Side A — ALWAYS ASK
"You should always ask for what you want at work. Defend this."
Starters:"You should be direct — if you don't ask, you'll never get it…""You could start by having a conversation with your manager…""You shouldn't wait — managers don't read minds…"
🔵 Side B — TIMING MATTERS
"You shouldn't always ask — timing is critical. Counter the argument."
Starters:"You should wait for the right moment — not every day is a good day…""You could prepare your case first before asking for a raise…""You shouldn't ask during a bad performance review — it could hurt you…"

🔄 Relay — Advice Strength Slider

8 min
How strong is your advice? Drag the slider — then say the sentence.
Nueva mecánica — Advice Strength Slider: El alumno arrastra el slider → pantalla muestra el modal correcto + oración de ejemplo. Teacher da una situación → alumno elige la fuerza apropiada → produce su oración propia.
Dinámica: "Situation 1 — which strength? Why?" → oral production immediate.
⚡ Advice Strength Slider
Drag to choose strength. Then produce your own advice sentence for each situation below.
GENTLE — couldSTANDARD — shouldSTRONG — must
should
You should talk to your manager. — A firm recommendation.
Use when you genuinely believe it is the right thing to do.
🎙️ Now Transform — Choose the strength, then speak
Situation 1 — New colleague, first week
"What advice do you give a new colleague on their first day?"
→ Choose gentle (could) or standard (should). Give 2 pieces of advice.
"You could introduce yourself to the team first." (gentle) / "You should arrive early and ask questions." (standard)
Situation 2 — Colleague always late
"Your colleague is late every day. The manager is very angry. What should they do?"
→ This needs STRONG advice. Should or must. Add a reason.
"You should talk to your manager before they take action." / "You shouldn't continue — it is seriously damaging your career."
Situation 3 — Fix the error
"I suggest you to prepare for the performance review."
→ Error! Fix it and say the correct version.
"You should prepare for the performance review." — Never "suggest + you + to"! Use should OR "I suggest preparing" (gerund).
Situation 4 — Soften the advice
"You must look for another job immediately."
→ Too strong. Make it gentler using could or should.
"You could start exploring other opportunities." / "You should think about your next career step."
Situation 5 — Your real life
"Think of one real challenge you have at work right now."
→ Give yourself advice: should + shouldn't + could. At least 3 sentences.
Open — "I should [action]. I shouldn't [bad habit I have]. I could [alternative I haven't tried]."

🔍 Source Analysis — Real Advice Messages

8 min
Read real messages. Find the advice language. Analyze the strength.
Dinámica: 4 fuentes reales en distintos formatos. Source 4 es metacognitiva — el alumno detecta qué estructura NO aparece en un texto cargado de advice language.
💬 WhatsApp — Friend's advice to Andrea
Andrea
My manager said my work wasn't good enough. I don't know what to do. Should I quit?
Friend
You shouldn't quit immediately. You could ask your manager for a meeting and request specific feedback. You should also talk to HR if the review was unfair. If I were you, I would wait one month and see if things improve.
❓ The friend uses three different modals. Which is the STRONGEST advice?
💼 LinkedIn Post — Career coach
"5 things you should do after a bad performance review: 1) You shouldn't react emotionally — wait 24 hours. 2) You should ask for specific examples. 3) You could request a follow-up meeting. 4) You shouldn't tell colleagues about it. 5) You should create an improvement plan."
❓ How many should / shouldn't / could are in this post?
📧 HR Email to Andrea
"Hi Andrea, after your review, we recommend you attend a training session next month. You could also speak with your direct manager about a development plan. You should not miss the follow-up meeting on November 15th — attendance is required."
❓ HR writes "You should not" instead of "You shouldn't." What is the difference?
🧠 Grammar Detective — Which modal is MISSING?
"If I were you, I would talk to your manager calmly. You could prepare a list of your achievements. You should explain how you feel — but you shouldn't be aggressive. My advice would be to ask for a specific action plan. Could you also speak to a mentor outside the company?"
❓ This text uses many advice structures. Which modal does NOT appear?
