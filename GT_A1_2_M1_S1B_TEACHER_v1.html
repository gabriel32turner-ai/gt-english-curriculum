Warm-Up ‚ÄĒ Your Workplace
Speaking activation ¬∑ 5 min ¬∑ Teacher-led Q&A
ūüďć Context ‚ÄĒ Visitor at the Door
A new colleague arrives at your company for the first time. They don't know where anything is. You are at the reception desk. They look confused. They need to find the kitchen, the meeting room, and the restroom ‚ÄĒ right now.

Teacher: Point to the student ‚ÄĒ you are the colleague. Ask naturally, no preparation needed.
Q1 ¬∑ Opener
"Tell me: where do you work? What floor is your desk on? Is there an elevator in your building?"
Q2 ¬∑ Locations
"Where is the kitchen in your building? Is it near your desk? What is there inside the kitchen ‚ÄĒ a coffee machine? A microwave?"
Q3 ¬∑ Facilities
"What facilities can you use at work? Is there a parking lot? A break room? A gym? Which ones can you use, and which ones can't you use?"
Q4 ¬∑ Tour prompt
"If I visit your workplace tomorrow, what should I know? Where is the main entrance? Where is the restroom?"
Capa 1 ¬∑ Pedagogical
  • Listen for: "is there" vs "are there" confusion, missing articles (a/an/the), and preposition errors ("in the second floor" ‚Üí "on the second floor").
  • Do NOT pre-teach prepositions. Let the student attempt description first ‚ÄĒ their natural errors here are the exact targets for ¬ß4.
  • If the student can't describe their workplace at all: accept "There is a desk. There is a kitchen." as minimal production. That's enough to activate the schema.
  • Warm-up is diagnostic ‚ÄĒ gather 2‚Äď3 real error examples to reference explicitly in ¬ß4 Grammar.
Capa 2 ¬∑ Engagement
  • "Close your eyes. Picture your workplace. Now describe it to me as if I've never been there." This visualization technique produces more authentic language than Q&A alone.
  • If the student is confident: "Give me a 30-second tour ‚ÄĒ go." Time them. Creates urgency and fluency pressure.
Capa 3 ¬∑ Growth & Retention
  • Connect explicitly to S1A: "Last session you told me you attend meetings on Mondays. Where is that meeting room? Describe it to me."
  • End with: "After this session, you'll be able to give someone a complete tour of your workplace in English ‚ÄĒ every location, every facility, every floor."

Capa 1 ¬∑ Pedagogical
  • For each location: student reads word ‚Üí IPA check ‚Üí reads context note ‚Üí produces their own true sentence about their actual workplace.
  • Key distinction to drill: lobby (entrance/reception) vs break room (sala de descanso). L1 Spanish speakers confuse these or use "cafeteria" for both.
  • Restroom vs bathroom: in professional/office English, "restroom" is standard. "Bathroom" is more domestic. Worth flagging.
  • Prepositions strip: test immediately. "Your kitchen ‚ÄĒ is it 'next to' or 'near' your office? What's the difference?" Student must articulate the distance distinction.
  • Facility items strip: ask student to assign each item to a location. "Where is the printer in your building? In the office or in the kitchen?"
Capa 2 ¬∑ Engagement
  • "Pick 3 locations and 3 facilities. Build real sentences about your workplace using the preposition strip." Student produces 3 sentences. You produce the 3 follow-up questions.
  • "Tell me one location you DON'T have in your building." Uses There isn't a ___ in my building. ‚ÄĒ reinforces negative form naturally.
Capa 3 ¬∑ Growth & Retention
  • Flag: elevator (US) / lift (UK). If student works with UK English clients, note the difference. Same for parking lot / car park.
  • The preposition "near" takes NO "to" ‚ÄĒ "near to the elevator" is a very common Mexican English error. Correct proactively.

‚ö†ÔłŹ Common A1.2 Errors ‚ÄĒ Locations
‚úó "The kitchen is in the second floor."
‚úď "The kitchen is ON the second floor."
Floors use ON, not in. Think of a surface you stand on.
‚úó "The restroom is near to the elevator."
‚úď "The restroom is near the elevator."
"Near" is never followed by "to" in standard English.
‚úó "Is there any printer in the office?"
‚úď "Is there A printer in the office?"
Singular questions use "a", not "any". "Any" is for plurals.
‚úó "There is three meeting rooms."
‚úď "There ARE three meeting rooms."
Numbers > 1 are always plural ‚Üí use "are".
‚úó "Between the office and the kitchen."
‚úď "Between the office AND the kitchen."
"Between" always needs "and" to connect the two reference points.

ūüĒĎ Key Rules ‚ÄĒ Locations & Facilities
‚ĖłUse There IS for singular things and There ARE for plural ‚ÄĒ never swap them.
‚ĖłFloors always use ON: "on the first floor", "on the third floor" ‚ÄĒ never "in".
