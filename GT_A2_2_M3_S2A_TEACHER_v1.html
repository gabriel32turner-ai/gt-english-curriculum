⚡ Warm-Up — Quick Fire

5 min
📍 Stress, sleep, balance — do you take your own advice?
⚡ Quick Win — 20 sec
Say this out loud: "You should take a break because you're exhausted." — Now add YOUR real reason. That's giving advice with a because!
🎯 Objetivo: Activar conversación sobre bienestar y hábitos laborales. Muy relevante para profesionales en manufactura del Bajío — trabajo por turnos, estrés, poco descanso. La gramática (should + because) emerge de forma natural.
Nota: Si dicen "you must rest" → anota. §5 cubre should vs must.
Q1 T→S
Do you have good work-life balance? What do you sacrifice?
Q2 T→S
What is one thing you know you should do for your health — but don't?
Q3 S→S
Your partner looks exhausted. Give them 3 pieces of advice using should/shouldn't.
Q4 S→S
Is it easy or difficult to follow advice? Tell your partner about a time you ignored good advice.
Layer 1: ¿Dan razones? "You should sleep more" → push: "…because WHY?"
Layer 2: ¿Confunden "should" con "must"? Anota para §5.
Layer 3: "Why is it hard to follow your own advice?"

🗣️ Speaking — Andrea's Wellbeing Crisis

8 min
Andrea is working 12-hour days, skipping meals, and can't sleep. What should she do?

Help Andrea — She's Burning Out

Andrea has been working 12-hour days for 3 weeks. She skips lunch, sleeps 5 hours, and hasn't exercised in a month. Her manager noticed she looks exhausted. What should she do?

Round 1 · 60 seg
T→S "Give Andrea 3 pieces of advice. Use 'should' and add a REASON for each one."
Round 2 · 90 seg
S→S "Should Andrea talk to her manager? What should she say? What shouldn't she say?"
Round 3 · 90 seg
Class "Why don't we all share one bad work habit? Then give advice to the person next to you."
Model Answer

"Andrea, you should talk to your manager because this situation is not sustainable. You shouldn't skip meals — it's not worth it. Why don't you block your lunch hour in your calendar? You could also try leaving the office at 7 PM maximum. Have you thought about working from home one day a week? You should set clear limits because burnout is much worse than a delayed project."

§4

💬 Colloquial — Advice That Sounds Natural

5 min
Sound like a native English speaker when giving advice
Have you thought about…?
When: making a gentle, open suggestion
"Have you thought about asking your manager for more support?"
It's not worth it.
When: telling someone something isn't a good idea
"Don't work all weekend. It's not worth it — you need to rest."
My advice would be…
When: giving formal, professional advice
"My advice would be to talk to HR about the workload situation."
Why don't you / we…?
When: making an informal, friendly suggestion
"Why don't we have a quick meeting to reorganize the priorities?"
Drill rápido: Teacher da situación → alumno produce frase coloquial. "Andrea no duerme bien. ¿Qué le dices?" → "Have you thought about going to bed earlier?" / "Tu colega trabaja el sábado. ¿Qué le dices?" → "It's not worth it!"
§5

🎯 Grammar — Should with Reasons & Questions

10 min
Good advice always has a reason — "You should… BECAUSE…"
REFERENCIAL. S2 builds on S1's basic should/shouldn't. New focus: adding reasons, short answers, should questions, and "Why don't you/we". Same wellbeing context as warm-up.
Key upgrade: Without reasons, advice sounds weak. With "because", it sounds professional. Drill this today.
📊 The Advice Ladder — From Gentle to Strong
Gentle
Could / Why don't you…?
"You could try sleeping earlier." / "Why don't you take a break?"
Advice
Should + reason
"You should rest because you need energy for tomorrow."
Strong
Shouldn't — it's a bad idea
"You shouldn't work tonight because it's damaging your health."
Obligation
Must / Mustn't
"You must see a doctor." (rule / serious — NOT just advice)
FormStructureWellbeing Example
Should + reasonshould + base + because + reasonYou should sleep more because sleep affects concentration.
Should questionShould + I/we + base verb?Should I talk to my manager today? — Yes, you should.
Short answersYes, you should. / No, you shouldn't."Should I skip lunch to finish?" — "No, you shouldn't."
Why don't you…?Why don't + you/we + base verb?Why don't you block your calendar for lunch every day?
Could (gentle)could + base verbYou could try a 10-minute walk after lunch.
⚠️ SHOULD vs MUST — Never confuse
should = my opinion / recommendation ("You should sleep more") — you can choose to ignore it
must = obligation, rule, no choice ("You must wear safety equipment") — not optional

🎧 Listening — Andrea Gets Advice

8 min
📍 Andrea meets with a career coach at ConnectPro to talk about her burnout
🎙️
ElevenLabs — "Andrea's Coaching Session"
Voice: Sarah · Speed: 0.82 · A2.2 M3 S2A
Coach: Andrea, you mentioned you're feeling exhausted. Should I be worried about you?

Andrea: Honestly, yes. I've been working 12-hour days for three weeks. I don't have any energy.

Coach: OK. My first recommendation is this: you should set a hard stop time every day — 7 PM maximum — because your brain needs time to recover. Why don't you put it in your calendar right now?

Andrea: I could try that. But I always have emails after 7.

Coach: You shouldn't check work email after 7 because it prevents your brain from switching off. That's the problem. You could use a different phone profile — work mode off after 7.

Andrea: And the stress? Should I talk to my manager about the workload?

Coach: Yes, you should — and you should prepare examples because managers need data, not just feelings. Say: "I'm managing X projects with Y resources. My recommendation is to either hire help or delay project C." That's professional advice, not a complaint.

Andrea: Should I mention that I'm feeling burned out?

Coach: You could mention it carefully. But my advice would be to focus on solutions, not problems. And one more thing: you should sleep seven hours because sleep is your most important productivity tool. Everything else follows from that.
