Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Q4 is the most diagnostic: can student say today's full date in English? Note errors in: ordinal (22nd vs "22"), month name, preposition ("on the 22nd of April" — not "in the 22").
  • Don't correct yet — just observe. All errors in Q4 are addressed directly in §3 and §4.
  • Bridge from S1: "In S1 you said HOW OFTEN. Today we add WHEN — specific dates."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "What's your most stressful deadline right now? Can you say the date in English? By the end of today you can."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Every email you send to an international client will include a date. This session is one of the most practical in the whole programme."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Key pronunciation traps: "February" /ˈfebrueri/ (silent 1st R), "Wednesday" /ˈwenzdeɪ/ (silent D), "budget" /ˈbʌdʒɪt/ (not "bood-JET").
  • Months drill: teacher says a number (1–12), student says the month. Then reverse. Then "What month is Q1? Q2? Q3? Q4?"
  • "Quarter" — clarify: Q1=Jan–Mar, Q2=Apr–Jun, Q3=Jul–Sep, Q4=Oct–Dec. Use student's real company context.
  • "Agenda" false friend: in Spanish "agenda" = planner. In English "agenda" = list of topics for a meeting. Note the difference.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "What quarter is the most stressful at your company? Say it in English: 'Q___ is always the hardest because the deadline is in ___.' "
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention

GT Memory Rule — Think by size:
AT = smallest (exact moment: 9 AM) → ON = medium (a day: Monday / 20th) → IN = largest (a month or year: March / 2025)
⚠ Most Common Errors — Correct immediately
❌ "in Monday"→✅ ON Monday
❌ "on March"→✅ IN March
❌ "in the 15th"→✅ ON the 15th
❌ "the deadline is in Friday"→✅ The deadline is ON Friday
❌ "the meeting is on the morning"→✅ The meeting is IN the morning

      § 05
      Reading
      8–10 min
      FROM
      Sarah Mitchell <s.mitchell@novatech.mx>
      TO
      Team Celaya
      SUBJECT
      Q2 Key Dates — Please save to your calendar
      DATE
      Monday, 31 March 2025

      Hi team,

      I'm writing to share our updated agenda for Q2. Please save these key dates in your calendar.

      The first team review is on Monday, the 7th of April. The agenda is already in your email — please read it before the review starts.

      The client presentation is on April 22nd at 10 AM. This is a very important date. Please confirm your attendance before the 15th.

      Our project deadline is on May 30th. All reports must be ready before that due date. We have a budget review in the last week of May to check progress.

      The final delivery to the client is in June — specifically, on the 3rd. The contract for this project also ends in June.

      Remember: the office is closed on May 1st for Labour Day.

      Thank you — see you on Monday!

      Sarah Mitchell
      Project Manager · NovaTech Solutions · Ext. 204
      Comprehension
      Answer the questions.
      When is the first team review?
      What time is the client presentation?
      When is the project deadline?
      The office is closed on May 1st for Labour Day.
      The final delivery to the client is in May.
      The team must confirm attendance by April 15th.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Email format is deliberate — this is exactly the type of email students will receive from international contacts.
      • Ask student to highlight all prepositions of time (green = ON, blue = IN, red = AT). Count: 9+ instances.
      • TR2 trap: "final delivery is in June" — students often say TRUE because they see "June" but miss that the text says "in June, specifically on the 3rd." NOT in May.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Does Sarah's email look similar to emails you receive at work? Can you read it faster than in S1's reading? You can."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "In A2, you'll write emails like this. In A1.2, you read and understand them. That's your current target — and you just hit it."

      ▶ Show ElevenLabs Script

      Good morning, everyone. Thanks for joining the call. I just want to go over a few important dates for this month.

      First, the team training is on Thursday, the tenth. Please arrive at nine AM — we start on time, so don't be late.

      ⏱ Target: 75–85 sec · Voice: Sarah · Speed: 0.80 · Stability: 0.6

      Listening Activities
      Listen, then answer.
      When is the team training?
      Where is the quarterly review?
      The training starts at 9 AM.
      The delivery is on the 22nd — a Wednesday.
      The quarterly review starts at 2 PM.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • LL2 trap: the script says "that's a Monday" — so the 22nd is a Monday, NOT a Wednesday. Students who don't listen carefully to the detail will answer TRUE.
      • Second listen: focus on numbers. "She says 'tenth', 'twenty-second', 'thirty-first' — what do you hear?"
      • Shadow exercise: repeat each sentence after the speaker, paying attention to ordinal pronunciation.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Sarah speaks at a professional meeting pace. By S5, you'll follow this at 100% speed without effort."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "Homework: listen 3× and write down every date you hear. Check your answers against the script. Perfect ordinal practice."

      • ON = days/dates · IN = months/years · AT = clock times.
      • I know ordinal numbers: 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th… 31st.
      • I know 8 new words: deadline, agenda, contract, quarter…
      • I can read and understand a professional email with dates.
      Key Summary
      ON · IN · AT
      on Monday · on the 15th   in March · in Q2   at 9 AM
      Ordinals
      1st · 2nd · 3rd · 4th–20th (+th) · 21st · 22nd · 23rd…
      Remember
      Date format in business: April 15, 2025 (US) or 15 April 2025 (international) — never 04/15 or 15/04 in emails

      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Presentation: give student 90 seconds to mentally prepare using their real calendar. Then speak for 60–90 seconds minimum.
      • Evaluate: ON/IN/AT correct? Ordinals used? At least 5 sentences? Real dates used?
      • Error correction: wait until the presentation is complete. Then give 1 correction maximum — the most important one.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "You just presented your work calendar in English. A skill most of your colleagues don't have."
      • "What was easier — ON, IN, or AT? Which do you need to practice more?"
