⚠ No -ed Rule — These Must Be Memorized
go → went ·  have → had ·  make → made ·  give → gave ·  meet → met ·  take → took ·  write → wrote ·  get → got
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Memory patterns — teach these in pairs: meet/met · get/got (short vowel change); make/made · give/gave · write/wrote (vowel+d); go/went (completely different); have/had · take/took (internal change).
  • Drill method: teacher says BASE, student says PAST immediately. Then reverse. Then teacher says PAST, student says BASE. Speed is the goal.
  • "had" is the most important — "I had a meeting / I had a call / I had a problem" covers 30% of daily work reporting.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Which of these 8 did you use this week? Make a true sentence — I'll time you: 5 seconds per verb." — creates urgency without pressure.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention

§ 04
Grammar — Irregulars + Wh- Questions
15 min
Irregular Past — All 4 Forms
FormStructureExample — irregular verb
AffirmativeSubject + irregular pastShe went to Dallas. / We had a meeting. / They made a deal.
NegativeSubject + didn't + BASE verbShe didn't go. / We didn't have time. / They didn't make a decision.
Yes/No QDid + subject + BASE verb?Did she go? / Did you have a meeting? / Did they make a deal?
Wh- QWh- + did + subject + BASE?Where did she go? / Who did you meet? / What did they make?
Critical rule: Didn't and Did always take the BASE verb — never the irregular past form.
❌ "She didn't went" → ✅ She didn't go
❌ "Where did she went?" → ✅ Where did she go?
Wh- Questions in Simple Past
What

"What did you do at the meeting?" 🔊

"Where did you go last week?" 🔊

"Who did you meet at the conference?" 🔊

"When did they arrive at the office?" 🔊

"How did the presentation go?" 🔊

"Why did she go to Dallas?" 🔊
⚠ Most Common Errors — S3
❌ "I didn't went to the meeting."→✅ I didn't go. (base after didn't)
❌ "Where did she went?"→✅ Where did she go? (base after did)
❌ "He maked a decision yesterday."→✅ He made a decision. (irregular — must memorize)
❌ "They didn't had time for the report."→✅ They didn't have time. (base verb after didn't)

      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Q3 is the Wh- question diagnostic — "did you go" vs "did you went". Both forms wrong in the distractors. If student chooses "went you" — they're applying Spanish word order (¿fuiste tú?).
      • After activities: oral Wh- drilling. Teacher describes a scenario in one sentence. Student asks a Wh- question about it. "Carlos went somewhere last week." → "Where did he go?" Fast rounds.
      • Drag & Drop: both are Wh- questions. Note that Word ORDER matters — Wh- always goes first.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "I'll make a statement. You ask me a Wh- question about it. Ready? 'I went somewhere interesting last week.' — What's your question?"
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention

      § 05
      Reading — Trip Report
      8–10 min
      FROM
      Carlos Mendoza <c.mendoza@nortek.mx>
      TO
      Ana Rivera — VP Sales
      ✈️ Trip Report — Dallas Sales Conference
      Carlos Mendoza
      Sales Manager · Grupo Industrial Nortek
      Watch for: irregular past verbs (went/had/made/gave/met) · Wh- question forms · negatives · outcomes

      Hi Ana,

      Here is my summary of the Dallas Sales Conference last week.

      On Monday, I went to the airport at 5 AM and took the early flight to Dallas. I met our regional contact, Jim Carter, at the hotel. We had lunch and discussed the Q4 strategy.

      On Tuesday, I gave our main presentation to 40 participants. The response was very positive — we got excellent feedback from three potential clients. After the presentation, I met with the Director of Operations and we made an agreement to schedule a follow-up call next month.

      On Wednesday, I wrote a summary of all the conversations and took notes on the action items. I didn't have time for the networking dinner — I had an early flight the next morning.

      Overall, the trip was very productive. I can answer your questions in our Monday call. What did you want to know about the presentation?

      Carlos Mendoza · Sales Manager · Ext. 108
      Comprehension — Wh- Questions
      Answer using information from the trip report.
      Where did Carlos go last week?
      Who did Carlos meet at the hotel?
      What did Carlos make with the Director of Operations?
      Carlos gave a presentation to 40 participants on Tuesday.
      Carlos attended the networking dinner on Wednesday evening.
      They got positive feedback from three potential clients.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Comprehension questions are written as Wh- questions — this mirrors the grammar of §4. Student reads the text and answers Wh- questions naturally.
      • Irregular verb count: went, took, met, had x2, gave, got, met (again), made, wrote, took (again), didn't have, had. Count with student: 12+ uses in one email.
      • R2 trap: "didn't have time for the networking dinner" — clear negative. Students who don't read carefully answer TRUE.
      • After: "Can you ask me 3 Wh- questions about Carlos's trip?" — reverse production: student generates the questions.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Have you ever written a trip report in English? After today you have the grammar for it."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "In Moodle, there's a writing task: write your own 5-sentence trip report using at least 4 irregular verbs. That's your homework."

      Ana: Perfect. Did you have time for the networking dinner?

      Ana: I'll read your report today. Great work, Carlos.

      Listening Activities
      Listen and answer — note the Wh- questions.
      Who did Carlos meet on Monday?

      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Focus task: "Count how many Wh- questions Ana asks." (Answer: 5 — How, Who, What, And what about, When + Did you). This builds awareness of the frequency of Wh- questions in real conversation.
      • Role-play: after listening, student plays Carlos. Teacher plays Ana and asks the same Wh- questions. Student answers from memory using irregular past verbs.
      • LQ3: "Wednesday evening" — students who only skim for "wrote" might choose "Monday night" or "on the flight home". Specific detail listening.
      Capa 2 · Engagement

      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention

      Today I learned…
      • 8 irregular past forms: went · had · made · gave · met · took · wrote · got.
      • Negative: didn't + BASE — NEVER the irregular form. "Didn't go" NOT "didn't went".
      • Wh- questions: What/Where/Who/When/How/Why + did + subject + BASE.
      • I can write a trip report using irregular past verbs.
      • I can answer Wh- questions about my past work experiences.
