How often do you check your email?

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • El timer corre 30 segundos por pregunta — el maestro controla el ritmo con START/RESET. Presiona START al hacer Q1, luego START de nuevo para Q2, etc.
  • Si el alumno responde antes de 30s: ¡excelente! Haz clic en la tarjeta y empieza la siguiente. Si se acaba el tiempo: anota qué no pudo decir — abórdalo en la sesión.
  • Q2 (fecha de hoy) es el repaso de S2. Q4 (hobby + frecuencia) es repaso de S1. Ambas deben ser automáticas a estas alturas.
  • Haz 2 rondas si el tiempo lo permite — la velocidad mejora significativamente en la ronda 2.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Ronda 2 — más rápido. Tu cerebro ya sabe estas respuestas. Confía en él."
  • Usa el timer como juego: "¡Beat the clock!" — crea presión positiva sin estrés.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Nota qué tan rápido llegan esas respuestas. En S1 cada pregunta tomaba 30 segundos. Hoy respondiste en 10. Así se construye la fluidez."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Las 8 palabras son nuevas — sin repetición de S1 o S2. Confirma con el alumno: "¿Cuál de estas es completamente nueva para ti?"

  • "progress" — dos pronunciaciones: sustantivo /ˈprɒɡres/ vs verbo /prəˈɡres/. En A1.2, solo sustantivo.
  • Expresiones de frecuencia: nota que cada oración usa HE/SHE (3ª persona). Esto conecta directamente con §4 gramática.

§ 04
Grammar — 3rd Person Simple Present
12–15 min
Rule — He / She / It + verb + S
In simple present, he / she / it always adds -s (or -es / -ies) to the verb.
I work → She works · You send → He sends · We check → It checks
I / You / We / They
work
"I work on Mondays."
"They send reports weekly."
He / She / It
works
"She works on Mondays."
"He sends reports weekly."
Special endings
-es / -ies
go → goes
study → studies
do → does
How often does she…? — 3rd person questions
QuestionShort answerFull answer
How often does she send the report? Once a week. She sends the report once a week.
How often does he check the targets? Every day. He checks the targets every day.
How often does the team meet? Twice a month. The team meets twice a month.
How often does she travel? Rarely. She rarely travels for work.
⚠ Most Common Errors — Correct immediately
❌ "She send the report every week."→✅ She sends the report every week.
❌ "How often does she sends?"→✅ How often does she send? (no -s in questions)
❌ "She don't travel often."→✅ She doesn't travel often.
Activity 1 — Choose the Correct Verb Form
Haz clic en la opción correcta.
She ___ a progress report every Monday.
How often ___ Carlos check his targets?
The team ___ performance three times a year.
She ___ travel often — maybe once a quarter.
How often does she give a presentation? — "She ___ every month."
Activity 2 — Sentence Order
Arrastra las piezas para formar la oración correcta.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Q2 es la clave: "How often DOES Carlos check" — nota que en la pregunta el verbo pierde la -s (check, no checks). Esto sorprende a los alumnos. Explica: does lleva el marcador de 3ª persona en su lugar.
      • Q3 error: "review" vs "reviews" — the team = it = 3ª persona. Los alumnos frecuentemente tratan sustantivos colectivos como plural (I/we) y olvidan la -s.
      • Después de las actividades: producción oral. El maestro da sujeto + frecuencia: "Carlos — every week." El alumno forma la oración completa: "Carlos sends a report every week."
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Every time you talk about a colleague in English, you'll use this -s. It's a tiny rule with a giant impact."
      • Señala al colega del alumno: "Tell me about your manager using today's grammar — 3 sentences."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "In A2 you'll describe routines using 'used to' and future plans. But the -s rule stays forever in simple present."
      § 05
      Reading — Carlos Reyes: My Work Week
      8–10 min
      Carlos Reyes
      Operations Manager · Grupo Industrial Nortek · Monterrey, Mexico

      My name is Carlos Reyes. I am the Operations Manager at Grupo Industrial Nortek. Let me tell you about my typical work week.

      I always arrive at the office at 8 AM. Every morning, I check my emails and organize my tasks for the day. I usually have a short call with my team at 8:30 — a 15-minute standup to check progress.

      My coordinator, Ana, sends me a weekly update every Monday. She also prepares the conference call agenda twice a month. Ana always meets her targets — her performance is excellent.

      The team gives a presentation to senior management once a quarter. I never miss these — they are very important for our targets.

      I rarely work on weekends. I finish at 6 PM. A good work-life balance is important!

      Comprehension
      Responde las preguntas.
      How often does Ana send Carlos an update?
      How often does the team give a presentation?
      Carlos never arrives at the office before 9 AM.
      Ana prepares the conference call agenda twice a month.
      Carlos often works on weekends.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Los verbos en 3ª persona están resaltados en verde en el texto — pide al alumno que encuentre y lea en voz alta TODAS las formas verbales resaltadas.
      • Trampa de comprensión: RQ1 — "twice a month" también está en el texto (conference call). Los alumnos que leen superficialmente la eligen. Lee con cuidado.
      • Después de la comprensión: "Describe a Ana en 3ª persona. ¿Qué hace? ¿Con qué frecuencia?" — transferencia de producción usando el texto como modelo.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "¿La rutina de Carlos se parece a la tuya? Describe una diferencia en 3ª persona: 'My manager ___ every ___.' "
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "Este es exactamente el tipo de texto que escribirás sobre tu propia rutina en S5."

      § 07
      Wrap-Up
      5 min
      Today I learned…
      • He/She/It + verb-s: she sends, he checks, the team meets.
      • Questions: "How often does she send the report?"
      • Negative: "She doesn't travel often." (not "don't")
      • I know 8 new words: report, task, target, performance…
      • I can describe my colleague's routine in English.
      Key Summary
      3rd Person -S
      She sends · He checks · The team meets
      Question form
      "How often does she send?" → no -s in question
      Remember
      Does = question · Doesn't = negative. Both carry the 3rd person so the main verb loses its -s.
