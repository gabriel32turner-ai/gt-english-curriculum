S1 — Hot Take
Was It Worth It?
60-second timed opinion · was/were production under pressure · Valeria's dilemma
5 MIN
Valeria Torres — Solaris Manufacturing, Celaya
"The supplier review meeting was 3 hours long. Only 4 of 6 suppliers were present. The final decision wasn't made. Was the meeting worth it — or was it a waste of time?"
You have 60 seconds. Give your opinion using was/were. Was it productive? Were the attendees engaged? Was the outcome useful, even if incomplete? Defend your position with at least 3 sentences.
🕑
60 secondsSpeak continuously. No pausing to translate. Use was/were at least 3 times.
💬
Full sentences"The meeting was... / There were... / It wasn't..." — complete grammar required.
🏆
Badge earnedUse was/were correctly 3+ times in 60 seconds → badge unlocked!
60s
Hot Take Timer
"The meeting was worth it because..." OR "The meeting wasn't worth it because..."
💡 Energy note: This is a hot take — there's no wrong answer. Push students to COMMIT to a position and DEFEND it. Anyone who says "it depends" loses a point. Force them to choose a side and use was/were under pressure. That's the real goal.
S2 — Pitch Round
The Great Meeting Debate
Two sides · was/were in argument context · Professional persuasion
10 MIN
📚 Setup: Divide the group into two sides. Each side has 3 minutes to prepare their argument using the starters below. Then each side presents for 90 seconds. Audience (or teacher) votes on who used was/were most accurately and naturally.
SIDE A — The Meeting Was Worth It
"Even though the decision wasn't made, the meeting was valuable because..."
  • "The meeting was necessary because the data wasn't shared before."
  • "There were 10 people — that kind of alignment was important."
  • "The action points were clear and the follow-up was confirmed."
  • "Even though it wasn't perfect, the outcome was useful."
SIDE B — The Meeting Was a Waste
"The meeting was too long and the result wasn't worth 3 hours because..."
  • "Two key suppliers weren't present — the meeting wasn't complete."
  • "The agenda wasn't followed — it was disorganized."
  • "There were too many people who weren't prepared."
  • "The decision wasn't made — that was the only goal."
90s
Pitch Timer — 90 seconds per side
Present your argument. Partner counts every correct was/were. Highest count wins!
S3 — Relay
Meeting Chain Relay
New Visual · Transform 5 present-tense meeting situations into past using was/were + Simple Past
8 MIN
Interactive Relay — Meeting Chain
Transform Each Situation to the Past
Each card shows a CURRENT meeting situation. Transform it to the past using was/were + Simple Past. Say your answer out loud before clicking to reveal. Work through the chain in sequence — each situation builds on the previous one.
Relay 1 — The Opening
PRESENT: "The meeting starts at 2 PM. There are 10 people. The agenda is clear."
💬 Say it in the PAST. What was the situation when Valeria wrote her report on Monday?
"The meeting started at 2 PM. There were 10 people. The agenda was clear."
Past: started / were / was
Relay 2 — The Problem
PRESENT: "Two suppliers are absent. The representatives aren't prepared. The data isn't accurate."
💬 Transform to past. What did Valeria report about the attendees?
"Two suppliers were absent. The representatives weren't prepared. The data wasn't accurate."
Past: were / weren't / wasn't
Relay 3 — The Discussion
PRESENT: "The team discusses the problem for 2 hours. The meeting is productive but difficult."
💬 Transform to past. Valeria tells Daniel what happened during the discussion.
"The team discussed the problem for 2 hours. The meeting was productive but difficult."
Past: discussed / was
Relay 4 — The Result
PRESENT: "The final decision isn't made. The outcome is useful but incomplete. Three action points are assigned."
💬 Transform to past. This is the core of Valeria's report to Daniel.
"The final decision wasn't made. The outcome was useful but incomplete. Three action points were assigned."
Past: wasn't / was / were
Relay 5 — The Follow-Up ⭐ Challenge
PRESENT: "The minutes are sent. The follow-up meeting is confirmed for next Friday. Valeria is satisfied with the outcome."
💬 Transform EVERYTHING to past AND add a negative question: "Wasn't the decision made?"
"The minutes were sent. The follow-up was confirmed for next Friday. Valeria was satisfied."
Neg. Q: "Wasn't the decision made? — No, it wasn't."
Past: were / was / was + wasn't neg question
⭐ Scoring tip: Award 1 point per correct was/were transformation. Maximum 12 points across all 5 relays. Students who reach 10+ are ready for the Module 3 exit task. Add scores to the leaderboard in S7.

S5 — Solo Challenge
Valeria's Full Report
2-minute timed solo production · Complete meeting report · was/were + Simple Past throughout
8 MIN
Solo Challenge — Valeria Torres, Solaris Manufacturing
You are Valeria Torres. Your manager Daniel just called you and asked: "Tell me everything about Friday's supplier meeting. Don't leave anything out." Give a complete spoken report in 2 minutes. No notes.
  • Say what time the meeting was and how long it lasted
  • Describe who was there and who wasn't present
  • Explain what was discussed and what wasn't resolved
  • Describe the outcome and the follow-up plan
  • Use was/were at least 6 times. Use at least 1 negative question.

S6 — Source Analysis
Real Meeting Documents
4 post-meeting communications · Analyse was/were in authentic business writing
8 MIN
💬 WhatsApp — Team Group
"Hi team, the Friday meeting was very productive! There were 3 big decisions. The suppliers weren't happy with the timeline but the outcome was positive. Follow-up is next Friday."
❓ Underline every was/were. Are they all correct? Would you write this differently in a formal email?
💌 Email — Meeting Minutes Summary
"Dear team, please find attached the minutes from Friday's supplier review. The meeting was scheduled for 1 hour but lasted 3. There were 10 attendees. Two suppliers were absent. The final decision was postponed."
❓ This is formal writing. How does was/were work here vs the WhatsApp? What's different about the tone?
👤 Spoken — Hallway Report
"So, how was it? — It was a mess, honestly. There were too many people and the agenda wasn't clear. But the action points were good. Wasn't the director there? — No, he wasn't. That was the problem."
❓ Find the negative question. Find all negative forms. How does spoken English handle was/were differently from written?
📄 LinkedIn Post — Professional Update
"Proud of the team today. The supplier review was challenging but the outcome was exactly what we needed. There were 10 professionals in that room and every voice was heard. Wasn't easy, but it was worth it."
❓ How is was/were used to build a professional narrative here? Why does the writer choose these specific details?
💡 Metacognitive question: Ask students: "Which of these 4 uses of was/were felt most natural to you? Which was hardest to understand? Why?" This builds awareness of register (formal vs informal) — a key B1.1 preparation skill.

3 Phrases to Use in Your Next Meeting
PHRASE 1
"There were [n] attendees — two weren't present."
Use this in any post-meeting email or report. It instantly tells your manager who was there and who wasn't — the two most important facts after every meeting. Natural, professional, and precise.
PHRASE 2
"Wasn't the deadline already confirmed?"
The negative question — one of the most powerful tools in a meeting. Use it to challenge something politely, to clarify information, or to recall what was agreed. It shows confidence and precision.
PHRASE 3
"The outcome was positive, but the decision wasn't final."
The perfect summary sentence for any inconclusive meeting. Balanced, honest, and professional. Use it in your next follow-up email and your manager will notice the difference immediately.
