💬 Colloquial — Giving Advice Naturally

5 min
How native speakers actually give advice — learn these phrases
If I were you…
When: giving strong personal advice
"If I were you, I would update my CV this weekend."
Have you thought about…?
When: making a gentle suggestion
"Have you thought about asking your manager for help?"
It's worth trying.
When: encouraging someone to try something
"A new job? It's worth trying. You should apply."
My advice would be…
When: offering professional advice formally
"My advice would be to prepare three strong examples for the interview."
Práctica rápida: "Tu amigo quiere pedir un aumento pero le da miedo. ¿Qué le dices?" → "If I were you, I would talk to my manager." / "It's worth trying."
Nota: "If I were you" es un chunk fijo a A2.2 — NO es second conditional (B1.2). No expandir.
§5

🎯 Grammar — Should / Shouldn't / Could

10 min
Should = recommendation · Must = obligation · Could = polite suggestion
REFERENCIAL. Lee la tabla, da un ejemplo personal, activa el Poll.
Error #1: "I suggest you to call" → You should call. Drill con escenarios reales.
Key contrast: "You should prepare" (recommendation) vs "You must wear a helmet" (obligation from M2). Students WILL confuse these.
FormStructureWork Example
Should (advice)Subject + should + base verbYou should update your CV before the interview.
Shouldn't (negative)Subject + shouldn't + base verbYou shouldn't promise something you can't deliver.
Should…? (question)Should + subject + base verb?Should I call or send an email? — You should call.
Could (suggestion)Subject + could + base verbYou could try networking at the conference.
Why don't you…?Why don't you/we + base verb?Why don't you talk to your manager about the workload?
⚖️ SHOULD vs MUST (from M2)
should = recommendation (my opinion, good idea) — "You should prepare examples."
must = strong obligation (rule, law, no choice) — "You must wear safety equipment."

⚠️ ✗ "I suggest you to update" → ✅ "You should update" — no "to" after should!

🎧 Listening — Andrea's Career Dilemma

8 min
📍 Andrea talks to a mentor about her career options
🎙️
ElevenLabs — "Andrea's Career Dilemma"
Voice: Sarah · Speed: 0.82 · A2.2 M3 S1A
Andrea: I have a question. I got an offer for a new job at a multinational company in Querétaro. But I also have a chance for a promotion at my current company. I don't know what to do. What do you think?

Mentor: That's a good problem to have! First, you should think about what you want in your career. Do you want new challenges or stability?

Andrea: I think I want to grow. But I'm afraid of change.

Mentor: You shouldn't be afraid. Change is normal in a career. My advice would be: make a list of the pros and cons. You could also talk to someone who works at the new company. Have you thought about that?

Andrea: No, I haven't. That's a good idea. Should I tell my current manager about the offer?

Mentor: Not yet. You shouldn't mention it until you make a decision. But you should prepare for both options. Update your CV, practice for the interview, and also think about what you would want in a promotion — a raise, a new title, or more responsibility?

Andrea: OK. I'll make the list this weekend. Thank you for the advice.

Mentor: You're welcome. And remember: whatever you decide, it should feel right for you. If I were you, I would follow my instinct.
