§ 01
🔥 Hot Take — Cold Start
5 min
🔥
Hot Take · Cold Start
No notes. No warm-up. You tell the story — NOW.
60 SEC
🔥 Hot Take · 60 seconds · No preparation
"Tell me a work story from the last two weeks. Use FIRST, THEN, AFTER THAT and tell me how it ended."
⏱ 60 sec — no stopping
🚫 No notes
✅ Min. 3 connectors
🎯 Min. 3 irregular verbs
📌 Outcome phrase at the end
Cold Start — regla de oro
Zero contexto extra. El alumno empieza al tick del timer. Si para antes de 20 seg: levanta la mano (gesto, no palabras) para que continúe. Intervén solo al final.

Post Hot Take: repite 1–2 oraciones del alumno con la forma correcta implícita: "Nice — so they made a decision, right?" No corrijas directo durante la producción.
Qué observar
¿Usa connectors o los omite? ¿Los irregulares son correctos? ¿Cierra con una outcome phrase? Toma nota mental de UN error recurrente para feedback al final — solo uno.
§ 02
🎯 Debate — Assigned Sides
10 min
🎯
Debate · Assigned Sides
You don't choose your position — you defend what you're given. Real English.
90 SEC / SIDE
Regla de sesión B
El alumno NO elige su lado — el teacher lo asigna. Esto rompe la zona de confort y obliga a producción en Simple Past narrativo sin soporte. Si hay 2+ alumnos, asigna lados opuestos.

Topic: "Telling a story with connectors makes you sound more professional in English."
Post-debate
"What was ONE sentence you used that you're proud of?" → refuerza positivamente. Luego: UN error para mejorar, no una lista.
🟣 Side A — AGREE
"Yes — connectors make stories more professional. Defend this for 90 seconds."
Starters: "I think connectors are important because…" "When I told my manager what happened, I used First and Then…" "Without connectors, a story sounds like a list — not a story."
🔵 Side B — DISAGREE
"It's not the connectors — it's the verbs and facts. Counter the other side."
Starters: "I disagree — what matters is the information, not the connectors." "A story went really well without First and Finally when…" "The most important thing is the irregular verb, not the connector."
§ 03
🔄 Relay — Transform Without Support
8 min
🔄
Relay · No Support
No grammar table. No vocabulary list. Just you and the language.
8 MIN
Instrucción
"I give you a story fragment. You transform it. No looking at notes. Speak out loud." El Show Answer es solo para verificación post-producción — no para que el alumno lea y repita.
Dinámica
T→S Teacher lee el input, alumno transforma. Producción libre, no hay opciones. El relay usa exactamente las estructuras de §5 de S4A — ahora en producción espontánea sin andamiaje.
Transform 1 — Add the Connector
"I arrived. I prepared the materials. I met the client."
→ Connect these three actions into one story using First / Then / After that.
"First, I arrived. Then I prepared the materials. After that, I met the client."
Transform 2 — Make It Negative
"She gave a presentation. She spoke to the director."
→ Say what she did NOT do (negative form of both verbs).
"She didn't give a presentation. She didn't speak to the director." (didn't + BASE verb — never 'didn't gave / didn't spoke')
Transform 3 — Add an Outcome
"We had the meeting with the client from Japan. It was three hours long."
→ Close this story with a natural outcome phrase in English.
Open: "In the end, it went really well." / "Finally, it didn't go as planned — we need a second meeting." (any authentic outcome phrase)
Transform 4 — Present → Past Narrative
"Every week, Valeria writes a report, sends it to her manager, and takes notes in the meeting."
→ Retell this as a past story: "Last week, Valeria…"
"Last week, Valeria wrote a report, sent it to her manager, and took notes in the meeting." (wrote / sent / took — all irregular)
Transform 5 — Open Story Opener
"Something happened in the meeting yesterday. It was very unexpected."
→ Reopen this story using a natural colloquial opener from Session A.
"So, what happened was — something very unexpected occurred in the meeting yesterday." (uses the colloquial opener + connector)
§ 04
⚡ Speed Round — Say the Past, Fast
6 min
⚡
Speed Round
Click the card → say a FULL sentence in Simple Past. No pauses. 20 sec max per card.
7+ = BADGE
Reglas
El alumno ve el verbo → produce UNA oración completa en Simple Past en 20 segundos. Badge al completar 7+ tarjetas. El timer de abajo es para cronometrar cada tarjeta individual — resetea entre tarjeta y tarjeta.

Ejemplo: Tarjeta "make → ?" → alumno dice "We made a decision last Friday about the new contract."
Nivel de exigencia
La oración debe incluir: (1) sujeto + pasado correcto, (2) complemento real de trabajo, (3) idealmente un marcador de tiempo. No aceptes "She gave" solo — exige la oración completa.

§ 05
🎤 Solo Challenge — 2 Minutes, One Story
7 min
🎤
Solo Challenge · 2 minutes
No stops. No help. Your English, your story, your structure.
BADGE AWARDED
🎤 Solo Challenge · 2 min · Badge awarded on completion
"Your manager missed last week's client visit. Tell them the whole story — what happened, who you met, what decisions were made, and how it ended."
Use ALL 4 connectors: First / Then / After that / Finally
Use at least 5 different irregular verbs (had, went, gave, met, spoke, wrote…)
Include at least 1 negative: "She didn't / They didn't…"
Close with an outcome phrase: "In the end, it went really well" or similar
Open with a colloquial opener: "So, what happened was…"
Setup
"Pretend I'm your manager and I wasn't there. Tell me everything." — No más contexto. El alumno habla, el teacher escucha.

Badge se otorga automáticamente al completar el timer — criterio es intentar 2 minutos, no perfección.
Mientras habla
Cuenta mentalmente: (1) connectors usados, (2) irregulares correctos, (3) irregulares incorrectos. Post-solo: menciona UNA cosa muy bien hecha + UN error específico con la forma correcta.
§ 06
🔍 Analyse — What's the Story?
8 min
🔍
Source Analysis
Real workplace messages — read, understand, reconstruct the story behind them
8 MIN
Dinámica
El alumno lee la cita → responde la pregunta cerrada (MCQ) → luego el teacher pregunta la extensión oral abierta. Las preguntas cerradas tienen respuesta correcta. Las abiertas (en extensión) no — el objetivo es producción oral con Past narrativo.
Extension oral
Después de cada fuente: "Now retell this message to me as a spoken story — use First, Then, In the end."
📧 Trip report email — from Valeria to Daniel
"First, I went to the venue on Tuesday. Then I met the logistics team and they gave me a full briefing. After that, the event ran for three hours. In the end, two clients sent enquiries."
❓ Which connector signals the FINAL outcome?
💬 WhatsApp — colleague to colleague
"The audit went really well — but long story short, we found a problem in production. We spoke to the director and he made a decision the same day."
❓ How many irregular past verbs are in this message?
📋 Verbal report to manager
"So what happened was — the client didn't come to the meeting. We waited 40 minutes. After that, we got a message and found out there was a flight delay."
❓ What does "didn't come" tell us about the verb form?
📞 Voicemail summary — from sales coordinator
"Hi — just a quick update. We had the product launch yesterday. The director gave a great presentation, and we got feedback from 15 clients. It went really well overall."
❓ What outcome phrase does the speaker use?
