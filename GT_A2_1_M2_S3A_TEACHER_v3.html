Warm-Up — This or That · The Debrief Edition
5–7 min
❓
Warm-Up
This or That · Debrief — Which question opens the conversation?
5–7 MIN

Your colleague just came back from a 3-day business trip to Monterrey. Which question would YOU ask? Pick one from each pair.

01 · First question
How did it go?
general feeling
OR
What happened at the meeting?
straight to facts
Which gets you more useful information in a corporate debrief?
02 · About people
Who did you meet?
key contacts
OR
Were there many people?
group size only
Which gives more strategic intelligence about the trip?
03 · About the outcome
What did they decide?
opens the story
OR
Did they make a decision?
yes/no only
Which question opens the conversation more?
04 · About duration
How long did the meeting take?
← today's grammar
OR
Was the meeting long?
yes/no only
Which is more professional — and which is S3A grammar?
Teacher insight: Today's grammar — Wh- questions in the past — is the difference between a yes/no answer and a real narrative. "Did they decide?" → "Yes." / "What did they decide?" → a full explanation. This is the tool that opens conversations.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • ACCIÓN: Antes de mostrar las opciones, di: "Tu colega acaba de llegar de un viaje. ¿Cuál es la primera pregunta — en inglés?" Diagnóstico: ¿produce "What did you…" o "Did you…?"
  • S3A es la sesión más importante de M2 para conversación real. Los Wh- questions en pasado transforman "I went to Monterrey" en una conversación fluida.
  • El contraste yes/no vs Wh- es el key insight. Señálalo: "¿Cuál da más información?" → siempre el Wh-.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "La diferencia entre un 'Did you…?' y un 'What did you…?' es la diferencia entre una conversación de 10 segundos y un debrief real. Hoy aprendes a abrir conversaciones."
Capa 3 · Retención & Upsell
  • "Al final de M2 (S5) narras un evento corporativo completo y contestas todas las preguntas de tu manager. Wh- questions son las herramientas de esa conversación."

§ 04
Grammar — Wh- Questions · Full System
15–18 min
⚙️
Grammar
Wh- Questions Simple Past — Full system + Subject exception + How long did it take?
15–18 MIN
🚨 Real Situation — Monday Morning Meeting
Your manager came back from a 2-day conference. You want to know: what happened? Who did she meet? You know: "Did it go well?" — but that only gets you "yes" or "no". You need: "What happened? Who did she meet? Why did it take so long?"
❓ What is the structure of these questions? Why is "What happened?" different from "Who did she meet?"
✅ The System: Wh- word + did + subject + BASE verb (+ object)?
Exception: "What happened?" and "Who came?" — Wh- word IS the subject → no "did", past form goes directly.
Wh- Questions in Simple Past — Full Reference Table

Who (object)person — objectWho + did + S + BASE?"Who did you meet?" / "I met the director."
WhereplaceWhere + did + S + BASE?"Where did they hold the event?" / "In Guadalajara."
WhentimeWhen + did + S + BASE?"When did the trip start?" / "Monday morning."
WhyreasonWhy + did + S + BASE?"Why did they cancel?" / "Budget cuts in Q3."
How longdurationHow long + did + S + BASE?"How long did it take?" / "It took two days."
Who (subject)person — subjectWho + PAST VERB directly"Who came to the meeting?" / "The whole team came."
What (subject)thing — subjectWhat + PAST VERB directly"What happened next?" / "The client cancelled."
⭐ How long — Special case
Q: "How long did the meeting take?"
A: "It took three hours."

Q: "How long did the trip last?"
A: "It lasted two days."

take = time for a task · last = duration of an event
⚠ Subject vs Object Wh-
"What did you discuss?" — you = subject
"What happened?" — what = subject ← no "did"

"Who did you meet?" — you = subject
"Who came?" — who = subject ← no "did"
⚠ Top 4 Errors — Fix Every Time
✗ "What you discussed?" / "Who you met?"→✓ What did you discuss? / Who did you meet?
Missing "did" — past auxiliary is essential in Wh- questions.
✗ "What did you discussed?"→✓ What did you discuss?
After "did", always BASE FORM — never past form.
✗ "How long did it lasted?"→✓ How long did it last?
Same rule: did + BASE VERB. Not "lasted" after did.
✗ "What did happened?" (adding "did" to subject Wh-)→✓ What happened? (no "did" — "what" is the subject)
When Wh- word IS the subject, use past tense directly.

      Production — Debrief Your Last Business Event
      8–10 min
      🗣
      Production
      Wh- Questions in action — Real events · Real answers · No notes
      8–10 MIN
      Speaking Practice — A2.1 Wh- Questions
      Ask and answer about real corporate events.
      The teacher asks each question. You answer in full sentences using Simple Past + irregular verbs. No notes for questions 3–6.
      Q1 — Where
      Where did you go for your last important work trip, meeting, or event? Was it in another city?
      💡 "I went to [city] for [event]. It was at [location]."
      Q2 — Who
      Who did you meet at that event? Who did you speak to? Was there anyone important?
      💡 "I met / spoke to / had a meeting with [person + role]."
      Q3 — What
      What happened at the most important moment? What did you discuss? What did you decide?
      💡 "We discussed / decided / made / found / gave…"
      Q4 — How long
      How long did the event take? How long did the most important meeting or session last?
      💡 "It took [time] / It lasted [time]." — Not "It was [time] long."
      Q5 — Why
      Why did you go to that event — or why was it important? What was the main reason?
      💡 "I went because… / The main reason was… / We needed to…"
      ⭐ Q6 — Full Debrief Challenge (90 seconds)
      Give a 90-second full debrief of that event. Structure: Where → Who → What → How long → Why it mattered. No notes. Teacher times you.
      💡 A2.1 target: 4+ irregular past verbs, all 5 Wh- question types answered, 1+ duration expression (it took / it lasted), 90 seconds continuous speech.
      🎯 A2.1 Debrief Challenge
      "Your manager asks: 'How was the event?' — Don't say 'It was good.' Give a real, structured debrief using all 6 Wh- answers. 90 seconds. Go."
      A2.1 success: student produces 4+ correct irregular past forms, uses at least 4 Wh- question types in their narrative structure, includes one How long / How did it go expression. That is the full S3A achievement.

      • Éxito A2.1: 4+ irregulares correctos, 4+ Wh- types usados, 1+ duración. Celebra con especificidad.
