§ 01
Picture Prompt — Company Then & Now
5–8 min

Elige la foto que más se parece a la transformación de una empresa que conoces. Describe qué SOLÍA ser y cómo cambió. Usa "would" para acciones repetidas · "used to" para estados.

A
From cubicles to open spaces
✓ Picked
B
Small office → global headquarters
✓ Picked
C
Paper processes → digital systems
✓ Picked
🎙 Q1: What did this company's workplace USE TO BE like before the change? Describe the STATE — size, culture, leadership style.
Scaffold: "The company used to be ___. The headquarters used to have ___. The leadership used to be ___." (states → only "used to")
🎙 Q2: What WOULD employees do regularly before the transformation? Describe repeated ACTIONS.
Scaffold: "Employees would ___ every ___. The team would always ___. Directors would ___ every quarter." (repeated actions → "would" works)
🎙 Q3: What specific event CHANGED the company? Use Simple Past. When did it happen?
Scaffold: "The company merged / expanded / outsourced ___ in ___. That was when everything changed."
Preguntas de seguimiento
"What policy didn't use to exist that is standard now?"
"Did the strategy use to focus on local markets or was it always global?"
"What would managers do regularly that they don't do any more?"
"Can you say 'The leadership would be traditional'? Why or why not?" (trap — state!)
Capa 1 · Pedagógica
  • Q1 fuerza estados (be, have, feel) → solo "used to". Q2 fuerza acciones repetidas → "would" posible. Si el alumno usa "would be" en Q1 — corrige inmediatamente: "Be is a state — only used to."
  • Última pregunta del follow-up es una trampa deliberada. Si el alumno dice "The leadership would be traditional" → pausa: "Be is a state. Can you fix it?" → "The leadership used to be traditional."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Elige la foto que más se parezca a un cambio real. La historia que cuentes es la tuya — no la de un libro."
Capa 3 · Retención
  • "Acabas de describir una transformación corporativa separando estados de acciones — esa es la diferencia entre A2 y B1."
§ 02
Recap Speedrun — Would vs Used To
5 min

💡 Di la respuesta en voz alta ANTES de voltear. Ronda 1 normal · Ronda 2 a velocidad real. La clave: ¿es estado o acción?

1
The company ___ be family-owned.
would or used to?
used to (NOT would)
"Be" = state verb. Would NEVER works with states.
2
Directors ___ present results every quarter.
would or used to?
Both work ✓
"Present" = action verb + "every quarter" = repeated. Would and used to are both correct.
3
They ___ with a US firm in 2016.
which tense?
merged (Simple Past)
"In 2016" = specific event. Neither used to nor would — only Simple Past.
4
outsource · headquarters · policy
one sentence each
Free production
"We used to outsource nothing." · "The HQ used to be small." · "There didn't use to be a remote policy."
5
I ___ feel like the company was a family.
would or used to?
used to (NOT would)
"Feel" = state verb here. "I used to feel" ✓ · "I would feel" ✗
Capa 1 · Pedagógica
  • Tarjetas 1 y 5 son las trampas clave: "be" y "feel" son estados — would no funciona. Si el alumno las responde correctamente sin dudar en Ronda 2 = automaticidad.
  • Tarjeta 4 pide 3 oraciones — cronometra: menos de 30 segundos = excelente, más de 60 = necesita más producción.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Ronda 2 — responde antes de que yo termine de mostrar la tarjeta. Si puedes hacerlo, ya dominas la regla."
Capa 3 · Retención
  • "Estas 5 tarjetas cubren las 3 formas del módulo: used to, would, Simple Past. Si las produces sin pensar — estás listo para S4."

🎙 Ahora Tú — 60 segundos · Sin notas
Describe la transformación de TU empresa (o una que conozcas) con la misma estructura:
1️⃣ Estados pasados: "The company used to be ___ / used to have ___"
2️⃣ Acciones repetidas: "We would ___ every ___ / Managers would ___"
3️⃣ Evento de cambio: "Then in ___, we merged / expanded / outsourced ___"
4️⃣ Presente: "Nowadays, we ___ / The strategy is ___"
Capa 1 · Pedagógica
  • Frase 1 tiene 2 usos de "would" (meet, review) — ambas acciones repetidas. Frase 3 tiene 2 usos de "used to" (be, feel) — ambos estados. La diferencia debe ser audible en la entonación del alumno.
  • "Ahora Tú": si el alumno usa "would be" o "would have" para describir estados → pausa y corrige antes de continuar.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Sofía lo dice en 60 segundos — con 3 formas gramaticales diferentes. Tu turno: misma estructura, tu empresa."
Capa 3 · Retención
  • "Estas 3 frases son el template de un executive pitch sobre transformación corporativa. Mismo patrón, cualquier empresa."

Role Play — Magazine Interview
12–15 min

3 rondas. Contexto: una revista de negocios entrevista al alumno sobre cómo su empresa ha cambiado. El maestro es el periodista. Cada ronda reduce el andamiaje.

Ronda 1 · Guiada
Script visible — follow the structure
3–4 min

Contexto: Journalist (teacher) interviews you about how your company has transformed. You are a director describing the before/after.

Business Magazine Reporter

"What did the team use to do regularly back then?"

"How is the company different today?"
Student → Director
You · Company Director

Questions only — no script for answers

Same interview — but now without the scaffold. Teacher adds improvised follow-ups. 2+ sentences per answer minimum.

Journalist · Improvised

Checklist

Ronda 3 · Libre
2 minutes · Full transformation narrative · No scaffold
2 min

Challenge final: The journalist says: "Tell me the full story of your company's transformation — from the beginning to today. I want the complete picture."

Journalist's Final Question

Cronómetro — 2 minutos. Arranca cuando el alumno diga la primera palabra.
Meta: used to (estados) + would (acciones) + Simple Past (evento) + present contrast. Las 4 formas en una narrativa continua.
2:00
Capa 1 · Pedagógica
  • Ronda 3 es de 2 minutos (más que S2B) porque la narrativa corporativa requiere más contexto. Si el alumno logra 90 seg con las 4 formas = exit standard cumplido.
  • Error más esperado: "The leadership would be hierarchical" → corrige post-turno: "Be is a state — used to only."
  • Protege Ronda 3. Es el momento de mayor valor de toda la sesión.
Capa 2 · Engagement

§ 05
WOW Component — Correct Me!
8–10 min
⚡ WOW Component · S3B Exclusive
"Correct Me!" Game
El maestro lee cada oración. Algunas tienen errores (would con estados, Simple Past donde debería ser used to, etc.). El alumno tiene 5 segundos para decir si es CORRECTA o INCORRECTA — y si es incorrecta, corregirla en voz alta. 12 rondas.
0
/ 12
Ready to start
Capa 1 · Pedagógica
  • Lee cada oración en voz alta al alumno. El alumno dice "Correct!" o "Wrong!" — si dice "Wrong!", debe producir la versión correcta oralmente.
  • Las trampas: oraciones que PARECEN correctas pero usan would con estados (be, have, feel), o used to con eventos específicos.
  • Meta de mastery: 9/12 o más. Si el alumno corrige las oraciones incorrectas espontáneamente y produce la versión correcta = automaticidad real.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Eres el profesor ahora — corrige MIS errores. ¿Puedes encontrar todos los errores antes de que yo te diga la respuesta?"
Capa 3 · Retención
  • "Cada error que corregiste = un error que YA NO vas a cometer. Esa es la diferencia entre entender la regla y usarla automáticamente."
§ 06
Debate — Companies & Change
8–10 min

3 preguntas de discusión. No hay respuesta correcta. Usa el vocabulario de S3A (expand, merge, outsource, leadership, policy, strategy) y las 3 formas gramaticales libremente.

Debate Question 1
"When a company merges with another, employees always lose something. Is the growth worth the cultural loss?"
PRO — Growth is worth it
More opportunities · better salaries · global exposure · innovation · the old culture was limiting
CON — Cultural loss matters
Identity disappears · relationships break · stress increases · loyalty decreases · you used to feel like family
💡 "The company used to feel like ___. After the merger, employees would no longer ___. The biggest loss was ___."
Debate Question 2
"Should companies outsource important functions (IT, HR, logistics) — or keep everything in-house for quality control?"
PRO — Outsource
Cost reduction · access to specialists · focus on core business · scalability · modern companies outsource
CON — Keep in-house
Quality control · data security · company culture · faster decisions · we used to handle everything internally
💡 "Companies used to handle everything internally. Now they outsource ___. The advantage used to be ___. Nowadays the priority is ___."
Debate Question 3
"Think about YOUR company or industry. What is ONE thing that used to be done manually that is now automated — and is it better this way?"
Open Reflection
Personal experience · specific process · human cost · efficiency gain · what you miss vs what improved
💡 "We used to ___ manually. Employees would spend ___ hours on ___. Then the company ___ (Simple Past). Now ___."
Capa 1 · Pedagógica
  • Q1 y Q2 tienen dos lados — el maestro toma el contrario para forzar defensa. Q3 es personal y emocional — deja que fluya.
  • Si el alumno usa "would" correctamente (solo con acciones) durante el debate espontáneo = mastery real. Anota cada uso correcto e incorrecto para el feedback final.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Q3 es tu historia — tu empresa, tu industria. Cuéntamela con opinión y con ejemplos reales."
Capa 3 · Retención
  • "Acabas de debatir sobre transformación corporativa en inglés — con opiniones, vocabulario técnico, y gramática precisa. Eso es comunicación ejecutiva real."
