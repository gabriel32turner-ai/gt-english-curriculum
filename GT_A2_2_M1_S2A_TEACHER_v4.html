Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Q2 is a direct S1 review checkpoint. If student says "I use to manage" — address immediately. S1 form must be automatic before introducing the used to vs Simple Past contrast.
  • Q3 is the bridge to today's grammar: "I GOT this role" (Simple Past, specific event) vs "I used to work there" (past habit). This is the exact contrast of §4.
  • Note errors that mix the two: "I used to get a promotion last year" = a very common A2.2 error. Save it — use it as an example in §4.

  • "Tell me the story of your biggest career change in 3 sentences: what you used to do, what happened (specific event), and where you are now."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Today you learn when NOT to use 'used to' — which is just as important as knowing when to use it. Used to vs Simple Past is a common error in job interviews in English."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • "promotion" — deliberately shown with Simple Past in the example ("I got my promotion"). This pre-teaches the grammar contrast before §4.
  • "responsibility" — 6 syllables. Common mispronunciation: re-SPON-si-bil-i-ty. Drill syllable by syllable. Worth 30 seconds.
  • "deadline" — point out: "dead" + "line". The metaphor: after the line = project is dead. Makes it memorable. Very frequent in professional English.
  • Micro-speaking: student chooses 3 nouns and makes true sentences — at least 2 with "used to" and 1 with Simple Past.

§ 04
Grammar — Used To vs Simple Past
12–15 min
The Key Contrast
FormWhen to use itCareer example
Used to A repeated habit or state in the past — no longer true. Implies contrast with present. "I used to travel every month."= I traveled regularly — I don't now.
Simple Past A specific completed action or event — one time, with a date or clear time reference. "I travelled to Houston last April."= One specific trip — not a habit.
Simple Past An event that caused a change — promotion, new job, move, switch. "I got my promotion in 2021."= Specific moment — NOT "I used to get my promotion".
Used to A past state (role, feeling, situation) that no longer exists. "She used to work in sales."= Her past role — not just one day.
The quick test: If you can say "every day / every week / regularly / always" — use used to.
If you can say "last Monday / in 2019 / when I was 25 / once" — use Simple Past.
If it's a career change event (got a promotion, switched companies, joined) — always Simple Past.
⚠ The Most Common Confusion at A2.2
❌ "I used to go to the office yesterday."→✅ I went to the office yesterday. (specific day → Simple Past)
❌ "I used to get a promotion last year."→✅ I got a promotion last year. (specific event → Simple Past)
❌ "I used to work there for 3 years." (when specifying duration)→✅ I worked there for 3 years. (duration with "for" → Simple Past)
❌ "She worked there every day." (habitual, no longer true)→✅ She used to work there every day. (regular habit → used to)
Activity 1 — Used To or Simple Past?
Choose the correct form for each sentence.
She ___ travel to clients every week, but now everything is online.
I ___ a promotion last year — and everything changed after that.
Did your company ___ a dress code before the culture changed?
I ___ work overtime, but now I have a much better work-life balance.
Which sentence is correct?
Activity 2 — Sentence Order
Drag the pieces into the correct order.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Q3: "Did your company use to have" — note "use to" (not "used to") after "did". Students often write "Did your company used to have" — address explicitly.
      • Q5: "last Tuesday" = specific time reference → always Simple Past. "I used to attend" + "last Tuesday" is a double contradiction. The incompatibility should be obvious after explaining the quick test.
      • Activity 2, sentence 1: "but I switched roles in 2020" — this is the career change event in Simple Past, appearing naturally alongside "used to". This is the A2.2 production target.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Quick oral test: I say a time expression, you tell me which tense. 'Every morning' → used to. 'Last April' → Simple Past. 'Three times a week' → used to. 'When I was promoted' → Simple Past. 5 seconds per answer."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "S3 adds 'would' for past habits — a more formal alternative to 'used to'. Perfect for business writing."
      § 05
      Reading — My Career in Two Chapters
      8–10 min
      FORMAT
      Internal company blog — "My Career Story"
      AUTHOR
      Miguel Vargas · Customer Relations Manager
      Miguel Vargas
      Customer Relations Manager · Vertex Solutions · Chicago
      Watch for: used to (orange) · Simple Past for specific events (cyan) · present contrast (green)

      When I started at this company eight years ago, my role was completely different. I used to work in the warehouse, managing inventory. I used to start at six in the morning and finish at three. I didn't use to have an office — or even a desk.

      My routine used to be very physical. I used to check stock levels every morning, write daily reports, and deal with suppliers. My biggest challenge used to be communicating with the Chicago team — I didn't use to write emails in English at all.

      Then everything changed. I got a promotion three years ago — a colleague recommended me for a new position in customer relations. That was the moment everything switched. I left the warehouse and moved to the corporate office.

      Nowadays, I manage a team of eight people and deal with clients across five countries. I lead weekly calls, write proposals, and handle complex responsibilities I didn't use to have. I don't work overtime any more — but I earn more and I learn more every day.

      My colleagues are my best resource now. Back then, I used to feel invisible. These days, I feel like the company depends on me — and I like that feeling.

      Miguel Vargas · Customer Relations Manager · Vertex Solutions
      Comprehension — Mixed Tenses
      Answer using information from the text.
      What did Miguel use to do in his first role?
      What specific event changed his career? (Use Simple Past)
      Miguel used to have a very flexible schedule in his first job.
      Nowadays Miguel still works overtime regularly.
      Miguel's promotion happened three years ago.
      GT Originals — Career Narrative Structure
      Miguel's text has a clear 4-part structure: 1. Past habits (used to) → 2. The change event (Simple Past) → 3. Present reality (present simple) → 4. Emotional contrast (back then/now).
      This is the exact structure of a strong "Tell me about yourself" answer in an English job interview. Point this out to the student — it's not just grammar practice, it's a career template.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Color system: orange = used to, cyan = Simple Past (specific events), green = present contrast. Ask student to count each: used to (7), Simple Past events (got/recommended/switched/left/moved = 5), present (6+).
      • RQ2 tests the core contrast: "He used to get promoted" is wrong — it was a specific event. If student chooses this, revisit §4 quick test: "Can you say 'three years ago'? Yes → Simple Past."
      • After comprehension: "Tell me Miguel's story in your own words. Then tell me YOUR story with the same 4-part structure."
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Miguel says he used to feel invisible — now he feels the company depends on him. What's YOUR emotional contrast? What did you use to feel about your work that has changed?"
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "In §7 you'll produce YOUR version of this 4-part narrative. That's the Info Gap speaking task. The template is Miguel's story — the content is yours."

      Listening — Things Were Different Back Then
      10–12 min
      🎧
      A Career Conversation — Carmen & Miguel
      A2.2 · M1 · S2 · Dialogue · ~90 seconds · American English

      🔈 Audio placeholder. Replace with Google Drive embed.
      ElevenLabs voice: Sarah + Michael · Rate: 0.82 · Stability: 0.65

      ▶ Show ElevenLabs Script

      Carmen: Hey Miguel, how long have you been with the company?

      Miguel: About eight years. Things are very different from when I started.

      Carmen: Really? What used to be different?

      Miguel: Well, I used to work in the warehouse. I didn't use to have an office. I used to start at six in the morning and finish at three.

      Carmen: Did you use to like that schedule?

      Miguel: Honestly? No. I didn't use to have time for my family in the evenings. But the team was great — we used to have lunch together every day.

      Carmen: What about the work itself? What used to be your main responsibility?

      Miguel: I used to manage inventory — just numbers and spreadsheets. I didn't use to deal with clients at all. Now I'm in customer relations. It's completely different.

      Carmen: What changed?

      Miguel: I got a promotion three years ago. A colleague recommended me. After that, everything switched. I joined the corporate team and started managing accounts.

      Carmen: Do you miss the old routine?

      Miguel: Sometimes. I used to feel less pressure. But I earn more now, and the challenges are bigger. So no complaints.

      ⏱ Target: 85–95 sec · Two voices or single narrator · Speed: 0.82 · City: Chicago

      § SP
      Speaking Practice
      10–12 min
      🗣 Guided Speaking — Career Changes
      Before → Change Event → Now
      Use the 4-part career narrative structure: past habits + change event + current situation. Real professional context only.
      Question 1
      "Describe your professional life before your current role using used to and would."
      Hint: I used to work in ___. I would ___ every day. My responsibilities used to be ___.
      Question 2
      "What was the specific change event in your career? Use Simple Past."
      Hint: In ___ (year), I ___ (Simple Past). That was the moment when ___. My company decided to ___.
      Question 3
      "How is your professional situation different now? Contrast before and after."
      Hint: I used to ___, but now I ___. Before, I would ___, but today ___. The biggest difference is ___.
      Question 4
      "Describe a change in your industry or sector — not just your personal career."
      Hint: Companies in my sector used to ___. The industry would ___. Then in ___ (year), everything changed when ___.
      Question 5
      "What does your career story tell about how your professional skills have evolved?"
      Hint: I used to focus on ___. Over time, I developed ___. Now my main strengths are ___.
      ⏱ 10–12 min📋 5 questions🎯 4-part narrative structure👥 Teacher-led

      Wrap-Up
      5 min
      Today I learned…
      • Habit · routine · promotion · responsibility · deadline · colleague · overtime · challenge.
      • Used to = repeated past habit. Simple Past = specific event or date.
      • Career change events always use Simple Past: "I got a promotion / I switched / I joined."
      • I can tell a career story using both forms correctly in the same narrative.

      Used to
      I used to commute · I used to manage · I didn't use to deal with clients
      Simple Past (events)
      I got / I switched / I joined / I left / I started
