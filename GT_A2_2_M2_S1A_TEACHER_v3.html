Warm-Up — Your Company's Rules
5–7 min
Maestro hace las preguntas. El alumno responde con lo que puede. No hay respuesta incorrecta — es activación diagnóstica del campo semántico de reglas laborales.
01 · Dress code
👔 Formal
suit / business attire required
OR
👕 Casual
no dress code enforced
Do you have to wear a specific uniform or dress code at work?
02 · Attendance
🕗 Fixed
must arrive at a set time
OR
🕐 Flexible
schedule is your choice
Do you have to sign in or clock in at work? What time must you arrive?
03 · Confidentiality
🔒 Strict
high data/IP protection
OR
📂 Open
general information sharing
Are there things you must NOT share with people outside the company?
04 · Meetings
✅ Compulsory
attendance is required
OR
⚪ Optional
up to you to decide
Do you have to attend all the meetings in your company? Any exceptions?
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • ACCIÓN: Muestra la imagen antes de las tarjetas. "What kind of workplace is this? Does it have strict rules or flexible ones?" — activa el campo semántico visual.
  • Esta warm-up es diagnóstica: nota qué modal usa el alumno naturalmente. Si dice "I need to arrive at 8" en vez de "I have to" — perfecto. Eso es exactamente lo que M2 va a convertir a inglés corporativo preciso.
  • No corrijas forma aquí. Solo escucha y anota qué palabras de obligación ya conoce. La sesión empieza en §3.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Las reglas de tu empresa — en inglés corporativo, estas se expresan con must y have to. Al final de esta sesión podrás decirle a cualquier colega extranjero exactamente cómo funciona tu empresa."
Capa 3 · Retención & Upsell
  • "En el Exit Task de M2 el alumno describe 4 reglas reales de su empresa con must, mustn't, have to y don't have to. Lo que está diciendo ahora — eso es lo que convierte en inglés corporativo hoy."
§ 02
Vocabulary — Workplace Rules & Obligations
8–10 min
🏢 Workplace Rules & Policies
policy
"Our company has a strict data privacy policy."
procedure
"You must follow the safety procedure before entering the lab."
requirement
"Attending the induction is a compulsory requirement."
dress code
"We don't have to follow a strict dress code on Fridays."
confidential
"You mustn't share confidential client information."
badge / access
"Employees must wear their badge at all times."
attendance
"Attendance at the monthly review is compulsory."
punctuality
"Punctuality is a must — meetings start on time."
⚖️ Obligation Words — Two Registers
forbidden
"Smoking is forbidden in all areas."
prohibited
"Photography is prohibited inside the factory."
required
"A safety helmet is required on the production floor."
compulsory
"The induction training is compulsory for all new staff."
optional
"The Friday lunch is optional — you don't have to attend."
permitted
"Working from home is permitted on Wednesdays."
privacy
"Client data must be kept private at all times."
deadline
"You have to submit the report before the deadline."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Colores en las obligation words: rojo = prohibición, naranja = obligación, verde = opcional. Esta codificación de color se conecta directamente con §3.
  • "compulsory" vs "required" vs "necessary" — todos significan "obligatorio" pero tienen registros distintos. compulsory = formal/legal. required = corporate. necessary = informal. Díselo al alumno.
  • Pronunciación clave: "prohibited" /prəˈhɪbɪtɪd/ — muchos la pronuncian "pro-HI-bi-ted" con acento incorrecto. Drill directo.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "¿Cuántas de estas palabras ya usaste en español en tu empresa? compulsory, prohibited, confidential — estas palabras viven en tu workplace. Ahora las tienes en inglés."
§ 03
Grammar — Must · Mustn't · Have to · Don't have to
12–14 min
The Obligation System — 4 Modals, 4 Meanings
ModalMeaningRegisterBusiness Example
muststrong internal obligation — I decide / the company decidesformal / written policy"Employees must wear their badge at all times."
mustn'tPROHIBITION — it is forbidden, not allowedformal / signs / rules"You mustn't share confidential data with clients."
have toexternal obligation — the law / the company requires itspoken / everyday"I have to submit this by 5 PM — it's the deadline."
don't have toNO obligation — it is optional, not necessaryspoken / everyday"You don't have to attend the Friday lunch. It's optional."
Structure — all modals follow the same pattern:
must/mustn't + BASE VERB (infinitive without "to")
have to/don't have to + BASE VERB
✅ "You must submit" · ✅ "She has to attend" · ❌ "You must to submit" · ❌ "She have to attend"
FormMustHave to
AffirmativeI/You/He must sign in.I have to / She has to sign in.
NegativeYou mustn't share passwords. (forbidden)You don't have to wear a tie. (optional)
QuestionMust I bring my badge? (formal)Do I have to attend? / Does she have to go?
Short Answer—Yes, you do. / No, you don't.
⚡ The Critical Contrast — Mustn't vs Don't Have To
mustn't = FORBIDDEN ❌
You mustn't do it.
It is prohibited. It is not allowed. You cannot do it. There are consequences if you do.
"You mustn't share your password with anyone."
"Employees mustn't use social media during working hours."
"You mustn't enter the server room without authorization."
don't have to = OPTIONAL ✅ or ❌ your choice
You don't have to do it.
It is not necessary. It is optional. You can do it if you want, but it's not required.
"You don't have to wear a tie — business casual is fine."
"She doesn't have to attend the Friday meeting."
"You don't have to submit the report today if you're not ready."
⚠️ The most important distinction at A2.2 — globally one of the top 5 grammar errors:
"You mustn't come to work early" = It is FORBIDDEN to come early. (Very strange!)
"You don't have to come to work early" = It's optional — you can if you want. ✅
They look similar but mean the OPPOSITE.
⚠ Most Common Errors — A2.2 Students
❌ "You must to sign in."→✅ You must sign in.
must + bare infinitive — never "to" after a modal
❌ "She have to attend the meeting."→✅ She has to attend the meeting.
3rd person singular: have to → has to
❌ "You mustn't wear a tie." (meaning: it's optional)→✅ You don't have to wear a tie.
mustn't ≠ don't have to — the #1 contrast error
❌ "Do I must attend?"→✅ Do I have to attend? / Must I attend?
must doesn't use "do" as auxiliary in questions
❌ "I don't must share it."→✅ I mustn't share it. / I don't have to share it.
there is no "don't must" — use mustn't or don't have to
⚡ Micro-Speaking — 90 seconds
Maestro pregunta → alumno responde sobre sus reglas reales de trabajo:
"Do you have to wear a specific dress code?" → Target: "I have to / I don't have to wear..."
"Is there anything you mustn't do in your office?" → Target: "We mustn't [base verb]... because..."
"Do you have to attend all the meetings?" → Target: "Yes, I do / No, I don't have to..."
"What must every employee do before entering the building?" → Target: "Every employee must [verb]..."

Activity 2 — True or False: The Grammar Rules
Is this rule correct?
"Mustn't" and "don't have to" mean the same thing — both show no obligation.
"You must sign in" = you are required to sign in. This is correct English.
"She have to attend" is the correct 3rd person form of "have to".
"Mustn't" describes something that is forbidden or not allowed.
"Do I must submit the report?" is a correct question form.

      § 05
      Reading — Company Policy Document
      8–10 min
      Keywords to spot: must / mustn't · have to / has to · don't have to / optional · policy vocabulary
      DocumentIndusMex — Employee Handbook Excerpt SectionWorkplace Conduct & Obligations Policy Version2024.3 — Effective January 1, 2025
      Section 4 — Workplace Conduct

      4.1 Access & Security
      All employees must wear their security badge at all times while on company premises. Visitors must sign in at the main reception before entering any work area. Employees have to accompany external visitors at all times. You don't have to wear your badge in the parking area.

      4.2 Confidentiality
      Employees mustn't share confidential client information, pricing data, or internal documents with external parties. You mustn't discuss project details in public spaces. Staff must lock their computers when leaving their desk. However, you don't have to encrypt internal emails between colleagues.

      4.3 Meetings & Attendance
      All employees have to attend the monthly team review — attendance is compulsory. The Friday social lunch is optional — you don't have to participate. However, if a client requests a meeting, employees must respond within 24 hours.

      4.4 Personal Devices
      Employees mustn't use personal mobile phones during client presentations. You don't have to use the company phone for internal calls — personal phones are acceptable for team communication.
      Human Resources Department — IndusMex S.A. de C.V.
      Celaya, Guanajuato · hr@indusmex.com · Version 2024.3
      Activity 4 — Reading Comprehension
      Select the correct answer based on the policy document.
      Do employees have to wear their badge in the parking area?
      Can employees share confidential client information with external parties?
      Is the Friday social lunch compulsory?
      What is the difference between Section 4.2's "mustn't" rules and the "don't have to" rule in that section?
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Alumno lee en voz alta. Señala los 4 modales mientras lee — colores en el texto ayudan. Después: "¿Cuántos mustn't encontraste? ¿Cuántos don't have to?"
      • Documento real: díselo al alumno. "Este es el tipo de texto que encuentras en employee handbooks de empresas multinacionales. Cuando tu empresa tenga una auditoría o una fusión con una empresa internacional — este es el inglés que aparece."
      • La pregunta 4 es la más importante — conecta gramática con lectura analítica. Si el alumno la resuelve bien → ha comprendido el contraste mustn't vs don't have to.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "¿Tu empresa tiene un reglamento interno? Si lo tradujeras al inglés usando must y have to — se vería exactamente así."

      Genera con ElevenLabs voz Bella o Rachel · Monologue · Professional HR tone · Clear diction · No music

      ▶ Show ElevenLabs Script
      MONOLOGUE SCRIPT — HR INDUCTION BRIEFING

      "Welcome to IndusMex. I'm Laura from HR, and I want to go through a few important things before you start.

      First, access. You must wear your security badge at all times — inside the building, in the corridors, everywhere. You mustn't lend your badge to anyone. Ever. If you lose it, you have to report it to security immediately.

      Confidentiality. You mustn't share any client data, pricing information, or internal documents outside the company. This includes by email, by phone, and especially on social media. You have to sign our NDA today — I'll email it to you this afternoon.

      Now, what you don't have to worry about: the dress code. We don't have to follow formal attire every day. Business casual is fine, Monday to Thursday. On Fridays it's completely relaxed — casual is fine. And the Friday afternoon social? Completely optional — you don't have to attend.

      One last thing — the monthly all-hands meeting. Every employee must attend. No exceptions. It's on the last Thursday of every month. Any questions? Great — welcome to the team."
      🔧 ElevenLabs: Multilingual v2 · Stability 65% · Similarity 80% · Style 30% · Rate = 0.90. Tono profesional de HR — cálido pero claro. Énfasis natural en los modales. Pausa leve entre secciones.
      Activity 5 — Listening Comprehension
      Listen once for general understanding. Listen again to answer.
      Employees must wear their badge inside the building but not in the corridors.
      Employees can share client pricing information by phone if necessary.
      The Friday dress code is completely relaxed — casual is permitted.
      Attendance at the monthly all-hands meeting is optional.
      Employees don't have to attend the Friday afternoon social.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • 1a escucha: global. 2a: T/F. 3a opcional: alumno cuenta cuántas veces escucha cada modal (must=5, mustn't=3, have to=2, don't have to=3).
      • Después del T/F: "¿En qué sección habla de obligaciones? ¿En cuál habla de prohibiciones? ¿Cuándo usa don't have to?" — análisis de función comunicativa.
      • Pronunciación: "mustn't" /ˈmʌsnt/ — la T es casi silenciosa en inglés natural. "have to" /hæftə/ — V se vuelve F. Drill breve post-listening.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Este monólogo es lo que escuchas en el primer día de trabajo en una empresa multinacional. Ahora entiendes exactamente qué dice HR cuando te explica las reglas."
      § 07
      Production — Your Company's Policy
      8–10 min
      📋 Company Policy Builder — Construye 5 reglas de tu empresa
      must
      All employees must ___ (obligación interna fuerte — tu empresa lo exige)
      mustn't
      Employees mustn't ___ (prohibición — está completamente prohibido)
      have to
      We have to ___ (obligación externa — la ley o el cliente lo requiere)
      don't have to
      Staff don't have to ___ (opcional — no es necesario pero pueden)
      has to
      The manager has to ___ (3ª persona — una persona específica, una responsabilidad)
      Speaking Practice
      Explain Your Workplace Rules
      Maestro hace las preguntas. Alumno responde usando los 4 modales de M2. Mínimo 2 oraciones por respuesta — rule + reason.
      Question 01
      What are 2 things all employees in your company must do every day?
      Target: "We must [verb] because [reason]." Use 2 different must sentences.
      Question 02
      Is there anything that is completely forbidden at your workplace? Why?
      Target: "We mustn't [verb] — it's forbidden because [reason]."
      Question 03
      What do you have to do before every important meeting or client interaction?
      Target: "I have to [verb] because [reason]. My manager has to [verb] as well."
      Question 04
      What are 2 things that are optional or not required at your workplace?
      Target: "We don't have to [verb]. It's optional because [reason]."
      Question 05
      If a new employee asks "Do I have to [X]?" — what would you tell them?
      Target: The student explains a rule from their company naturally — mix of all 4 modals.
      🎯 Speaking Challenge — 90 seconds, no notes
      "You are the HR manager. A new employee just joined your company. Give them a quick welcome briefing — explain 4 rules using must, mustn't, have to, and don't have to."
      Target: the student produces all 4 modals correctly in a connected 90-second monologue. This is the S1A exit standard for M2. Minimum: 1 must + 1 mustn't + 1 have to + 1 don't have to in natural context.
