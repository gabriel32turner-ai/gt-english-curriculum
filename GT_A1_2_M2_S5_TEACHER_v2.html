Warm-Up — Quick-Fire Review
⏱ 8 min · 4 questions · 90 sec timer
📋 Logistics

Final session of Module 2. Warm-up reviews S1–S4 content (company description, products, quantities, orders) without framing it as a test. Keep it conversational. Badge ⚡ triggers on timer completion.

🎯 Pedagogical Focus
  • Q1: company description — what it does, products (S1)
  • Q2: student asks teacher — reversal builds confidence
  • Q3: quantities — how much/many, some/any (S2–S3)
  • Q4: orders and stock — student-to-student (S3–S4)
💡 Common Issues
  • "informations" → information / "equipments" → equipment
  • "fabric" used for factory → false friend, correct gently
  • Wrong quantifier: "much employees" → many employees

⚡ QUICK-FIRE · 90 SECONDS PER STUDENT

Q1 — What does your company do? What products or services does it offer?
Push: "How many employees does it have? Is there a warehouse?"
⭐ Student → Teacher

Push: "Now ask about the products — use 'What do you…?'"
🟢 Student → Student

Push: "What do you do when you run out of stock?"
🟣 Teacher → Student
Q4 — We need to order office supplies. What do we need?
Push: "Use: we need some… / we don't have any… / how many?"

Speaking Topic — My Company
⏱ 12 min · 3 rounds
📋 Logistics

3 rounds, 3–4 min each. Student speaks about their own company — real context. Teacher redirects only. Model answer only if student is completely stuck.

🎯 Pedagogical Focus
  • Round 1: basic company description — what it does, size, location
  • Round 2: products/services + quantities — push for some/any/how much/many
  • Round 3: opinion — what the company does well, what it needs
💡 Color Code

Blue = key vocab · Gold = grammar structure · Green = colloquial phrase

🏭
My Company
Round 1 — Describe
Tell me about your company. What does it do? How many employees does it have? Where is it?
Round 2 — Products & Quantities
What products or services does your company offer? Use: some / any / a lot of / how much / how many…
Round 3 — Your Opinion
What does your company do well? What does it need? Use: There is / There isn't / There are / There aren't…

I work for an automotive supplier in Silao. We produce parts for car factories. We have about 300 employees and two warehouses. We offer a lot of different products — metal parts, rubber components, and some plastic parts. We are pretty busy right now because there is a lot of demand. There isn't any space left in warehouse two. We need more equipment and more staff.

§4
Colloquial Phrases in Context
⏱ 10 min · 5 phrases · Student explains in English
📋 Logistics

5 phrases from M2 S1–S4. Student explains each phrase in English — no translation. Every example uses M2 grammar (some/any, countable/uncountable). Ask student to produce their own sentence before moving on.

🎯 Pedagogical Focus

Don't accept "I don't know" — ask "When do people say this at work?" or "Can you give me a situation?" The goal is active production, not passive recognition.

💡 Extension

Advanced: combine phrase + grammar. "We are pretty much out of stock — there isn't any paper left in warehouse two."

We are out of stock.
There are no more units available — production/sales context
"Sorry, we are out of stock. There isn't any left in the warehouse. I will place a new order today."
Let me check.
Used to pause and verify information — professional and natural
"How many units do you need? Let me check — I think we have some in the other warehouse."
That works. / That works for me.
Informal agreement — replaces "That is acceptable" in workplace English
"The delivery is on Friday? That works. Please send the invoice before Thursday."
One moment, please.
Polite pause — used on calls, in meetings, at reception
"You need the total for the order? One moment, please — I need to check the budget."
How many do you need?
Standard question for orders, supplies, quantities — direct and professional
"We need more folders and markers for the office. How many do you need? — About 50 of each."

Grammar — Countable / Uncountable + Quantifiers
⏱ 10 min · Reference Only · Poll + 2 Activities
📋 Logistics

REFERENTIAL — not a drilling session. Tables visible throughout. Quick Poll: 3 questions, 10 pts each. Activity A: choose the correct quantifier (3 items). Activity B: countable or uncountable? (3 items). Badge 🎯 unlocks at combined 4+/6.

🎯 Pedagogical Focus

The two tables (countable/uncountable + quantifiers) are the entire grammar system of M2. Point at the table — don't re-teach. Students should self-correct using the reference.

💡 Top Errors in M2
  • "informations / equipments / furnitures / staffs" → no plural on uncountables
  • "How much employees?" → How MANY (countable)
  • "We need some any paper" → pick one: some (aff.) / any (neg./Q)
  • "fabric" → FALSE FRIEND — means cloth, not factory

COUNTABLE vs UNCOUNTABLE — REFERENCE

TypeRuleExamples
Countable ✓Can be counted · has plural1 product / 2 products · 1 order / 3 orders · 1 employee / 50 employees
Uncountable ✗Cannot be counted · NO plural · NO a/aninformation · equipment · furniture · software · staff · money · time · space
QuantifierUse withContextExample
someboth (affirmative)Offers, requests, statementsWe have some stock. / I need some paper.
anyboth (negative / question)Negatives, questionsThere isn't any stock. / Do you have any folders?
how manycountable onlyAsking quantityHow many units do you need?
how muchuncountable / priceAsking quantity or priceHow much information do you have? / How much is it?
a lot ofboth (affirmative)Large quantityWe have a lot of orders this month.
much / manyneg. / questionFormal or negativeWe don't have much time. / We don't need many folders.

Listening — Marco Díaz: The Stock Problem
⏱ 12 min · Audio + MC + T/F + Speaking Response
📋 Logistics

ElevenLabs placeholder — record "Marco Díaz" voice (Spanish-accented male, professional, 85% speed, ~200 words). Scenario: Marco calls a supplier about a stock problem. 2 MC + 3 T/F. Play once for gist, twice for detail.

🎯 Pedagogical Focus
  • Target: recognize some/any, how much/many, countable/uncountable in natural speech
  • Highlighted words in script = direct link to §3 quiz vocabulary
  • Speaking Response: "What would you do in Marco's situation?"
💡 False Friend Alert in Script

"fabric" does NOT appear — intentional. If students ask about the word "fabric" thinking it means factory, use this moment to address the false friend. Answer: fabric = cloth/tela. Factory = planta/fábrica.

Hello, this is Marco Díaz from Silao Operations. I am calling about our order from last week — number 4471. We have a problem.

We don't have any stock left in warehouse two. There isn't any space in warehouse one either — it is full. We need the delivery this week. Not next week. This week.

We need about 500 units — how many do you have available right now? And how much is the total with express delivery? We have some budget left but not a lot.

Also — please send the invoice today. Our supplier manager needs it for the report. One moment, please — let me check the purchase order number... yes, it is PO-2026-0441. Thank you.

MULTIPLE CHOICE

1. Why is Marco calling the supplier?

2. What does Marco ask for at the end of the call?

TRUE / FALSE

Warehouse one has plenty of space for the new delivery.

Marco needs the delivery next week.

Marco's company has some budget available for express delivery.

🗣 Speaking Response

What would you do in Marco's situation? What do you say to your team?
