§1
What Are You Going to Do?
Activate future thinking · Connect past reports to future plans · Introduce "going to"
⏱ 5–8 MIN
▶
📋 From past to future: Last module you learned to say what happened in meetings. Now think: what's your plan for next week at work? Tell your partner ONE thing you are going to do.
💬 Quick translation: How do you say "Voy a llamar al cliente mañana" in English? Try: "I'm going to _____ the client _____." Don't worry if it's wrong — just try!
🧠 Think: What's the difference between "I call the client every Monday" (routine) and "I'm going to call the client tomorrow" (plan)? Which one is about the future?
⚡ Teacher note: M4 introduces the first FUTURE tense at A2.1. Students have only used Simple Present and Simple Past until now. The key conceptual shift: "going to" = a plan already in your head. Write 3–4 student attempts on the board. Don't correct yet — use them in §5.
⚡ Quick Win — 20 seconds
Say this out loud NOW: "I'm going to [something real from your job] [tomorrow / next week]."

§2
Planning Your Work Week
Valeria's project plan · Talk about scheduled activities · Going to in real context
⏱ 8 MIN
Context: Valeria Torres has a big week ahead at Solaris Manufacturing. She has 5 tasks planned. She's telling her manager Daniel about her plans for the week.
"Daniel, I want to tell you about my plans for this week. I'm going to visit the new supplier on Tuesday. I'm also going to prepare the Q1 report before Friday..."
💡 Pedagogical: This is students' first real production of "going to." Expect errors: "I going to" (missing am/is/are), "I'm going to prepared" (adding -ed). Correct gently — they're building the pattern for the first time.
Round 1 — My Plans
Tell your partner 3 things you are going to do at work this week. Use "I'm going to..." + a future time expression (tomorrow, next Monday, this Friday, etc.)
💡 Model: "I'm going to prepare the slides for the client meeting on Wednesday."
Round 2 — Are You Going to…?
Ask your partner 3 questions about their work plans: "Are you going to [attend/prepare/visit/send]…?" Answer with "Yes, I am" or "No, I'm not + reason."
💡 Ideas: meetings, reports, client calls, training sessions, deadlines
Round 3 — Valeria's Plan
Imagine you ARE Valeria. Tell Daniel 4 things you're going to do this week at Solaris Manufacturing. Include at least 1 negative: "I'm not going to…"
💡 Challenge: Use 4 different verbs + 3 different time expressions
"Daniel, here are my plans for this week. On Tuesday, I'm going to visit the new supplier in León. On Wednesday, I'm going to prepare the Q1 budget report. I'm not going to attend the 3 PM meeting on Thursday because I have a client call. On Friday, I'm going to present the report to the team."

§4
Everyday Plan Talk
5 natural phrases that native speakers use when talking about plans at work
⏱ 5 MIN
"We're good to go."
= Everything is ready, we can start
"The presentation is done, the room is set up — we're good to go!"
"It's happening."
= The plan is confirmed, it's going to happen
"The client approved the project — it's happening!"
"We're on track."
= The project is progressing as planned
"Don't worry, Daniel — we're on track. We're going to meet the deadline."
"That's the plan."
= Yes, that's what we intend to do
"Are you going to present on Friday?" — "That's the plan."
"Let's make it happen."
= Let's do it! (motivational)
"We have the budget, we have the team — let's make it happen."
🎭 Engagement: Have students practice these in pairs — one says a plan with "going to" and the partner responds with one of the colloquial phrases. It gets fun fast: "I'm going to finish the report tonight." — "Let's make it happen!"

Be Going To — Full System
Your first future tense · Plans you already have in your head · Aff / Neg / Questions
⏱ 15–18 MIN
🚨 Real Situation — Friday Afternoon
Your manager asks: "What are your plans for next week?"

You already decided: visit a client on Monday, prepare a report on Wednesday, organize a meeting on Friday.
In Spanish: "Voy a visitar al cliente el lunes. Voy a preparar el reporte el miércoles."
❓ How do you say "voy a" in English? What structure do you use for plans you already have?
✅ The Tool: Be Going To + Infinitive
"I'm going to visit the client on Monday."
"I'm going to prepare the report on Wednesday."

Key insight: Use going to when you already have a PLAN in your head — you decided before speaking.

⚠️ Top 4 errors — correct immediately:
✗ "I going to call" → ✅ I'm going to call — don't forget am/is/are!
✗ "She's going to prepared" → ✅ She's going to prepare — infinitive, no -ed!
✗ "Are you going attend?" → ✅ Are you going to attend? — never drop "to"
✗ "I'm going to go to go" → ✅ I'm going to go to the meeting — only one "going to"

GT Original — "Gonna" vs "Going to"
  • 🔊 Pronunciation awareness: In natural speech, "going to" sounds like "gonna" /ˈɡʌnə/. Teach awareness, NOT production — at A2.1 students should SAY "going to" but RECOGNIZE "gonna" when they hear it.
  • ⚠️ Writing rule: NEVER write "gonna" in professional emails, reports, or messages. Always "going to."
  • 📊 Going to vs Simple Present: "I'm going to visit the client tomorrow" (my plan) vs "The meeting starts at 10" (fixed schedule, not my choice). Both are future — different reasons.
  • 🌍 Cultural note: In US business, "going to" is the default way to talk about work plans. "What are you going to do about this?" is the most common question from managers.

Project Planning Call — What We're Going to Do
Valeria explains her project plan for next quarter · Listen for "going to" throughout
⏱ 8 MIN
Project Planning Call — Q2 Plans
A2.1 · M4 · S1A · Voice: Sarah (American English) · Speed: 0.82
Good morning, everyone. Thanks for joining the call. I want to talk about our plans for the next quarter.

First, we're going to launch the new packaging line in March. The timeline is ready — we're going to start production on March 3rd. I'm going to visit the supplier in León next week to confirm the materials.

Second, I'm going to prepare the Q1 budget report. The deadline is March 15th. I'm going to present it to the board on March 20th. Daniel, are you going to attend that meeting?

Third, the operations team is going to implement the new inventory system. They're going to set it up during the first two weeks of April. We're not going to postpone this — it's a priority.

I'm also going to organize a training session for the warehouse team. They're going to need it before the new system goes live.

Finally, we're not going to change the delivery schedule this quarter. The client confirmed — the current timeline is fine.

Any questions about the plan? Remember — we're going to have a follow-up meeting next Friday at 10 AM.

My Plans — Going to in Action
Real production · Use "going to" to talk about your own plans · Pair activity
⏱ 12 MIN
💡 Pedagogical: Students answer using "going to" + future time expressions. Partner marks: (1) missing am/is/are, (2) adding -ed after "going to." Correct gently — this is first production.
"Planning is the key to success at work."
50%
Question 1 — My Weekly Plan
Tell your partner 4 things you are going to do at work this week. Use a different future time expression for each one (tomorrow, next Monday, this Friday, in two days).
💡 Model: "Tomorrow I'm going to prepare the quarterly report. On Wednesday, I'm going to..."
Question 2 — Are You Going to…?
Partner A asks 4 questions using "Are you going to…?" about work plans. Partner B answers "Yes, I am + detail" or "No, I'm not + reason." Then switch.
💡 Ideas: "Are you going to attend any training? / visit any clients? / finish the project?"
Question 3 — Project Plan Pitch
Imagine you're presenting your team's project plan to your manager. Describe 3 things the team is going to do and 1 thing you're NOT going to do. Use different subjects (I / we / the team / she).
💡 Challenge: Include "We're not going to..." with a reason why.
Question 4 — What About the Problem?
Your partner describes a work problem (delay, budget issue, cancelled meeting). Ask: "What are you going to do about it?" They answer with their plan. Give 2 problems each.
💡 Model: "The supplier is late." → "I'm going to contact them today and arrange a new delivery date."
⭐ Challenge — 60 Second Plan
In 60 seconds, give a project update to your "manager" (partner). Include: 3 things you're going to do, 1 thing you're NOT going to do, and use at least 3 different future time expressions. No notes!

3 Phrases You Can Use Starting Tomorrow
PHRASE 1
"I'm going to [verb] [object] [by/on + date]."
Use this in standups, WhatsApp updates, or emails to say what you plan to do. Replaces "I do it tomorrow" with correct future English.
PHRASE 2
"Are you going to attend the meeting on [day]?"
Perfect for asking colleagues about plans. Natural and professional. Use it in your next team chat or email.
PHRASE 3
"We're on track — we're going to meet the deadline."
The perfect manager update. Combines colloquial + going to. Shows confidence and clarity in English.
