📌 Teacher Follow-Up Questions
"How long have you been working on that? Is it since a specific date or for a number of months?"
"Have you ever achieved something like this before in your career?"
"What haven't you finished yet that is connected to this achievement?"
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Free production diagnostic. Do NOT correct during the warm-up. Note: how many spontaneous present perfect uses? Do they avoid specific past time markers when using PP? Do they self-correct simple past to present perfect?
  • The follow-up questions are designed to elicit specific PP forms: "since/for" (question 1), "ever" (question 2), "yet" (question 3). Use them selectively based on what the student produces naturally.
  • If the student uses only simple past: in §4 coaching, highlight that every sentence they said in the warm-up could be reframed in present perfect — and ask them to do it.
  • B1.1 hallmark: picture prompt produces more spontaneous, connected speech than scenario. Students at this level should attempt 5+ sentences. Note if they fall short — that gap drives §4 preparation.
Capa 2 · Engagement

Capa 3 · Growth & Retention

§ 02
Recap Speedrun — S1A in 5 Minutes
5 min

💡 Tap each card to check. Activation before free production — no new grammar here.

01
PP Structure
tap to check
have / has + past participle
"I have exceeded. She has led. We have delivered."
02
Irregular Participles
tap to check
gone · done · led · driven · built · grown · made · written
NOT "have went / have drived"
03
ever / never
tap to check
ever → questions · never → negative statements
"Have you ever led a team?" / "I have never missed a deadline."
04
for / since
tap to check
for = duration · since = start point
"for 3 years / since 2021 / since January"
05
just · yet · already
tap to check
just = very recently · yet = until now (neg/Q) · already = before expected
"just launched · haven't finished yet · already exceeded"
Quick verbal check: "Give me a sentence with 'already.' Now one with 'yet' in a negative. Now ask me a question with 'ever.'" Three clean answers = ready for free production.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • The verbal check is non-negotiable. Three sentences minimum: already + yet (negative) + ever (question). This forces production of the three most commonly misused adverbs.
  • If the student says "I have already exceeded my target last month" → immediate correction: "last month" = simple past, remove it OR remove the time marker. The rule is absolute.
  • If irregular participles are weak, do a 30-second rapid-fire: "drive?" → "driven" / "lead?" → "led" / "build?" → "built." Three rounds, 60 seconds. Then move on.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "In S1A you learned the grammar. Right now you activate it. In §4 you own it. In §5 you USE it. That's the arc of today."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Speed matters. If recap takes more than 5 minutes, a rule from S1A was not consolidated. Note it and revisit at the start of S2A.

Listening — Two Executives Recap Their Year
10 min
🎧
Leadership Roundtable — Year-End Executive Conversation
B1.1 · Two speakers · ~120 seconds · American English

🔈 Audio placeholder. Replace with Google Drive embed.
ElevenLabs voices: Sarah (exec 1) + Adam (exec 2) · Rate: 0.85 · Stability: 0.6

▶ Show ElevenLabs Script
SARAH — VP of Operations

I have to say, this has been the most challenging year I have ever managed. But I am genuinely proud of what we have built. Our team has delivered every single project on time — that has never happened before in this division.

ADAM — VP of Commercial

Same on our side. We have exceeded revenue targets by 28%. I have never seen the sales team this focused. We have secured 11 new enterprise accounts since January — and we have already started onboarding 4 of them for Q1.

SARAH

Have you faced any major setbacks this year?

ADAM

Honestly, yes. We haven't broken into the international market yet. That was a priority for Q3 and we haven't achieved it. But we have learned a lot from that. We have built the right partnerships and I believe we will be ready in Q2 next year.

SARAH

I think that is the right attitude. We have done a lot this year. We have grown. And we have set the foundation for a strong 2025. I have already spoken with the board and they have approved the investment for the expansion.

ADAM

That is great news. Have you told the team yet?

SARAH

Not yet. I am saving that for the all-hands on Friday.

⏱ Target: 115–125 sec · Voices: Sarah (VP Ops) + Adam (VP Commercial) · Speed: 0.85 · Count PP uses: 16+ instances
Listening Activities
First listen: global. Second listen: answer these questions.
By how much has Adam's team exceeded its revenue targets?
What has Sarah already done regarding the board and expansion?
What hasn't Adam's team achieved yet?
Sarah's team has delivered every project on time — and this has happened before in their division.
Sarah has not yet told the team about the board's approval for expansion.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • 16+ present perfect instances in ~120 seconds. This is B1.1-appropriate density for executive conversation. Listen 1: global. Listen 2: questions. Listen 3: count every PP use — this trains metalinguistic awareness at B1.1.
  • L1 trap: "That has never happened before in this division" — Sarah says projects were delivered on time AND this has never happened before. Not that projects were NOT delivered. Two facts combined. Students often hear only one.
  • Key PP pattern to highlight after: "We haven't broken into the international market YET" + "we haven't achieved it" = unfinished goal. Ask student: "What is one thing YOU haven't achieved yet this year?"
  • After §3: "Retell Adam's year in 4 sentences using present perfect — third person. 'He has exceeded…'" 60-second oral transfer task.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "That conversation is indistinguishable from a real executive year-end roundtable at a Fortune 500 company. You just understood all of it — and you know WHY every verb is in present perfect."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In §5 you are Sarah or Adam. Your year, your results, your language. That's the transfer goal."
§ 04
Speaking Preparation — Your Year in Review
15 min
Your Mission for §5
Present your year in review to your CEO. 90 seconds. No notes.
Use present perfect at least 8 times.
Include: accomplishments (what you have achieved), challenges (what you haven't finished yet / what has been difficult), and lessons (what you have learned).
Use at least one "ever" or "never" and at least one "just," "already," or "yet."
Presentation Scaffold — Organize your thoughts in the next 5 minutes
Opening
This has been a [challenging / productive / transformative] year. Here is what my team and I have achieved.
Key Win 1
We have [verb] [result]. This is the [first time / most / best] we have ever [verb].
Key Win 2
I have also [verb] [specific outcome]. We have already [verb + result] for next year.
Challenge / Gap
We haven't [verb + goal] yet. However, we have [learned / built / identified] [what changed].
Lesson Learned
The most important thing I have learned this year is [insight]. That has changed how I [approach / lead / think about]…
Closing
Overall, I have never been more [confident / motivated / prepared] for what is ahead. We have built the foundation for [next goal].
8 Advanced Phrases — Click to hear and internalize
"I have managed to…"
"I have managed to reduce the team's response time by 50%."
"We have successfully…"
"We have successfully launched our second product line."
"One thing I have learned is…"
"One thing I have learned is that speed matters more than perfection."
"I haven't quite finished…"
"I haven't quite finished the international strategy yet."
"We have just launched…"
"We have just launched a new quality process this month."
"I have been responsible for…"
"I have been responsible for three cross-functional initiatives."
"I have never been more…"
"I have never been more confident in the direction we are taking."
"What I am most proud of is…"
"What I am most proud of is how the team has grown this year."
⏱
5 minutes to prepare mentally. Read the scaffold. Choose your 3 key wins. Choose your 1 challenge. Pick 2 advanced phrases. Identify which adverbs you will use (just / already / yet / ever / never). Then close everything — in §5, you speak without any notes or scaffold.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Walk through the scaffold orally with the student: "Tell me your opening line." → "Good. Now your first key win — say it in present perfect." → "Now the challenge — use 'yet.'" This oral rehearsal IS the preparation. Not silent reading.
  • The 8 phrases are calibrated for B1.1 density. "I have been responsible for" introduces a form of PP continuous without explicitly teaching it — the student absorbs it through use. Do not explain the grammar; just have them repeat and practice.
  • B1.1 minimum for §5: 8 PP uses + 1 ever/never + 1 just/already/yet + 1 challenge framed with "haven't yet" + 60 continuous seconds. All 5 = full mastery.
  • If student chooses wins with specific past times (e.g. "I exceeded my target in Q2"): coach them to reframe → "I have exceeded my target this year" (remove the specific time or change to "this year" which keeps PP valid).
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "The scaffold is your last safety net. After this, no tables, no hints, no grammar rules. Just you and the grammar you've already internalized."
  • "The phrase 'I have never been more confident' — that's the kind of sentence that stops a room. It's powerful. Use it."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "This scaffold structure — opening, wins, challenge, lesson, closing — is the exact structure of a board presentation in English. You will use this professionally."
§ 05
Speaking Free — Your Real Year Recap
15 min
You are presenting to your CEO. 90 seconds. No notes. No scaffold. Present your year in review using present perfect at least 8 times. Include one achievement, one challenge, and one lesson. Start with: "This has been a year where I have…"
CEO Follow-Up Prompts — Use selectively after the 90-second presentation
"What's the biggest milestone you've hit this year?"
"Have you faced any setbacks? How have you handled them?"
"What have you learned about leadership this year?"
"Have your priorities changed since January?"
"What haven't you finished yet — and what is the plan?"
"How has your team grown since you took the role?"
"What's the most important lesson this year has taught you?"
"Have you taken any risks that paid off?"
"What would you do differently if you could start January again?"
"What's your top priority for next year — and what have you already done to prepare?"
Self-Evaluation — After Your Presentation
  • I used present perfect at least 8 times correctly
  • I used at least one "ever" or "never" correctly
  • I used at least one "just," "already," or "yet" correctly
  • I described a challenge using "haven't… yet"
  • I spoke for 60+ seconds without stopping
  • I avoided specific past time markers with present perfect
  • I used at least one advanced phrase from §4
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Do NOT interrupt during the 90-second presentation. Take notes on: (a) PP uses — correct or incorrect, (b) any specific past time markers used with PP (main error), (c) which adverbs appeared, (d) overall fluency and confidence.
  • Post-presentation feedback protocol: (1) state one thing that was excellent with a specific example, (2) state one grammar correction with a full example sentence, (3) ask them to repeat that one sentence correctly. Maximum 3 minutes of feedback.
  • Optional second round: student presents again — 60 seconds, same content, with corrections incorporated. The second delivery is always noticeably stronger at B1.1. This second round is the most valuable 60 seconds of the session.
  • B1.1 mastery threshold: 8+ PP uses + zero specific-time PP errors + 60+ continuous seconds + 1 ever/never + 1 just/already/yet = full mastery signal. Note in your records.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You just presented your professional year to your CEO in English. In present perfect. For 90 seconds. That is a genuinely rare skill — and you did it."
  • If the presentation was strong: "That is exactly what I want to hear in every board meeting. You sounded like a confident international professional."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In S2A you'll add wh-questions to this — 'What have you achieved? / How long have you worked here? / How has your team grown?' You'll be the interviewer and the interviewee. More depth, more fluency."
