Warm-Up — New Scenario · S1A Recall
Different scenario, same grammar — no preparation allowed
5–8 min
📍 Scenario — The Confidential Information
You are at work. You overhear a private conversation between two senior directors. You discover that the company is planning significant layoffs next month — but the announcement hasn't been made yet. Three of your close teammates don't know. They are making long-term plans based on their job security.
If you were in this situation — what would you do? Would you tell your teammates? Would you stay silent? Would you speak to HR? Respond with at least two 2nd conditional sentences. No notes. No hesitation.
45
—
Press START when student begins speaking
Teacher Follow-Up Prompts
Q1If your closest teammate asked you directly: "Is everything okay?" — what would you say?
Q2If your manager asked you to sign a document confirming you never shared the information — what would you do?
Q3If you could speak to company leadership right now — what would you tell them to do differently?
Q4If your teammates found out later that you knew — and said nothing — what would happen to your relationships?
Q5Which vocabulary word from S1A describes exactly how you feel in this scenario right now?
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This scenario is calibrated to force 2nd conditional IMMEDIATELY: "If I told my teammate, I would risk..." / "If I stayed silent, I would feel..." Both branches require the structure. The situation is genuinely uncomfortable — that's intentional. Discomfort activates authentic language.
  • Q5 is the vocabulary re-activation trigger. Target words: accountability, integrity, transparency, dilemma, navigate. If student uses any of these in a 2nd conditional sentence in the first 30 seconds — that is the S1B benchmark moment. Note it.
  • Common error: "If I will say something..." → Redirect: "After 'if' in 2nd conditional, no future. Which form do we use? Past simple. Try again."
  • If student says "I would to tell them..." → missing base verb rule. Quick fix: "Would is already the modal — no 'to' after would. 'I would tell.'"
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "This is not a grammar exercise. This is a real situation that happens in real companies. Have you ever been in a position where you knew something your colleagues didn't? Tell me — in 2nd conditional."
  • Micro-celebration trigger: If student produces a fluent 2nd conditional in 45 seconds without notes — stop everything. "Did you hear that? No script. No hesitation. That was B1.2 English. That's what today is about."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In S1A you learned the structure. Today you're going to use it in a role-play where there is no correct answer — only stronger and weaker arguments. At the end of class, you'll handle a real business negotiation entirely in English, under time pressure, with no script."
  • Upsell seed: "The professionals who can argue competing positions in English are the ones who get asked back to the table. That's what we're building today."

✏️ Activity — Word in Context
Read the situation. Choose the S1A word that fits best. Think about collocations.
Your colleague is arguing loudly with a client on the phone. You are the most senior person nearby. Which verb best describes what you should do?
Your manager gives you conflicting priorities and an impossible schedule. You need to talk to them about it. Which noun describes what you would bring to that conversation?
Two directors disagree about the project direction in a meeting. They cannot reach consensus. As the facilitator, you need to ___ the competing priorities.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • The 5 nouns are in reduced cards (no IPA, no full definition) — the focus is production: "Use the word in a 2nd conditional sentence. Your sentence. Your real job." This is S1B — no re-teaching, only application.
  • Collocation grid: ask the student to PICK one collocation from each row and build a full 2nd conditional. "If my manager escalated the issue to HR, what would happen?" That's the target.
  • WH2: "tension" fits best because the student is addressing a conflict of priorities — they are bringing the tension to the conversation, not their personal accountability or integrity. Common distractor: students choose "accountability" — redirect: "What WORD describes what the problem is in that situation? What is the name of the feeling/state?"
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Choose one colloquial phrase. Give me a real sentence — something that has actually happened to you, or to someone you know. Use 2nd conditional. Go." If student says "My colleague dropped the ball on a report last month" — push: "Good. Now put it in 2nd conditional: 'If my colleague dropped the ball again, I would...'"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Vocabulary activation note for §SP: In the role-play later, student should use at least 3 vocabulary words from S1A + 1 colloquial phrase. Remind them now: "In the role-play, I am listening for these words. If you use them naturally — that is a B1.2 production score."
§ 03
Dilemma Card Deck
8 real workplace scenarios · tap to reveal · produce 2nd conditional + S1A vocabulary
8 min

How to use: Tap a card to flip it. Read the dilemma. Respond using 2nd conditional + at least one vocabulary word from S1A. Work through as many as time allows.

Dilemma 1
Sales
The Overpromise
Your company promised a client a product feature in 30 days. Your tech lead just confirmed it requires 90 days minimum. The contract is already signed. The client presentation is next week.
If you were the account manager, what would you do? What would you say to the client?
Try: transparency · handle · address
Dilemma 2
Ethics
The Silent Partner
You notice a colleague is doing paid freelance work for a competitor during company hours, using company equipment. You know them well. They have never caused problems before.
If you discovered this, what would you do? Would you speak to them first or go directly to management?
Try: accountability · speak up · navigate
Dilemma 3
HR
The Unfair Review
Your performance review contains serious inaccuracies — achievements you delivered are attributed to a colleague who was barely involved. Your manager signs it without asking for your input.
If this happened to you, what would you do? Would you sign the review? Would you push back?
Try: integrity · push back · defuse
Dilemma 4
Finance
The Shortcut
Your director asks you to approve a financial report that contains a significant estimation error — one that makes the department look profitable when it actually isn't. "No one will audit this quarter," they say.
If your director asked you to approve this, what would you do? Would you refuse? What would you say?
Try: accountability · credibility · escalate
Dilemma 5
Team
The Blame Game
A project fails publicly. Your manager, in the post-mortem with senior leadership, implies that the failure was due to "team execution issues" — placing responsibility on people you know were given inadequate resources and unrealistic timelines.
If you were in that post-mortem meeting, what would you say? Would you defend your team publicly?
Try: tension · mediate · push back
Dilemma 6
Client
The Impossible Request
A key client demands that you personally fire one of your team members — a high performer who had a difficult conversation with them. The client says the contract renewal depends on it.
If a client demanded this, what would you do? Would you comply? What would you say to the client?
Try: integrity · handle · set boundaries
Dilemma 7
Confidential
The Inside Information
You accidentally receive an email meant for someone else. It contains confidential merger information that would significantly affect a colleague's decision to accept a new job offer at a competitor.
If you received this email by mistake, what would you do? Would you say anything? To whom?
Try: transparency · navigate · accountability
Dilemma 8
Leadership
The Credit Claim
Your manager presents a strategic proposal in a board meeting — a proposal that is almost word-for-word what you wrote and shared with them privately three weeks ago. No attribution. No mention of your name.
If this happened to you, what would you do? Would you address it with your manager directly?
Try: credibility · confront · speak up
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Flip 4–6 cards minimum. For each: student responds in 2nd conditional + at least 1 vocabulary word. Mark ✓ mentally when both criteria are met. If only one met — push for the other.
  • Card 7 (Inside Information) and Card 8 (Credit Claim) tend to generate the most emotional response — use these for peak engagement. Save one for the role-play in §5.
  • Challenge mode at B1.2: after student gives their answer, take the opposite position. "Actually — if I were in your situation, I would NOT address it directly. Here's why..." Force them to rebut in 2nd conditional.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Pick any card that feels closest to something you've actually experienced at work. Don't tell me what happened — tell me what you WOULD do if it happened again, with everything you know now."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Every card you just responded to — those are real situations from real companies. The language you used to describe your decisions — that is B1.2 English in a professional context."
§ 04
Grammar Check — Error Correction Only
No re-teaching · Find and fix · Produce corrected sentences from your real context
10 min
🔍 Activity 1 — Spot the Error
Each sentence contains one 2nd conditional error. Identify the correct fix.
Find the error: "If I would speak up in that meeting, my manager would get angry."
Find the error: "If I am in your position, I would handle it very differently."
Find the error: "If the client found out, the consequences will be serious."
🔀 Activity 2 — Build the 2nd Conditional
Tap fragments in the correct order to build a B1.2 2nd conditional sentence. Press CHECK when ready.
I would address it with management directly.
If my colleague took credit for my work,
but first I would document everything.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This is NOT a re-teaching section. If student gets all three correct immediately — spend the saved time on oral production from their real job context.
  • After each activity: "Now produce your own version — same structure, your real situation. 'If [real scenario from your job], I would [your real decision].' Go."
  • GC1 targets the #1 B1.2 error (would in if-clause). GC2 targets the formal "were" rule. GC3 targets the 1st/2nd conditional confusion in the main clause.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Three error types. Three minutes. Let's see how fast you can identify them — then immediately produce a correct version about your own work situation."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "GC1: the 'would in the if-clause' error — this is what separates B1.1 from B1.2. If you can avoid it automatically, you are at B1.2. If you have to think about it — almost there."
§ 05
Reading — The LinkedIn Post
Mexican professional · Real ethical dilemma · 2nd conditional reflection · Production trigger
8 min
Rodrigo Fuentes
Operations Manager · Montoya Group · Guadalajara, México
LinkedIn Post

Last year, I faced one of the most difficult situations of my career. My director asked me to sign off on a supplier contract that I knew — based on information I had seen — was significantly overpriced. The difference was approximately 18% above market rate.

I had a choice. If I signed without questioning it, I would have protected my relationship with my director — but I would have compromised something more important: my accountability to the company.

That being said, speaking up to a senior director is not easy. If I had pushed back aggressively, I might have created a conflict that damaged the team. On the other hand, silence would have made me complicit in a decision I didn't agree with.

I chose to address it. Privately. I said: "I want to make sure we have considered all options before we finalize this. I'd like to navigate this together — can we review the figures once more?" It was uncomfortable. He didn't love it. But in the end, we renegotiated. The company saved money. And I kept my integrity.

Perhaps the question worth asking is not 'what happened' — but rather: if I had said nothing, what would I have lost? And what does your answer to that question say about how you navigate pressure?

"If I had said nothing, I would have lost something more important than a job title." — Rodrigo Fuentes

Color key: 2nd conditional (green)  ·  discourse markers (cyan)  ·  S1A vocabulary (teal bg)

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This text is calibrated for two purposes: (1) reading comprehension, and (2) production trigger. After RT3, ask: "Rodrigo ends with 'If you were in this situation — what would you do?' Answer him. Your actual answer. 2nd conditional. Go."
  • TF1 target: Students who skim may think he was immediately certain. He was uncertain about HOW to push back — not IF to push back. Distinguish approach uncertainty from values clarity.
  • Post-reading oral production: "Rodrigo wrote 'if I had said nothing, I would have lost something more important than a job title.' Rewrite that sentence using a vocabulary word from S1A in place of 'something more important': 'I would have lost my accountability / my integrity / my credibility.'"
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Rodrigo says the answer to his question 'says a lot about how you navigate pressure.' What does your answer say about you? Tell me — in 2nd conditional."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Rodrigo wrote this in English on LinkedIn. He is a Mexican professional, in a Mexican company, writing publicly about integrity in English. That is the professional identity we are building here. The role-play in §SP is your version of this story."

Voices: Sarah (Daniela) · George (Miguel) · Speed 0.92 · American English

▶ Show ElevenLabs Script (Teacher Only)
DANIELA (Sarah)

Miguel, this presentation is tomorrow at nine. Please tell me this is fixable.

MIGUEL (George)

Daniela, that is exactly what I am trying to tell you. If we sent this proposal without fixing it, we would be promising a 45-day implementation that takes at least 90.

DANIELA

I know. But if we called the client now, we would lose everything we built this quarter. Two years of relationship, gone.

MIGUEL

And if we said nothing and they found out after signing — what would happen then?

DANIELA

I do not know... maybe we could manage it.

MIGUEL

"Maybe we could manage it"? If I were in the client's position, I would want to know before I signed anything. Period.

DANIELA

And if you were in my position — with the team's targets and the Q2 bonus on the line — what would you do?

MIGUEL

I would bite the bullet and call them. Tonight.

DANIELA

That is easy to say.

MIGUEL

Daniela, if we compromised our integrity on this deal, what would that do to the next one? And the one after that?

DANIELA

(pause) Fine. If I called them tonight, what would we say?

MIGUEL

We say the truth. "We identified an issue and we would not be doing our job if we did not address it before tomorrow."

DANIELA

You know what? If they respected us for being honest, it would actually strengthen the relationship.

MIGUEL

That is what accountability looks like.

⏱ Target: ~130 sec · 2nd conditional: 9 instances · Colloquial: "bite the bullet" · Rate: 0.92 · Speakers: Sarah (Daniela) / George (Miguel)
✏️ Listening Activities
Listen and answer. Focus on the argument structure — who says what, and why.
What specific problem did Miguel discover with the proposal?
What does Miguel argue would happen if they won the deal dishonestly?
✅ True or False
Mark each statement True or False based on the listening.
Daniela's initial instinct is to call the client immediately and be transparent about the problem.
Miguel uses the colloquial phrase "bite the bullet" to describe the action he would take — calling the client that evening.
By the end of the conversation, Daniela realizes that being honest with the client might actually strengthen the relationship.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • 9 instances of 2nd conditional in ~130 seconds. Second listen if needed — natural at B1.2. After comprehension: "Who did you agree with more — Daniela or Miguel? Defend your position in 2nd conditional. Right now. Go."
  • LTF1 trap: Daniela's INITIAL instinct is NOT to call the client — she is worried about losing the relationship. Students who listen superficially may assume she's the one pushing for transparency. She arrives there only at the end.
  • After listening: role-play trigger — "You are now Miguel. I am Daniela. The call to the client starts NOW. What do we say? Use 2nd conditional throughout the call." This is the bridge to §SP.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Count the 'would' instances. Answer: 9. That is 9 production opportunities in 130 seconds. That is the density we are aiming for in the role-play."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Notice how the audio ends — with 'that is what accountability looks like.' That word — accountability — is from §2. Hearing it in a natural, high-stakes dialogue is how vocabulary moves from knowledge to fluency."
  • Upsell: "In S2A you'll use the 2nd conditional for a different type of hypothetical — ethical decisions around innovation and change. Same structure, completely different context."

Wrap-Up — Speaking Challenge + FCW

§SP — Role-Play · Your Exit Task
You are Miguel from the listening. You must now make the call to the client. I am the client. No notes. No script. Use 2nd conditional throughout. Include at least 3 vocabulary words from S1A and 1 colloquial phrase.

After 60 seconds I will push back. You must defend the decision in English. That is the exit task for S1B.
The call begins now. You are the operations manager. The client just answered the phone. Open with something like: "I wanted to call you personally because if we sent you the proposal as written, it would not be accurate — and I would not be doing my job if I let that happen." — Then handle whatever I say next.
Teacher Role-Play Prompts — Use these as the "client"
"What exactly is the problem? Why didn't you tell us sooner?"
"If you can't deliver in 30 days, why did your company promise it?"
"Should I be worried that there are other things you haven't told me?"
"If we went with your competitor instead, what would you say to that?"
"What would you do if this were your company's money on the line?"
"How do I know I can trust you going forward?"
"If I gave you one more week to fix this — what would that fix exactly?"
"What would you do differently if you could start this proposal over?"
Self-Evaluation — After the Role-Play
  • I opened the call with a 2nd conditional sentence naturally
  • I used at least 3 vocabulary words from S1A in context
  • I used at least 1 colloquial phrase naturally (not forced)
  • I responded to at least 2 client challenges in 2nd conditional
  • I spoke for 60+ seconds without stopping to translate
  • I kept "would" out of the if-clause throughout
