💬 Colloquial — How Gen Z Talks About Addiction

5 min
These phrases are used daily — master them and you'll sound completely natural
"I'm hooked."
When: addicted or obsessed with any app, series, or habit
"I started using TikTok 'just to see what it was' and now I'm completely hooked."
"It blew up."
When: something became viral or popular extremely fast
"He posted a video of his cat and it blew up overnight — 2 million views."
"That's sus."
When: something seems suspicious or dishonest
"He deleted all his posts from 2019 the same day. That's sus."
"Low-key obsessed."
When: secretly or quietly addicted to something
"I'm low-key obsessed with checking my follower count, honestly."
Drill: Teacher provides context → student produces phrase. "Tu colega revisó Instagram 40 veces hoy." → "He's hooked." / "Un video tuyo tiene 500K reproducciones." → "It blew up!" / "Alguien borró todos sus tweets de 2020." → "That's sus." / "Describes your own secret app obsession." → "I'm low-key obsessed with…"

"A notification is sent."
The app sends you a notification — but the passive hides who sent it. You feel the urge; they take the credit. By whom? The platform. The algorithm. The design team. You never know — and that's the point.
Form: be (present) + past participle → Present Passive · Agent (by…) is optional — often hidden intentionally.
REFERENCIAL. S1 covered introduction. S2 goes deeper: why the Passive is CHOSEN in tech/news contexts — to hide the agent, to sound objective, to avoid blame. This is the B1.2 insight: not just "how" but "why".
The critical question: "Who IS the agent?" — "Your data is collected BY WHOM?" Push students to find the hidden agent in every passive sentence.
TenseActive → PassiveSocial Media Context
Present Simplebe + past participleYour data is sold to advertisers every day.
Present Continuousis/are being + ppYou are being tracked right now.
Past Simplewas/were + ppThese apps were designed to maximize engagement.
Present Perfecthas/have been + ppMy screen time has been monitored for years.
With agentpassive + by [agent]The feed was curated by an algorithm — not a human.
Agentlesspassive (no by)Thousands of hours are wasted on this app every day.
⚠️ KEY INSIGHT — WHY PASSIVE IS CHOSEN
"Data is collected" → sounds neutral, avoids blame. Active: "Facebook collects your data" — that's confrontational. Tech companies prefer the passive. Now you know why.

🎧 Listening — Carlos's Wake-Up Call

8 min
🎙️
ElevenLabs — "Carlos's Wake-Up Call"
Voice: Marcus · Speed: 0.85 · B1.2 M3 S2A
I've always thought of myself as someone who uses technology intentionally. Then last week my phone showed me my weekly screen time report. Four hours and thirty-seven minutes. Per day. That's over thirty hours a week. I was shocked. More than a full-time job — being wasted on Instagram and TikTok.

And the worst part? I can remember almost nothing I watched. Hours of content were consumed, and I have nothing to show for it. No new ideas were formed. No skills were built. The time was just... taken from me. Or I gave it away — I'm not sure which.

I started researching how these apps work. What I found was disturbing. These platforms were specifically designed by teams of engineers and behavioral psychologists. The infinite scroll was invented so that you never reach a natural stopping point. Notifications are sent at calculated intervals — not when something happens, but when you're most likely to re-engage. Your weaknesses are being studied and exploited every time you open the app.

I'm not saying the apps are evil. But I do think we should know the truth: we are not the customers. We are the product. Our attention is being sold to advertisers while we scroll for free.

I deleted Instagram and TikTok two weeks ago. I've been called dramatic. I've been told "it's just an app." But those hours have been reclaimed. I've read two books. I've been sleeping better. My concentration has improved. And honestly? I don't miss it. I miss who I thought I was on it. And that's a different problem entirely.
📎 ElevenLabs: Voice — Marcus · Speed 0.85 · B1.2 M3 S2A
