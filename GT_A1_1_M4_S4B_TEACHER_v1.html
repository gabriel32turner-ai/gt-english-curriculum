Hot Take — What Floor? What Colour?
Ordinals + colours under pressure · Full description in 60 seconds · No notes
⏱ 5 MIN
Scenario — Celaya, Guanajuato
Your European client is standing outside your building and calls: "I'm outside — I can see several buildings. Which one is yours? And which floor are you on?" Describe your building completely in 60 seconds so they can find you.
Describe your building (colour, floors, what's on the ground floor) AND give the floor of your office using the correct ordinal. Start with: "Our building is the [colour]…"
🔥Badge earned: complete building description (colour + floors + ordinal) before timer ends
⭐Bonus: include a landmark nearby — "next to the [colour] building / opposite the [place]"
🎯Teacher: tally ordinals + colours used. Write them on the board. Class record!
1:00
Hot Take
📌 Teacher Note

The goal is spontaneous production of ordinals and colours in context — not memorised phrases. Students who self-correct mid-sentence ("it's on the... fourth — no, fifth floor") are demonstrating real cognitive processing. Celebrate the self-correction as much as the accuracy.

§2
Pitch — Big City vs Small Town
Argue using ordinals, colours, distances · Grammar embedded in real debate
⏱ 10 MIN
Team A — Big City is better for business
Everything in one place!
  • "There are twenty-floor buildings — the first floor has a bank, the second a pharmacy..."
  • "The hotel is two blocks from the office — it's a tall grey building on the corner."
  • "How far is the airport? About thirty minutes — not far for a big city."
  • "There are blue, grey, and white buildings everywhere — you can find anything on the third or fourth floor."
Team B — Small Town is better for business
Everything is close!
  • "The bank is on the ground floor of our building — no need for the third floor of a skyscraper!"
  • "How far is anything? One or two blocks. It's a short walk — not thirty minutes."
  • "Our office is a white two-floor building. Easy to find — it's the first building on the left."
  • "There aren't any grey towers — just clean, small buildings. No confusion, no traffic."
⚡ Badge ⚡ — Pitch Note

Badge ⚡ earned when a student uses an ordinal + colour + distance expression in a single argument turn. That's the S4 target sentence — celebrate it publicly when it happens. Class vote at end. Tally total ordinals used across both teams on the board.

Solo Challenge — 2 minutes · Full M4 Integration
You're the official guide for a new international employee. Describe everything they need to know about the building and neighbourhood.
  • Use there is/are at least 4 times (S1 vocabulary)
  • Use at least 3 prepositions of place (S2 vocabulary)
  • Give directions to one destination inside the building (S3 vocabulary)
  • Use at least 2 ordinal numbers for floors (S4 vocabulary)
  • Describe the building using 2 colours (S4 vocabulary)
  • Give the distance to 1 nearby place using blocks or minutes (S4 vocabulary)
  • No notes. Partner tracks which M4 structures you use. Goal: all 6 boxes checked.

§6
Source Analysis — Ordinals & Colours in the Wild
4 real-world sources · Identify ordinals + colours + distances · Produce responses
⏱ 8 MIN
📱 WhatsApp — New Employee Welcome
"Hi! Welcome to Solaris. Your desk is on the fifth floor, Room 5B. The building is the grey and blue one — second building on the right from the main gate. There are 8 floors. Cafeteria is on the third floor."
Your friend asks: "Which floor is the cafeteria?" and "How many floors?" Write both answers using full sentences with there is/are.
📧 Hotel Booking Confirmation
"Your room is on the seventh floor. The hotel has twelve floors. There is a restaurant on the ground floor and a gym on the tenth floor. The building is the white and orange tower opposite the central park."
A colleague asks you to describe the hotel. Say: which floor their room is on, what the building looks like, and one thing on the ground floor.
🏢 Building Directory — Floor Index
"G: Reception · Security. 1st: Sales · Marketing. 2nd: HR · Finance. 3rd: Engineering. 4th: Management. 5th: IT · Training. 6th: Conference Rooms A–F. 7th–8th: Maintenance."
Give directions to Engineering from reception — include the floor (ordinal) and one preposition of place for where to go when they exit the elevator.
🗺️ Visitor Information Sheet
"Near Solaris Manufacturing: Banco Nacional (grey building, 2 blocks north) · Farmacia del Centro (red and white building, 1 block east, ground floor only) · Hotel Colonial (white building, 10 min on foot, 6 floors)."
A visitor asks: "Is there a pharmacy near here?" Give a complete answer: yes/no, colour, distance, and one direction to get there.
📌 Source Analysis Note

Source 3 (directory) is the hardest — it requires reading for information AND producing directions with ordinals + prepositions. Source 4 is the M4 capstone: "Is there a…?" (S1) + preposition (S2) + direction (S3) + colour + distance (S4) all in one answer. Slow down on Source 4. Elicit 2–3 different versions from different students and compare them.

3 Phrases — Use These at Work This Week
M4 S4 complete — full building + city description in your pocket
Phrase 1
"It's the [colour] building — [X] floors. We're on the [ordinal]."
The complete building identification phrase. Use on any call where someone needs to find your building — WhatsApp, Teams, or when an international visitor is outside. Three facts in one sentence.
Phrase 2
"There is a [place] — it's the [colour] building, about [X] blocks from here."
The complete nearby-place description. Combines there is + colour + distance. Use when any international colleague asks about hotels, banks, or restaurants near your office. Sounds completely natural.
Phrase 3
"How many floors? [X]. How far? About [X] minutes on foot."
The two most common visitor questions — answered back-to-back. Use this rhythm in any written or spoken reply. Quick, precise, professional. It shows your English is efficient and complete.
