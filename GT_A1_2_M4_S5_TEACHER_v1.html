⚡ Teacher Note — Module 4 Exit Session
S5 is a standalone review and exit task — no new grammar or vocabulary. The goal is to consolidate all M4 content: have/has (S1), imperatives + possessive pronouns (S2), should + body vocabulary (S3), have to + safety (S4). The exit task is a 4-minute integrated role-play. Students should be able to produce all grammar without hesitation before attempting the role-play. Use §1-§3 to diagnose remaining gaps. If a student cannot complete the Grammar Review Wheel (§2), they are not ready for the exit task — give them targeted feedback before proceeding.
Sessions 1-2 — HR & Health
Tell your partner: one thing you can now say about your health in English that you couldn't say before Module 4. Include a possessive pronoun and a have/has sentence.
💡 "I have a sore throat. This certificate is mine. I am allergic to penicillin."
Sessions 3-4 — Ergonomics & Safety
Tell your partner: one safety rule from your real workplace and one ergonomics change you should make. Use should, have to, and an imperative — one of each.
💡 "You have to wear PPE. My back hurts — I should take more breaks. Don't sit for more than 45 minutes without standing."
The 3 Forbidden Errors — Can You Remember Them?
Without looking at your notes: what are the 3 grammar errors you must NEVER make in Module 4 English? Write them on a piece of paper. Check with your partner.
💡 Targets: "should to" / "have to to" / "don't to" — all wrong. Also: "It's my" instead of "It's mine."

✅ Correct Forms
I have a headache.
She has a fever.
My throat hurts.

That form is mine.
The certificate is hers.
The file is ours.
❌ Error Traps
"It's my" → It's mine
"Is your?" → Is it yours?
"I have headache" → I have A headache
"She have a fever" → She HAS
"It's her form" = possessive adj ✓
"It's hers" = possessive pronoun ✓

S2 · Imperatives + Allergy Structure
✅ Correct Forms
Take a painkiller.
Rest today.
Drink water.
Don't come in today.
Don't push too hard.

I am allergic to penicillin.
I am allergic to dust.
❌ Error Traps
"Take to a painkiller" → Take a painkiller
"Don't to come in" → Don't come in
"I am allergic to THE penicillin" → no article after "allergic to"
"I have allergic" → I am allergic

✅ Correct Forms

I shouldn't sit too long.
My back hurts.

❌ Error Traps
"Should to stretch" → should stretch
"My back is hurt" → My back hurts
"I am always sit" → I always sit
"She don't take breaks" → She doesn't take breaks

✅ Correct Forms

"Don't to enter" → Don't enter
"Do not to touch" → Do not touch
"Should to report" → should report

✏️ Activity B — True or False (M4 Integration)
"You should to report the incident" is correct English.
"My back hurts" is the correct way to describe ongoing back pain in Simple Present.
"I am allergic to the penicillin" is correct — the article is required.
"It's mine" and "it's my form" can both be correct — but "it's mine" uses a possessive pronoun and "it's my form" uses a possessive adjective.
"Don't to enter the restricted area" is correct formal English for a safety sign.
"Have to" expresses obligation; "should" expresses recommendation — they are not interchangeable in safety English.

🎯 Exit Task
Module 4 — Integrated Role Play
4-minute role play · 3 scenarios · All M4 grammar in real context · Assessed with rubric
⏱ 30 MIN
⚡ Exit Task Structure
The exit task has 3 sequential scenarios, all set on the same day: Monica calls in sick, then gets an ergonomics assessment, then gives a safety briefing to a visitor. Each scenario targets a different M4 grammar cluster. Time limit: 4 minutes total for all 3 scenarios. Partner plays all other roles. Teacher uses the rubric below to assess. Minimum passing score: 12/20.
🎯 Module 4 Exit Task
Monica's Day at Solaris Manufacturing
Today you are Monica Herrera, Sales Coordinator at Solaris Manufacturing, Celaya. You have a difficult but important day. You need to: (1) call in sick to your manager, (2) complete an ergonomics assessment with the company nurse, and (3) give a safety briefing to a new international visitor. Use everything you learned in Module 4 — all 4 grammar points, all relevant vocabulary. You have 4 minutes total.
Scenario 1 · 60 seconds
You are calling your manager Daniel to report a sick day. You have a bad sore throat and a mild fever. You need to tell him about your symptoms, mention that you are allergic to penicillin (in case you need medication), say that you will send your medical certificate to HR, and use a possessive pronoun at least once.
Your targets: have/has + symptoms · I am allergic to · send it to HR (possessive pronoun) · sick day email language
Grammar must-haves: "I have a…" · "I am allergic to [noun without article]" · one possessive pronoun (mine/his/hers/theirs)
Scenario 2 · 90 seconds
The company nurse visits your workstation. She asks about your physical discomfort. You describe 2 body parts that hurt and explain the habit that causes each problem. She gives you 2 recommendations — you ask one clarifying question and agree to follow the advice. Your partner plays the nurse.
Your targets: My [body part] hurts/aches because I always/never… · respond to "you should" recommendations · ask one question using Simple Present
Grammar must-haves: "My [body part] hurts/aches" · frequency adverb placed correctly · "should/shouldn't" in response (no "to")
Scenario 3 · 90 seconds
An international visitor from the US parent company arrives at the plant. You are the floor captain. You give them a 90-second safety briefing covering: 2 things they have to do, 2 things they must not do, and 1 recommendation using "should." End with "Safety first."
Your targets: have to + base verb · Don't / Do not + base verb · should + base verb · safety vocabulary (PPE, restricted area, authorization, hazard)
Grammar must-haves: "have to" (no double "to") · imperative prohibition (no "to" after Don't) · "should" (no "to")
