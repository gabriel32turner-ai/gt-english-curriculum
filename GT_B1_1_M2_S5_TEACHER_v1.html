📋 Logistics

🎯 Pedagogical Focus

💡 GT Original — Double Negative Error

"I haven't never worked remotely" → WRONG. Correct: "I've never worked remotely" OR "I haven't ever worked remotely." English does NOT use double negatives — one of the top B1.1 errors. Note silently here — correct in §8.

Speaking Topic — The Future of Work
⏱ 12 min · 3 rounds
📋 Logistics

3 rounds, 3–4 min each. This is the session with highest opinion density — students love debating AI. Push for Present Perfect throughout. Model answer only if completely blocked.

🎯 Pedagogical Focus
  • Round 1: personal experience — what has already changed at their company?
  • Round 2: opinions — which skills will matter most? Use PP + will/going to mix
  • Round 3: debate stance — agree/disagree with a provocation → Exit Task prep
💡 GT Original — AI Debate

The AI topic naturally generates Present Perfect: "AI has already changed…" / "It hasn't replaced… yet." / "I've never used AI at work." Push students to notice they are using the grammar — it flows naturally in opinion contexts.

🤖
The Future of Work
Round 1 — Experience (Present Perfect)
How has technology already changed your job? What tools have you started using? Is there anything that hasn't changed yet?
Round 2 — Prediction (Will / Going to)
Which skills will matter most in 10 years? Will AI replace your job — or parts of it? What do humans do better?
Round 3 — Debate Stance
"Creativity and empathy are more important than technical skills in the future." Do you agree or disagree? Why?

I've already seen big changes at my company. We have just started using AI tools for reports and data analysis. Things are changing fast — two years ago, nobody talked about automation here.

I think the skills that will matter most are adaptability and creativity. AI can automate repetitive tasks, but it hasn't replaced human judgment yet. You need to keep learning — if you stop, technology moves ahead of you.

The most important skills are human — empathy, critical thinking, leadership. A machine can write a report. But can it innovate? Can it understand a frustrated client? I have never seen a machine do that well.

Colloquial Phrases — M2 Review
⏱ 10 min · 5 key phrases from all sessions
📋 Logistics

5 phrases across M2 S1–S5. Student explains in English — no translation. Each example uses Present Perfect or M2 vocabulary. Push for own sentence before moving on.

🎯 Pedagogical Focus

These 5 phrases separate A2 speakers from B1 speakers. A student who naturally says "That's a deal-breaker for me" or "Things are changing fast" sounds immediately more professional. High ROI phrases for corporate use.

💡 Extension

Chain two phrases: "Things are changing fast — the most important skills are human. That's why I need to keep learning."

Things are changing fast.
Observation about pace of change — universal business context
"Things are changing fast in our industry. AI has already automated several processes we used to do manually."
You need to keep learning.
Advice about continuous development — professional growth context
"The workforce is evolving. You need to keep learning — if you stop, technology moves ahead of you. I have just started a data course."
The most important skills are human.
Opinion about AI vs humans — debate and presentation context
"AI can do reports and analysis. But the most important skills are human — empathy, creativity, leadership. Machines haven't replaced those yet."
That's a deal-breaker for me.
Something so important that you would not accept a job without it
"No flexibility? That's a deal-breaker for me. I have already turned down a higher salary because the benefits were terrible."
If I could change one thing…
Hypothetical opener for workplace improvement discussion
"If I could change one thing about my workplace, it would be the communication. The hierarchy hasn't evolved yet — information doesn't flow."

Grammar — Present Perfect: just / already / yet / never / ever
⏱ 10 min · Reference + Poll + 2 Activities
📋 Logistics

REFERENTIAL — adverb table visible. Poll: 3 questions, 10 pts each. Activity A: choose the correct adverb (3 items). Activity B: transform sentence with PP (3 items). Badge 🎯 at combined 4+/6.

🎯 Pedagogical Focus

PP adverbs add precision: just = very recently · already = before expected · yet = until now (neg./Q) · never/ever = lifetime experience. Point at the table — don't re-teach the whole PP system.

💡 GT Original from Program
  • ⚠️ Double negative: "I haven't never" → I've never OR I haven't ever — NEVER both
  • 🔊 "already" /ɔːlˈredi/ — stress on second syllable, often mispronounced
  • 💼 S3 negotiation: "I've already received one offer, but I'd like to discuss the benefits package" — PP in real context
  • 🧠 AI debate forces natural PP: "has already changed" / "hasn't replaced yet"

PRESENT PERFECT + ADVERBS — REFERENCE TABLE

AdverbMeaningPositionExample
justvery recentlyhave/has + just + past participleWe've just started a new policy.
alreadybefore expectedhave/has + already + past participleI've already sent the report.
yetuntil now (neg. / Q)end of sentenceThey haven't updated the system yet. / Have you finished yet?
neverat no time in lifehave/has + never + past participleI've never worked remotely.
everat any time (questions)have/has + subject + ever + PPHave you ever worked in a toxic environment?

⏱ 12 min · Audio + MC + T/F + Speaking Response
📋 Logistics

ElevenLabs placeholder — professional male voice, neutral accent, 85% speed, ~220 words. Scenario: team leader addressing employees about AI changes in the company. 2 MC + 3 T/F. Play once gist, twice detail.

🎯 Pedagogical Focus
  • Target: recognize PP adverbs (just/already/yet/never) in natural speech
  • Content connects all 5 sessions: environment → remote → benefits → culture → AI

The monologue uses ALL 5 adverbs naturally — students should count how many they hear. This doubles as a grammar hunt without making it feel like a grammar exercise.

Good morning everyone. I want to talk about something important — the future of our company. Things are changing fast, and we need to be ready.

First, the environment. We have just finished redesigning the office layout. The new open spaces are already improving collaboration. We have never had this kind of setup before — and the feedback has been very positive.

Remote work. We haven't finalized the new hybrid policy yet, but it is coming. We know flexibility matters. Some of you have already started working two days from home — and productivity has not dropped.

Now — AI and automation. We have just launched a pilot program with AI tools for data analysis. This has already reduced report time by 40 percent. But — and this is key — AI hasn't replaced anyone yet. The skills that matter most are still human: adaptability, creativity, empathy.

You need to keep learning. We will offer training on the new tools. Have you ever used an AI assistant? If not — now is the time. The most important skills are human. AI is a tool. You are the workforce.

MULTIPLE CHOICE

1. What has the company just finished doing?

2. What has the AI pilot program already achieved?

TRUE / FALSE

The company has already finalized the new hybrid work policy.

AI has already replaced some employees at the company.

The team leader says the most important skills are technical, not human.

🗣 Speaking Response

What has your company already done about AI or automation? What hasn't changed yet? What would you like to see?

Starters: "We've already… / We haven't… yet. / I've never…"
