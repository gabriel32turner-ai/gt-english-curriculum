§ 04
Grammar Check — Error Correction Only
No re-teaching · Find the error · Produce corrected sentences from your real context
8 min
⚠️ S3B — Fix These 4 PPC Errors
❌ 1. "She is thriving since the promotion."→✅ "She has been thriving since the promotion." — PPC, not present simple
❌ 2. "He has been work alone all morning."→✅ "He has been working alone all morning." — need -ing form
❌ 3. "How long she has been feeling this way?"→✅ "How long has she been feeling this way?" — auxiliary before subject in questions
❌ 4. "I've been recharge after the holiday."→✅ "I've been recharging since the holiday." — verb-ing + since for starting point
🔍 Activity — Spot the Error
Each sentence has one PPC error. Choose the correct fix.
Find the error: "My output has been improve since I started working from home."
Find the error: "The open office has been overwhelm her for months."
Find the error: "How long has he been prefer working alone?"
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • All 3 errors are the same type — missing -ing. This is the most persistent B1.2 error with PPC. After each correct answer: "Now produce the corrected sentence about your real work situation."
  • If student gets all 3 immediately → spend the saved time on oral production. "Three PPC sentences about your team. Go. 90 seconds."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "3 errors. 3 minutes. Then immediately produce the corrected version about your real team."

▶ Show Script — Focus Task

Manager: So, how has the week been? I've been noticing you seem a bit tired lately.

Employee: Yeah, honestly — I've been struggling a bit. The new project has a lot of group calls, and I've been finding it hard to focus afterwards. I need quiet time to process things, and I haven't been getting that.

Manager: I didn't know that about you. How long has this been affecting you?

Employee: Since the project started — so about six weeks. I've been doing my best work early in the morning before anyone else is online. That's when I've been getting the most done. But the afternoon calls have been draining me.

Manager: That makes sense. Have you been able to tell the team you prefer to work that way?

Employee: Not really. I didn't want to seem difficult. But I've been thinking about it more lately — I think I need to communicate it.

Manager: You should. The team has been working well together, but we've all been assuming everyone works the same way. Clearly we don't. What do you need to recharge between calls?

Employee: Even thirty minutes of quiet would help. No messages, no questions — just time to think.

Manager: Done. I'll block it in your calendar. And honestly though — I'm glad you told me. I've been missing this kind of conversation.

✅ 11+ PPC instances highlighted in bold. Focus task: student counts them before answering the comprehension questions below.

✏️ Second Listen — Production Focus
This time, focus on HOW the PPC is used — not just what is said.
The employee says "I've been doing my best work early in the morning." Why PPC and not "I do my best work"?
The manager uses "we've been assuming" to describe a collective team habit — not a one-time mistake. The PPC shows this has been ongoing.
The employee uses "I didn't want to seem difficult" — simple past — because this was a one-time past thought, not an ongoing current feeling.
At the end, the manager says "I've been missing this kind of conversation" — using PPC to describe something still true at the moment of speaking.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Second listen goal: recognition → production. Before comprehension questions: "Count the PPC instances. Answer: 11+. That's the density you're aiming for in the §6 role-play."
  • LQ1: this is the key metacognitive question of the session. Student must understand WHY PPC is better than present simple here — specificity, ongoing nature, current relevance.
  • TF2: the contrast between simple past (one-time thought) and PPC (ongoing pattern) is the critical distinction. "I didn't want" = past thought. "I've been struggling" = current reality.
  • Post-listening production bridge: "You are now the employee in this dialogue. What would YOUR version of this conversation sound like? Give me 3 PPC sentences about your real situation."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "The manager says 'I've been missing this kind of conversation.' Has your manager been missing this kind of conversation with you? When did you last have a real 1:1 about how you work?"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "This dialogue is the §6 role-play script — except you're going to improvise it from your real situation. The model is in your ear. Now you do it."
§ 06
Role-Play — Your Real 1:1
You play the employee · Your real situation · 4+ PPC sentences · S3A vocabulary · No script
15 min
The 1:1 starts now. I am your manager. You are the employee. This is a real check-in — not a grammar exercise.

Your goal: describe how you've been working lately — what has been giving you energy, what has been draining you, and what you need. Use PPC throughout. Use at least 3 vocabulary words from S3A and 1 colloquial phrase.

🎭 Choose your scenario — then the 1:1 begins immediately.

Scenario A
The Drained Introvert
You've been struggling since the team moved to daily 2-hour video calls. You're an introvert. Your output has been declining.
Goal: communicate what's been happening + what you need to recharge, using PPC throughout.
Scenario B
The Thriving Extrovert
You've been thriving since the office reopened — but the two remote days per week have been isolating. You need more collaboration.
Goal: communicate your work style needs clearly + propose a solution, PPC as evidence.
Scenario C · Hard Mode
The Adaptor
You've been adapting to a new manager whose style is very different from your previous one. It's been challenging — but you've been learning.
Goal: describe the adaptation process in PPC + identify one thing the manager could do differently.
Scenario D · Expert Mode
Your Real Situation
Use your actual work situation right now — whatever has been happening in your team, your energy, your output. Real content only.
Goal: real PPC about real events. At least 5 PPC sentences. At least 2 vocabulary words. No invented scenarios.
The conversation starts now. I (the manager) will open: "So — how has the week been? I've been noticing things have been a bit different lately." You respond and lead the conversation from there. Aim for 90+ seconds of unscripted English.
Teacher Manager Prompts — Use mid role-play
"How long has this been affecting you? When did it start?"
"Has the team been noticing? Or have you been managing it alone?"
"What has been your most productive time of day lately?"
"Have you been able to tell anyone on the team how you work best?"
"What's been your output like since this started? Has it been improving or declining?"
"If I blocked 30 minutes per day for you — how would that change what's been happening?"
Self-Evaluation — Después del role-play
  • I used PPC at least 4 times correctly (has/have + been + -ing)
  • I used at least 3 vocabulary words from S3A naturally (drain / recharge / thrive / adapt / observe / collaborate)
  • I used at least 1 colloquial phrase (I'm not gonna lie / honestly though / fair enough / it's a bit much)
  • I described a specific situation — not just "meetings are tiring" but WHICH meetings, for HOW LONG, with WHAT effect
  • I proposed at least one change or need — "What I need is…"
  • I spoke for 90+ seconds without stopping to translate
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • NO interrumpas durante el role-play. Mantén el personaje del manager. Toma nota de PPC correctos, vocabulario usado, colloquial phrases. Corrección post-role-play: 1 elogio específico + 1 corrección.
  • Scenario D (Real Situation) es el más desafiante y más valioso. Si el alumno lo elige, dale tiempo extra — puede ir a 120 segundos.
  • Segunda ronda opcional: misma escena, pero el alumno ahora juega el papel del manager. "Now YOU are the manager. I'm the employee with the same situation. How do you run this 1:1?"
  • B1.2 mastery standard: 4+ PPC + 2 vocabulary words + 1 colloquial phrase in 90 seconds = S3B role-play passed.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You just had a real work conversation in English — about your actual situation, your actual needs. That is not a language exercise. That's a professional skill."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "S4A — Your Boss Is Wrong — uses the same PPC in a harder context: 'My manager has been making decisions without the team.' Same grammar, much higher stakes. Today was the preparation."
