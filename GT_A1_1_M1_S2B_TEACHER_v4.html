👉 Say 4–5 sentences about your chosen photo. Use: "He is…" / "She isn't from…" / "They are…" / "Are they…?" / "His nationality is…"
Scaffold (student sees this):
"He is ___."  ·  "She isn't ___."  ·  "They are from ___."  ·  "He/She is ___ (nationality)."  ·  "Are they ___? Yes/No, they ___."
Teacher Follow-Up — Push for Negatives & Questions
"Is she Mexican? No? Then say it: 'She isn't Mexican. She is ___.' "
"Are they from the same country? How do you say that in English?"
"Is the manager American or British? Make a guess — use 'I think he is ___.' "
"Now describe Photo 2 also — 2 sentences with a negative form."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • El objetivo en §1 de la B es activar el vocabulario de S2A SIN referencia a la tabla. El alumno debe hablar de memoria.
  • Si usa forma afirmativa solamente: empuja explícitamente. "Ahora di lo que NO es. 'She isn't ___.' " Una forma negativa mínimo.
  • Si el alumno no recuerda una nacionalidad: no la des inmediatamente. Di "Think about it — Japan → Japanese. Germany → ?" Espera 5 segundos.
  • Cronometra: 5 min estrictos. Este es el activador, no el ejercicio principal.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Mira — ya usas negativos. Eso es grammar intermedia. En S2A apenas lo aprendiste y ya lo produces. Eso es progreso real."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "En S3, vas a hacer esto mismo con trabajos y profesiones. Ya tienes la estructura — solo cambiarás el vocabulario."

Mark (Adam): "Hi everyone! I am Mark. I am American, from Boston. Who is on this call?"

Mark: "Yes, I am! And Yuki — is your team in Japan?"


Yuki: "No, my team isn't in Japan. We are in Mexico City. We are not Japanese only — Carlos is Mexican, Ana is Brazilian."


Lucia: "That is a very international team! Are you happy, Mark?"

Listening Task — Oral Response
Escucha una vez. Sin escribir. Responde oralmente al maestro.
1. "Where is Mark from? What is his nationality?"
2. "Is Lucia the manager? What IS she?"
3. "Is Yuki's team in Japan? Where ARE they?"
4. "How many nationalities are in the conference call? Name them."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Este audio extiende el de S2A (1 speaker) a 3 speakers — incremento de dificultad intencional de la A a la B.
  • Las preguntas de §3B son ORALES — no clic. El alumno habla, no toca botones. Eso es producción real.
  • Si el alumno no entiende todo: "Normal — es inglés real con 3 voces. ¿Qué SÍ entendiste? Eso es suficiente."
  • Pregunta 2 es trampa pedagógica: el alumno debe usar una negativa. "She isn't the manager — she is the project coordinator." Empuja si dice solo la respuesta corta.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Acabas de escuchar inglés americano, colombiano-accentuado y japonés-accentuado. Eso es el mundo real."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "En S3 escucharás una conferencia de trabajo completa. Hoy construiste los cimientos para entenderla."
§ 04
Game — Nationality Challenge
10–12 min
🎮 Wordwall — External Game
Match the Country — Flag to Nationality
🔗 Inserta aquí el link o iframe del Wordwall.
Sugerencia: "Match-up — Country flag → Nationality adjective" (8 pares: Mexico/Mexican, USA/American, Brazil/Brazilian, France/French, UK/British, Japan/Japanese, Colombia/Colombian, Germany/German).
Formato recomendado: Match-up o Quiz. Tiempo: 60 segundos.
Instrucción para el alumno: "Conecta cada bandera con su adjetivo de nacionalidad. Tienes 60 segundos. ¡Sin ver las notas!"
🎲 GT Oral Game — Numbers + Nationalities
El maestro dice un número (0–20). El alumno dice ese número de nacionalidades diferentes de la sesión. Ejemplo: Maestro: "Three" → Alumno: "Mexican, Japanese, British."
Luego al revés: el alumno dice un número, el maestro responde. Si el maestro falla, el alumno gana un punto.
Variación: el alumno usa la forma negativa — "Three nationalities I am NOT: American, Japanese, British."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • El Wordwall recicla el vocabulario de §3 de S2A de forma gamificada — reduce la ansiedad y aumenta la repetición sin que se sienta como estudio.
  • GT Oral Game: combina números (vocab §2A) + nacionalidades (vocab §2A y §3A) — recycling multidimensional.
  • La variación con negativas sube la exigencia cognitiva — úsala si el alumno terminó el juego básico con facilidad.
  • Si el alumno le gana al maestro: celebra. "¡Ganaste! Eso significa que tu inglés ya funciona."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • Juega con energía. Los juegos en clase 1:1 pueden sentirse awkward — el maestro marca el tono. Si tú te diviertes, el alumno se divierte.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "En B2 harás este mismo juego con vocabulario de negociaciones internacionales. El juego no cambia — el vocabulario sí."
§ 05
Role Play — International Office Scenarios
12–15 min
Speaking Template — Producción guiada · 3 min
1
My name is ___. I am ___ (nationality). I am from ___ (city).
2
I am not from ___. My colleague ___ is ___ (nationality).
3
Are you from ___? — Yes/No, ___.
4
In our team, we are from ___ countries. We are international.
🎭 Role Play 1 — The New International Colleague
Monday morning. A new colleague walks into your office. She doesn't speak Spanish. You need to introduce yourself and find out where she is from.
Role A — You (student)
You are Carlos/Ana/[nombre real] from Celaya. You are Mexican. You work in operations. Start the conversation.
Role B — Teacher
You are Sarah. You are British, from London. You are the new quality manager. You are NOT Mexican. Wait for the student to speak first.
"Hi! Good morning. I am…"
Objetivo: el alumno usa mínimo 1 pregunta (Are you from…?), 1 negativa (I'm not…), y dice su propia nacionalidad. El maestro introduce variaciones: responde en preguntas, da información que el alumno debe procesar.
🎭 Role Play 2 — The Conference Call Introduction
You are on a video call with international colleagues. Each person introduces themselves. You go last and must ask TWO questions to the group.
Role A — You (student)
You are [nombre real]. You are Mexican, from [ciudad real]. You work at [empresa real o ficticia]. You are NOT the manager.
Role B — Teacher (2 personas)
Person 1: Kenji, Japanese, from Osaka, IT manager.
Person 2: Gabriela, Colombian, from Bogotá, sales coordinator.
[Maestro empieza como Kenji] "Hello! I am Kenji. I am Japanese. I am from Osaka. I am the IT manager. Are you Mexican?"
Objetivo: el alumno responde a Kenji, se presenta completo, hace 2 preguntas (mínimo una con negativa o número). "Are you from Japan? Is Gabriela Colombian? How many people are on this call? Three?"
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Role Play 1: empuja para que el alumno inicie. Si espera que el maestro hable primero, di: "You start — you are the host." Rompe la pasividad.
  • Role Play 2: es el más cercano a la realidad laboral del alumno — conferencia internacional. El vocabulario de hoy tiene uso REAL aquí.
  • Si el alumno se bloquea en el Role Play: regresa al scaffold del inicio de §5, lee la línea 1, luego tapa el scaffold y repite. 30 segundos máximo de apoyo.
  • No interrumpas para corregir errores menores durante el role play. Nota los errores y revísalos AL FINAL del ejercicio, no durante.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Acabas de tener una conversación internacional en inglés. Eso NO es A1.1 básico — eso es ya A1.2 habilidad."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Upsell: "En S3 vamos a hacer esto mismo pero tu colega también tiene un trabajo y tú tendrás que preguntar sobre él. El mismo role play, más vocabulario."
§ 06
Debate — National vs. International
8–10 min
🗣 Free Speaking — Debate
"Is it better to work in a national or an international team? You have 2 minutes to defend your position — then we switch sides."
👍 National team is better
"A Mexican team is better because…"
"International teams aren't always easy…"
"In Mexico, we are…"
"Communication isn't a problem in a national team."
👍 International team is better
"An international team is better because…"
"A national team isn't very diverse…"
"My team isn't from Mexico only — we are…"
"Japanese and American colleagues are…"
I think… It is better because… They aren't… In my experience… We are from… She/He isn't…
🎤 Optional Extension — 60-Second Monologue
"Describe your real team at work — who is on it, where are they from, and what nationalities are represented."
Si el alumno trabaja en empresa multinacional: úsala. Si trabaja en empresa mexicana: imagina que contratan un colega de otro país. ¿Quién sería? ¿De dónde? ¿Por qué?
Mínimo 4 oraciones. 1 pregunta retórica. 1 forma negativa. Sin notas.
Teacher Follow-Up — After Debate
1."Is your actual team national or international? Tell me in English — 2 sentences."
2."Are your clients from Mexico only? Or are some of them international?"
3."Is English more important for a national or international career? Answer in English."
4."If you could add ONE nationality to your team, what would it be? Why?"
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Debate: el maestro toma el lado opuesto del alumno. Si el alumno elige "international", el maestro defiende "national" — esto fuerza al alumno a escuchar y responder, no solo monologar.
  • Regla: el alumno DEBE usar al menos 1 forma negativa y 1 pregunta en el debate. Si no las usa, el maestro para y dice "Now use 'isn't' or 'aren't' in your next sentence."
  • Switch de lados: esto es B2-level thinking en A1.1 vocab — muy valioso. No lo saltes.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Defendiste una posición en inglés Y luego la contraria. Eso es pensamiento crítico en un segundo idioma. Eso es nivel avanzado de habilidad."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Testimonial trigger: "Si alguien te pregunta en una entrevista 'What do you think about international teams?' — ya sabes la respuesta en inglés."
  • Upsell suave: "En S3 tendrás que describir no solo de dónde viene tu colega, sino también qué hace. El vocabulario de trabajos te da más poder en estos debates."
§ SP
Speaking Practice
10–12 min
🗣 Guided Speaking — Free Production
The World at Your Desk
No cards. No notes. Answer each question using nationalities and "to be" — affirmative, negative, and questions. This is your real English now.
Question 1
"Describe the photo you chose in the warm-up — 4 sentences minimum. Who are they? Where are they from?"
Hint: She is ___ . He isn't from ___ . They are ___ (nationality).
Question 2
"Tell me about your real team at work — nationalities, countries, who is NOT from Mexico."
Hint: In my team, ___ is from ___ . ___ isn't Mexican. We are from ___ countries.
Question 3
"Ask me 2 questions about MY nationality — use 'Are you…?' and 'Is your company…?'"
Hint: Are you from ___ ? Is your company ___ (nationality)?
Question 4
"Name 3 international companies in your city or region — what nationality are they?"
Hint: ___ is a ___ (nationality) company. It is from ___ . It isn't Mexican.
Question 5
"Is it better to work with a Mexican team or an international team? Say 3 sentences defending your answer."
Hint: I think ___ is better because ___ . An international team is / isn't ___ .
⏱ 10–12 min total 📋 5 questions 🎯 No notes allowed 👥 Teacher-led · Free production
⚡ Speaking Challenge — Storytelling Close
"Tell me about your perfect international team. Who is on it? What nationalities? Why those countries? 60 seconds. No notes. Go."
Free production — no scaffold. Evaluate: at least 4 nationalities used, correct is/isn't/are/aren't, at least 1 question produced. Come back to warm-up photo: "Earlier you described that photo with 4 sentences. Can you do it again — better?" This bookending shows measurable progress in 60 minutes.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • S2B is mastery — the hints are visible but the student should NOT need them. If they're reading the hints for every sentence, they need more drill. Note this for S3A opener.
  • Q3 is the highest-value question — it forces the student to PRODUCE a question, not just answer one. A1.1 students often freeze here. Scaffold: "Start with 'Are you…' — that's the question. Now finish it."
  • Q5 connects to the debate in §6 — if they already argued this well there, ask them to add one new argument they didn't use before.
  • Speaking Challenge: evaluate 3 things — (1) nationality adjectives correct, (2) negative form used at least once, (3) student spoke for at least 45 seconds without stopping. If all 3: session complete. If not: 60-second mini-drill and repeat.
  • Bookend with warm-up: pull up the photo they chose in §1. "Say 4 sentences about this photo — now, without the scaffold." This comparison is the WOW moment of S2B.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "In S2A you needed the cards to remember the nationalities. Right now you just used 4 of them from memory. That's the difference between knowing and owning."
  • Specific celebration: name the nationality they used best. "I heard 'Brazilian' and 'Colombian' — perfectly. Your clients from those countries would notice that."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "S3A adds jobs and professions — then you can say WHO you are, WHERE you're from, and WHAT YOU DO. Complete professional introduction. That's Monday."
  • Gentle upsell: "¿Tienes algún cliente o colega internacional? La próxima vez que los veas, di: 'Are you from ___?' Una pregunta. Eso es suficiente para empezar."
