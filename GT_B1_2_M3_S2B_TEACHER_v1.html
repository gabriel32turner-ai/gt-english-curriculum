🔥 Hot Take — Cold Start

5 min
Carlos's data has been collected, analyzed, and sold. 60 seconds. Respond — Passive Voice.
Cold start: Push for Passive immediately. "Is he being exploited? By whom? Has his data been sold?" If they go active: "Now say the same thing in Passive." Target: 3+ passive structures in 60 seconds.
🔥 Hot Take — 60 sec
Carlos just discovered: his screen time was 4h37min yesterday. His data was sold to 47 advertisers. His feed was curated based on his deepest insecurities. He feels violated. You have 60 seconds — is he the victim, or is he responsible? Use Passive Voice.

🎯 Pitch — Assigned Sides

10 min
Should social media be regulated like tobacco? Argue your assigned side.
Topic: "Social media companies should face the same regulations as tobacco — usage warnings, age restrictions, and legal liability for addiction." Push for Passive in both sides: "has been proven" / "will be regulated" / "was designed".
🟣 Side A — REGULATE IT
"Social media must be regulated like tobacco. The harm has been proven. Defend it."
Starters: "It has been proven that social media causes measurable psychological harm…" "Children are being exposed to content that was designed to damage their self-image…" "Billions of hours have been stolen from productive lives. It must be regulated."
🔵 Side B — PERSONAL FREEDOM
"Regulation is censorship. Adults make their own choices. Counter the argument."
Starters: "The same argument was made about TV, video games, and the internet. Nothing was banned…" "Tools have been created — screen time limits exist. They just aren't being used…" "If social media is banned, free speech will be suppressed. That's a worse outcome."

🎙️ Now Transform — Active → Passive
Transform 1 — Present Simple
Active: "Instagram collects your data for advertising."
→ Say the same thing in Present Simple Passive. Who is the agent?
"Your data is collected by Instagram for advertising." — "by Instagram" can be dropped if obvious.
Transform 2 — Past Simple (hide the agent)
Active: "Facebook engineers invented the infinite scroll to maximize engagement."
→ Make it Passive AND remove the agent. Why might a company prefer this version?
"The infinite scroll was invented to maximize engagement." — No "by Facebook engineers". Removes blame. This is why companies write passively.
Transform 3 — Present Continuous Passive
Active: "The algorithm is currently analyzing your psychology."
→ Present Continuous Passive. What is the difference in effect?
"Your psychology is being analyzed right now." — The continuous aspect shows it's happening at this exact moment. More alarming. More powerful.
Transform 4 — Present Perfect Passive
Active: "Researchers have shown that social media causes depression in teenagers."
→ Present Perfect Passive. Focus on the result, not who proved it.
"It has been shown that social media causes depression in teenagers." — "by researchers" dropped. The fact speaks for itself.
Transform 5 — Your own data, Passive Voice
Think about the data Carlos's audit revealed: 4h37min, 94 unlocks, 11 late-night sessions.
→ Describe YOUR screen time data in 3 Passive Voice sentences. Be honest.
Open — "X hours have been spent on [app] this week." / "My behavior is being analyzed by the algorithm." / "This data will be sold to [X] companies."

🔍 Source Analysis — Passive in the Wild

8 min
Real texts. Passive Voice everywhere. Find it, analyze it, challenge it.
Dinámica: 4 fuentes reales. Each has a different analytical challenge — not just "find the passive" but "why was it chosen?" and "who benefits from this passive?"
🐦 Tweet Thread — Tech journalist
"THREAD: Your attention is being harvested. Your insecurities are being exploited. Your data is being sold. All of this is being done right now, while you read this. And it was all made legal in a 47-page terms & conditions that was never meant to be read. [1/12]"
❓ Count the Passive structures. Which one is the most powerful — and why?
📰 News Headline — BBC Technology
"Social Media Firms Could Be Held Liable For Addictive Features, Says UK Government. Platforms have been warned that changes are expected to be introduced before the end of the year. Children are not being adequately protected, according to new research that was published this week."
❓ This headline uses Passive Voice 4 times. Why would a journalist choose Passive over Active here?
📋 Terms & Conditions — Instagram (excerpt)
"By using our services, your content may be used, reproduced, modified, and distributed. Data that is collected may be shared with third-party partners. Advertisements may be shown based on information that is gathered from your activity. You agree that your information can be transferred and processed in countries where different privacy laws apply."
❓ Why are ALL verbs in Passive Voice in legal terms & conditions?
🧠 Grammar Detective — Passive for Power
Compare these two sentences:

A) "Facebook deliberately exploited the psychological vulnerabilities of its users to maximize engagement and profit."

B) "Psychological vulnerabilities were exploited to maximize engagement."
❓ Both say the same thing. Which is more powerful — and which would Facebook prefer?
