§ 01
Warm-Up — Rapid Fire Review
5–8 min

Reglas: El maestro lee cada pregunta en voz alta. El alumno responde inmediatamente — ¡sin tiempo para pensar! Usa todo de S1, S2 y S3. Haz clic en la tarjeta cuando hayas respondido. ⚡

Q1
What is your name?
Oración completa: "My name is ___."
✓
Q2
What is your job?
Usa A o AN: "I am a/an ___."
✓
Q3
Where are you from?
"I am from ___, Mexico."
✓
Q4
What is your nationality?
"I am ___." — adjetivo de nacionalidad
✓
Q5
Who is your manager?
"My manager is ___. His/Her job is ___."
✓
Q6
Is your company big or small?
"My company is ___. It is in ___."
✓
⏱ 30 seg por pregunta
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Rapid Fire repasa todo de S1–S3 en menos de 8 minutos. La velocidad importa — no permitas tiempo para traducir internamente.
  • Respuestas aceptables: cualquier respuesta gramaticalmente cercana. Oraciones completas preferidas pero no requeridas aquí.
  • Hesitaciones comunes: Q2 (a/an aún incierto) y Q5 (his/her). Anótalas — abórdalas en §4 si es necesario.
  • Usa el botón del timer para añadir energía. Si el alumno es lento: 2 rondas — la velocidad mejora dramáticamente.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Ronda 2 — ¡más rápido! Esta vez ni siquiera piensas. Tu cerebro ya sabe las respuestas."
  • "Eso te tomó 4 segundos. En S1 tomaba 30. Así se ve el progreso."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Hoy agregas preguntas Wh- para que puedas HACER estas mismas preguntas — no solo responderlas."
  • Upsell: "En S5 harás este mismo Rapid Fire con todo el módulo. Checkpoint de retención perfecto."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Las 10 palabras son nuevas — sin repetición de S1–S3. Confirma que el alumno reconoce la diferencia.
  • Pronunciaciones clave: "colleague" /ˈkɒliːɡ/ — el GUE es mudo. "pleased" /pliːzd/ — D es sonora. "introduce" — acento en DU-CE.
  • Frases clave: drillala "Nice to meet you too!" — esta es la respuesta que los alumnos olvidan más. Practica el intercambio 3 veces.
  • "This is my colleague, Ana" — practica con nombres reales de su lugar de trabajo.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "These are the words of a first impression. You only get one chance — so the pronunciation matters."
  • Role-play el intercambio de saludo: maestro dice "Nice to meet you!" — alumno responde instantáneamente "Nice to meet you too!"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Los profesionales internacionales juzgan el idioma en los primeros 10 segundos de una reunión. Estas frases son tus primeros 10 segundos."

§ 04
Grammar — Wh- Questions
12–15 min
Structure — Wh- word + to be + subject
Wh- WordQuestionNatural Answer
What What is your name? My name is Ana. / I'm Ana.
What What is your email? My email is [email protected]
What What is your phone number? My number is 461 147 2119.
Where Where is your office? My office is on the 3rd floor.
Where Where are you from? I'm from Celaya.
Who Who is your manager? My manager is Carlos.
How How are you? I'm fine, thanks! / Very well, thank you.
Short Answers — Yes / No
QuestionYESNO
Are you from Mexico? Yes, I am. No, I'm not.
Is she a manager? Yes, she is. No, she isn't.
Are they in the meeting? Yes, they are. No, they aren't.
⚠️ Important: Nunca uses contracción en respuestas cortas positivas.
✅ "Yes, I am."    ❌ "Yes, I'm." — siempre incorrecto en respuestas cortas.
Activity 1 — Choose the Correct Wh- Word
Haz clic en la opción correcta.
___ is your email address?
___ is your office? — It's on the 2nd floor.
___ is your manager? — His name is Roberto.
___ are you? — I'm fine, thank you!
Is the meeting at 9 AM? — Yes, it ___.
Activity 2 — Match the Question to the Answer
Lee la respuesta. Haz clic en la pregunta que corresponde.
Answer: "I'm a coordinator at Grupo Norte."
Answer: "My office is in Monterrey."
Answer: "No, she isn't. She's a coordinator."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Error común: "Yes, I'm." en respuestas cortas — refuerza que las respuestas cortas positivas NUNCA usan contracción.
  • Activity 2 es formato inverso (respuesta → pregunta) — esto es más difícil y construye producción activa. Espera ritmo más lento.
  • Después de ambas actividades: oral drill. El maestro da una respuesta, el alumno forma la pregunta instantáneamente. 3 rondas.
  • Conecta con el Rapid Fire de §1: "Now you can ask all those questions too — not just answer them."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You just learned the 4 most important questions in professional English: What, Where, Who, How."
  • Cuando el alumno logre Activity 2: "You're thinking in English now — not translating."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "En A1.2 agregarás 'When' y 'How many' — tu toolkit de preguntas estará completo."
  • Upsell: "Imagina abrir una reunión en inglés: 'Good morning! Where are you from? What is your role here?' — ese eres tú en el Módulo 2."
§ 05
Reading — David's Week in Celaya
8–10 min
David Chen
Engineer · Vancouver, Canada · Visiting Celaya
Read · ~90 words · A1.1 level

Hello! My name is David Chen. I am from Vancouver, Canada. I am visiting the Grupo Norte office in Celaya this week.

My contact at Grupo Norte is Ana García. Her office is on the third floor. Her email is [email protected]. Her phone number is 461 147 2119.

Ana is a great colleague. She introduces me to her team. "This is David. He is an engineer from Canada. Nice to meet you, David!" — "Nice to meet you too! Pleased to be here!"

The meeting is tomorrow at 9 AM. I am ready!

Comprehension
Responde las preguntas.
1. David is from Mexico.
2. Ana's office is on the 3rd floor.
3. The meeting is in the afternoon.
What is David's job?
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Este texto integra todo el vocabulario de S4 de forma natural — pide al alumno que encuentre y subraye: email, phone, office, floor, contact, colleague, introduce, meeting, pleased.
  • Q3 es trampa de detalle: "meeting is tomorrow at 9 AM" = mañana, no tarde. Los alumnos que leen superficialmente fallan aquí.
  • Lee en voz alta la sección de diálogo: modela las voces de David y Ana. Luego el alumno repite ambos roles.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "David is a Canadian engineer visiting Celaya — that's a real situation in your industry. This is your world."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "En tu próxima visita internacional o videollamada, usarás cada oración de este texto."

§ SP
Speaking Practice
10–12 min
🗣 Guided Speaking — Teacher-Led
Ask Me! — Wh- Questions in Action
Use What / Where / Who / How + "to be" to answer and ask each question. Aim for 2 full sentences per answer.
Question 1
"What is your job? What is your company?"
Hint: My job is ___. My company is ___.
Question 2
"Where is your office? Where are you from?"
Hint: My office is in ___. I am from ___.
Question 3
"Who is your manager? Who is your colleague?"
Hint: My manager is ___. He/She is very ___.
Question 4
"How is your team? How is your English level today?"
Hint: My team is ___. My English is getting better!
Question 5
"Now YOU ask ME a Wh- question. Any topic — about my job, my name, my country."
Hint: What / Where / Who / How + is/are + subject?
⏱ 10–12 min total 📋 5 questions 🎯 Min. 5 Wh- questions used 👥 Teacher-led
⚡ Speaking Challenge — Closing
"60 seconds. Ask me 3 Wh- questions — then answer 2 of mine. Go."
A1.1 success = student produces 3 correct Wh- questions with "is/are" in the right position. Celebrate each question unprompted. One correction maximum — choose the highest-frequency error only.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Q5 is the key transfer task: student generates a question WITHOUT a model. If they freeze, prompt: "Choose one: What / Where / Who / How — then add 'is' and a noun. Go."
  • Most common error at A1.1: "What is you?" instead of "What is your job?" — the missing noun. Recast: "What is your NAME? What is your JOB? — you need the noun." Write it if needed.
  • Word order trap: "Where you are from?" → recast to "Where ARE you from?" — inversion is the A1.1 discovery of S4A. Reinforce it here every time.
  • If student is strong: push for follow-up answers — "What else? Tell me more about your office."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You now have 4 question words. That means you can open ANY conversation in English. What, Where, Who, How — those 4 words never stop being useful."
  • After Q5: "You just asked ME a question in English. That is already better than most people who study for a year."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In S4B you will use these questions to have a full networking conversation — 5 minutes, no template. You are building toward that right now."
  • Gentle preview only at A1.1. No upsell, no testimonial trigger.

  • I know: "Yes, I am." — not "Yes, I'm." in short answers.
  • I can greet: "Nice to meet you!" → "Nice to meet you too!"
  • I know 10 new words: email, phone, office, meeting, colleague…

🗣 Speaking Challenge
Format · Professional Presentation
Prepara una presentación profesional corta sobre ti mismo — 5 pasos estructurados. Entrégala al maestro como si estuvieras en un evento de networking. Sin notas después del primer ensayo.
1
Greet: "Hello! Nice to meet you. My name is ___."
2
Job + Company: "I am a/an ___ at ___."
3
Origin: "I am from ___, Mexico. I am Mexican."
4
Introduce a colleague: "This is my colleague, ___. He/She is a/an ___."
5
Close: "My email is ___. It was nice to meet you!"

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Presentación: da al alumno 2 min para prepararse mentalmente (sin escribir). Luego entrega. Luego repite sin ninguna preparación — la segunda entrega siempre es significativamente mejor.
  • Evalúa: ¿Usó A/AN correctamente? ¿Usó his/her? ¿Hizo al menos una pregunta Wh-? Anota para revisión en S5.
  • Si el alumno se bloquea en el paso 4 o 5: aceptable omitir un paso. Core = pasos 1–3.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You just gave a professional presentation in English. Four sessions ago you couldn't say hello. Look at where you are now."
  • Celebra con especificidad: "Your a/an was perfect. Your greeting was natural. Next time — more eye contact, less hesitation."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "S5 es tu sesión de consolidación — todo de S1 a S4 se une. Ven preparado."
  • Testimonial trigger: "How would you describe your English level today versus 4 sessions ago?"
  • Upsell: "En A1.2 empiezas a hablar de tu rutina diaria, tu agenda, tu empresa — con la misma estructura que construiste aquí."
