§ 01
Warm-Up — Scenario
5–8 min
📍 The Scenario
Situation
It's Monday morning. You have a video call at 9 AM with David Chen, your contact in Canada. Before starting the agenda, you want to open the conversation naturally — not go straight to business. What do you say? How do you open the call?
1
How do you greet David at the start of the call?
Try: "Good morning, David! How are you?"
2
How do you ask about the weather in a natural way?
Try: "How's the weather over there in ___?"
3
David forgot to send the agenda before the call. What do you say?
Try: "Please ___" or "Could you ___?" — polite instruction
4
At the end: how do you tell everyone NOT to forget the next meeting date?
Try: "Don't ___. Our next call is on ___."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This scenario targets today's TWO grammar points: imperatives (Q3, Q4) and weather small talk (Q2). Student probably can't do either perfectly yet — that's fine. This is activation.
  • Q3 most likely answer: "Please send the agenda." Note if they say "Please to send" — that's today's key error. Address in §4.
  • Q4: "Don't forget the next meeting." Check: do they use "Don't" or "No forget"? Spanish interference is strong here.
  • Timing: 7 min max. Keep it conversational and light — this is a preview, not a test.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "This is a real Monday morning at your company. Do you have calls like this with international contacts?"
  • "By the end of today, opening this call in perfect English will be automatic. Let's see how you do."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Native speakers notice the first 30 seconds of a call. Today we perfect those 30 seconds."

/əˈtætʃ/

avoid
/əˈvɔɪd/

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • 4 work verbs + 4 weather adjectives = 8 total. Clean division, easy to remember.
  • "attach" — only used with files/documents. "Please attach your CV" ✓. "Please attach your idea" ✗ — not a physical attachment.
  • "avoid" + gerund: "Avoid sending ___ " (A2 grammar). At A1.2: just "Don't avoid ___" is sufficient.
  • Weather: click each button to hear. Drill: teacher mimes a weather, student says the word. Then student describes today's weather in Celaya.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "What's the weather like today? Describe it in English. Is it sunny? Warm? Cloudy?" — immediate real-world practice.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Temperature tip: with American clients, convert to Fahrenheit. 28°C = 82°F. Always say the unit: '82 degrees Fahrenheit' or just '82 degrees.'"

§ 04
Grammar — Imperatives & Small Talk
12–15 min
Structure — Imperatives at Work
TypeStructureExampleRegister
AffirmativeBase verb (no subject)Confirm your attendance.Direct / neutral
PolitePlease + base verbPlease confirm by Thursday.Formal / polite
NegativeDon't + base verbDon't forget the deadline.Direct / neutral
Polite neg.Please don't + base verbPlease don't share this yet.Formal / polite
Key rule: No subject in imperatives. The verb always comes first.
✅ "Open the file." — ✗ "You open the file." (sounds unnatural / not an imperative)
✅ "Don't forget." — ✗ "You don't forget." (this is a negative statement, not a command)
⚠ Most Common Errors
❌ "No forget the report."→✅ Don't forget the report.
❌ "Please to confirm the meeting."→✅ Please confirm the meeting. (no "to" after Please)
❌ "You save the file please."→✅ Please save the file. (no subject)
❌ "Don't to use your phone."→✅ Don't use your phone. (don't + base verb only)
Weather Small Talk — The Professional Call Opener
You — Houston
Good morning, David! How's the weather over there in Vancouver?
David — Canada
Hi! It's freezing here today — minus eight! How about Houston?
You — Houston
Oh wow! It's sunny and warm here — about 75 degrees. Very pleasant!
David — Canada
Lucky you! Anyway — let's get started.
You — Houston
Great. Please confirm receipt of the agenda I sent this morning.

Activity 2 — Complete the Imperative
Type the correct form. Use the verb in brackets.
1. ___ the report to your manager before 5 PM. [send]
2. ___ your laptop during the security training. [not open]
3. Please ___ the signed contract to your reply. [attach]
4. ___ confidential files by regular email. [not share]
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Q2 most common error: "No use your phone" (direct Spanish transfer: "No usar el teléfono"). Reinforce: in English it's always DON'T, not NO.
  • Activity 2 (fill-in): accept capitalization variations. "send" or "Send" both correct for Q1. Focus on the verb form, not capitalization at A1.2.
  • After activities: student opens the call scenario from §1 again — this time correctly. Real improvement loop.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • Weather conversation: role-play it live. Teacher is David in Canada. Student opens the call. Keep it going for 60 seconds.
  • "Notice how the call feels different with small talk? More professional, warmer. 30 seconds of weather = much better relationship."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In A2 you'll use 'Could you please confirm?' — more formal still. For now, 'Please confirm' is exactly right."
§ 05
Reading
8–10 min
FROM
Patricia Morales <p.morales@vertex.mx>
TO
All Staff
📎 Office Guidelines — Please Read Carefully

Dear team,

Welcome to our updated office guidelines. Please read carefully and follow these instructions.

Working hours:
Arrive at the office by 8:30 AM. Don't be late to team meetings — they start on time. Please inform your manager if you are absent.

Digital tools:
Confirm all external emails within 24 hours. Attach relevant documents before sending. Don't avoid responding to client messages — this is a professional priority.
Please don't share confidential information via personal email.

Meetings:
Don't use your phone during presentations. Please turn it to silent. Send your update before the meeting — don't wait until the last minute.

Follow these guidelines and we will have a productive, professional environment. Thank you!

Patricia Morales · HR Manager · Vertex Industrial Group
Comprehension
Answer the questions.
What should you do if you are absent?
What should you do before a meeting?
You should NOT share confidential information via personal email.
It is OK to use your phone during presentations.
External emails must be confirmed within 24 hours.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Green text = affirmative/polite imperatives. Red text = negative imperatives. Ask student to count: how many green? How many red?
  • RQ2 trap: "Confirm the agenda" sounds right but the text says "Send your update before the meeting." Students who skim choose the wrong one.
  • Reading aloud: teacher reads a section, student repeats each imperative. Focus on intonation — imperatives fall in pitch at the end.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Does your company have guidelines like this? Are there any rules you wish they had? Say one: 'Please ___.' or 'Don't ___.' "
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In A2 you'll write emails like this — giving instructions professionally. Today you read them. Tomorrow you write them."

▶ Show ElevenLabs Script

Good morning, everyone! Thanks for joining the call. Before we start — how's the weather over there in Chicago, Tom?

[pause for response]

Really? It's freezing there! Well, it's sunny and warm here in Houston — about 75 degrees. Lovely! Anyway, let's get started.

I have a few important instructions for the team this week. First — please confirm receipt of the contract I sent yesterday. Don't forget to check the attachment. Second — don't avoid responding to the client's emails. They are waiting for an answer. Please reply by Thursday.

Also — please attach the Q2 progress report to your next update. Don't send it without the report — it's very important.

And finally — please don't share the budget numbers outside the team. This information is confidential. Follow these instructions and we'll have a great week. Any questions? Perfect. See you Thursday!

⏱ Target: 80–90 sec · Voice: Rachel · Speed: 0.83

Listening Activities
Listen, then answer.
What is the weather in Chicago?
What should the team attach to their next update?
The team must reply to the client by Thursday.
The budget numbers can be shared with anyone.
The weather in Houston is warm and sunny.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Focus task for listen 1: "Count how many imperatives you hear." (Answer: 8+ — please confirm, don't forget, don't avoid, please reply, please attach, don't send, please don't share, follow).
  • LQ2 trap: "the contract" sounds correct (she mentions it) but she says to confirm the contract, NOT attach it. Attach = the progress report.
  • After activities: shadow the instructions section. "Please confirm… Don't forget… Don't avoid…" — drill the intonation.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Did you notice how Rachel opened the call? Weather first — then business. That's professional English. Use this on your next international call."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Homework: listen 3× and write down all the imperatives you hear. "8 imperatives in 90 seconds — that's how often they appear in real professional English."

🗣 Speaking Challenge
Format: Info Gap

"You are a manager opening a Monday call. Give your team 5 instructions — they must write them down and read them back."

Teacher is the "team". Student gives 5 instructions (mix of affirmative, negative, and polite). Teacher writes them down. Then teacher reads back — student checks if they're correct.

Manager (Student) — Give these
Open with weather small talk — 2 sentences.
Give 2 affirmative imperatives about today's tasks.
Give 1 "Please + verb" instruction about a deadline.
Give 1 "Don't + verb" instruction about sharing files.
Close: "Please confirm receipt of this call." or similar.
Team (Teacher) — Write down & read back
Write each imperative as you hear it.
After 5 instructions, read them back aloud.
Student confirms: correct or incorrect?
Error in teacher's version = student must repeat the instruction correctly.
Optional: swap roles — teacher gives instructions, student writes.
