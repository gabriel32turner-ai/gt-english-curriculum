Warm-Up — Module 3 Quick-Fire
⏱ 8 min · 90 sec per student · Full M3 activation · Badge ⚡
📋 Logistics

90-second Quick-Fire activates ALL of Module 3: past regular/irregular verbs (S1–S2), was/were in meeting context (S3), negatives + duration (S4). Run as a race — first student to produce a complete answer with correct tense wins the round. Never correct mid-answer — mark errors and address after.

🎯 Pedagogical Focus
  • Q1: Simple Past irregular — S2 content (went/had/gave/met)
  • Q2: was/were + meeting context — S3 content
  • Q3: didn't + base verb + duration — S4 content
  • Q4: 4-Step Presentation structure — S4A synthesis
💡 S5 Purpose

This is not a new lesson — it is a performance session. Students should feel the accumulated weight of 4 sessions. If the warm-up reveals specific gaps (e.g., "didn't finished" reappears), note them for targeted correction during the Exit Task feedback. Never re-teach in S5 — redirect: "What did we say about 'didn't' in S4?"

⚡ QUICK-FIRE · 90 SECONDS PER STUDENT

🔵 Irregular Past — S2 Review
Your manager asks: "What happened in last Monday's supplier meeting?" Tell them 4 things using past irregular verbs: went / had / gave / met. Each sentence must include a past time expression.
Push: "Did you make any decisions? Did the supplier say anything about the delay?"
⭐ Was/Were — S3 Review
Describe last week's most important meeting using was/were. Include: where it was, how many people were there, what the topic was, and whether it was productive or not. Use both affirmative and negative forms.
Push: "Was the agenda clear? Were all the action items completed? What wasn't finished?"
🟢 Didn't + Duration — S4 Review
Tell your partner 2 things you didn't complete at work last week. For each one: what you didn't finish, how long you worked on it, and what the reason was. Use two different duration expressions.
Push: "Did you explain this to your manager? How long did the whole project take — not just last week?"
🟣 4-Step Structure — S4A Review
Give a 60-second results mini-presentation. Open → give one data point → mention one problem or negative → close with a next step. Use past tenses throughout. No notes.
Push: "Was the result above or below target? What was the impact? What will you do next?"

Speaking Topic — Your Real Results
⏱ 12 min · 3 rounds · Real data · Full tense integration
📋 Logistics

3 rounds integrating all Module 3 grammar in a real work context. Students use their actual job data — no invented scenarios. Push for varied tenses: past regular, past irregular, was/were, didn't, duration. Model answer shows B1-level aspiration — frame it always as "here's where you're going, not where you must be today."

🎯 Pedagogical Focus
  • Round 1: metacognitive — what changed in 4 sessions of this module
  • Round 2: real past event using the full tense repertoire (action + state + negative + duration)
  • Round 3: full 4-step presentation — directly bridges to the Exit Task
💡 Retention Moment

Ask: "Before Module 3, could you say 'The meeting was productive but we didn't reach a decision — we worked on it all afternoon'?" Most will say no. Ask them to say it now. That single sentence is the evidence of Module 3. Plant this moment — it becomes the upsell to Module 4.

📊
Your Module 3 World — Results, Meetings, Past Tense
Round 1 — What I Can Say Now
Tell your partner: "Before Module 3, I couldn't say… Now I can say…" Give 3 specific examples — real sentences you can now produce in English. Partner listens and marks: did you use at least 1 past tense, 1 was/were, and 1 negative correctly?
💡 This builds awareness of your own progress AND gives you language to report your learning to your manager or HR.
Round 2 — A Real Past Event at Work
Describe a real event from the last month: a meeting, a project deadline, a supplier issue, or a result you presented. Use at least: 3 past action verbs, 2 was/were, 1 negative with "didn't," and 1 duration expression. Partner marks tally: 1 point per correct form heard.
💡 Aim for 7+ tally marks in 2 minutes. If you get 10+, you're ready for B1.1.
Round 3 — 90-Second 4-Step Presentation
Use the 4-step structure from S4: Open → Data → Problem → Close. Present real results from your work — a process metric, a delivery number, a quality result, a budget item. 90 seconds. No notes. Partner marks the 4 steps as you pass through them.
💡 This is a direct rehearsal for the Exit Task. Think of it as your dress rehearsal.

"Good morning. I'm going to talk about our Q3 supplier performance. Last month, we received 94% of orders on time — that was above our 90% target. However, we didn't reach our cost reduction goal. We worked on the renegotiation from Monday to Thursday but the supplier didn't approve the new rates. The meeting was productive — we identified the main blockers. Next step: we will send a revised proposal by Friday."

Contains: 3 action verbs · 2 was/were · 2 negatives · 1 duration · 1 number · 4-step structure ✅

Grammar Review — Full M3 Integration
⏱ 14 min · 3 polls · Activity A (MC) · Activity B (drag & drop) · Hot Seat Dice · Badge 🎯
📋 Logistics

3 class polls (instant error correction, no shame), Activity A (5 MC — mixed M3 grammar), Activity B (2 drag-and-drop — scrambled), Hot Seat Dice (6 integration prompts). Badge 🎯 at 4/5 in Activity A. Polls: class votes, reveal, brief correction — 30 seconds each max. Never dwell. Keep energy high.

🎯 Error Hierarchy — In Order of Priority
  • 🔴 P1: "didn't finished" — correct immediately, every time
  • 🔴 P2: "was" with plural subject ("The results was good") — correct immediately
  • 🟡 P3: "during" for "for" — flag, correct, move on
  • 🟡 P4: "didn't went" — correct, note irregular verbs don't change after didn't
  • 🟢 P5: Missing duration expression — prompt, don't correct
💡 S5 Grammar Philosophy

No new grammar in S5. The goal is fluency and confidence with existing forms. A student who says "The meeting was productive and we achieved our target — but we didn't finish the follow-up" is demonstrating Module 3 mastery. Celebrate that sentence.

Good morning, team. I'm going to give you a quick update on our Q3 performance.

First, the good news. Our delivery rate was 96% — that was above the 90% target. The operations team worked very hard. We also implemented two new processes in the warehouse — both were successful.

§7 — MODULE EXIT TASK
The 4-Step Results Presentation — Your Performance
⏱ 18 min · No notes · 3 min minimum · Real data · Module evidence
📋 Logistics

This is the Module 3 Exit Task. Students choose ONE scenario (A, B, or C) and perform a 3-minute minimum spoken results presentation using real data from their job. No notes. Partner marks the rubric in real time. Teacher does NOT interrupt during performance. After: 2-minute feedback using the rubric cards. Badge 🌟 for completing.

🎯 Exit Task Rubric
  • ✅ 4-Step structure clearly followed (Open → Data → Problem → Close)
  • ✅ 3+ past action verbs used correctly (identified, achieved, implemented…)
  • ✅ 2+ was/were forms used correctly (results were / meeting was)
  • ✅ 1+ negative with didn't + base verb (didn't finish / didn't reach)
  • ✅ 1+ duration expression (for X / all week / from X to X / until X)
  • ✅ At least 1 real number or data point
💡 Corporate Evidence Moment

This performance IS the product. The moment a student says "The results were above target — we achieved 96% delivery rate, but we didn't reduce costs as planned" in fluent, confident English, that is evidence of ROI. Record it (with permission). That 30-second clip is: HR report · Manager update · Program renewal conversation. Never skip this section. Never rush it.

SCENARIO A — My Department's Results
Present your department's real results from last month or quarter using the 4-step structure. Open with context → give one positive data point → mention one challenge or target you didn't reach → close with the next step. Minimum: 1 number, 3 past verbs, 1 was/were, 1 didn't.
💡 Use real metrics: delivery rate, defect rate, cost savings, attendance, units produced, on-time delivery, budget compliance.
SCENARIO B — A Meeting I Chaired or Attended
Report on a real meeting from the last month. What was the topic? Who was there? What was decided? What action items weren't completed? How long did it last? Use was/were for state + past action verbs + at least 2 negatives with "didn't."
💡 Partner asks: "Were all action items completed? What was the biggest challenge? How long did it take?"
SCENARIO C — A Problem We Didn't Solve (Yet)
Present an ongoing challenge at your company: what the problem is/was, what you tried (3 past action verbs), what didn't work (2 negatives with didn't), how much time you spent on it, and what the plan is. This is professional honesty — it's what managers respect in English.
💡 Use: "We tried to… but we didn't… / We worked on it for… / The main blocker was… / Next step: we will…"
