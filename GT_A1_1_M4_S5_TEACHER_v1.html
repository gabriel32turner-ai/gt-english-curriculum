Warm-Up — M4 Quick-Fire
⏱ 8 min · One question per session · Full module activation
S1–S4 Review
📋 Logistics

S5 is a performance session — not a new lesson. Each warm-up question targets one of the four prior sessions. Run as a 90-second individual race: first complete answer earns ⚡ badge. Never re-teach in S5. If a student can't answer, ask "What did we do in S1?" and move on. Gaps at S5 belong in revision time, not session time.

🎯 Pedagogical Focus
  • Q1 → S1: there is/are · office rooms
  • Q2 → S2: prepositions of place
  • Q3 → S3: giving directions + imperatives
  • Q4 → S4: ordinals + colours + distance
💡 Upsell Note

Frame S5 as the moment students "own" the module. Say: "Everything you've practised for 8 sessions is now yours. Today you use it in real English. After today, you can describe any building, give any direction, and navigate any city in English." That's the emotional anchor for the certificate moment in §8.

⚡ QUICK-FIRE · 90 SECONDS · One question, one student

🔴 S1 — There is/are
Tell your partner 5 things that are in your office building. Use there is and there are. Include at least one negative: "There isn't / There aren't."
Push: "Now say what floor each one is on: 'There is a cafeteria on the third floor.'"
🟡 S2 — Prepositions of Place
Look around the room. Say where 5 things are using prepositions: next to, on, under, in front of, behind, between. One sentence each. Partner listens and checks the preposition.
Push: "Now describe where your desk is relative to two other objects: 'My desk is between the window and the door.'"
🟢 S3 — Directions
A new colleague is at the main entrance and needs to reach your office. Give them step-by-step directions using imperatives: Go straight, Turn left, Take the elevator, It's on the right. Minimum 4 steps.
Push: "Add the floor and a landmark: 'Take the elevator to the third floor — it's the first door on the left, next to the blue sign.'"
🔵 S4 — Ordinals · Colours · Distance
Describe your company building to a visitor who's never been to your city: colour, number of floors, which floor is your office, one nearby landmark and how far it is. No notes — from memory.
Push: "Can you use all four S4 structures in two sentences? Ordinal + colour + distance + 'there are'?"

Speaking — The Visitor's Question
⏱ 10 min · 3 speaking rounds · Integrated output
📋 Structure

Three rounds of 2 minutes each. Round 1 is warm — students describe their building freely. Round 2 raises the stakes: they have to help a lost visitor in real time. Round 3 is the integrated challenge — ALL of M4 in one continuous description. This builds toward the Exit Task without announcing it.

🎯 Error Watch
  • "in the third floor" → "on the third floor" — collect every instance on the board
  • "the building blue" → "the blue building" — adjective order is key at A1.1
  • "during five minutes" → "for five minutes / five minutes on foot"
💡 Upsell

After Round 3, say: "You just described your entire building — in English — with no preparation time. A year ago, you couldn't do that. That's real progress." Let it land. Then move to §3.

🏢
Your building. Your words. Right now.
Three rounds — each one goes a little further than the last
Round 1 — 2 min · Building Description
Describe your company building to your partner: colour, how many floors, what's on the ground floor, what floor your office is on. Use "there is / there are" for each detail. Partner notes: how many M4 structures did you use?
Teacher push: After 90s, ask "Now add a negative: 'There isn't…' or 'There aren't…' — what doesn't your building have?"
"The building is grey and blue. There are eight floors. On the ground floor, there is a reception area and there is a security desk. My office is on the fifth floor, next to the elevator. There isn't a gym, but there is a cafeteria on the third floor."
Round 2 — 3 min · The Lost Visitor Role-Play
Partner A calls: "I'm outside your building but I can't find the entrance. I'm at a corner with a red pharmacy." Give step-by-step directions from that corner to your office — floor, door, landmark. Use imperatives + prepositions + ordinals.
Switch roles after 90s. Partner A should give different directions — to a meeting room, not the student's desk.
"OK! From the pharmacy, go straight — don't turn. The entrance is the second door on the right — there's a blue sign above it. Go in and take the elevator to the fifth floor. Turn left when you exit. My office is the third door on the left, next to the coffee machine. You can't miss it!"
Round 3 — 3 min · The Complete Description Challenge ⭐
In one continuous 60-second description, cover ALL of M4: there is/are + prepositions + directions + ordinals + colours + distance. Imagine you're helping a new employee on their first day. No notes. Partner tallies M4 structures used.
Class challenge: who uses the most M4 structures in 60 seconds? Write the top score on the board. Beat it in the Exit Task.
"Welcome! Our building is the grey and blue one — there are six floors. The entrance is on the ground floor, next to a small café. Take the elevator to the fourth floor. Turn right — my office is between the conference room and the break room. There's a green plant in front of the door, you can't miss it. The nearest hotel? It's the white building about two blocks from here — go straight and turn left at the corner. About five minutes on foot."

⏱ 10 min · 16 key M4 words · Say it before you flip
📋 Logistics

Pair activity: one student says the English word aloud before flipping. Partner checks pronunciation. Goal: say it BEFORE reading — then flip to confirm. 🎯 Priority Words
  • "between" — the most mispronounced preposition at A1.1
  • "third floor" — the /θ/ sound needs drilling
  • "ground floor" — students default to "zero floor"
  • "opposite" — often confused with "in front of"
  • "How far is it?" — the full question form, not just "how far?"

Colloquial Review — Phrases That Sound Natural
⏱ 6 min · 6 M4 phrases in real-world context
📋 Logistics

Quick-fire poll format: teacher reads the phrase, class votes. Then drill the correct pronunciation. Each phrase is a common A1.1 location/direction/description error turned into a natural alternative. The goal is replacing clunky textbook-English with how people actually talk in Celaya's industrial corridor when speaking English.

🎯 Focus Phrases
  • "You can't miss it" — the gold standard of building description
  • "It's just a short walk" — replaces "it is close"
  • "a few floors up" — natural when you don't know the exact floor
  • "It's at the corner" — preposition choice matters (at, not on or in)
"You can't miss it."
No puedes perdértelo — es muy visible.
"Our building is the big grey one at the corner — you can't miss it."
Use after any building description. Reassures the visitor without extra words.
"It's just a short walk."
Está a poca distancia caminando.
"The hotel is two blocks away — it's just a short walk."
More reassuring than "it is not far." Natural in any city context.
"It's a few floors up."
Está unos pisos más arriba.
"The conference room? It's a few floors up — take the elevator."
Use when you can't remember the exact ordinal. Casual, natural, functional.
"It's at the corner."
Está en la esquina.
"Go straight and turn right — our building is at the corner of that street."
Always "at the corner" — NOT "in the corner" (that's inside a room).
"Follow the signs."
Sigue las señales / letreros.
"Take the elevator and follow the signs — you'll see HR on the left."
Useful when directions are too complex. Professional and efficient.
"About [X] minutes on foot."
Aproximadamente [X] minutos caminando.
"How far is the hotel? About ten minutes on foot — or five by taxi."
The standard A1.1 distance answer. "On foot" = caminando. Never "by walk."

Grammar Review — M4 Full Structure Map
⏱ 12 min · Error correction · 2 integrated activities
📋 Structure

S5 grammar review has one table, one error correction activity, and one drag-and-drop. It's diagnostic — use the error correction to identify which structure students still confuse before the Exit Task. Common S5 errors: "in the floor" (→ on), "building blue" (→ blue building), "during 5 minutes" (→ for / on foot), "Go to right" (→ Turn right). Collect errors on the board and let students self-correct.

🎯 Exit Task Preview

After activity B (drag-and-drop), say: "The Exit Task asks you to do exactly this — put these structures together in a continuous spoken tour. You have 4 minutes. The rubric has 6 checkboxes — each one is a structure from S1–S4." Seeing the rubric NOW (before the task) lowers anxiety significantly.

⚠️ M4 Top Errors — One last correction before the Exit Task:
✗ "It's in the third floor" → ✅ It's on the third floor (always ON with floors)
✗ "The building blue" → ✅ The blue building (colour BEFORE noun in English)
✗ "Go to right" → ✅ Turn right (imperative without "to")
✗ "during five minutes" → ✅ for five minutes / five minutes on foot
✗ "In the corner" (for buildings) → ✅ at the corner

Listening — Ana's First Day
⏱ 10 min · Full M4 integration in a real scenario · ElevenLabs
📋 Script Design

The listening integrates ALL four M4 structures: Ana uses there is/are (S1), prepositions (S2), directions (S3), and ordinals + colours + distance (S4). The scenario is realistic for Celaya's industrial corridor. First listen: comprehension questions. Second listen (optional): students note every M4 structure they hear. This primes their output for the Exit Task.

🎯 Pre-Listening

Before playing: ask "What do you think Ana's first day was like?" Show the photo. Students predict: "There is / There are… I think she turned left / She took the elevator…" This activates M4 vocabulary before they hear it.

💡 Retention Note

After the comprehension questions, ask: "Which M4 phrase from the audio is most useful for YOU this week?" Students say one phrase aloud. This personalisation step increases retention by connecting the language to real future use — not just test answers.

Ana's First Day — Solaris Manufacturing, Celaya
A1.1 · M4 · S5 · Voice: Sarah (American English) · Speed: 0.78 · All four M4 structures
Hi! My name is Ana García. Today is my first day at Solaris Manufacturing in Celaya, and I want to tell you about the building.

When I arrived this morning, I went to the main entrance. There is a large reception area on the ground floor. There are two security guards next to the main door, and there is a sign-in system for visitors in front of the reception desk.

My new office is on the fourth floor. The building is grey and blue — there are eight floors in total. To get to my office: go straight from reception, take the elevator, and turn right when you exit. My desk is the second door on the left — it's between the window and the printer. You can't miss it.

There is a cafeteria on the third floor — I had coffee there this morning. It's next to the break room. There isn't a gym in the building, but there is a park about five minutes on foot from the main entrance. It's opposite the white hotel — the tall one at the corner.

I love my new office. The view from the fourth floor is great — you can see the grey mountains in the distance. And the team is very nice. It's a good first day!
📎 ElevenLabs: Voice — Sarah (American English) · Speed 0.78 · A1.1 M4 S5 — All four M4 sessions integrated

"The cafeteria is on the third floor, next to the break room."

"Ana's desk is between the window and the printer."

§7 — EXIT TASK
The Complete Building Tour
⏱ 20 min · 4-minute spoken performance · M4 rubric · Certificate
⭐ Module Assessment
📋 Task Structure

4 minutes total. Students give a spoken "welcome tour" for a new international employee or client. No notes. The 6 rubric boxes correspond directly to the 4 sessions of M4 — each S1 through S4 structure appears in the rubric. Audience (partner) marks rubric boxes in real time. After the tour, the audience gives 2 things that went well and 1 suggestion.

🎯 Minimum Criteria
  • 5+ uses of there is/are (including at least 1 negative)
  • 3+ prepositions of place
  • Complete directions (4+ steps, imperative verbs)
  • At least 2 ordinals for floor numbers
  • Building colour description
  • 1 distance expression (blocks or minutes on foot)
💡 Upsell Script

Before starting, say: "This is exactly the situation you'll face when a supplier, client, or new colleague visits your plant. What you say in the first 4 minutes shapes their impression of you AND your company. Let's practice that conversation right now." Then start the timer. Don't give extra prep time — real life doesn't give prep time.

⭐ M4 Exit Task
The Welcome Tour — 4 Minutes in English
No notes · Real building · Real English · All 4 M4 sessions in one performance

🎯 Your Task

A new international colleague arrives at your company for the first time. Welcome them and give a complete building tour in English. Cover: what's in the building (there is/are), where things are (prepositions), how to get to key spaces (directions), and what the building and neighbourhood look like (ordinals, colours, distance). 4 minutes. No notes. All 4 M4 sessions.

📋 Rubric — Partner marks each box when they hear it

S1 — There is/are
Uses there is / there are at least 5 times, including 1 negative (there isn't / there aren't)
S2 — Prepositions of Place
Uses at least 3 different prepositions: next to, on, in front of, behind, between, opposite
S3 — Directions
Gives step-by-step directions using imperative verbs: Go straight / Turn / Take the elevator / It's on the…
S4 — Ordinals
Uses at least 2 ordinal numbers correctly for floors: "on the third floor", "on the ground floor"
S4 — Colours
Describes the building with at least 1 colour: "the grey and blue building / the white hotel"
S4 — Distance
Uses a distance expression: "[X] blocks from here" or "[X] minutes on foot" or "just a short walk"
