🎯 Grammar — Passive Voice

10 min
Passive Voice — when the action matters more than who does it
REFERENCIAL. Show both forms side by side: "Someone collected your data" vs "Your data was collected." Ask: which sounds more professional? Which is used in news? Natural grammar teaching.
Error: "My data collected" → missing "was". Always need be + past participle.
TenseActivePassive (be + past participle)
Present SimpleThey collect your data.Your data is collected.
Present ContinuousThey are replacing jobs.Jobs are being replaced.
Past SimpleThey designed these apps.These apps were designed to keep you scrolling.
Present PerfectThey have sold your data.Your data has been sold.
Future (will)AI will replace these jobs.These jobs will be replaced by AI.
⚠️ KEY RULE
Use passive when: the agent is unknown ("My data was sold"), obvious ("She was hired"), or unimportant ("The system was updated").

✗ "My data collected" → ✅ "My data was collected" — always need a form of "be"!

🎧 Listening — Carlos on AI & His Industry

8 min
📍 Carlos Mendez reflects on how AI is changing his industry
🎙️
ElevenLabs — "AI Changed My Industry"
Voice: James · Speed: 0.85 · B1.2 M3 S1A
Three years ago, my company started using AI in our manufacturing process. At first, people were scared. They thought their jobs were being threatened. And honestly? Some jobs were replaced. About 40 repetitive tasks were automated in the first year alone.

But here's what surprised everyone: new roles were created. AI trainers were hired. Data analysts were brought in. The people who were affected were offered retraining programs — most of them adapted. The factory wasn't destroyed by AI. It was transformed.

The real problem isn't AI. The real problem is companies that don't invest in their people. If workers aren't retrained, they'll be left behind. If skills aren't updated, entire departments will become obsolete.

I'm hooked on following this space. The technology evolves every month. New tools are being deployed faster than we can learn them. But I believe the human skills — empathy, creativity, leadership — those can't be automated. Not yet, anyway.
