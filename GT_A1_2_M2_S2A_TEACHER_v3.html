Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • ACCIÓN: Usa el Number Builder como el centro de §2. No leas todos los números de corrido — deja que el alumno los diga PRIMERO antes de ver el texto. Es producción oral desde el minuto 1.
  • Regla crítica de pronunciación americana: en inglés americano NO se dice "two hundred AND fifty thousand" — el "and" después de "hundred" es correcto pero opcional en American English. Sin embargo, el "and" entre miles y centenas es MÁS común en American English informal. No hay error, solo registro.
  • ERROR CRÍTICO a prevenir: "one thousand two hundred AND fifty" — el alumno quizás diga "twelve hundreds" (calco del español "mil doscientos"). Corrige inmediatamente con recast: "Yes — one thousand two hundred."

§ 04
Grammar — Numbers in Context: The Problem
10–12 min
The situation: Your supplier sends you a quote for $125,450 MXN. Your manager asks: "What's the total? How many units? What's the unit price? Is it within budget?" — You need to say those numbers accurately and naturally in English. Right now, can you do it?
The Tool — How to Build Big Numbers in English
StructureNumberHow to say itExample in context
Hundreds100–999X hundred (+ and + rest)"We need two hundred and fifty units."
Thousands1,000–9,999X thousand (+ hundreds if needed)"The price is four thousand five hundred."
Ten-thousands10,000–99,999XX thousand (+ rest)"Our budget is fifty thousand dollars."
Hundreds of thousands100,000–999,999X hundred thousand (+ rest)"We produce two hundred thousand parts per year."
Talking About Prices — Simple Present + can
FunctionStructureExample
State a priceIt costs / The price is"The monitor costs four thousand eight hundred pesos."
Ask for priceHow much is / How much does it cost?"How much is the unit price for this product?"
Confirm budgetThat's within / over budget"Fourteen thousand four hundred? That's within budget."
Request priceCan you send me a quote for…?"Can you send me a quote for 500 units?"
NegotiateCan we get a discount on…?"Can we get a discount on the total amount?"
⚠️ Common Number Errors — Hispanohablantes
❌ "twelve hundreds"→✅ "one thousand two hundred" / "twelve hundred"
Spanish: "mil doscientos" → English: "twelve hundred" or "one thousand two hundred". Both correct. "Twelve hundreds" = never.
❌ "four thousands five hundreds"→✅ "four thousand five hundred"
NO plural on hundred/thousand when used as part of a compound number. "Four thousands" by itself is incorrect.
❌ "It costs 28,500 pesos" (said as numbers only, no words)→✅ "It costs twenty-eight thousand five hundred pesos"
In spoken English, you always say the full words — never "twenty-eight comma five hundred." Read numbers as words.

      • ACCIÓN: Primera escucha: sin script. Segunda escucha si es necesario. Luego preguntas de comprensión. Si el alumno no entiende los números hablados — eso es exactamente la dificultad a resolver en §7.

      § 07
      Production — Budget Debate
      12–14 min
      📢 Speaking Activity — Budget Debate
      New Equipment vs. Budget Control
      "Our company needs new monitors and chairs for the office. The total cost is $250,000 MXN. Should we approve this purchase — or wait until next quarter?"
      ✅ APPROVE — Arguments for
      "The current monitors are old. They affect productivity — that costs more in the long run."
      "We have some money in the budget — about $280,000. The total is within budget."
      "If we wait, the price might go up. The unit price for monitors is already $4,800."
      "The lead time is only 3 to 5 days. We can have everything installed next week."
      ❌ WAIT — Arguments against
      "Two hundred and fifty thousand pesos is a lot of money for Q3. Q4 budget is larger."
      "We don't have any confirmed orders this month. It's not the right time to spend."
      "Can we get a discount? The supplier offers 5% off orders over $100,000. Let's negotiate first."
      "How many monitors do we really need? 10 units is a lot. Start with 5 and review."
      💡 Language required: numbers (100,000–999,999) · some/any · how much/many · within/over budget · "I think we should…" / "I don't think we should…" / "In my opinion…"
      § Speaking Practice
      Debate: Should We Approve This Purchase?
      Guided debate — 10–12 minutes. Teacher assigns "Approve" or "Wait" side. Student defends using today's numbers and vocabulary. Then switch sides.
      Question 01
      The total cost is $250,000 MXN. Is that within your company's budget right now? How do you say that number out loud?
      Hint: "Two hundred and fifty thousand pesos. That's [within / over] our budget."
      Question 02
      How many monitors does the company need? Is that a lot or a little? Use "how many" and "how much" in your answer.
      Hint: "We need [X] monitors. That's a lot of equipment. How much money is in our budget?"
      Question 03
      The payment terms are 30 days. Is that good for your company? What's your company's typical payment situation?
      Hint: "Thirty days is [good / not ideal]. We usually pay [in X days / immediately]."
      Question 04
      There's a 5% discount available for orders over $100,000. Should you add more products to get the discount? Calculate and say the numbers out loud.
      Hint: "If we add [X] units of [product], the total is over one hundred thousand. We can get five percent off."
      Question 05
      Make a final recommendation in English: "I think we should / shouldn't approve this purchase because…" Use at least two numbers in your argument.
      Hint: "I think we should approve it. The total is two hundred and fifty thousand — within budget. And the lead time is only three days."
      🏆 Speaking Challenge — 90 Seconds
      "You are the Purchasing Manager. Your CEO asks: 'Give me a 90-second summary of this supplier quote and your recommendation.' Go — no notes."
      Must include: total amount (in words) · at least 3 product prices · payment terms · lead time · your recommendation: approve or wait. Minimum 5 sentences.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • ACCIÓN: Asigna el lado del debate ANTES de que el alumno lea los argumentos. El alumno tiene 30 segundos para prepararse — luego habla. Esa presión productiva es la práctica real.
      • Estándar de éxito A1.2: el alumno dice al menos 3 números grandes correctamente (sin "thousands" plural), usa "within/over budget" al menos 1 vez, y da una recomendación coherente. Eso es 100% de éxito para este nivel.
      • Speaking Challenge: el Purchasing Manager que da un resumen de 90 segundos simula exactamente la situación real de un profesionista mexicano que presenta a un jefe americano. Ese es el ROI de la sesión.
      • Corrección: recast silencioso. Si dice "two hundreds thousands" → tú dices "yes, two hundred thousand — that's a big number." Sin interrumpir el flujo.
      • Si hay tiempo (2 min extra): cambia de lado. "Now defend the opposite position." Eso refuerza el vocabulario desde ambos ángulos.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Acabas de presentar una recomendación de compra en inglés con números reales. Eso lo hacen los Purchasing Managers, los coordinadores de compras, los ingenieros que gestionan presupuestos. Tú acabas de hacerlo."
      • Celebra lo específico: "Dijiste 'two hundred and fifty thousand' sin dudar. Hace 50 minutos ese número te costaba trabajo. Ahora sale natural."
      Capa 3 · Retención & Upsell
      • "En S2B haces el rol del proveedor: recibes el pedido, contestas preguntas, manejas la negociación. Sin el scaffolding de hoy — pura producción libre. Ese es el método GT."
      • Testimonial trigger: "¿Qué parte de hoy fue más sorprendente? ¿Cuándo dijiste un número grande en inglés y salió bien?" — Esta pregunta ancla el aprendizaje.
