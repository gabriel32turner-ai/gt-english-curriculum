Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Student clicks their choice — teacher asks "Why?" in English. Accept simple answers: "I like Mexico." "Japan is good."

  • All 4 pairs connect to today's vocabulary — countries and nationalities. This is intentional scaffolding.
  • Don't correct yet — this is warm-up. Let language flow naturally. Timing: strict 7 min max.

Capa 3 · Growth & Retention

§ 04
Grammar — Negative & Questions
12–15 min
To Be — Negative forms
SubjectTo BeNotShort formExample
IamnotI'm notI'm not from France.
YouarenotYou aren'tYou aren't from the USA.
He / She / ItisnotHe/She isn'tHe isn't Brazilian.
We / TheyarenotThey aren'tThey aren't Japanese.
Yes / No Questions with To Be
To BeSubjectYES answerNO answer
Areyou from Mexico?Yes, I am.No, I'm not.
Isshe a manager?Yes, she is.No, she isn't.
Arethey American?Yes, they are.No, they aren't.
⚠ Common Mistakes
❌ I no am from Japan.→✅ I am not from Japan.
❌ Are you French? — Yes, I'm.→✅ Yes, I am. (never "Yes, I'm.")
❌ She is not from Brazil? (question)→✅ Is she from Brazil?
Activity — Choose the correct form
Click the correct option to complete each sentence.
Ana ___ from Brazil. She's Brazilian.
Robert and Lisa ___ from Mexico. They are American.
___ you from Celaya?
Are you French? — No, I ___.
Is Sofia Colombian? — Yes, she ___.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Key error to anticipate: "Yes, I'm" — reinforce that positive short answers NEVER use contractions.
  • After activity: student reads each corrected sentence aloud. Pronunciation + grammar in one step.
  • If student gets Q4 wrong: "Why not 'isn't'? — Because the subject is I, not she. 'isn't' = is not."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Negatives are just as important as positives. In professional emails, you often need to say what something is NOT."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "This same pattern — negative + question — works in every tense. You're building a permanent foundation."
§ 05
Reading
8–10 min
Carlos Mendoza
Project Coordinator · Celaya, GTO
My name is Carlos. I am from Mexico. I am Mexican. I am from Celaya, in the state of Guanajuato. My colleague Ana is not from Mexico. She is from Brazil. She is Brazilian. Her city is São Paulo. Our manager Robert is not Mexican and he is not Brazilian. He is American. He is from Chicago, in the United States. In our team, we are from three countries: Mexico, Brazil, and the United States. Are you from Mexico? Where are you from?
~80 words · A1.1 level
Comprehension
Read the text. Answer the questions.
1. Carlos is from Brazil.
2. Ana is Brazilian.
3. Robert is from Chicago.
How many countries are in the team?
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Highlighted words (kw, kw2, kw3) draw attention to the nationalities and countries — read them together first.
  • Ask student to find all negative forms in the text: "is not from Mexico", "is not Mexican", "is not Brazilian".
  • Reading aloud: student reads one sentence at a time. Teacher corrects pronunciation of nationality words only.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Does your team look like Carlos's team? How many countries are represented in your company?"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In your Moodle practice, you'll see this same text with an audio version — listen and follow along."

Hello! My name is Sofia. I am Colombian. I am from Bogotá.

Our manager is Tom. Tom is American. He is from New York, in the United States.

Tom is not Mexican. He is not Colombian. He is American.

In our team, we are international. We are from three countries: Colombia, Mexico, and the United States.

Are you Mexican? Where are you from? I am Sofia. I am Colombian. Nice to meet you!
Listening Activities
Listen, then answer.
The speaker's name is…
Sofia is from…
Tom is NOT…
How many countries in their team?
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Listen 1: just listen, no questions. Listen 2: attempt activities. Listen 3: verify if needed.
  • Q3 (Tom is NOT…): common error is "a manager" — students hear "manager" and click it. Remind: the question asks what he is NOT. He's not Mexican, not Colombian.
  • After activity: "Listen again and repeat every sentence after Sofia." Shadowing builds pronunciation.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Sofia speaks the same way your international colleagues will speak to you. This is real-world English."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Homework: listen to the audio 3 times before S3. "Each time you'll understand more — I guarantee it."

GT Originals
🌎 "I'm American" technically includes ALL people from the Americas — say "from the US" to be precise
🔊 "Colombia" NOT "Columbia" (that's a city in the USA)
🇬🇧 "British" = from the UK. "English" = from England only — not the same!
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Debate: this is lightly scaffolded — student only needs 3 sentences. Don't push for complex arguments.
  • Checklist first, then debate. Checklist functions as a self-assessment warm-down.
  • If debate goes well: challenge student to use one negative form ("International teams are better. National teams aren't as diverse.").
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You defended a position in English. That's a skill most people spend years developing."
  • End with: "What was your favorite part of this session?"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Testimonial trigger: "If your manager asked you 'How was your English class?', what would you say in English?"
  • Upsell: "In S3 you'll add jobs and professions — your complete professional introduction takes shape."
