§2
Speaking Topic — Describe Your Workplace
Activate prior knowledge · Real workplace context · Set up module theme
⏱ 8 MIN
Module 4 Theme
Places & Work
In Mexican and international companies across the Bajío, being able to describe your workspace and give directions is an essential daily skill. Today you start building that vocabulary — starting with what you already know about your own office.
Round 1 — 2 min
Describe your desk or workspace right now. What's on it? What's near it? Use simple words — even in Spanish if you need to.
💡 Starter: "On my desk there is/are…"
Round 2 — 3 min
With your partner, describe your company building. How many floors? What spaces are there? What's on each floor? Give your partner a "virtual tour."
💡 Starter: "In my company, there is/are… The [space] is on the [floor] floor."
Round 3 — 3 min
Tell your partner 3 places near your workplace. Are they useful for work? (Bank? Restaurant? Pharmacy?) Try to say if they're close or far.
💡 Starter: "Near my office there is a… It's [close / not far]."

Teacher model: "In my company, there is a reception on the ground floor and meeting rooms on the second floor. There are computers in every office. Near the building, there is a bank and a restaurant. The parking lot is behind the building."

📌 Point out: "there is" + singular, "there are" + plural. This is the core structure of today's session.

GT Original — Pronunciation Alert

§4
Colloquial — Everyday Office Phrases
Authentic language for navigating workplaces · WhatsApp English included
⏱ 5 MIN
"Where's the restroom?"
¿Dónde está el baño?
Use "restroom" in American English corporate contexts. BrE: "Where's the loo?" / "Where's the toilet?" — both acceptable. Very common with visitors.
"It's just around the corner."
Está justo a la vuelta.
Used to say something is very close. Natural response when giving directions. "The break room? It's just around the corner — turn left."
"Help yourself!"
¡Sírvete! / ¡Tómate lo que necesites!
Offer visitors or new colleagues to take something freely — coffee, snacks, a seat. Very warm and common in corporate hospitality settings.
"WFH today / BRB / In a meeting"
Trabajo desde casa / Regreso enseguida / En reunión
WhatsApp status phrases used daily in bilingual corporate environments in Celaya, Monterrey, Guadalajara. Set your status in English — it signals professionalism.
📌 Drill Note

Drill "Where's the restroom?" → "It's just around the corner." as a mini role-play. One student is a visitor, one is the host. Switch after 60 seconds. Then elicit: "Help yourself!" — have students offer the visitor a seat or coffee. This activates the colloquial in a realistic scenario before grammar work.

§5
Grammar — There is / There are
Affirmative · Negative · Questions · Prepositions of place · Any in negations
⏱ 15–18 MIN
🚨 Real Situation
A foreign supplier visits your plant in Celaya for the first time. He asks: "Is there a cafeteria? And where are the meeting rooms?"

You want to answer, but you don't know how to say "hay" or describe where things are in English.
❓ How do you say "Hay una cafetería en el tercer piso" — and how do you say "No hay estacionamiento"?
✅ The Tool: There is / There are

"Yes, there is a cafeteria — it's on the third floor. There are meeting rooms on the second floor."

Singular: There is a cafeteria / a meeting room / an elevator
Plural: There are chairs / computers / meeting rooms

FormStructureExample
Affirmative (singular) There is + a/an + noun There is a cafeteria on the third floor.
Affirmative (plural) There are + noun(s) There are three meeting rooms upstairs.
Negative (singular) There isn't a/an + noun There isn't a gym in this building.
Negative (plural) There aren't any + noun There aren't any chairs in the corridor.
Question (singular) Is there a/an + noun? Is there a printer near here?
Question (plural) Are there any + noun? Are there any computers in that room?
Short answers Yes, there is/are · No, there isn't/aren't "Is there a bank?" — "Yes, there is."
⚠️ Top errors — correct every time:
✗ "There is chairs" → ✅ There are chairs (plural!)
✗ "There are a printer" → ✅ There is a printer (singular!)
✗ "Are there a cafeteria?" → ✅ Is there a cafeteria? (singular = is)
✗ "There aren't chairs" → ✅ There aren't any chairs (any in negations + plurals)

Listening — The New Colleague Tour
A new employee gets a tour of the company · There is/are + prepositions throughout
⏱ 10–12 MIN
The New Colleague Tour — Solaris Manufacturing, Celaya
A1.1 · M4 · S1A · Voice: Sarah (American English) · Speed: 0.78
Good morning! Welcome to Solaris Manufacturing. My name is Ana, and I'm your guide for today. Let me show you around!

OK, so we are on the ground floor. There is a reception here, right at the entrance. There are security desks on both sides. Please always show your ID badge when you enter.

There is an elevator next to the reception. It goes up to the fourth floor. There are stairs behind the elevator too, if you prefer.

The cafeteria is on the third floor. There are tables for about eighty people! There is a kitchen and a coffee machine. Help yourself to coffee in the mornings.

On the second floor, there are three meeting rooms. Meeting Room A is next to the elevator. Meeting Room B is in front of it. And Meeting Room C is at the end of the corridor.

There isn't a gym in the building, but there is a break room on every floor. There aren't any vending machines on the first floor, but there are some on the second and third floors.

And outside — there is a parking lot behind the building. It's free for employees! There is also a small park in front of the entrance — perfect for lunch in good weather.

Any questions about where things are? Great — let's go up to your new office!

§8
Wrap-Up — What I Learned Today
Consolidate · Check progress · My Win Today
⏱ 5 MIN
01
There IS vs There ARE. Singular = IS. Plural = ARE. Simple rule, huge impact. Your manager will notice the difference immediately.
02
ANY in negations and questions. "There aren't ANY chairs." "Are there ANY computers?" — "any" signals you're looking for something you're not sure about.
03
Short answers are enough. "Is there a cafeteria?" → "Yes, there is." You don't need to repeat the whole sentence. Clean, professional, correct.

3 Phrases You Can Use at Work This Week
Starting tomorrow — use these in real situations
Phrase 1
"There is a meeting room on the [floor] floor."
Use when a visitor or new colleague asks where to go. It's precise, professional, and 100% correct. Much better than just pointing!
Phrase 2
"Is there a [bank / pharmacy / restaurant] near here?"
Ask this when you're in a new area or guiding international visitors. It's the most natural way to ask about nearby places — and it shows your English is real.
Phrase 3
"There isn't a [space] here, but there is a [alternative]."
Use this when someone asks for something you don't have but you can offer an alternative. "There isn't a gym, but there is a break room." This sounds friendly and professional.
