Hot Take — 60 Seconds, No Notes
⏱ 5 min · Imperatives + Object Pronouns at speed
📋 Logistics

This is a pressure activity. 60 seconds per student — no pauses, no notes. Goal: automatic production of imperatives + object pronouns, NOT perfection. Count every object pronoun used. 4+ in 60 seconds = badge 🔥 earned.

🎯 Pedagogical Focus

If student freezes: say "I'll meet…" — that single prompt should restart them. The imperative verbs from S4A must be automatic now. Monitor for: "Go to straight" (wrong) vs "Go straight" (correct), and "Call to me" (wrong) vs "Call me" (correct).

💡 Upsell

Tell students: "In S4B you don't study — you perform. Everything you learned in S4A is now the tool. The goal is fluency in real situations." This reframes the session as a showcase, not a test.

🚨 Scenario — 11:45 PM, GTO Airport
Your VIP client from Germany just landed. He has his luggage. He calls you in a panic — his phone battery is at 2%. You have 60 seconds to give him complete instructions: from baggage claim to your car outside at ground transportation. After that, his phone dies.
Go. Use imperatives. Use object pronouns. Don't stop.

Debate — Business Class vs Economy
⏱ 10 min · Object pronouns in arguments · Debate meter
📋 Logistics

Assign sides randomly. 90 seconds per side, then switch. Debate meter: teacher clicks Agree/Disagree after each argument. Winner: most object pronouns used correctly + strongest argument. 2 rounds per student.

🎯 Pedagogical Focus

Every argument MUST include at least one object pronoun. If student argues without one: "Say that again — but replace the noun with him/her/it/them." This forces real-time self-correction.

💡 Corporate Link

This debate is 100% relevant: many companies in Bajío sector have expense policies for business vs economy. Students who can argue this in English can participate in real procurement conversations.

Your company sends clients and staff on business trips. Which seat class should they book — and why?

✈️ SIDE A — Business Class

Clients deserve comfort. Tired executives don't perform well. The extra cost is worth it.

  • "Book it for them — they fly 10 hours. They need it."
  • "Show them respect — our clients expect it."
  • "Give them a comfortable seat and they'll trust us more."
💺 SIDE B — Economy

Economy is professional too. Save the company money. Use the savings to improve services.

  • "Book it in economy — the company needs to save it for other things."
  • "Tell them economy is fine — a 2-hour flight doesn't need it."
  • "Use the money — invest it in better products for them."

Relay — Airport Instruction Board
⏱ 8 min · 5 real situations · Transform & reveal
📋 Logistics

5 relay items — each shows a Spanish situation. Student must give the English instruction in 15 seconds. Then click "Reveal" to compare. Run as a relay race: Partner A does 1, 3, 5 · Partner B does 2, 4. Timer pressure optional — use the 90s timer from §2.

🎯 Pedagogical Focus

Each situation requires: imperative verb + object pronoun. If student uses subject pronoun instead ("call he" instead of "call him") — correct immediately and repeat the item. Pattern drilling disguised as relay.

💡 Badge Rule

Badge 🎤 unlocks when student completes all 5 relay items with at least 3 correct object pronouns. Teacher tracks — student self-reports at the end of the relay.

Each situation is in Spanish. Transform it into English — use an imperative and an object pronoun. Then click Reveal to compare.

🛫
Airport Instruction Relay
5 situations · Transform · Reveal · Compare
RELAY 1
🇲🇽 "Espérame en la sala de llegadas. Llegaré en 5 minutos." → Say this in English to your client.
Must include: imperative + me
"Wait for me in the arrivals hall. I'll be there in 5 minutes." — ✅ Imperative "wait for" + object pronoun "me"
RELAY 2
🇲🇽 "Recógela en el área de maletas — la vuelta ya aterrizó." → Tell the driver to pick up your colleague (female).
Must include: imperative + her
"Pick her up at baggage claim — her flight already landed." — ✅ "Pick her up" is one phrasal imperative
RELAY 3
🇲🇽 "No pierdas tu pase de abordar — lo vas a necesitar en el filtro de seguridad." → Warn a colleague.
Must include: negative imperative + it (twice)
"Don't lose your boarding pass — you'll need it at security. Keep it in your hand." — ✅ Negative imperative + "it" used twice
RELAY 4
🇲🇽 "Lleva a los clientes al área de transporte terrestre — el chofer los está esperando allá." → Tell your assistant.
Must include: imperative + them
"Take them to ground transportation — the driver is waiting for them there." — ✅ "Take them" + "for them" = 2 object pronouns
RELAY 5
🇲🇽 "¿Cuánto tiempo tarda en llegar a nuestra oficina desde el aeropuerto?" → Client asks you this question. Answer AND ask back.
Must include: "How long" + "It takes" + you / us
"It takes us about 40 minutes by car. — It's a bit of a walk to the parking area, but I'll walk with you." — ✅ "It takes us" + "with you"

Speed Round — Airport Flash Cards
⏱ 6 min · 10 cards · Click to reveal answer · Score yourself
📋 Logistics

10 flash cards. Student says the answer aloud BEFORE clicking. Click to reveal. Self-score: +1 if correct, 0 if not. No penalty for wrong — this builds risk-taking. Target: 8/10. Score above 8 = add +10 leaderboard points.

🎯 Pedagogical Focus

Speed is the goal — not perfection. This round targets automaticity: the student should not need to think about whether to use "him" or "he" at this point in the session. If they hesitate: back to basics in next session.

💡 Competitive Option

Run as class race: first student to answer all 10 correctly wins 5 leaderboard points. Adds energy without pressure on individual errors.

1. "Avísame cuando llegues." → In English with an object pronoun:
"Let me know when you arrive." ✅
2. Where do passengers collect their suitcases?
Baggage claim ✅
3. Your client is a woman. She's at Gate B3. How do you text her: "I'll meet ___ at baggage claim"?
"I'll meet her at baggage claim." ✅
4. "¿Cuánto tiempo tarda?" — Full question in English:
"How long does it take?" ✅
5. Two clients just landed. Tell the driver: "Go pick ___ up."
"Go pick them up." ✅
6. "La sala de llegadas" in English:
The arrivals hall ✅
7. How do you say "no puedes perderte" in casual English?
"You can't miss it." ✅
8. "Saca tu laptop de tu bolsa" — imperativa negativa equivalente:
"Take your laptop out of your bag." / "Don't leave it inside." ✅
9. "Es un poco de camino" — colloquial English phrase:
"It's a bit of a walk." ✅
10. "I have three luggages." — What's wrong? Give the correct sentence.
"Luggage is uncountable! → I have a lot of luggage." ✅

Solo Challenge — Full Airport Phone Call
⏱ 8 min · 2 minutes · No notes · No help · Imperatives + pronouns throughout
📋 Logistics

Individual performance. 120-second arc timer. Student plays Mónica Herrera — guiding a client from the moment the plane lands to the car. No interruptions. Teacher only marks errors on paper — correct after timer stops.

🎯 Pedagogical Focus

Success criteria: 5 imperatives + 4 object pronouns + at least 1 "How long does it take" + at least 1 colloquial phrase. Note which criteria students miss — that's S5 content for review.

💡 Evidence for Corporate Clients

This is the moment to record (with student permission). 2 minutes of fluent business airport English is concrete evidence of A1.2 progress. HR departments at Bajío manufacturers respond to audio samples — it's worth capturing.

🎤 YOU ARE MÓNICA HERRERA

Your client James just called. He landed 5 minutes ago, has his luggage, and is somewhere near security. Guide him all the way to your car. In 2 minutes. Cover:

  • Where he is now + what to do first (imperative + object pronoun)
  • Step-by-step directions to ground transportation (4 imperatives minimum)
  • How long it will take him to reach you
  • What he'll see when he gets there (use "you can't miss it")
  • A warm closing line

Source Analysis — Real Airport Messages
⏱ 8 min · 4 authentic texts · Find the grammar · Metacognition
📋 Logistics

4 real-world texts (WhatsApp, airline announcement, email, hotel message). Students read and answer the metacognitive question about grammar. No right/wrong — this is critical analysis. Badge 🔍 when student completes all 4.

🎯 Pedagogical Focus

The metacognitive question is more important than the reading. "Why did they choose 'it' here?" forces conscious grammar awareness — the final step before automaticity. Don't rush this section.

💡 Colloquial Note

Source 2 contains "Ladies and gents" (informal airline). Use this to contrast: "Ladies and gentlemen" (formal) vs "Ladies and gents" (conversational). A small but memorable distinction for A1.2 students.

💬 WhatsApp — Colleague Message
Hey! I just landed. I have my luggage. Where are you? — I'm waiting for you in arrivals. Walk past the pharmacy and follow the signs for exits. You can't miss me — I have a blue ConnectPro jacket. Let me know when you see me!
❓ Count the object pronouns: how many are there? Which verb + pronoun pattern appears most often?
📢 Airline Announcement — Boarding Call
Ladies and gentlemen, we are now boarding Flight AM492 to Monterrey. Please have your boarding pass and ID ready. Show them to the agent at the gate. Don't place your carry-on luggage in the aisle — put it in the overhead bin. We'll ask you to take your seat as quickly as possible. Thank you for flying with us.
❓ How many different object pronouns appear? Circle each one. Why does the announcement use "them" for "boarding pass and ID"?
📧 Email — Client Arrival Confirmation
Dear James, We are confirming your arrival at BJX Airport on Friday at 18:30. Our driver will be there to meet you at arrivals — look for him, he'll have a ConnectPro sign. Call me if you have any problems finding him. It takes about 45 minutes to get you to our offices. We look forward to seeing you. — Mónica
❓ "him" appears twice — both refer to the driver. Why does good writing use "him" instead of repeating "the driver"? What rule is this?
📱 Hotel Text — Automated Message
Welcome, Mr. Weber! Your room at Hotel Ejecutivo is ready. Take the shuttle from ground transportation — tell the driver we sent you. Call us if you need any help. It takes you about 12 minutes to arrive. We'll be waiting for you. You can't miss us — the hotel sign is very large.
❓ This text uses "us" three times — who is "us"? And "you" appears four times — is it always the same person? What grammar point links all four texts?

Self-Rating & Class Leaderboard
⏱ 5 min · 3 sliders · Dynamic feedback · Rankings
📋 Logistics

3 sliders — students self-rate 1–10 on: object pronoun accuracy / airport vocabulary / speaking fluency. Feedback generates dynamically based on average. Leaderboard: teacher adds names + session points (speed round score × 5 + relay points). Visible to class on main screen.

🎯 Pedagogical Focus

Self-rating is NOT teacher assessment. Never comment publicly on scores. If student rates themselves low, ask privately: "Which part felt hardest?" → use for S5 review planning. Students who rate themselves high AND performed well get S5 preview homework.

💡 Upsell Moment

After leaderboard: "In S5 — Module 3 Exit — you will present a full business trip from hotel check-in to airport navigation in front of the class. Start thinking about your real travel story." This creates anticipation and homework motivation without formal homework assignment.
