Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • "headquarters" — always plural in form but singular in meaning: "The headquarters IS in Chicago" (not "are"). Common error at A2.2.
  • "merge" — note the example uses Simple Past ("they merged") — it was a specific event. But the resulting CULTURE change = "used to" territory.
  • "workplace culture would change" — first appearance of "would" for past habit in the examples. Don't explain yet — just let the student read it. Address in §4.
  • Production task: student uses each word in a true sentence about their OWN company — past or present.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Does your company outsource anything now? Did it use to do it internally? What changed?" — drives authentic production with 3 of today's vocab words in one question.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "'Expand, merge, outsource' — these three verbs appear in every business news article about company transformation. Reading business English becomes much easier once these are automatic."

§ 04
Grammar — Would for Past Habits
12–15 min
Would — Formal Alternative to "Used To"
FormUseCompany example
used to Past habit OR past state — no longer true. Works for both actions and states. "The company used to be family-owned." (state)
"We used to meet every Monday." (action)
would Past repeated action only — more formal register. Cannot describe states. "We would meet every Monday." ✅ (action)
"The company would be family-owned." ✗ (state — wrong)
would ✗ Never use "would" for past states: be · have · feel · believe · know · seem "The HQ would be in Chicago." ✗
→ "The HQ used to be in Chicago." ✅
When "would" and "used to" are both possible:
✅ "Every quarter, the directors used to present results to the board." (habit = used to)
✅ "Every quarter, the directors would present results to the board." (habit = would — more formal)

When only "used to" works:
✅ "The company used to be a family business." (state: "be")
✗ "The company would be a family business." (state: cannot use "would")
The quick test for "would": Can you add "every day / every week / regularly / always"? And is it an action verb (not be/have/feel)?
"They would review the strategy every quarter." → ✅ repeated action → "would" works.
"The culture would be very formal." → ❌ "be" = state → only "used to" works.
⚠ Common Errors — S3
❌ "The company would be very small before."→✅ The company used to be very small. ("be" = state, not action)
❌ "I would have a different boss." (state: have)→✅ I used to have a different boss. ("have" = state here)
❌ "Would you use to travel for work?" (question form)→✅ Did you use to travel for work? ("would" doesn't form questions this way)
❌ "The team would hold meetings last month." (specific time)→✅ The team held meetings last month. (specific → Simple Past)
Activity 1 — Used To, Would, or Neither?
Choose the correct option for each sentence.
Every morning, the team ___ gather for a quick briefing before starting work.
The headquarters ___ a small office on the outskirts of the city.
The company ___ with three competitors in 2019 — now it's the market leader.
The leadership ___ have a very formal management style — no flexibility.
Directors ___ present quarterly results to the full company — it was a company tradition.
Activity 2 — True or False: Would Rules
Is this statement about the grammar correct?
"Would" can replace "used to" in all cases — they are always interchangeable.
"Would" cannot be used with state verbs like be, have, feel, or know to express past habits.
"Would" for past habits is more formal than "used to" and is often found in business writing.
"The company would merge in 2019" is a correct use of "would" for past habits.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Q4: "leadership used to have a formal style" — "have" here is a state (possession of a quality). "Would have" is also acceptable in some contexts but sounds unnatural here. Teach: when in doubt about states, default to "used to".
  • T4: "would merge in 2019" — specific date = Simple Past, not a habit. "Would" for past habits requires a repeated pattern, not a one-time event. Reinforce the S2 rule: specific date → Simple Past.
  • After activities: oral practice — teacher describes a past company situation, student must say whether "would" is possible or not, and produce the sentence.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Restate one sentence from Q1-Q5 but about YOUR company — using 'would' if possible."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In B1 business writing, 'would' appears frequently in company histories: 'The CEO would review all proposals personally before the restructuring.' You now have this form."
§ 05
Reading — A Company That Changed Everything
8–10 min
Sofía Ramírez
Director of Transformation · Grupo Alante · Dallas
Magazine Interview
Watch for: used to (orange) · would (violet) · Simple Past events (cyan) · present contrast (green)

Q: Grupo Alante has changed significantly in the last decade. What was the company like before?

When I joined in 2012, it was a completely different organization. The company used to be a family business with one headquarters and about 80 employees. We would meet every Monday morning in a single conference room — all departments together. The director would walk through each team's results personally. It was very personal, very traditional.

Q: What were the biggest habits or policies that changed?

The leadership used to have a very hierarchical style — every decision went to the top. Employees didn't use to make independent decisions. The strategy used to focus exclusively on the local market. We would outsource almost nothing — everything was done in-house.

Q: What caused the transformation?

The company merged with a US firm in 2016. That single event changed everything. We expanded from one city to twelve countries in four years. The headquarters moved to Dallas. The leadership style shifted — suddenly people had autonomy and responsibility at every level.

Q: What does the company look like now?

Nowadays, we have a remote work policy that didn't use to exist. We outsource our IT and logistics. Leadership is distributed — I lead a team of directors across three continents. We expand into a new market every 18 months. The strategy that used to be local is now global.

Q: What do you miss about the old company?

Honestly? I miss the simplicity. The team would have lunch together every Friday. We all knew each other by name. Now we have 4,000 employees. You used to feel the company was a family. These days, it feels more like a corporation. Both have their advantages.

Comprehension — Used To, Would & Simple Past
Answer using information from the interview.
What did the director use to do every Monday at Grupo Alante?
What specific event started the company's transformation?
"The team would have lunch together every Friday" — "would" is used correctly here because it describes a repeated action.
The company used to have a very decentralized, autonomous leadership style.
Nowadays Grupo Alante outsources its IT and logistics.
GT Originals — Would in Business Writing
"The director would walk through each team's results personally" — this is the kind of sentence that appears in company annual reports, executive biographies, and business case studies. It's more formal and literary than "used to walk". At A2.2, introducing "would" makes students sound significantly more sophisticated in written English — even with the same meaning.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Color-coded grammar: orange = used to, violet = would, cyan = Simple Past events, green = present contrast. Ask student: "How many 'would' examples are in the text?" (Answer: would meet, would walk, would outsource, would have = 4)
  • All 4 "would" examples describe repeated actions — never states. Point this out explicitly after reading.
  • RQ2 tests S2 grammar: "The merger with a US firm in 2016" = specific event = Simple Past, NOT used to. If student says "the company used to expand" — address: expand was the result, the merger was the event.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Has your company ever had a major transformation like Grupo Alante? What used to be different? What's the same? Was there a specific event that changed everything?"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Sofía's interview is the template for §7's role-play. You'll describe YOUR company's transformation using the same structure — with used to, would, and Simple Past for the change event."

▶ Show ElevenLabs Script

I joined this company fifteen years ago, when it was a very different place. Back then, it used to be a small, family-owned business. We would all meet in the same room every Monday morning. The CEO would personally review every major decision. It used to feel like a team of friends, not a corporation.

The workplace used to be very traditional. Employees would arrive at eight, leave at five, and there was no flexibility. There didn't use to be any remote work policy. The strategy used to focus on one product line and one market — the local region.

Then the company merged with an American group in 2017. That changed everything. We expanded from 150 employees to over 2,000. The headquarters moved to Dallas. The leadership structure changed completely.

Nowadays, teams would no longer all fit in one room. We outsource a lot of our operations. People work from different cities, different countries. The strategy is now global.

Do I miss the old way? Sometimes. I used to know everyone's name. We would celebrate birthdays in the office, share lunch, talk about our families. These days, I manage people I've never met in person. It's efficient — but it's different. You used to feel a sense of belonging that's harder to find now.

⏱ Target: 85–95 sec · Voice: Sarah · Speed: 0.84 · City: Dallas · Mix of used to + would

Listening Activities — Used To, Would & Simple Past
Listen carefully — identify the grammar form, not just the content.
What would the CEO do personally before the transformation?
What was the specific event that started the transformation? (Simple Past)
Before the transformation, there used to be a flexible remote work policy.
The speaker says they would celebrate birthdays together in the office before.
The speaker feels completely positive about the new way of working.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Focus task: count how many times "would" appears (would meet, would review, would celebrate, would all fit, would no longer). Answer: 5 instances. Count "used to" separately: used to be x2, used to feel x2, used to focus, didn't use to, used to know = 7 instances.
  • LQ2 directly tests S2 and S3 grammar together: the merger = specific event → Simple Past. If student says "would move" — that's a specific event, not a habit. Reinforce.
  • L3: speaker says "it's efficient — but it's different" and "a sense of belonging that's harder to find now." Not completely positive — nuanced. Students should pick up the ambivalence.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Do you relate to this speaker? Does your company feel more like a family or more like a corporation? Use 'used to' to describe how it was before."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Homework: listen 3× and write every "would" sentence and every "used to" sentence in two separate columns. "This is also writing practice — you're producing the grammar by transcribing it."
§ 07
Wrap-Up
5 min
Today I learned…
  • expand · merge · outsource · headquarters · workplace · leadership · policy · strategy.
  • "Would" = formal alternative to "used to" for repeated ACTIONS in the past.
  • States (be, have, feel) can only use "used to" — never "would".
  • Company change events = Simple Past (merged, expanded, moved, shifted).
  • I can describe a company transformation using all three forms.
Key Summary
Actions → both
used to / would meet · review · present · outsource
States → used to only
used to be · used to have · used to feel — never "would be"
Change events
merged · expanded · moved · shifted → Simple Past

GT Originals
The would rule in one sentence: "Would" replaces "used to" only when the verb describes a repeated HABIT or ACTION — never a state. If you're uncertain, "used to" is always safe.

Practical application: In company annual reports and executive presentations, "would" creates a more authoritative, literary tone. "The board would review all proposals annually" sounds more formal than "used to review" — same meaning, different register.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Role-play: teacher card hidden in Student Mode. The 5 journalist questions map directly to the 5 lines in the student's response scaffold — but student shouldn't read them during the role-play.
  • Evaluate: at least 2 correct "would" uses (action verbs, no states)? "used to be/have/feel" for states? Simple Past for the change event? If student uses "would be" — stop and correct immediately.
  • The role-play can be based on real information (student's actual company) or a fictional company they invent.
