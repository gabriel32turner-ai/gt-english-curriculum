§2
Speaking Topic — Describe Your Workspace Precisely
From "it's there" to "it's next to the printer" · Real precision · Pair work
⏱ 8 MIN
S2 Theme — Precision
Where exactly is it?
In Mexican manufacturing and corporate environments, being able to say exactly where something is — not just "over there" — is a critical skill. "The documents are on the desk next to the printer" is what separates functional English from fluent English at A1.1.
Round 1 — 2 min
Describe 4 objects on or near your desk. Where exactly is each one? Your partner draws a quick map of what you describe.
💡 Use: "My [object] is on / next to / under / in front of my desk."
Round 2 — 3 min
Partner A describes 3 things using "This is my…" (close) and "That is the…" (far). Partner B points to the correct object. Switch after 90 seconds.
💡 "This is my pen — it's next to my notebook. That is the printer — it's behind the desk."
Round 3 — 3 min
Think of one object in your office that's always in the wrong place. Where is it usually? Where should it be? Describe both locations using prepositions.
💡 "The [object] is usually between… but it should be next to / in front of…"

Teacher model: "On my desk, there is a laptop. My notebook is next to the laptop, on the left. Under the desk, there is a bag. The printer is behind me — it's between the cabinet and the window. This is my pen. That is the stapler — it's in front of the monitor."

📌 Key structures: on / next to / under / behind / between / in front of — all seven will be drilled in §5.

§4
Colloquial — Asking Where Things Are
Natural phrases for locating things · Office small talk · This/that in casual use
⏱ 5 MIN
"Do you know where the [thing] is?"
¿Sabes dónde está [la cosa]?
More polite than just "Where is the [thing]?" Use this with colleagues or managers when you're not 100% sure they know the answer. Natural in any corporate environment.
"It's right here / over there."
Está justo aquí / allá.
"Right here" = very close, I'm pointing at it. "Over there" = in that direction, further away. Both are extremely natural and informal — perfect for office daily use.
"Can you check behind / under the [thing]?"
¿Puedes revisar detrás / debajo de [cosa]?
Uses prepositions of place in a real request. Very common when something is lost or misplaced. Notice: "Can you check behind the printer?" = preposition in a real sentence.
"What's that over there?"
¿Qué es eso de allá?
"That" points to something far from the speaker. Contrast: "What's this?" (I'm holding it) vs "What's that?" (I can see it but it's not near me). This/that distinction is today's grammar point.
📌 Drill Note

Do a quick round: teacher holds up an object (close) → students say "This is a…". Teacher points far (at the board, the door) → students say "That is the…". Repeat 6-8 times. Then pairs practice: one partner hides an object, the other asks "Do you know where the [thing] is?" — reply with a preposition phrase. 2 minutes total.

Grammar — Prepositions of Place + This / That / These / Those
in · on · under · next to · in front of · behind · between · this/that/these/those
⏱ 15–18 MIN
🚨 Real Situation
Your Chicago colleague calls and asks: "Where are the signed contracts? I can't find them in the shared drive."

You walk to the physical office and see them. You want to say: "Están en el cajón debajo de la impresora, entre los folders azules."
❓ How do you describe exactly where something is in English? You need more than just "there is" — you need precision words.
✅ The Tools: Prepositions of Place

"The contracts are in the drawer, under the printer, between the blue folders."

Location = Subject + to be + preposition + object
"The stapler is on the desk." · "The folders are in the cabinet." · "The bin is under my desk."

PrepositionMeaningOffice example⚠️ Watch out
inInside a container or spaceThe pen is in the drawer.in a room · in a box · in a bag
onOn top of / touching a surfaceThe laptop is on the desk.on the wall · on the floor · on the shelf
underBelow, not touchingThe bin is under the desk.NOT "below the desk" in common speech
next toBeside, very closeThe printer is next to the door."beside" = same meaning, more formal
in front ofFacing you, on this sideThe chair is in front of the desk.3 words — always "in front OF"
behindAt the back, the other sideThe whiteboard is behind me.opposite of "in front of"
betweenIn the middle of two thingsThe monitor is between the speakers.always "between X and Y"
THIS / THAT / THESE / THOSE
WordSingular/PluralDistanceExample
thisSingularClose to me 📍This is my keyboard.
thatSingularFar from me 📍→That is the printer.
thesePluralClose to me 📍📍These are my folders.
thosePluralFar from me 📍→📍→Those are the client files.
⚠️ Top errors — correct every time:
✗ "The pen is in front the desk" → ✅ in front of the desk (never forget "of"!)
✗ "It's on the desk next to of the monitor" → ✅ next to [no "of"] the monitor
✗ "The papers are between the printer and the door of the right" → ✅ between the printer and the door (on the right)
✗ "This are my folders" → ✅ These are my folders (plural = these/those)

§6
Listening — The Lost Contract
Ana helps a colleague find a missing document · Prepositions of place throughout
⏱ 10–12 MIN
The Lost Contract — Solaris Manufacturing Office
A1.1 · M4 · S2A · Voice: Sarah (American English) · Speed: 0.78
Carlos: Ana, do you know where the Martinez contract is? I can't find it.

Ana: Did you check the cabinet next to the printer? I put some folders in there yesterday.

Carlos: Yes, I checked. It's not in the cabinet. I looked under all the papers on my desk too.

Ana: Hmm. Is it in your drawer? The one under your laptop?

Carlos: Let me check… No, there are only pens and a stapler in there.

Ana: OK, what about the shelf behind your desk? On the left, between the red binders and the legal folders?

Carlos: Oh! Yes! I can see a white folder over there. That folder — the one on the right side of the shelf.

Ana: Is that it?

Carlos: Yes! The Martinez contract is right here — it was between the red binder and my notebook all along. Thank you, Ana!

Ana: No problem! Next time, put it in the cabinet. In front of my desk, in the blue folder. That's where all the client contracts go.

Carlos: Got it. The blue folder, in front of your desk, in the cabinet. Perfect. Thanks again!

Question 1 — Treasure Hunt
Partner A hides a small object somewhere in the classroom or office. Partner B asks: "Is it on / under / next to / behind / in front of / between [something]?" Only Yes or No answers! Find it in 5 questions maximum.
💡 Practice both asking (Are there? / Is it?) and answering (Yes, it is! / No, it isn't.)
Question 2 — Describe Your Desk
Without your partner looking, describe exactly where 5 objects are on your desk or in your workspace. Partner listens and draws a map. Then compare! How accurate was the description?
💡 Every sentence must have a preposition: "My [object] is [prep] the [reference]."
Question 3 — This, That, These, Those
Point at things around the room. Close = "This is my… / These are my…". Far = "That is the… / Those are the…". Your partner corrects any errors with this/that/these/those. Do 6 objects each.
💡 Challenge: combine both — "This is my notebook. It's on my desk, next to my keyboard."
Question 4 — Help Me Find It!
Role-play: you are a new employee. Your colleague asks you to find 3 things in the office (printer, the client folder, the key for the cabinet). Describe where each thing is using full sentences. Your partner checks you use prepositions correctly.
💡 "The printer is next to the door, behind the reception desk." — full sentences always!
Question 5 — Phone Call: Where Is It?
Simulate a phone call (or Teams call). Partner A is in the office. Partner B is working from home and needs to find something. Partner A describes where it is using prepositions — Partner B must understand without seeing. Switch after 2 minutes.
💡 "It's on my desk, in front of the monitor, next to the stapler." — Be precise!
Question 6 — 60s Precision Challenge ⭐
In 60 seconds, describe 6 objects in your real workspace using all 7 prepositions (in, on, under, next to, in front of, behind, between) at least once each. No repeating the same preposition twice. Partner counts and tracks.
💡 Goal: use all 7 in 60 seconds. Teacher tallies on the board. Class record!

3 Phrases You Can Use at Work This Week
Starting tomorrow — in emails, calls or face-to-face
Phrase 1
"It's in the cabinet next to the printer."
Use when a colleague or visitor asks where a document or object is. Combines "in", "next to" and a real office object in one natural sentence. Say it once — they'll know you're fluent.
Phrase 2
"The [document] is on my desk, between the [A] and the [B]."
Perfect for Teams or phone calls when someone asks where to find something. Two prepositions in one sentence — sounds completely natural and professional at A1.1.
Phrase 3
"This is my [object] — that is the [other object] over there."
Use when showing someone around your workspace for the first time — a new colleague, a visitor, or during a virtual tour on Zoom. This/that contrast is immediately noticeable and impressive at A1.
