§ 02
⚔️ Pitch Battle — Defend Your Side
12 min
Format: Teacher assigns a side. Student MUST defend it — even if they personally disagree. 90 seconds to open. Teacher challenges once. Student responds 60 seconds. Then switch sides.
✅ Side A — Side Hustle Is the Future
Financial reality. One salary is no longer enough to build real security. The data supports it.
Skill development. People have told us that managing clients has taught them more than 5 years of corporate life.
Identity beyond a title. Studies have mentioned that people with side income report higher life satisfaction.
The gig economy is growing. Companies have said they prefer freelance talent for specialized work — that trend is accelerating.
❌ Side B — The Side Hustle Myth
Burnout is real. Most people who tried have mentioned they had been exhausted within 6 months of managing both.
It glorifies overwork. The system has told us that working extra hours is ambition — but it's exploitation rebranded.
Not everyone can. Someone once said that side hustles are a privilege — you need time, capital, and skills most people don't have.
Corporate energy is finite. Research has shown that people who side hustle perform worse in their primary role over time.
1:30
⏱ 90 sec · Opening Pitch
Defend your assigned side — RS required throughout
Press START when student begins their opening
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • The pitch cards are pre-loaded with RS — point this out after: "Studies have mentioned / People have told us / Someone once said / Research has shown." The student should use these as models and build their own RS around them.
  • Teacher challenge prompts: "But someone told you that burnout was the main risk — how do you respond to that?" / "He mentioned that most side hustles fail within a year. What do you say?" Forces live RS production under debate pressure.
  • Key error to track: did they use RS with tense shift, or did they just use direct speech? "Studies say" vs "Studies have said / mentioned." The latter is B1.2.

🔄 RS Relay — Convert & Report
10 min
Format: Each item has a direct speech quote. Student converts it to Reported Speech immediately — no preparation, no notes. Click ✓ when done. Badge 🔄 unlocks when all 6 are checked.
1
"I am building my first freelance platform — it's been 6 months."
→ Report using told me. Apply full tense shift. Add a colloquial phrase at the end.
✓
2
"I have been trying to scale my side hustle for a year — but the burnout is real."
→ Report using mentioned. Perfect Continuous → Past Perfect Continuous. Minimum 2 sentences.
✓
3
"I will quit my corporate job as soon as my revenue reaches $20,000 pesos a month."
→ Report using said. Will → Would. Add your reaction to what they said.
✓
4
"Are you happy with your current income? Wouldn't you want to build something of your own?"
→ Report using asked me. Convert both questions. Use: if / whether.
✓
5
"I'm not going to keep managing clients and a full-time job. One of them has to go."
→ Report using told me. Present Continuous + going to → both shift. Add: what did you think when you heard that?
✓
6
"My passion project has already generated more joy than ten years of corporate work."
→ Report using mentioned. Present Perfect → Past Perfect. This one is yours: make it sound natural and add your own reaction.
✓
RS Relay: 0 / 6 completed
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This is the core grammar production test. Items 2 and 4 are the hardest — Present Perfect Continuous shift and question conversion. If the student fails item 2, address it immediately: "had been trying" is the target form. One correction, one retry.
  • The "add your reaction" instruction in items 3, 5, 6 forces the student beyond mechanical conversion into genuine opinion production. That's B1.2 — grammar serving communication, not grammar as an end in itself.
  • Target pace: 90 seconds per relay item. 6 items = 9 minutes. Move fast.

🎤 Solo Challenge — 3 Minutes · No Notes
10 min
🎤 B1.2 Mastery Standard — 3 Minutes · No Notes · No Prompts
"Report a real conversation about money, work, or ambition you had this week."
At least 4 RS reporting verbs · Full tense shift on every instance · 6+ vocabulary words from S3A · 2 colloquial phrases · No notes · No support. This is B1.2.
1
Open: "This week, a [colleague / friend / family member] told me that..." — set the context in RS immediately.
2
Develop: report 2–3 exchanges from the conversation. Use said / told / mentioned / asked — vary the verbs.
3
Analyse: "What they said made me think about..." — add your own position using topic vocabulary.
4
Close: "Honestly — no way / it adds up / not gonna happen" — end with a colloquial phrase + your real conclusion.

§ 06
🌐 Gig Economy Debate — Report, React, Defend
10 min
Format: Teacher reads one "expert claim." Student reports it in RS, then agrees or disagrees with evidence. No notes. Real vocabulary. Natural pace.
Expert Claim 1
"Researchers have found that workers with multiple income streams report 40% higher financial resilience than those with a single employer."
→ Report this claim in RS. Then agree or disagree: does financial resilience justify the burnout risk?
Expert Claim 2
"The ILO has stated that the gig economy has created new forms of precariousness — freelancers have no sick pay, no pension, and no legal protection in most countries."
→ Report this in RS (the ILO has stated / has said / mentioned). Does this change your view on going freelance?
Expert Claim 3 — México Context
"INEGI has reported that over 55% of Mexico's working population generates some form of informal income alongside formal employment."
→ Report + react. Is Mexico's informal economy a side hustle — or something different? What has this data told you about your own country?
1:30
⏱ 90 sec · Per claim
Report → agree or disagree → evidence
Press START for each claim
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Expert claims are the most advanced RS format — reporting third-party data with credibility markers. "Researchers have found / The ILO has stated / INEGI has reported" — these are journalistic RS structures that B1.2 students need for professional English.
  • Claim 3 (INEGI / México) is the emotional peak. Most students will have a strong personal reaction. Let it run — authentic outrage or pride generates the most natural language.
