Hot Take — Hotel Emergency
60 seconds to respond · Object pronouns under pressure · No notes allowed
⏱ 5 MIN
🔥 Scenario — Mónica Herrera
Mónica arrives at Hotel Ejecutivo in Monterrey at 11 PM after a delayed flight. The receptionist says: "I'm sorry, we don't have your reservation. Can you give me your confirmation number?"
Your task: In 60 seconds, explain the situation to the receptionist. Use at least 4 object pronouns (me, it, her, us, them). Ask for help. Be polite but clear.
1:00
🏆 Badge Rules
🔥 Complete the timer = earn badge · ⚡ Speed Round 8+ = earn badge · 🎤 Solo Challenge = earn badge · 🔍 Source Analysis participation = earn badge · 🏆 My Win = earn final badge
🧠 Pedagogical: This Hot Take tests whether students internalized the object pronoun system from S3A. The scenario is intentionally stressful (late arrival, missing reservation) because that's when English breaks down. Listen for: "give me" / "send it" / "call her" / "help us." Mark every instance on the board.
§2
Debate — Big Hotel or Small Hotel?
Two sides · Object pronouns integrated into every argument · 3 minutes per side
⏱ 10 MIN
Side A — Big Chain Hotels
Marriott, Hilton, Holiday Inn...
  • "Big hotels give you everything: gym, pool, restaurant."
  • "They have a loyalty program. It saves me money."
  • "If there's a problem, they fix it immediately."
  • "I can call them 24 hours. The reception helps us with everything."
Side B — Small Boutique Hotels
Local hotels with personality...
  • "Small hotels know you by name. They remember you."
  • "The staff treats you like family. They help you with local tips."
  • "The room has character. I love it more than a standard room."
  • "The owner shows you the best restaurants. Chain hotels don't do that for us."
3:00
🎯 Engagement: Assign sides randomly. Students must use at least 3 object pronouns per argument. After the debate, ask: "Who used the most object pronouns?" Award bonus points. The grammar goal is hidden inside the fun.
§3
Relay — Hotel Problem Solver
5 real hotel problems · Solve each one using object pronouns + imperatives · Click to reveal the model solution
⏱ 8 MIN
🔧
Hotel Problem Solver
Read the problem → Say your solution out loud → Click to check the model answer
🔴 Problem 1: No Wi-Fi in your room
You're in room 412. The Wi-Fi doesn't work. You need it for a video call with your client in 30 minutes. You call reception.
✅ "Hi, this is room 412. The Wi-Fi doesn't work. Can you help me? I need it for a video call. Please send someone to fix it. Thank you."
🔴 Problem 2: Wrong room type
You booked a double room, but they gave you a single room. Your colleague arrives tomorrow and you need the double room for both of you.
✅ "Excuse me, I booked a double room, but you gave me a single room. My colleague arrives tomorrow — I need it for both of us. Can you change it, please?"
🔴 Problem 3: The A/C doesn't work
It's August in Monterrey. Your room is very hot. The air conditioning is not working. You want them to fix it or move you to another room.
✅ "Hello, the air conditioning in my room doesn't work. It's very hot. Can you fix it? If you can't fix it today, please move me to another room."
🔴 Problem 4: Extra charge on the bill
At checkout, you see a charge for minibar items — but you didn't use the minibar. You need them to remove it from your bill before you pay.
✅ "Excuse me, there's an extra charge on my bill for the minibar. I didn't use it. Can you remove it from my bill? I need the correct receipt — please give it to me before I leave."
🔴 Problem 5: Your colleague lost the key card
Your colleague María lost her key card. She's locked out of room 305. You go to reception to ask for a new one for her.
✅ "Hi, my colleague lost her key card. She's in room 305 but she can't open it. Can you give her a new one? Or give it to me and I'll take it to her."
🧠 Pedagogical: Students must say their solution OUT LOUD before clicking reveal. Partner checks: did they include the object pronoun? "I didn't use it" not "I didn't use." "Give it to me" not "Give to me." Every pronoun counts. This is the accuracy drill disguised as problem-solving.

Solo Challenge — The Full Hotel Stay
2 minutes · Tell the complete story of a hotel stay · Object pronouns throughout · No notes
⏱ 8 MIN
🎤 Solo Challenge — Mónica's Full Hotel Story
Tell the complete story of Mónica Herrera's business trip to Monterrey. Cover ALL of these moments:
1️⃣ Arrival: She arrives at the hotel. What does she say?
2️⃣ Check-in: The receptionist gives her the key card and Wi-Fi password.
3️⃣ Problem: Something is wrong with the room (you choose the problem).
4️⃣ Solution: She calls reception. They fix it or move her.
5️⃣ Checkout: She checks out and asks for the receipt for her company.
🎯 Goal: Use at least 6 different object pronouns. Partner counts them.

§6
Source Analysis — Real Hotel Messages
4 real-world hotel texts · Find the object pronouns · Understand who is talking to whom
⏱ 8 MIN
📱 WhatsApp from Mónica to her boss
"Hi Daniel, I just checked in at the hotel. They gave me a nice room on the 4th floor. The Wi-Fi works perfectly. I'll call you in the morning before the meeting. See you tomorrow!"
❓ How many object pronouns can you find? Who is "me," "you," and what is "the meeting" referring to?
📧 Email from Hotel to Guest
"Dear Ms. Herrera, Thank you for choosing Hotel Ejecutivo. We have your reservation. Please show us your ID at check-in. If you need anything, call us at extension 100. We look forward to welcoming you."
❓ The hotel uses "us" and "you" — who do these pronouns refer to? What does "extension 100" mean?
⭐ Hotel Review (TripAdvisor)
"I stayed here for a client visit. The staff was very friendly — they helped me with everything. When the Wi-Fi crashed, they fixed it in 10 minutes. I asked them for a late checkout and they gave it to me for free. I recommend it!"
❓ Count ALL object pronouns (me, it, them). How many did you find? Which verb goes with each pronoun?
🔴 Complaint Message to Hotel Manager
"Good morning. I'm in room 412 and the air conditioning doesn't work. I called reception but nobody helped me. My colleague in room 305 has the same problem — nobody helped her either. Please send someone to fix them today. Thank you."
❓ The guest uses "me," "her," and "them" — who/what does each one replace? Is this message polite or rude?
🧠 Pedagogical: Source Analysis trains students to NOTICE object pronouns in real texts. After analyzing, ask: "Write your own WhatsApp message to your boss about a hotel check-in. Use at least 3 object pronouns." This bridges reading → writing → speaking.

3 Phrases You Mastered Today
PHRASE 1
"Can you help me? The [problem] doesn't work."
Works at hotels, offices, and airports. Replace [problem] with: Wi-Fi, air conditioning, elevator, printer. Universal complaint formula.
PHRASE 2
"I booked a [room type] but you gave me a [different type]. Can you change it?"
Polite but firm. Three object pronouns in action: "gave ME" + "change IT." This is how you solve problems without being rude.
PHRASE 3
"My colleague lost her key card. Can you give her a new one?"
Helping a colleague in English = leadership skill. "Give HER a new one" shows you can use third-person pronouns naturally.
