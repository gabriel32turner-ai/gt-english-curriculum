Q4 S→S
What did you do FIRST when you arrived at work today?
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
No corrección mid-utterance. Objetivo: activar Simple Past espontáneo. Apunta en el board 2–3 verbos irregulares que el alumno use bien → recíclalos en §3.
Capa 2 · Error watch
El error más común en este arranque: "I did go" (incorrect emphasis) / "I goed" (regularization de go). Corrección diferida al final del warm-up, nunca durante.
Capa 3 · Extension
Si el alumno da una respuesta corta → prompt: "And then what? How did it end?" — esto prepara el terreno para los conectores de §5.

Tell Me What Happened

Describe a recent work situation — a meeting, a problem, a visit — from beginning to end.
Round 1 · 60 sec
T→S "Tell me about something that happened at work recently. What was it?"
Round 2 · 60 sec
S→S "Ask your partner Wh- questions about their story."
Round 3 · 90 sec
Class "Who had the most interesting work story this week?"
Model Answer — Valeria Torres

"So, what happened was — last Tuesday we had a surprise visit from a German client. First, I went to the conference room and prepared the materials. Then the client arrived and we had a two-hour meeting. After that, my manager asked me to send a follow-up email. In the end, the meeting went really well — they made us a proposal."
■ Story connectors ■ Simple Past irregular ■ Colloquial
Capa 1 · Coaching
Si el alumno da hechos sin estructura (lista de eventos) → interviene: "Great. Now connect them — use FIRST, THEN, AFTER THAT." El objetivo es la narrativa, no los hechos aislados.
Capa 2 · Gramática implícita
Cuenta mentalmente cuántos irregulares usa correctamente. Si usa 3+ = sólido. Si usa 0–1 = target de práctica en §3.

Capa 1 · Pattern grouping
Señala los pares fonéticos: have/had · make/made — mismo sonido final /d/. meet/met · get/got — vocal acortada. speak/spoke · write/wrote — vocal desplazada. El patrón, no la memorización individual.
Capa 2 · Collocation drill
Nunca "gave" solo — siempre "gave a presentation / gave feedback / gave approval". El verbo irregular se aprende en su colocación natural, no como forma aislada.
Capa 3 · TTS
El botón LISTEN activa Web Speech API (Chrome). El quiz de arriba pronuncia cada palabra automáticamente. Pide al alumno que repita antes de elegir la opción.
§ 04
Colloquial — How Storytellers Really Talk
5 min
💬
Colloquial
Real storytelling phrases — what native speakers say to open and close a story
5 MIN
"So, what happened was…"
When: opening a story / building suspense
"So, what happened was — the client arrived two hours early and nobody was ready."
"How did it go?"
When: asking about a past event you care about
"You had the audit yesterday — how did it go? Did everything go well?"
"It went really well."
When: reporting a positive outcome
"It went really well — the client loved the proposal and asked for a second meeting."
"Long story short…"
When: summarising after too much detail
"There were a lot of problems with the delivery, but — long story short — we solved it."
Práctica rápida
Di el contexto en español → alumno produce la frase en inglés. "Tu colega te pregunta cómo salió la reunión con el cliente alemán." → "It went really well." / "Tu jefe quiere saber qué pasó con el retraso." → "So, what happened was…"
Integración
"Long story short" + "So, what happened was" son las frases que más diferencian a un hablante A2 de un hablante B1 en conversaciones informales de trabajo. El alumno que las usa naturalmente suena inmediatamente más fluido.
§ 05
Grammar — Story Connectors + Irregular Consolidation
12 min
⚙️
Grammar
Story Connectors — Full system + Outcome expressions + Irregular negative rule
12 MIN
Regla de oro §5
Esta sección es REFERENCIAL. Lee la timeline con el alumno, da un ejemplo tuyo personal, luego activa el Poll. Nunca pidas que completen oraciones fuera de contexto narrativo. La gramática se practica contando historias, no conjugando en tablas.
✅ Story structure: First → Then → After that → Finally + outcome: "It went really well / It didn't go as planned"
FIRST
"First, I went to the conference room and prepared the materials."
→ The first action in the story. Sets the scene.
THEN
"Then the client arrived and we had a two-hour meeting."
→ Next action. Most flexible connector — can repeat.
AFTER THAT
"After that, my manager asked me to send a follow-up email."
→ Something that happens as a consequence of the previous action.
FINALLY / IN THE END
"Finally, they made us a proposal. In the end, it went really well."
→ The closing action or outcome. Use one — not both together.
StructureFormBusiness Example
Affirmative irregularSubject + past form"She gave a presentation. They made a decision."
Negative irregularSubject + didn't + BASE verb"He didn't go to the meeting. She didn't have time."
Outcome — positiveIt went really well."The audit took 3 hours. It went really well."
Outcome — negativeIt didn't go as planned."We had the meeting but it didn't go as planned."
⚠ Critical Error — Negative Irregulars
✗ "She didn't went to the meeting."→✓ She didn't go to the meeting.
didn't already marks past — the main verb returns to BASE form.
✗ "They didn't made a decision."→✓ They didn't make a decision.
make → didn't make (not "made"). Most common A2 error globally.

Listening — Valeria's Product Launch Story

🎧
Listening
Valeria Torres — telling her team what happened at the product launch in Querétaro
8 MIN
🎙️
ElevenLabs Audio — "The Product Launch Story"
Duration: ~1:20 · Valeria Torres · Querétaro context · Story with connectors
VALERIA TORRES — coordinating a product launch, Celaya office

"OK, so — first, let me tell you what happened last Thursday in Querétaro.


We had the product launch for our new industrial component. I went up on Tuesday evening to prepare everything. First, I met with the logistics team and they gave me a full briefing. Then we went to the venue and set up the displays.


On Thursday, about 40 clients came to the event. The director gave a 20-minute presentation. After that, I spoke to several clients individually and took notes on their feedback.


Finally, we had dinner with three key clients and got really positive reactions. In the end, it went really well — two clients already sent us enquiries this week.


Long story short — the launch was a success. We didn't have any major problems and the team did an excellent job."

Pre-listening
"Valeria was in Querétaro last week. Listen and count: how many story connectors does she use? And what was the outcome?" — Activa escucha activa con tarea específica.
Post-listening
Pide al alumno que reconstruya la historia de Valeria de memoria: "Tell me what happened in Querétaro — without looking at the script." El color azul = conectores, oro = irregulares, verde = outcomes en el script.
Multiple Choice
1. Why did Valeria go to Querétaro on Tuesday?
2. What did Valeria do AFTER the director's presentation?
True / False
3. The launch had major problems.
4. Two clients sent enquiries after the event.
5. Valeria used "Long story short" to open the story.
