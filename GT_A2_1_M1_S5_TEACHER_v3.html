§ 02
Vocabulary — M1 Full Review
8–10 min
S1–S2 — Regular Past Actions
  • finished completed a task successfully🔊
  • submitted sent formally to someone🔊
  • confirmed made something certain🔊
  • cancelled stopped a planned event🔊
  • attended was present at a meeting🔊
  • prepared got something ready in advance🔊
  • presented showed results to a group🔊
  • notified informed someone officially🔊
S3 — Irregular Past Verbs
  • went (go) traveled to another place🔊
  • had (have) experienced, participated in🔊
  • made (make) created, decided, produced🔊
  • gave (give) presented or offered something🔊
  • met (meet) saw someone for a meeting🔊
  • took (take) received, used, or traveled by🔊
  • wrote (write) put ideas down in text🔊
  • got (get) received or obtained🔊
S4 — Verbs for Negative Reports
  • mentioned briefly said something🔊
  • reached achieved or arrived at🔊
  • received got something that was sent🔊
  • replied answered a message🔊
  • responded reacted to a situation🔊
  • resolved found a solution to a problem🔊
S1–S4 — Time & Duration
  • yesterday the day before today🔊
  • last week the previous week🔊
  • two days ago 48 hours before now🔊
  • for three hours total time spent🔊
  • all afternoon the entire afternoon🔊
  • until 7 PM end point — when it stopped🔊
  • from 2 to 5 PM exact start and end🔊
  • on time not late — as planned🔊
⚡ Challenge — 60 seconds
Choose 1 word from each group. Make 4 sentences — one per group — all about your real work this week. Use the words exactly as they appear above. Time yourself.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Click any word to hear it — use this for quick pronunciation review. Focus on words student mispronounced in S1–S4.
  • Challenge: if student struggles to make 4 sentences in 60 seconds, identify which GROUP is weakest — that's the drill priority before §7.
  • Watch for: "notified" pronunciation (/ˈnəʊtɪfaɪd/), "responded" vs "replied" confusion, "from 2 to 5" vs "from 2 until 5" (both acceptable).
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "These are ALL the verbs from M1. You now have over 24 business past tense verbs. That's more than most A2 coursebooks cover in an entire course."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "M2 adds future tenses (going to / will) — you'll describe your PLANS for the week. Same structure, forward-facing."

Reading — Valeria's Full Week
8–10 min
FROM
Valeria Torres <v.torres@solarismanufacturing.com>
TO
Daniel Ochoa — Project Manager
📋 Weekly Summary — M1 Exit Report
Valeria Torres
Project Coordinator · Solaris Manufacturing
M1 Exit Reading — watch for ALL grammar types: regular past (cyan) · irregular past (violet) · negatives/didn't (red) · duration (gold)

Hi Daniel,

Here is my summary of this week.

On Tuesday, I went to the Chicago office for the regional Q3 review. I gave a presentation to 35 participants and met the Director of Operations. We had a very productive meeting and made an agreement to schedule a follow-up next month.

On Wednesday, I attended the Q4 budget review. The meeting lasted from 2 PM to 5 PM — the whole afternoon. We didn't reach a final decision. We didn't resolve the main cost issue. The discussion was difficult but necessary.

On Thursday, I wrote the trip report and updated the project timeline. I didn't reply to the Houston email — I had an urgent client call all morning. I also didn't receive the supplier's response to our price request.

Today, I finished the supplier proposal and submitted it to the finance team. I replied to the Houston email and notified the operations team about the new schedule. I worked until 6 PM to complete everything on time.

Overall, it was a challenging but productive week. I completed 7 of my 9 tasks. The two unresolved items will be my priority next Monday.

Valeria Torres · Project Coordinator · Solaris Manufacturing · Ext. 312
M1 Exit Reading — Mixed Comprehension
All question types used in this module.
Where did Valeria go on Tuesday?

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This is the M1 Exit Text — it contains ALL grammar types from S1-S4. After activities: ask student to count: regular past verbs (cyan), irregular past (violet), negatives (red), duration expressions (gold).
  • Counts: regular = finished, attended, lasted, updated, replied, notified, worked, submitted, completed ≈ 9. Irregular = went, gave, met, had, made, wrote, had (again) ≈ 7. Negatives = didn't reach, didn't resolve, didn't reply, didn't receive ≈ 4. Duration = on Tuesday, from 2 to 5, the whole afternoon, all morning, until 6 PM, today ≈ 6.
  • After reading: "In §7 you'll produce something similar — spoken, from memory. This email is your model."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Valeria's email has 9 regular verbs, 7 irregular, 4 negatives, and 6 time/duration expressions. That's professional-level past tense density. You've studied all of this in M1."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "This is the kind of weekly email that international project managers write every Friday. After today, you can write this — and say it out loud."
