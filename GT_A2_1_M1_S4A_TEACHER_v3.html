§ 01
Warm-Up — Scenario
5–8 min
📍 The Scenario
Situation
It's Friday at 5 PM. Your manager asks you: "What happened this week? I see some tasks are not complete on the tracker." You had a very busy, difficult week — many things were delayed or didn't happen. How do you explain?
1
What didn't you finish this week? Name 2–3 things.
Try: "I didn't finish the ___ because…"
2
How long did you work on something that wasn't completed?
Try: "I worked on it for ___ hours / all morning."
3
Did you reply to all your emails this week? What about messages from clients?
Try: "I didn't reply to ___ because I didn't have time / I didn't receive…"
4
S3 review: What irregular past verb would you use to explain what you did in meetings?
went · had · made · gave · met · took · wrote · got
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Este escenario es emocionalmente real — todo profesional tiene "viernes difíciles." Activa lenguaje auténtico en pasado inmediatamente.
  • Q1 diagnóstico: watch for "I didn't finished" — error de S1 que persiste. Si aparece después de 3 sesiones: error fosilizado. Abórdalo explícitamente en §4.
  • Q2 es el puente al contenido de hoy: duración. Acepta cualquier respuesta aproximada. "I worked a lot" → "Great — how long? Today you'll learn to say that precisely."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "This exact conversation happened to someone at your company this week. The difference is they had to do it in English. By the end of today, you can."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Honest updates — with negatives and duration — are more professional than only reporting good news. International managers respect 'I didn't finish X because Y. I worked on it for Z hours.'"

Vocabulary — Verbs for Negative Reports
10–12 min
mention
→ mentioned
"She mentioned the issue in the meeting."
"He didn't mention the delay."
reach
→ reached
"We reached an agreement by noon."
"We didn't reach a decision all afternoon."
receive
→ received
"I received the files this morning."
"We didn't receive the supplier's documents."
reply
→ replied ⭐
"She replied to all client emails."
"I didn't reply to the Chicago email."
request
→ requested
"I requested the data from IT."
"He didn't request approval in time."
respond
→ responded
"The supplier responded quickly."
"They didn't respond to our emails."
achieve
→ achieved
"The team achieved its monthly target."
"We didn't achieve the Q3 goal."
resolve
→ resolved
"They resolved the issue by Friday."
"I didn't resolve the client complaint."
⚠ Remember — reply → replied (not replyed) — S2 -ied rule
La regla consonant+y → ied (de S2) aplica de nuevo: reply → replied · también: notify, study, apply, carry. En negativo: "didn't reply" (base, no past form).
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Cada tarjeta muestra BASE + ejemplo NEGATIVO. La forma negativa es el uso primario en S4. El alumno debe leer la oración negativa en voz alta para cada verbo.
  • "respond" vs "reply": reply = responder un mensaje específico. Respond = reaccionar a una situación. Ambos correctos en email corporativo.
  • "achieve" /əˈtʃiːv/ — los alumnos dicen "a-CHEEVE". Drill: uh-CHEEV.
  • Micro-speaking: elige 3 verbos y haz una oración negativa verdadera de esta semana.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Which of these 8 negative sentences could be from YOUR week? Make the honest one — use it in class."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "'didn't reach a decision · didn't resolve the issue · didn't achieve the target' — estas son las 3 negativas corporativas más usadas. Los managers las usan en cada review meeting."

§ 04
Grammar — Didn't + Duration
15 min
Didn't + Base Verb — Full Pattern
Subjectdidn'tBase verbObjectDuration / Reason
Ididn'tfinishthe report— I worked on it for 3 hours.
Shedidn'treplyto the email— she was in meetings all afternoon.
Wedidn'treacha decision— the call lasted from 2 to 5 PM.
Theydidn'trespondto our request— we waited most of the day.
Ididn'tgoto the meeting— I worked on the contract until 7 PM.
Rule reminder: didn't always uses BASE verb — applies to BOTH regular and irregular verbs.
Regular: didn't finish · didn't reply · didn't receive · didn't resolve
Irregular: didn't go · didn't have · didn't make · didn't give · didn't meet
Negative + Duration — The Professional Combination
I didn't finish the report + — I worked on it for 2 hours.
"I didn't finish the report. I worked on it for two hours, but I need more time."
She didn't reply + — she was in meetings all afternoon.
"She didn't reply to the client — she was in meetings all afternoon."
We didn't reach a decision + — from 2 to 5 PM.
"We discussed it from 2 to 5 PM but didn't reach a final decision."
⚠ Top Errors — S4
❌ "I didn't received the email."→✅ I didn't receive the email. (base verb)
❌ "The meeting lasted during two hours."→✅ The meeting lasted for two hours.
❌ "I worked until to 8 PM."→✅ I worked until 8 PM. (no "to" after until)
❌ "She was in meetings all the afternoon."→✅ She was in meetings all afternoon. (no "the")
❌ "I didn't went to the meeting yesterday."→✅ I didn't go to the meeting. (base after didn't — even irregular)
Activity 1 — Choose the Correct Form
Haz clic en la opción correcta.
I worked on the report ___ three hours — but I didn't finish it.
I didn't ___ the budget report to HR last Friday.
The budget review ran ___ 2 PM ___ 5 PM.
"Did the supplier respond to your emails?" — "___"
I ___ to the Chicago email — I was in meetings all afternoon.
Activity 2 — Find & Fix the Error
Haz clic en la corrección correcta.
❌ "I didn't received the email from Dallas."
Fix: "I didn't _____ the email."
❌ "The presentation lasted during two hours."
Fix: "The presentation lasted _____ two hours."
❌ "She didn't went to the client meeting last Monday."
Fix: "She didn't _____ to the meeting."
❌ "I worked until to 8 PM to finish the report."
Fix: "I worked _____ 8 PM to finish it."
Activity 3 — Sentence Order
Arrastra las piezas para formar una oración correcta de negativo + duración.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • La sección de combinaciones es el skill CLAVE nuevo: negativo + duración = update profesional completo. Drilla estas 3 oraciones modelo hasta que sean automáticas.
      • Q5: "didn't reply" (no "didn't replied") — y "replied" tiene el spelling -ied de S2. Doble capa de corrección.
      • Después de actividades: producción oral. El maestro da un escenario, el alumno hace una oración negativo + duración en menos de 10 segundos.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Say your own example from THIS week — negative + duration. 'I didn't ___ because I was ___ for ___ hours.' Time yourself: under 10 seconds."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "You now have the complete toolkit for past tense: affirmative (S1+S2), irregular (S3), negative + duration (S4). S5 puts it all together."
      § 05
      Reading — A Difficult Thursday
      8–10 min
      FROM
      Valeria Torres <[email protected]>
      TO
      Daniel Ochoa — Project Manager
      📋 Update — Thursday · A Difficult Day
      Valeria Torres
      Project Coordinator · Solaris Manufacturing
      Watch for: didn't + base verb · duration expressions (for/from-to/all/until) · outcome vocabulary · honest professional tone

      Hi Daniel,

      Here is my update for today. It was a difficult day — I want to be transparent about what happened.

      This morning, I worked on the supplier contract from 9 AM to noon. I didn't finish it — the data from the warehouse was incomplete. I spent three hours on it, but I still need two more hours tomorrow.

      I also attended the Q4 budget review at 2 PM. The meeting lasted the whole afternoon — from 2 PM to 5 PM. We didn't reach a final decision. We didn't resolve the main budget issue. We will continue tomorrow morning.

      I didn't reply to the email from the Houston office — I didn't have time. I was in meetings all afternoon. I also didn't receive the supplier's response to our last request — they still haven't replied.

      I didn't achieve my main goal for the day. I worked until 6:30 PM and didn't finish the agenda for Friday's client meeting. I will complete it tonight.

      Overall, I completed 2 of my 5 tasks. I understand this is below expectations. Tomorrow will be different.

      Best,

      Valeria Torres · Project Coordinator · Solaris Manufacturing · Ext. 312
      Comprehension
      Responde las preguntas.
      Why didn't Valeria finish the supplier contract?
      How long did the Q4 budget review last?
      Valeria replied to the Houston office email.
      The budget team reached a final decision in the afternoon meeting.
      Valeria worked until 6:30 PM.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Color coding: cyan = past verbs, red = negatives, gold = duration. Pide al alumno que cuente cada uno: ¿cuántos negativos? (7 negatives) ¿expresiones de duración? (5) ¿verbos pasados positivos? (4).
      • Este ratio (7 negativos vs 4 positivos) modela cómo se ve un update de día difícil en inglés profesional — y muestra que es normal y aceptable reportar fallas honestamente.
      • Trampa R2: muchos alumnos eligen "for one hour" porque recuerdan la sección de la mañana. La pregunta es sobre el budget review — 2 a 5 PM.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Notice Valeria says 'Tomorrow will be different.' This is professional English accountability. Not excuses — honest facts + next steps."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "In §7 you present YOUR difficult week report. This email is your model: what you did · what you didn't do · why · how long · what's next."

      What Didn't You Do — Monday Check-In

      ▶ Show ElevenLabs Script

      Voice: Sarah · Rate: 0.82 · Stability: 0.6 · Single speaker monologue ~90 sec


      Good morning, team. Before we start this week, I want to give you a quick update on what happened last Thursday — because some tasks are still open on our tracker.


      First, I didn't finish the supplier contract. I worked on it from nine AM to noon — three hours — but the data from the warehouse was incomplete. I'll complete it today.


      Second, we didn't reach a decision on the Q4 budget. The meeting lasted all afternoon — from two to five PM. We discussed everything but didn't resolve the main issue. We'll continue at ten this morning.


      Third, I didn't reply to the email from the Houston team. I was in meetings most of the afternoon and didn't have time. I'll respond before noon today.


      Finally — and this is important — we still didn't receive the supplier's response to our price request. I sent it last Tuesday and waited most of the week. I'll call them directly this morning.


      I know it wasn't a perfect week. But I want to be transparent. Any questions before we start? Let's make this week better.

      ElevenLabs: Sarah (voz femenina) · Rate 0.82 · Stability 0.6 · Single speaker · Sin nombres de ciudad que causen problemas de TTS · Sube a Google Drive con iframe /preview
      Listening Activities
      Escucha y responde.

      § SP
      Speaking Practice
      10–12 min
      🗣 Guided Speaking — Pair / Group Discussion
      What Didn't You Finish — And How Long Did It Take?
      Answer each question using today's grammar: didn't + base verb for negatives, and duration expressions — for / from-to / all morning / until / most of the day. Give honest, real answers about your work. Your teacher will ask follow-up questions.
      Question 1
      "Tell me about something you didn't finish at work last week. What was it — and why didn't you finish it?"
      Hint: I didn't finish… because… / I didn't complete… / I didn't submit… / There wasn't enough time…
      Question 2
      "How long did that task take? Use a duration expression: for, from-to, all morning, until, or most of the day."
      Hint: I worked on it for 3 hours… / from 9 AM to 1 PM… / all morning… / until 6 PM… / most of the day…
      Question 3
      "Did you attend any meetings that didn't go well last week? What happened?"
      Hint: I attended… but we didn't reach a decision… / The meeting lasted from… to… / We didn't agree on…
      Question 4
      "What did you work on for the longest time last week — and did you finish it?"
      Hint: I worked on… for… / I spent all morning on… / I didn't finish it — I worked until… / Yes, I completed it at…
      Question 5
      "Ask me a question using 'How long did you…?' or 'Did you finish…?' — your choice."
      Hint: How long did you work on…? / Did you finish the…? / How long did the meeting last? / Did you submit it by…?
      ⏱ 10–12 min total 📋 5 questions 🎯 2+ duration expressions 👥 Pair or teacher-led
      ⚡ Speaking Challenge — Closing
      "Give me a 60-second honest work update: what did you work on, what didn't you finish, and how long did each thing take. No notes. Use at least 2 negatives and 2 duration expressions."
      Teacher evaluates: didn't + correct BASE verb (not didn't + past tense) · at least 2 duration expressions (for / from-to / all morning / until / most of the day) · natural transition between what was done and what wasn't · time expressions used correctly (yesterday / last week / on Monday). Key error to watch: "I didn't worked" or "I didn't went" — recast immediately: "I didn't work — didn't always needs the base form."
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • S4A introduces two grammar points simultaneously: didn't + BASE and duration expressions. Q1 and Q2 work as a pair — Q1 gets the negative, Q2 gets the duration. Keep them together.
      • The most critical error at this stage: "I didn't worked" / "I didn't went" / "I didn't finished." Every time you hear this, recast naturally: "You didn't finish — and how long did it take?" Never say "that's wrong."
      • Duration expressions: many students will say "I worked 3 hours" (missing "for"). Push: "I worked FOR 3 hours — try it with 'for.'" This is a preposition habit that needs explicit drilling.
      • Q4 is the most diagnostic: it combines both grammar points (negative + duration) in one answer. If a student handles Q4 cleanly, they've internalized both structures.
      • Q5 (student asks you): target "How long did you…?" — the "did" inversion is still fragile. If they produce "How long you worked?" recast: "How long DID I work? I worked for…" and ask them to repeat.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "The fact that you can say 'I didn't finish it because the client changed the requirements' in English — that's a professional sentence. That's not beginner level."
      • After Speaking Challenge: "You just gave me a real work update with negatives and duration. That's exactly what a Monday standup sounds like in a bilingual company."
      • When student correctly uses a duration expression: "Say that again — faster. 'I worked on it for 3 hours.' Again. That repetition is what makes it automatic."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "In S4B you'll use negatives and duration in a real situation: your Friday update to your manager. No script — you'll report what got done, what didn't, and how long things took."
      • Upsell: "By S5 you'll combine everything: regular verbs, irregular verbs, negatives, Wh- questions, AND duration — in a single 90-second exit standup. Today's session is the last piece."
      • Testimonial trigger: "Think about your next real work week — which of today's expressions will you actually use? Pick one: 'I didn't finish because…' or 'I worked on it for…' — which one first?"
