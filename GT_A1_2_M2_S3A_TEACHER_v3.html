Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • ACCIÓN: Para cada palabra, haz que el alumno diga UNA oración verdadera sobre su empresa. "Stock — ¿hay stock de qué en tu almacén ahora mismo?" El vocabulario se ancla con contexto real.
  • "Out of stock" vs "in stock" — enseña como CHUNK, no como palabras sueltas. Son frases fijas que se dicen completas. "We're out of stock" / "It's in stock." No se separan.
  • "Inventory" pronunciación: /ˈɪnvəntri/ — el alumno hispanohablante dirá "in-VEN-to-ry" (4 sílabas). Americano es 4 sílabas pero con -tree, no -to-ry. Practica 3 veces.
  • "Shortage" — esta palabra aparece en emails de proveedores todo el tiempo. Ejemplo real: "Due to a shortage of materials, delivery will be delayed." Alta frecuencia en contexto de manufactura.
  • "Procurement" — muchos alumnos en industria ya conocen esta palabra en inglés. Pregunta si la usan en su empresa antes de enseñarla.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "'Is there any stock left?' — Esta es la pregunta que le haces a almacén antes de hacer un pedido. En inglés. Sin traductor. Ya la sabes."

§ 04
Grammar — some / any · How much / How many
15–18 min
⚙️
Grammar
some / any · How much / How many — the full system
15–18 MIN
🚨 Real Situation — Monday Morning, Warehouse
Your manager calls you: "Is there any paper left? How many ink cartridges do we have? We need some folders for the presentation — can you check?"

You go to the storeroom. There is no paper. There are 3 cartridges. There are some folders.
❓ How do you answer each question in English? What's the grammar you need?
✅ The Tool: some = affirmative sentences  ·  any = negatives and questions  ·  How much = uncountable  ·  How many = countable
some / any — The Full System
SOME — Affirmative ✅
Use some in positive sentences. There is a quantity — not zero.
"There are some folders in the storeroom."
"We have some paper left — about 50 reams."
"We need some ink cartridges for next week."
Also in offers and requests:
"Can I have some information about your products?"
"Would you like some help with the order?"
ANY — Negatives & Questions ❓
Use any in negative sentences and questions.
"Is there any stock left?" ← question
"Are there any folders on the shelf?" ← question
"There isn't any paper." ← negative
"We don't have any ink cartridges." ← negative
"There aren't any products in the warehouse." ← negative
How much / How many — Quantity Questions
HOW MUCH — Uncountable nouns
For things we cannot count individually: money, paper, time, equipment, information, software, furniture, space, staff.
"How much paper is there?" ✅
"How much money is in the budget?" ✅
"How much time do we have?" ✅
"How much folders?" ✗ — folders are countable!
HOW MANY — Countable nouns
For things we can count: boxes, folders, employees, units, cartridges, orders, suppliers, branches.
"How many units are in stock?" ✅
"How many employees does your company have?" ✅
"How many orders did we receive?" ✅
"How many money?" ✗ — money is uncountable!
StructureUse withCorporate example
There is some…Uncountable ✅"There is some paper in the storeroom."
There are some…Countable plural ✅"There are some folders on the shelf."
There isn't any…Uncountable ✅"There isn't any ink left."
There aren't any…Countable plural ✅"There aren't any cartridges in stock."
Is there any…?Uncountable ✅"Is there any budget for new equipment?"
Are there any…?Countable plural ✅"Are there any suppliers in Guadalajara?"
How much…?Uncountable ✅"How much paper do we need to order?"
How many…?Countable plural ✅"How many units are left in the warehouse?"
⚠️ Top Errors — Fix These Before the Activities
✗ "There isn't some paper"→✓ There isn't any paper
Negatives always use ANY, never some.
✗ "How many money do we have?"→✓ How much money do we have?
Money is uncountable — always How much.
✗ "There are some equipments"→✓ There is some equipment
Equipment is uncountable — no plural, use "is" not "are".
✗ "Is there some stock left?"→✓ Is there any stock left?
Questions use ANY, not some (except offers).
✗ "We don't have some folders"→✓ We don't have any folders
Negatives with don't/doesn't = ANY.

      Production — Your Stock Report
      8–10 min
      🗣
      Production
      Speaking Practice — Your Stock Situation
      8–10 MIN
      Speaking Practice — A1.2 Target
      Answer in full sentences. Use some, any, How much, How many.
      Real situations from your company. Minimum 1 complete sentence per question. The teacher marks every correct some/any usage.
      Question 1 — Your Office Supplies
      Is there any paper in your office right now? Are there any pens? What about folders?
      💡 Use: "Yes, there is/are some…" / "No, there isn't/aren't any…"
      Question 2 — Your Equipment
      Is there any equipment in your department that needs replacing? How many computers / screens / phones are there?
      💡 Remember: "equipment" is uncountable. "computers" are countable. Use the right question!
      Question 3 — Budget Question
      How much budget does your department have per month? Is there any money left this month — or is the budget over?
      💡 Use: "There is some budget left — about…" / "There isn't any budget left this month."
      Question 4 — Suppliers
      How many suppliers does your company work with? Are there any suppliers that cause problems with delivery or quality?
      💡 Use: "We have some suppliers — about [number]." / "There isn't any problem with most of them, but…"
      Question 5 — A Real Shortage
      Describe a real situation at work when there wasn't any stock or supply. What happened? What did you do?
      💡 Challenge: use Simple Present + some/any together: "There isn't any… We need some…" Minimum 3 sentences.
      ⭐ Question 6 — Stock Report to Your Manager (Challenge)
      You are the warehouse manager. Give a 60-second verbal stock report to your "manager" (teacher). Include: what supplies you have, what you don't have, how much budget is left, and what you need to order. No notes.
      💡 Model opening: "Good morning. I have some information about our current stock situation. There is some… There isn't any… How many… do you need?"
      🎯 A1.2 Speaking Challenge
      "Imagine you are Mónica Herrera. Your manager calls you and asks about the stock situation. Answer in English — use some, any, How much, How many at least once each. 60 seconds. No notes."
      A1.2 success standard: student uses some/any correctly 3+ times, asks or answers at least 1 How much and 1 How many question, and stays in English for the full 60 seconds. That's a complete A1.2 achievement.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • ACCIÓN: Para Q6 (challenge), TÚ juegas al manager y HACES las preguntas: "Is there any paper?" / "How many cartridges do we have?" — El alumno tiene que responder sobre la marcha. Esto crea producción espontánea, no leída.
      • Corrección A1.2: recast silencioso para errores menores (some/any). Corrección directa solo para "equipments" o "How many money" — los errores que más dañan la credibilidad profesional.
      • Estándar de éxito A1.2: el alumno usa some/any correctamente al menos 3 de 5 veces, usa How much o How many correctamente al menos 2 veces, y completa Q6 en inglés sin traducir. Ese es el 100% de S3A.
      • Si el alumno usa "there isn't some" → corrección directa: "Remember — 'there isn't ANY' — not some. Negatives always use any. Try again." Una sola corrección, una sola vez por error.
