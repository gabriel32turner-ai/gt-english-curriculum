§ 01
Warm-Up — This or That
5–8 min

Think about yesterday at work. Choose your reality and explain. No perfect sentences needed — just get talking.

1 · End of day
✅ Finished everything on my list
How did that feel?
OR
😓 Still had things to do at 6 PM
What was left?
Yesterday, did you finish your tasks?
2 · Meetings
📅 A meeting was cancelled
What was it about?
OR
📅 All meetings happened on time
How many?
Were your meetings cancelled or on schedule?
3 · Email
📧 Sent more than 10 emails
What about?
OR
📧 Replied to fewer than 5
Were any urgent?
How was your email volume yesterday?
4 · Reports
📄 Submitted a report
To whom?
OR
📄 Didn't submit anything
What's next?
Did you submit any documents yesterday?
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This is A2.1's entry point into simple past. The cards use past vocabulary naturally. Student will attempt past forms spontaneously — note errors and address in §4.
  • Key errors to listen for: "Yesterday I finish the report" (no -ed), "I didn't finished" (base verb required after didn't), "I sended" (irregular — address in S3).
  • The "This or That" format at A2 should involve a brief explanation (2–3 sentences), not just a single word. Push for elaboration.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "I'll remember what you said and ask you about it at the end of class. Let's see if you can say it better then."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In business English, every Monday morning starts with 'What did you do last week?' Today you get the language for that."
  • "By S5, you'll describe your full work week in past tense — that's a genuine professional skill."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Each card shows BASE + PAST form. Drill both: teacher says base, student says past; then reverse.
  • "submitted" /səbˈmɪtɪd/ — 3 syllables. Common error: "submited" (one t). CVC rule → double + ed.
  • Time expressions: place them at the START or END of a sentence. "Yesterday I submitted…" or "I submitted… yesterday." NOT in the middle.
  • Ask student to make one sentence per verb about their OWN real work this week.

§ 04
Grammar — Simple Past
15–18 min
Structure — Regular Verbs
FormStructureCorporate example
AffirmativeSubject + verb+edI finished the report yesterday.
NegativeSubject + didn't + base verbShe didn't submit the proposal last week.
QuestionDid + subject + base verb?Did you review the contract this morning?
Short answerYes, I did. / No, I didn't."Did you call the client?" — "Yes, I did."
Key rule: "Did" and "didn't" carry the past — so the main verb goes back to BASE FORM.
✅ "She didn't submit" — NOT "She didn't submitted"
✅ "Did you review?" — NOT "Did you reviewed?"
Spelling Rules — Adding -ed
Most verbs → + ed
finish → finished
confirm → confirmed
review → reviewed
Ends in -e → + d only
schedule → scheduled
update → updated
complete → completed
CVC → double consonant + ed
submit → submitted
cancel → cancelled
stop → stopped
⚠ Most Common Errors — A2 Students
❌ "I did finish the report."→✅ I finished the report. (no "did" in affirmative)
❌ "I didn't finished the task."→✅ I didn't finish the task. (base verb after didn't)
❌ "Did you reviewed the contract?"→✅ Did you review the contract? (base verb after Did)
❌ "Yesterday I work on the budget."→✅ Yesterday I worked on the budget.
Activity 1 — Choose the Correct Form
Click the correct option.
She ___ the presentation slides last Tuesday.
We ___ the meeting because the client was travelling.
___ you ___ the report to HR yesterday?
"Did the supplier deliver the order on time?" — "___"
The team ___ the contract last Thursday afternoon.
Activity 2 — True or False: Grammar Rules
Is this grammar rule correct?
In negative sentences, we use "didn't" + the BASE FORM of the verb (not the -ed form).
"Did you confirmed the meeting?" is grammatically correct.
The verb "stop" becomes "stopted" in the simple past.
"This morning I worked on the budget" uses the correct tense.
Activity 3 — Sentence Order
Drag the pieces into the correct order.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Q2 target error: "didn't cancelled" — very common. "Didn't" already carries the past. Main verb = base form. Say it explicitly: "after didn't, we always use the base verb."
      • Q4 (short answer): many students say "Yes, they delivered." — correct content but wrong short answer form. In English, short answers use DID/DIDN'T, not the full verb.
      • After activities: oral drilling. Teacher gives subject + verb + time expression. Student forms the correct past sentence aloud.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "You just answered 5 grammar questions about a tense that didn't exist in your English 60 minutes ago. That's progress."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "S2 adds more verbs and longer question/answer patterns. S3 introduces irregular verbs — which are the most common past tense verbs in English."
      § 05
      Reading — End-of-Day Report
      8–10 min
      FROM
      Valeria Torres <v.torres@solaris.mx>
      TO
      Daniel Reyes — Project Manager
      📋 Daily Update — Thursday
      Watch for: regular past verbs · past time expressions · did/didn't · outcome phrases (on time · delayed · cancelled)

      Hi Daniel,

      Here is my update for today.

      This morning, I reviewed the supplier contract and confirmed the delivery schedule. The supplier confirmed that the materials will arrive on time — next Monday.

      I also scheduled a video call with the Chicago team for next Wednesday. I emailed the agenda to all participants this afternoon.

      Unfortunately, the client cancelled the 3 PM meeting. I rescheduled it for Friday at 10 AM and notified the team immediately.

      I didn't finish the Q3 budget report — I need two more hours. I will submit it first thing tomorrow morning.

      Overall, today was productive. I completed 4 out of 5 tasks on my list.

      Best,

      Valeria Torres · Project Coordinator · Solaris Manufacturing · Ext. 312
      Comprehension
      Answer the questions.
      What happened with the 3 PM meeting?
      Why didn't Valeria finish the Q3 budget report?
      Valeria confirmed that the supplier materials will arrive on time.
      The video call with the Chicago team was scheduled for next Monday.
      Valeria completed all 5 tasks on her list.
      The rescheduled meeting is on Friday at 10 AM.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Past verbs highlighted in cyan. Ask student to find and count them: reviewed, confirmed (x2), scheduled, emailed, cancelled, rescheduled, notified, completed = 9 past verbs in one email.
      • Note "didn't finish" — the one negative. Ask: "What is the base form? Why didn't she use 'finished' here?"
      • R2 (video call): trap — students confuse "next Monday" (delivery) with "next Wednesday" (call). Teach scanning for specific information, not just global comprehension.
      • After reading: "Could you write a similar email about YOUR day yesterday? What would you say?"
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Valeria's email takes 30 seconds to read and covers a full workday. That's professional efficiency. By the end of M1, you'll write one just like it."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "This is the most practical text in A2.1. In your Moodle practice, there's an exercise where you write your own version of Valeria's email."

      Monday Morning Standup — Last Week Recap
      A2.1 · Single speaker · ~90 seconds · American English

      ▶ Show ElevenLabs Script

      Good morning, team. Thanks for joining. Let me give you a quick update on last week before we start.

      On Monday, I reviewed the Q3 targets with the finance team. We confirmed the budget numbers and submitted the final version to management that afternoon.

      On Tuesday and Wednesday, I scheduled two calls with our suppliers in Dallas. The first supplier confirmed the delivery — the materials arrived on time on Thursday. The second supplier cancelled our call, so I rescheduled it for this Friday at 2 PM.

      ⏱ Target: 85–95 sec · Voice: Sarah · Speed: 0.82 · No "Celaya" or Spanish city names

      Listening Activities
      Listen, then answer.
      What did the speaker do on Monday?
      What happened with the second supplier call?
      The first supplier's materials arrived on time on Thursday.
      The speaker submitted the final report on Thursday evening.
      The team completed 8 out of 10 tasks last week.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Listen 1: global — "What are the main topics?" Listen 2: focused — answer the comprehension questions. Listen 3 (optional): shadow the standup format.
      • L2 trap: "submitted the final report on Thursday evening" — the script says he DIDN'T finish it on Thursday; he submitted it Friday morning. Very common listening trap at A2.
      • After: "Can you do a similar 60-second standup about YOUR last week? Use at least 5 past verbs." — oral production transfer.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "This is exactly the Monday morning call at your company — in English. Can you see yourself doing this?"
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • Homework: listen 3× and write down every past verb you hear. "I counted 12+ past verbs in 90 seconds — that's the density of real professional English."
      § 07
      Wrap-Up
      5 min
      Today I learned…
      • Simple past regular verbs: finish → finished · submit → submitted.
      • Negative: "I didn't finish" — NOT "I didn't finished".
      • Questions: "Did you review?" NOT "Did you reviewed?"
      • Short answers: "Yes, I did." / "No, I didn't."
      • I can describe my work from yesterday using past tense.
      Key Summary
      Affirmative
      I finished · submitted · confirmed · reviewed
      Negative
      I didn't finish (base verb — never -ed)
      Question
      Did you review it? → Yes, I did.
      Spelling
      +ed · +d (ends in -e) · double+ed (CVC)

      Format: Debate

      "Is it better to send a detailed end-of-day report OR give a quick verbal update? Defend your position using past examples from your work."

      Use simple past throughout. Teacher takes the opposite view and challenges with questions: "Did you ever miss something important in a verbal update?"
