Preguntas de seguimiento para el maestro
"Is there anything specifically forbidden in this environment? Why?" — push for mustn't
"What do workers HAVE TO do by law in this place? Any safety requirements?" — push for have to

"How does this compare to your own workplace? Which rules are the same? Which are different?"
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • S1B: producción instintiva. "Choose one, go." Si el alumno usa un modal incorrecto (mustn't cuando debería decir don't have to) → anota, no corrijas. La corrección va al final.
  • Esta picture prompt activa los 4 modales en contexto visual — sin que el alumno tenga que "estudiar" nada. La imagen hace el trabajo cognitivo.
  • 5 minutos duros. El corazón de S1B está en §5 y §6.
Capa 2 · Engagement

§ 03
Live Game — Wordwall & Modal Buzz
10–12 min
Game 1 — 5 min · Wordwall
Must · Mustn't · Have to · Don't have to — Match the Situation
[Wordwall iframe — Match Up template]

Pairs: it's forbidden→mustn't / it's compulsory→must / it's optional→don't have to / the law requires it→have to / no choice→must / your decision→don't have to / not allowed→mustn't / external rule→have to

Round 1: normal pace · Round 2: beat your time
Maestro comparte pantalla. Alumno une cada situación con el modal correcto. 2 rondas. Para grupos: primer equipo en completar gana.
Game 2 — 5 min · Modal Buzz

Alumno responde ANTES de hacer clic. Para grupos: punto al que responde primero.

MODAL?
"It's forbidden to smoke here."
mustn't — "You mustn't smoke here." ❌ forbidden
MODAL?
"The meeting is optional."
don't have to — "You don't have to attend." ✅ your choice
ERROR?
"You must to sign in."
✅ "You must sign in." — no "to" after must!
3RD PERSON
"She have to attend."
✅ "She HAS to attend." — 3rd person: have → has
CONTRAST!
mustn't vs
don't have to
mustn't = FORBIDDEN ❌ / don't have to = OPTIONAL ✅ — opposite meanings!
CHUNK
"Attendance is ___."
compulsory — = must attend. "Attendance is compulsory — no exceptions."
MODAL?
"The safety helmet is required by law."
have to — "Workers have to wear a safety helmet." (external law)
ERROR?
"Do I must attend the meeting?"
✅ "Do I have to attend?" or "Must I attend?" — no "do must"!
CHUNK
"It's ___ to you."
up — "It's up to you." = you don't have to. Your choice.
MODAL?
"Company policy: wear your badge inside."
must — "Employees must wear their badge." (internal company rule)
CHUNK
"No ___."
exceptions — "No exceptions." = everyone must do it. Non-negotiable.
HR PHRASE
"It is not ___ to…"
permitted — "It is not permitted to use social media during work hours." = mustn't
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Wordwall 5 min, Buzz 5 min. Si el alumno falla en la Buzz card "mustn't vs don't have to" → ronda adicional solo con esa pareja crítica.
  • La Buzz card #5 (CONTRAST!) es la más importante del juego. Dale tiempo extra si necesita.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "¿Ves cómo ya no dudas entre must y have to? Eso es el sistema instalándose. Ya no buscas la regla — ya sale."

🎙
Monologue: "Your First Week — What You Need to Know"
Speaker: Roberto Salinas, Team Leader · Duration: ~90 seconds · A2.2 · Normal-fast pace

Genera con ElevenLabs voz Adam o Brian · Conversational team leader tone · Normal-fast pace · No music
Archivo: GT_A2.2_M2_S1B_Listening_v1.mp3

▶ Show ElevenLabs Script
MONOLOGUE — TEAM LEADER INDUCTION, FASTER THAN S1A

"OK, welcome to the team. Laura from HR already covered the official policies — I just want to give you the real picture.

First, the non-negotiables. You must be at your desk by 8:30 — our clients are in a different time zone and calls start early. And you mustn't be late to client calls — ever. That has to be your number one priority.

Confidentiality — you mustn't discuss project details outside the office. Not with friends, not with family. I know it sounds strict, but we have to follow this — it's in the contract.

Now what you don't have to worry about: the dress code. You don't have to dress formally unless you have a client visit. Jeans and a shirt are fine on normal days. And you don't have to eat lunch at your desk — we have a kitchen and a proper break room.

One thing you must do every Friday: send me a brief update on your week. Just three bullet points — what you completed, what's pending, and any blockers. Five minutes maximum. That's it. Any questions? Great. Let's get started."
🔧 ElevenLabs: Multilingual v2 · Stability 60% · Similarity 78% · Style 35% · Rate = 1.0. Tono de equipo — cálido, directo, coloquial. Ligeramente más rápido que el monólogo de S1A. Pausa natural entre secciones.
Listening Task — Identify the Modal
Listen once. Select the correct answer for each question.
What time must employees be at their desk?
Can employees be late to client calls?
Do employees have to dress formally every day?
What must employees do every Friday?
How many times does Roberto use "don't have to"? What does each one refer to?
🎙 Post-Listening — Your Team's Real Rules (90 seconds)
1
OK, so in my team / company, there are a few things you must do: [obligación importante].
2
You mustn't [algo prohibido] — it's in the [contract / policy / safety rules].
3
You also have to [obligación externa]. That's a hard requirement.
4
But you don't have to [algo opcional]. That's completely your choice.
5
Any questions? Great — let's get started. Welcome to the team.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • 1a escucha: global. 2a: preguntas. 3a opcional: alumno cuenta cuántas veces escucha cada modal (must=3, mustn't=2, have to=2, don't have to=3).
  • Monologue Imitation: 60 segundos de preparación → monólogo en voz alta usando SU empresa real. El scaffold es la red de seguridad.
  • El audio de S1B es intencionalmente más rápido y coloquial que el de S1A (Laura HR). Esto es progresión real — del lenguaje formal al registro de equipo.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Roberto usa los 4 modales en 90 segundos en una conversación real de equipo. Tú acabas de escucharlos — y ahora los vas a usar en tu propia versión."
§ 05
Role Play — HR Interview & Policy Briefing
12–15 min
Role Play 1 — Guided · 5 min
💼 New Employee HR Interview
Maestro = new employee joining the company. Alumno = HR manager explaining the company policies. El alumno usa el scaffold de su columna — describe reglas reales de su empresa o de IndusMex.
🎧 Maestro — New Employee
"Hi, I'm starting today. Can you tell me about the main rules?"
"Do I have to wear a badge at all times?"
"What things am I not allowed to do? What's forbidden?"
"Do I have to attend all the team meetings?"
"Is there a dress code? Do I have to dress formally?"
"What reports do I have to submit? How often?"
"Are there any rules I don't have to worry about?"
🗣 Alumno — HR Manager
"Welcome! Yes — all employees must [rule 1]."
"Yes, you must / have to wear your badge. No exceptions."
"You mustn't [prohibition]. That's in the policy."
"The team review is compulsory — you have to attend."
"You don't have to dress formally — business casual is fine."
"You have to send me a weekly update. Every Friday."
"The social lunch is optional — you don't have to come."
Role Play 2 — Free · 7 min · Sin scaffold
🏢 Your Company's Induction — No Script
Mismos roles, pero ahora el alumno habla de SU empresa real. Sin scaffold. El alumno hace el briefing de inducción completo usando los 4 modales correctamente. Mínimo 6 turnos.
🎯 Free Role Play Rules
El alumno debe incluir: must para una obligación interna real · mustn't para algo genuinamente prohibido · have to para algo que exige la ley o el cliente · don't have to para algo opcional de su empresa · Corrección espontánea si el maestro provoca el error mustn't vs don't have to.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • RP2: en el rol de new employee, provocar el error mustn't vs don't have to. Por ejemplo: "So I mustn't eat my lunch at my desk?" El alumno debe corregir: "No — you DON'T HAVE TO. It's optional."
  • Si el alumno confunde mustn't y don't have to en el RP libre → al final: "¿Cuál usaste aquí? ¿Mustn't o don't have to? ¿Cuál era correcto?" — metacognición activa.
  • Estándar A2.2: los 4 modales usados correctamente en contexto real = mastery de §5.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Acabas de dar una inducción de empresa en inglés — a un nuevo colega, con los 4 modales, con reglas reales de tu empresa. Eso es inglés corporativo nivel B1. En A2.2."
Capa 3 · Retención & Upsell
  • "En M3 usas should y could para dar consejos — 'You should update your CV' / 'You could try a different approach.' El sistema modal sigue creciendo. M2 es el fundamento."
  • Upsell: "El inglés de HR — policies, obligations, permissions — es exactamente lo que usan los managers y directores en empresas internacionales del Bajío. Este inglés tiene valor de negocio directo."
§ 06
Debate — Are the Rules Fair?
8–10 min
💬 Opinion Debate · A2.2 Level
Which workplace rules are fair — and which ones should change?
"Roberto told the new employee they must be at their desk by 8:30 every day — even when there are no early client calls. And they mustn't discuss work with family — ever. Are these rules fair? Do companies have the right to control employees this way?"
✅ The rules are fair — arguments
"Companies must protect client data — they have to follow the law."
"Employees have to be on time — clients mustn't wait."
"These rules exist because someone broke them. There must be a reason."
"When you sign a contract, you must follow the company's policies."
⚠️ Some rules should change — arguments
"Employees don't have to be at a desk by 8:30 if they work remotely."
"Companies mustn't control what you say to your family."
"Flexible rules attract better talent — rigid policies don't have to exist."
"Trust is more effective than rules — you don't have to control people to get results."
💡 Maestro: toma la posición contraria. Mínimo 4 turnos. Objetivo: el alumno usa los 4 modales ARGUMENTATIVAMENTE — no solo en respuestas reactivas sino en argumentos constructivos.
Preguntas adicionales para grupos o si sobra tiempo
"What rule in your company do you think MUST change? Why? Use: 'I think we must / shouldn't have to...'"
"Are there any rules that you HAVE TO follow but think are unfair? Why do they exist?" — debate obligation vs fairness
"What's one rule every company MUST have, in your opinion? Why is it non-negotiable?" — use must for genuine strong obligation
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • El debate es la prueba final de internalización. El alumno que usa must y mustn't en argumentos — no solo en ejemplos — ha internalizado la función comunicativa, no solo la forma.
  • Para grupos: 2 equipos, 4 minutos de debate, luego voto. Maestro es árbitro.
  • Corrección post-debate: 1 error modal + 1 celebración. Solo 1 de cada.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Acabas de debatir políticas de empresa en inglés — usando must, mustn't, have to y don't have to como argumentos, no como ejercicio. Eso es inglés real."
Capa 3 · Retención & Upsell
  • Testimonial trigger — MANDATORIO: "¿Cuál fue más difícil — S1A o S1B? ¿Cuál fue más tuya? ¿En cuál usaste más tu propio inglés?" — Deja que responda completamente.
  • "S2A trae más vocabulario de obligación + situaciones de seguridad y compliance internacional. El sistema modal que construiste hoy es el fundamento para M3 (should / could) y B1."
  • Upsell corporativo: "El inglés de obligaciones y permisos — must, have to, mustn't — es el primer inglés que te piden en empresas multinacionales. Ya lo tienes."

Key Summary — M2 · S1A + S1B
must
Strong internal obligation — company decides. Structure: must + BASE VERB. "Employees must sign in."
mustn't
PROHIBITION — forbidden, not allowed. mustn't + BASE VERB. "You mustn't share passwords."
have to / has to
External obligation — law or company requires it. "I have to submit this. She has to attend."
don't have to
OPTIONAL — not necessary. don't have to = your choice. NOT the same as mustn't!
Critical contrast
mustn't = forbidden ❌ · don't have to = optional ✅ — OPPOSITE meanings. The #1 error at A2.2.
