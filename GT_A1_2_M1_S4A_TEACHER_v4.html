§ 01
Warm-Up — Scenario
5–8 min
📍 The Scenario
Situación
Es lunes por la mañana. Tienes una videollamada a las 9 AM con David Chen, tu contacto en Canadá. Antes de comenzar la agenda, quieres abrir la conversación de forma natural — no ir directamente al negocio. ¿Qué dices? ¿Cómo abres la llamada?
1
How do you greet David at the start of the call?
Try: "Good morning, David! How are you?"
2
How do you ask about the weather in a natural way?
Try: "How's the weather over there in ___?"
3
David forgot to send the agenda before the call. What do you say?
Try: "Please ___" or "Could you ___?" — polite instruction
4
At the end: how do you tell everyone NOT to forget the next meeting date?
Try: "Don't ___. Our next call is on ___."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Este escenario activa los DOS puntos de gramática de hoy: imperativos (Q3, Q4) y weather small talk (Q2). El alumno probablemente no puede hacerlo perfectamente aún — está bien. Esto es activación.
  • Q3 respuesta más probable: "Please send the agenda." Anota si dicen "Please to send" — ese es el error clave de hoy. Abórdalo en §4.
  • Q4: "Don't forget the next meeting." Verifica: ¿usan "Don't" o "No forget"? La interferencia del español es fuerte aquí.
  • Tiempo: máximo 8 min. Mantén conversacional y ligero — es un preview, no un test.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "This is a real Monday morning at your company. Do you have calls like this with international contacts?"
  • "By the end of today, opening this call in perfect English will be automatic."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Native speakers notice the first 30 seconds of a call. Today we perfect those 30 seconds."

Vocabulary — Work Verbs & Weather
10–12 min
instruction
/ɪnˈstrʌkʃən/
"Follow the instructions on screen."
confirm
/kənˈfɜːrm/
"Please confirm your attendance by Friday."
attach
/əˈtætʃ/
"Please attach the report to your email."
avoid
/əˈvɔɪd/
"Don't avoid answering client emails."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • 4 verbos de trabajo + 4 adjetivos de clima = 8 total. División limpia, fácil de recordar.
  • "attach" — solo se usa con archivos/documentos. "Please attach your CV" ✓. "Please attach your idea" ✗ — no es un adjunto físico.
  • "avoid" /əˈvɔɪd/ — el sonido OI es inglés, no español. Practica: uh-VOID.
  • Clima: haz clic en cada botón para escuchar. Drill: el maestro imita el clima, el alumno dice la palabra. Luego el alumno describe el clima de hoy en Celaya.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "¿Cómo está el clima hoy? Descríbelo en inglés. ¿Sunny? ¿Warm? ¿Cloudy?" — práctica real inmediata.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Temperature tip: con clientes americanos, convierte a Fahrenheit. 28°C = 82°F. Siempre di la unidad: '82 degrees Fahrenheit' o solo '82 degrees.'"

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Card Deck: el maestro lee cada situación en voz alta, el alumno produce el imperativo ANTES de voltear. Luego voltea para comparar. Esto es práctica de producción, no solo reconocimiento.
  • Etiquetas de registro (Formal/Neutral/Informal) crean conciencia de que los imperativos varían por contexto — crítico para inglés corporativo.
  • Si el alumno produce un imperativo diferente pero correcto: acéptalo. La respuesta modelo es una opción, no la única.

§ 04
Grammar — Imperatives & Small Talk
12–15 min
Structure — Imperatives at Work
TypeStructureExampleRegister
Affirmative Base verb (no subject) Confirm your attendance. Direct / neutral
Polite Please + base verb Please confirm by Thursday. Formal / polite
Negative Don't + base verb Don't forget the deadline. Direct / neutral
Polite neg. Please don't + base verb Please don't share this yet. Formal / polite
Key rule: No subject in imperatives. The verb always comes first.
✅ "Open the file." — ✗ "You open the file." (sounds unnatural / not an imperative)
✅ "Don't forget." — ✗ "You don't forget." (this is a negative statement, not a command)
⚠ Most Common Errors — Correct immediately
❌ "No forget the report."→✅ Don't forget the report.
❌ "Please to confirm the meeting."→✅ Please confirm the meeting. (no "to" after Please)
❌ "You save the file please."→✅ Please save the file. (no subject)
❌ "Don't to use your phone."→✅ Don't use your phone. (don't + base verb only)
Weather Small Talk — The Professional Call Opener
You — Houston
Good morning, David! How's the weather over there in Vancouver?
David — Canada
Hi! It's freezing here today — minus eight! How about Houston?
You — Houston
Oh wow! It's sunny and warm here — about 75 degrees. Very pleasant!
David — Canada
Lucky you! Anyway — let's get started.
You — Houston
Great. Please confirm receipt of the agenda I sent this morning.
Activity 1 — Choose the Correct Imperative
Haz clic en la opción correcta.
Your colleague needs to send an important file. You say: "___"
Someone's phone rings during a presentation. You say: "___"
You ask a client to confirm a meeting. You write: "___"
A manager reminds everyone about a document: "___"
Activity 2 — Complete the Imperative
Escribe la forma correcta. Usa el verbo entre corchetes.
1. ___ the report to your manager before 5 PM. [send]
2. ___ your laptop during the security training. [not open]
3. Please ___ the signed contract to your reply. [attach]
4. ___ confidential files by regular email. [not share]
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Q2 error más común: "No use your phone" (transferencia directa del español: "No usar el teléfono"). Refuerza: en inglés siempre DON'T, no NO.
  • Activity 2 (fill-in): acepta variaciones de mayúsculas. "send" o "Send" ambos correctos para Q1. Enfócate en la forma del verbo, no la capitalización en A1.2.
  • Después de las actividades: el alumno abre el escenario de §1 de nuevo — esta vez correctamente. Bucle real de mejora.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • Weather conversation: role-play en vivo. El maestro es David en Canadá. El alumno abre la llamada. Mantenlo 60 segundos.
  • "Notice how the call feels different with small talk? More professional, warmer. 30 seconds of weather = much better relationship."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In A2 you'll use 'Could you please confirm?' — more formal still. For now, 'Please confirm' is exactly right."
§ 05
Reading — Office Guidelines Email
8–10 min
FROM
Patricia Morales <s.mitchell@celaya-corp.com>
TO
All Staff
📎 Office Guidelines — Please Read Carefully

Dear team,

Welcome to our updated office guidelines. Please read carefully and follow these instructions.

Working hours:
Arrive at the office by 8:30 AM. Don't be late to team meetings — they start on time. Please inform your manager if you are absent.

Digital tools:
Confirm all external emails within 24 hours. Attach relevant documents before sending. Don't avoid responding to client messages — this is a professional priority.
Please don't share confidential information via personal email.

Meetings:
Don't use your phone during presentations. Please turn it to silent. Send your update before the meeting — don't wait until the last minute.

Follow these guidelines and we will have a productive, professional environment. Thank you!

Patricia Morales · HR Manager · Vertex Industrial Group
Comprehension
Responde las preguntas.
What should you do if you are absent?
What should you do before a meeting?
You should NOT share confidential information via personal email.
It is OK to use your phone during presentations.
External emails must be confirmed within 24 hours.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Texto verde = imperativos afirmativos/polite. Texto rojo = imperativos negativos. Pide al alumno que cuente: ¿cuántos verdes? ¿Cuántos rojos?
  • Trampa RQ2: "Confirm the agenda" suena correcto pero el texto dice "Send your update before the meeting." Los alumnos que leen superficialmente eligen la incorrecta.
  • Lectura en voz alta: el maestro lee una sección, el alumno repite cada imperativo. Enfócate en la entonación — los imperativos bajan en tono al final.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "¿Tu empresa tiene lineamientos como estos? ¿Hay alguna regla que desearías que tuvieran? Di una: 'Please ___.' o 'Don't ___.' "
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In A2 you'll write emails like this — giving instructions professionally. Today you read them. Tomorrow you write them."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Tarea de enfoque para la 1ª escucha: "Cuenta cuántos imperativos escuchas." (Respuesta: 8+ — please confirm, don't forget, don't avoid, please reply, please attach, don't send, please don't share, follow).
  • Trampa LQ2: "the contract" suena correcto (ella lo menciona) pero dice confirmar el contrato, NO adjuntarlo. Adjuntar = el progress report.
  • Después de actividades: shadowing de la sección de instrucciones. "Please confirm… Don't forget… Don't avoid…" — drilla la entonación.

Capa 3 · Growth & Retention

§ SP
Speaking Practice
10–12 min
🗣 Guided Speaking — Pair / Group
Imperatives at Work
Answer each question using imperatives — positive and negative. Real work context. 2–3 sentences per answer.
Question 1
"Give me 3 instructions you hear regularly at work — positive imperatives."
Hint: At work, my manager says: "Send / Confirm / Check / Submit / Review ___."
Question 2
"What are 2 things that are NOT allowed in your workplace — use negative imperatives."
Hint: Don't use ___ in meetings. Don't forget to ___. Never send ___ without ___.
Question 3
"How do you start a Monday morning call with your team? Give 3 opening instructions."
Hint: Good morning everyone. Please check ___. Don't forget ___. Let's start with ___.
Question 4
"What instructions would you give a new colleague on their first day?"
Hint: Always arrive ___. Don't forget to ___. Check ___ every morning. Send ___ before ___.
Question 5
"What is the most important rule in your department? How would you say it as an imperative?"
Hint: The most important rule is: Always ___. Never ___. Always confirm ___ before ___.
⏱ 10–12 min📋 5 questions🎯 Positive + negative imperatives👥 Teacher-led
⚡ Speaking Challenge
"You are a manager opening a Monday call. Give your team 5 instructions — mix positive and negative. Use real work language. 60 seconds. No notes."
Evaluate: at least 3 positive imperatives, at least 2 negative imperatives (Don't/Never), professional work vocabulary used, student speaks 45+ seconds.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Error más común en A1.2: añadir sujeto al imperativo ("You send the report" en lugar de "Send the report"). Recast: "Imperative — no subject. Just 'Send.' Try again."
  • Q3 es la más valiosa — simula un contexto real de call apertura. Si el alumno lo hace bien, está listo para S4B role play.
  • Speaking Challenge: 3 positivos + 2 negativos + vocabulario profesional + 45 seg. Si los 4: S4A completada.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Acabas de abrir una reunión en inglés con 5 instrucciones reales. Eso es lo que hacen los managers internacionales cada lunes."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "En S4B haces un role play completo de una llamada internacional — apertura, instrucciones, preguntas. Hoy preparaste el lenguaje exacto."

🗣 Speaking Challenge
Format · Info Gap
"You are a manager opening a Monday call. Give your team 5 instructions — they must write them down and read them back."
El maestro es el "equipo". El alumno da 5 instrucciones (mix de afirmativas, negativas y polite). El maestro las escribe. Luego el maestro las lee — el alumno verifica si son correctas.
Manager (Student) — Give these
Open with weather small talk — 2 sentences.
Give 2 affirmative imperatives about today's tasks.
Give 1 "Please + verb" instruction about a deadline.
Give 1 "Don't + verb" instruction about sharing files.
Close: "Please confirm receipt of this call." or similar.
Team (Teacher) — Write down & read back
Write each imperative as you hear it.
After 5 instructions, read them back aloud.
Student confirms: correct or incorrect?
Error in teacher's version = student must repeat the instruction correctly.
Optional: swap roles — teacher gives instructions, student writes.

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Info Gap: la habilidad real es escuchar con precisión y escribir imperativos correctamente. Si el maestro escribe "Don't forget" y el alumno dio "Please don't forget" — acepta ambos como variantes correctas.
  • La ronda de intercambio (maestro da, alumno escribe) es el desafío más difícil. Úsala si el tiempo lo permite.
  • Evalúa: ¿formas de imperativo correctas? ¿Sin "No + verb"? ¿Sin "Please to + verb"? ¿Weather small talk natural?
Capa 2 · Engagement
