Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Timer runs 30 seconds per question — teacher controls pacing with START/RESET. Press START as you ask Q1, then START again for Q2, etc.

§ 04
Grammar — 3rd Person Simple Present
12–15 min
Rule — He / She / It + verb + S
In simple present, he / she / it always adds -s (or -es / -ies) to the verb.
I work → She works · You send → He sends · We check → It checks
I / You / We / They
work
"I work on Mondays."
"They send reports weekly."
He / She / It
works
"She works on Mondays."
"He sends reports weekly."
Special endings
-es / -ies
go → goes
study → studies
do → does
How often does she…? — 3rd person questions
QuestionShort answerFull answer
How often does she send the report?Once a week.She sends the report once a week.
How often does he check the targets?Every day.He checks the targets every day.
How often does the team meet?Twice a month.The team meets twice a month.
How often does she travel?Rarely.She rarely travels for work.
⚠ Most Common Errors
❌ "She send the report every week."→✅ She sends the report every week.
❌ "How often does she sends?"→✅ How often does she send? (no -s in questions)
❌ "She don't travel often."→✅ She doesn't travel often.
Activity 1 — Choose the correct verb form
Click the correct option.
She ___ a progress report every Monday.
How often ___ Carlos check his targets?
The team ___ performance three times a year.
She ___ travel often — maybe once a quarter.
How often does she give a presentation? — "She ___ every month."
Activity 2 — Sentence Order
Drag the pieces to make a correct sentence.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Q2 is the key: "How often DOES Carlos check" — note that in the question, the verb loses its -s (check, not checks). This surprises students. Explain: does carries the 3rd person marker instead.
      • Q3 error: "review" vs "reviews" — the team = it = 3rd person. Students often treat collective nouns as plural (I/we) and forget the -s.
      • After activities: oral production. Teacher gives subject + frequency: "Carlos — every week." Student forms full sentence: "Carlos sends a report every week."
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Every time you talk about a colleague in English, you'll use this -s. It's a tiny rule with a giant impact."
      • Point to student's colleague: "Tell me about your manager using today's grammar — 3 sentences."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "In A2 you'll describe routines using 'used to' and future plans. But the -s rule stays forever in simple present."
      § 05
      Reading
      8–10 min
      Carlos Reyes
      Operations Manager · Grupo Industrial Nortek · Monterrey, Mexico

      My name is Carlos Reyes. I am the Operations Manager at Grupo Industrial Nortek. Let me tell you about my typical work week.

      I always arrive at the office at 8 AM. Every morning, I check my emails and organize my tasks for the day. I usually have a short call with my team at 8:30 — a 15-minute standup to check progress.

      My coordinator, Ana, sends me a weekly update every Monday. She also prepares the conference call agenda twice a month. Ana always meets her targets — her performance is excellent.

      The team gives a presentation to senior management once a quarter. I never miss these — they are very important for our targets.

      I rarely work on weekends. I finish at 6 PM. A good work-life balance is important!

      Comprehension
      Answer the questions.
      How often does Ana send Carlos an update?
      How often does the team give a presentation?
      Carlos never arrives at the office before 9 AM.
      Ana prepares the conference call agenda twice a month.
      Carlos often works on weekends.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • 3rd person -s is highlighted in cyan in the text — ask student to find and read ALL highlighted verb forms aloud.
      • Q comprehension trap: RQ1 — "twice a month" is also in the text (conference call). Students who skim choose it. Read carefully.
      • After comprehension: "Describe Ana using 3rd person. What does she do? How often?" — production transfer using the text as model.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Is Carlos's routine similar to yours? Describe one difference using 3rd person: 'My manager ___ every ___.' "
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "This is exactly the type of text you'll write about your own routine by S5."

      ▶ Show ElevenLabs Script

      Welcome to the team! I'm Carlos, the Operations Manager. Let me explain how we work here.

      Our team always starts the day at eight AM. Every morning, my coordinator Ana sends a progress update by email. She also prepares the agenda for our Monday standups — she always does this on Sunday evening.

      We check our targets every week on Fridays. The team usually meets its targets — Ana's performance is always excellent.

      We have a conference call with our suppliers twice a month — on the second and fourth Tuesday. Ana usually joins these calls and takes notes.

      We give a presentation to management once a quarter. Ana always prepares the slides — she does this very well.

      We rarely work on weekends. We value work-life balance here. Do you have any questions about our routine?

      ⏱ Target: 80–90 sec · Voice: Rachel · Speed: 0.83 · Stability: 0.6

      Listening Activities
      Listen, then answer.
      How often does Ana send a progress update?
      When does Ana prepare the Monday agenda?
      How often do they give a presentation to management?
      The team rarely works on weekends.
      The conference call is with clients, not suppliers.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Focus task for listen 1: "Count how many times you hear 'she' or 'her'." (Answer: 6+ times.) Builds awareness of 3rd person in natural speech.
      • L2 trap: "with our suppliers" — students who process quickly might miss "suppliers" and answer "clients." Play that sentence again if needed.
      • After activities: shadow Carlos's sentences about Ana — "Ana sends a progress update every morning…" Drill the -s ending out loud.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Carlos is onboarding a new employee — that could be YOU at an international company. This conversation is real."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • Homework: listen 3× and write down all the 3rd person verbs you hear. "Ana sends, she prepares, she does…" — count them.
      § 07
      Wrap-Up
      5 min
      Today I learned…
      • He/She/It + verb-s: she sends, he checks, the team meets.
      • Questions: "How often does she send the report?"
      • Negative: "She doesn't travel often." (not "don't")
      • I know 8 new words: report, task, target, performance…
      • I can describe my colleague's routine in English.
      Key Summary
      3rd Person -S
      She sends · He checks · The team meets
      Question form
      "How often does she send?" → no -s in question
      Remember
      Does = question · Doesn't = negative. Both carry the 3rd person so the main verb loses its -s.
