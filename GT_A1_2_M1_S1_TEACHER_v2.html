Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Student clicks their choice — then explains HOW OFTEN. This is the bridge to today's grammar. Listen for any frequency adverbs they use naturally.
  • Expected answers: "I always check email", "I usually stay home on weekends", "I rarely exercise in the morning." Note errors in adverb position — correct in §4.
  • If student doesn't know adverbs yet: accept Spanish approximations ("siempre", "a veces") — translate and note them. This is pre-instruction activation.
  • Timing: max 7 min. 4 pairs × ~90 seconds each.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Your choices tell me a lot about your routine — and in English, we describe routines with very specific words. That's today's session."
  • If student picks the same option for all 4: "Interesting! You're very consistent. Let's build the English to describe that consistency."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Every performance review, every team update, every international email — they all use these same frequency words. Today you get the full toolkit."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Key pronunciation: "leisure" /ˈleʒər/ — NOT "lei-SURE". This catches most students. Drill it 3 times.
  • "schedule" — American: /ˈskedʒuːl/, British: /ˈʃedʒuːl/. Either is fine; context is American English.
  • Frequency scale: drill as a scale. Teacher says a percentage, student says the adverb. Then reverse.
  • Connect vocabulary to student's real life immediately: "Do you have a workout routine? Is it typical for you?"

§ 04
Grammar — Adverbs of Frequency
15 min
Rule 1 — Adverb position: BEFORE the main verb
Subject + adverb + verb (for all verbs EXCEPT to be)
Subject + to be + adverb (when the verb IS "to be")
SubjectAdverbVerbRestNote
Ialwayscheckmy emails in the morning.✓ adverb before verb
Sheusuallyhasa workout on Mondays.✓ adverb before verb
Wesometimestravelfor work.✓ adverb before verb
Heis always—on time.✓ AFTER to be
❌ I check always my emails.→✅ I always check my emails.
❌ I never not work on Sundays.→✅ I never work on Sundays. (never = already negative)
Rule 2 — How often…? Questions
QuestionShort answerFull answer
How often do you have a workout?Twice a week.I have a workout twice a week.
How often does she travel?Once a month.She travels once a month.
How often do they check the schedule?Every day.They check the schedule every day.
Frequency expressions
every day every Monday once a week twice a week three times a month on weekends

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Q2: "is never" — the rule exception (after to be). Students often answer "never is" — address the exception immediately.
  • Q5: "I run" — simple present conjugation. I/You/We/They = base form. He/She/It = add -s/-es. Don't over-explain here; just establish the pattern.
  • Spinner: do 5–6 rounds. Student must produce a TRUE sentence each time. If they repeat — spin again. The no-repeat rule adds productive pressure.
  • Spinner note: teacher mode shows the spinner. Student mode hides it to keep it as a classroom tool (teacher controls the pacing).
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Every spin, a new sentence. Let's see how many you can make — challenge yourself to use different verbs each time."
  • Celebrate creative sentences: "I never eat breakfast" → "Tell me more! Why never?" — use English to explore naturally.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "These sentences are the foundation of your monthly status report in English. 'I usually meet my targets. I always deliver on time.' That's real professional English."
§ 05
Reading
8–10 min
Read · ~90 words · A1.2 level

My name is Daniela Rojas. I am a project coordinator at Grupo Norte. I have a very active routine.

I always wake up at 6 AM. I usually do a workout before work — I run or go to the gym. I never skip breakfast.

At work, I often check my schedule first. I sometimes have a conference call in the morning. In my spare time, I usually read or take photos. Photography is my favourite hobby.

How often do you exercise? How often do you check your schedule?

Comprehension
Answer the questions.
1. Daniela never does exercise in the morning.
2. She sometimes skips breakfast.
3. Her hobby is photography.
How often does Daniela check her schedule?
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Ask student to underline all adverbs of frequency in the text: always, usually, never, often, sometimes, usually. Count: 6 adverbs in one short text.
  • Q1 trap: "usually does a workout" ≠ "never exercises". Students who skim fail this.
  • After comprehension: "Tell me about YOUR routine using the same adverbs as Daniela." Immediate production transfer.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Is Daniela's routine similar to yours? What would you change in her routine?"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In your Moodle practice, this text has an audio version. Listen and shadow Daniela's sentences."

▶ Show ElevenLabs Script

Hello! My name is Daniela. I am a project coordinator. I have a very active routine.

I always wake up at six in the morning. I usually do a workout before work. Sometimes I run, and sometimes I go to the gym. I never skip breakfast — breakfast is very important for me.

At work, I often check my schedule first thing in the morning. My schedule is always busy. I sometimes have a conference call at eight AM. I always prepare my agenda the night before.

In my spare time, I usually read or take photos. Photography is my favourite hobby. How often do you exercise? How often do you check your schedule? Tell me about your routine!

⏱ Target: 75–85 sec · Voice: Rachel · Speed: 0.85

Listening Activities
Listen, then answer.
How often does Daniela do a workout?
True or False: Daniela always skips breakfast.
What does she do in her spare time?
When does she prepare her agenda?
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Listen 1: global understanding. Listen 2: answer questions. Listen 3 (if needed): shadow specific sentences.
  • LQ3 (spare time): students often say "photography" — but the question asks what she DOES, not her hobby label. "Read or take photos" is the specific answer.
  • After: "Shadow Daniela — repeat each sentence immediately after her. Match her rhythm, not just her words."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Daniela's English is exactly what your A1.2 sounds like at the end of this module. That's your target."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Homework: listen 3× and shadow. "Try recording yourself on your phone — compare your rhythm to Daniela's."
