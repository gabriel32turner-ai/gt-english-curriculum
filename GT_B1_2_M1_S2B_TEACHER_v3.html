§ 01
Warm-Up — Picture Prompt
Activate S2A vocabulary · PPC from instinct · No notes
5 min

Look at the three images. Choose the one that most closely resembles a real situation you've witnessed at work — a moment involving gossip, trust damage, or a conversation that crossed a line. Then describe what has been happening — in PPC. No notes. No hesitation.

01
The Whisper — Someone has been saying something they shouldn't
SELECTED
02
The Tension — Trust has been deteriorating and nobody is addressing it
SELECTED
03
The Vent — Someone has been carrying something they finally had to say
SELECTED
Tell me about your image using PPC: "I chose image __ because in my team, [something] has been happening for [duration] — specifically, [person/group] has been [verb+ing]… and nobody has been [addressing/saying/confronting] it."
Teacher Follow-Up
"Who has been most affected by this situation? Give me a PPC sentence."
"Has trust been deteriorating as a result? How long has this been going on?"
"S2A review: was it gossip or venting? Apply the boundary rule — third party rule."
"What would you do if you were the most senior person in that room? 2nd conditional + PPC."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • S2B — el alumno llega con el vocabulario y la grammar de S2A. Si produce "trust deteriorated" (simple past) en lugar de "trust has been deteriorating" → recast: "Is it over? Is the damage still ongoing? → PPC."
  • La imagen que elige se convierte en el núcleo del Chain Challenge en §3. Nota qué eligió — es la evidencia que usará en el debate.
  • Output mínimo aceptable: "Trust has been deteriorating since / for…" con al menos un detalle específico = warm-up exitoso.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Last class you learned the language. Today you use it without the grammar table. Cualquier oración PPC con 'has been' + -ing = win."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In 50 minutes you'll defend a debate position for 2 minutes using only PPC as evidence. That's the S2B mastery target."

Recap Speedrun — S2A Key Points
Flip before using — pure activation, zero new content
5 min

⚡ Haz clic en cada tarjeta — la respuesta aparece al reverso. Di la respuesta ANTES de hacer clic.

1
PPC Formula
tap to flip
have/has + been + verb-ing
"Trust has been deteriorating since the merger."
2
Gossip Rule
tap to flip
Third party not present = gossip
"If someone who isn't there is being judged — that's gossip, not venting."
3
Key Error
tap to flip
"The rumor has been spread" = WRONG
"He has been spreading the rumor." — PPC is active, not passive.
4
PPC vs Past
tap to flip
"Damaged" = over. "Has been damaging" = still now.
PPC = the wound is still open and needs attention now.
5
Key Question
tap to flip
"How long has this been going on?"
Most natural, high-frequency PPC question in professional English.
✅ Mastery check S2A: el alumno produce "Trust has been deteriorating since…" o "Nobody has been addressing it for…" en menos de 10 segundos sin preparación. Si todavía usa simple past → un recordatorio: "Still happening now? → PPC."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • 5 tarjetas, 5 minutos. Alumno llama la respuesta antes de hacer clic. Ronda 2 a velocidad. El §3 Chain Challenge necesita esta activación. No gastes más tiempo aquí.
§ 03
Chain Challenge — Stop the Gossip
6 moments in a gossip chain · Intervene with PPC · Teacher marks each correct intervention
12 min
⚡ Engagement Resource — Gossip Chain Intervention
Each card below is a moment in a gossip chain. For each: the student INTERVENES — they play the person who stops the conversation at the right moment. Response must use PPC to describe what has been happening + redirect the conversation professionally.
Interventions ✓
0
Complete all 6 moments.
GT Originals — Scoring the Intervention
Mark ✓ only when the student: (1) uses PPC to name what has been happening, (2) redirects without moralizing — professional, not preachy. A good intervention sounds like: "Look — this has been affecting more people than just us. Let's not take it further." NOT: "This is wrong and we shouldn't gossip." The first is B1.2 leadership. The second is B1 preaching.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • 6 momentos. Para cada uno: alumno habla primero (30-45 segundos), luego hace clic en ✓ o ✗. No revelar el modelo antes. El alumno debe intentar antes de ver la referencia.
  • El reto: intervenir SIN sonar condescendiente. "We've all been venting — but this has been spreading beyond the two of us. Let's keep it here." = B1.2 professional. "Stop gossiping!" = no.
  • Momentos 4 y 5 son los más difíciles — involucran a superiores jerárquicos. Si el alumno se bloquea: "You're the most senior person here. What has been happening? And what do you want to protect?"
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "6 moments. You are the professional who stops the chain each time. The PPC is your evidence. Ready?"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Every intervention you just made is a real professional skill — knowing how to name what has been happening without assigning blame. That's management-level English."
§ 04
Role-Play — Run the Dialogue
You play Sarah from the listening · Stop Michael at the right moment · No script
12 min
S2A Listening: Sarah and Michael were talking about Daniel. When Carla's name entered the conversation, Sarah stopped it: "This is gossip now. We've crossed the line. Let's not take this any further."

Today: You play Sarah. I play Michael. Same conversation — but you must stop it at the right moment using PPC. No script. No notes. The moment you hear a third person's name — stop the chain.

🎭 Choose your role — then the role-play begins immediately.

Role A
Sarah
You start the conversation about Daniel. You notice the dynamic. Your job: stop it the moment it becomes gossip.
Goal: use PPC to name what has been happening — then close the conversation professionally.
Role B
Michael
You bring Carla's name into the conversation. You know it's too much — but it's been bothering you.
Goal: justify why you brought it up using PPC — then accept Sarah's stop with professionalism.
Role C · Hard Mode
The Manager
You walk into the conversation between Sarah and Michael at exactly the wrong moment. You hear what they're saying.
Goal: address what has been happening without exposing either person — leadership PPC throughout.
Role D · Expert Mode
HR Representative
Daniel has come to you. He's heard the rumor. You now need to investigate — starting with Sarah and Michael.
Goal: use PPC questions to investigate professionally. "How long has this been…? Who has been…?"
The role-play starts now. No notes. No script. I'll play the other character. You open with a PPC sentence. Stop the conversation at the right moment. Aim for: 4+ PPC uses, 1 gossip/vent vocabulary word, 0 simple past where PPC is needed.
Teacher Dialogue Prompts — Use these mid role-play
"How long has this been going on with Daniel, do you know?"
"Carla mentioned it to me too, actually. Do you think it's accurate?"
"Has Ana been doing anything about it, or has she been ignoring the tension?"
"Should we be having this conversation? What has been the effect on the team?"
"It's been bothering me too. But I'm worried this has been going further than just us two."
"If you were the manager — what would you do? What has been happening that's most serious?"
Self-Evaluation — Después del role-play
  • I used PPC at least 4 times correctly (has/have + been + -ing)
  • I used at least 1 vocabulary word from S2A (trust / rumor / spread / boundary / backfire)
  • I stopped the conversation at the right moment — when a third person entered
  • I sounded professional — not preachy, not aggressive
  • I spoke for 60+ seconds without stopping to translate
  • I kept "would" out of the if-clause if I used 2nd conditional
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • NO interrumpas durante el role-play. Mantén el personaje. Si el alumno hace un error de PPC — nota it, no corrijas en el momento. Corrección post-role-play: 1 elogio específico + 1 corrección con ejemploe.
  • Role D (HR) es el más desafiante — requiere preguntas PPC: "How long has Daniel been feeling this way?" "Who has been involved in spreading this?" Si el alumno puede hacer esto fluidamente = B1.2 mastery.
  • Si el alumno elige Role C o D, dale 2 minutos extra — son mucho más complejos en producción.
  • Segunda ronda opcional: mismo rol, incorporando correcciones. La segunda entrega siempre es notablemente más fuerte.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You just ran a real workplace conversation in English. No script. No grammar table. Just you, the language, and the situation. That is B1.2."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In S3A you'll describe your personality type — introvert vs extrovert — using PPC for observation patterns: 'I've been noticing that...' Same grammar foundation, completely different context."
§ 05
Debate Live — Your Position, Your PPC
Same motion from S2A · Your position · 2 minutes · No scaffolding
15 min
Choose your side. 2 minutes. No notes. Defend: "Gossip has been destroying professional environments for years — and companies have been enabling it by ignoring it."

Every argument must be backed by a PPC sentence. Use at least 3 colloquial phrases. Concede one point. The teacher will argue the opposite. No mercy.
✅ FOR the motion — Your ammunition
Reputations have been damaged by unchecked rumors for decades — I've been witnessing this.
HR has been ignoring informal communication networks — that IS complicity.
Companies haven't been training managers to identify gossip chains early enough.
The least-heard employees have been the most affected — and nobody has been measuring this.
❌ AGAINST the motion — The counter-arguments
Gossip has been part of human organizations since organizations existed — it's not new.
Informal channels have been filling the gap where official communication has been failing.
Companies have been improving — DEI, anonymous reporting, open-door policies have been working.
The real disease is poor management. Gossip has been a symptom — not the cause.
2:00
⏱ 2-Minute Debate Timer
Choose your side → press START → defend your position with PPC
▶ Show B1.2 Model Debate Answer (reveal AFTER student speaks)
"I'm arguing FOR the motion — and honestly though, I think most people in this room have been watching this happen for years without naming it. Companies have been treating gossip as a personality issue — 'oh, that's just how she is' — when actually, the real issue is that management hasn't been creating enough transparent communication. When people don't get information officially, they fill the gap. And what has been filling that gap? Rumor. Speculation. Damage. I'm not gonna lie — I think the company is as responsible as the individual. Fair enough, there will always be people who gossip more than others. But if the culture has been enabling it — through silence, through closed doors, through favoritism — then the company has been complicit. That's the motion I'm defending."
Teacher Challenge Prompts — Argue the opposite genuinely
"Honestly though — haven't companies been getting better? What about anonymous reporting tools?"
"I'm not gonna lie — hasn't informal communication been filling gaps where HR has been failing?"
"Fair enough — but hasn't gossip also been a way for powerless employees to share information?"
"If companies have been enabling gossip — what specifically have they been doing wrong? Give me PPC evidence."
"Trust has been deteriorating — but hasn't it been deteriorating because of management, not gossip itself?"
"What has YOUR company been doing about this? Has it been working?"
I've been noticing this pattern… Trust has been deteriorating… Companies have been enabling… Honestly though… I'm not gonna lie… Fair enough, but…
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • NO interrumpas durante los 2 minutos. Toma nota de: (a) PPC correctos, (b) colloquial phrases usadas, (c) si concede un punto (señal de B1.2 maturity).
  • Protocolo post-debate: (1) nombra el argumento PPC más fuerte, (2) señala el lugar donde podría haber concedido más efectivamente, (3) da la respuesta modelo. Compara.
  • Umbral mastery B1.2: 3+ PPC en 2 minutos + 2 colloquial phrases + 1 concesión = debate B1.2 pasado.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You argued about a real workplace issue — in English — with grammar, vocabulary, and argument structure. Not a drill. Not an exercise. A real position. That's what B1.2 sounds like."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "The colloquial phrases — 'honestly though', 'I'm not gonna lie', 'fair enough' — you used them naturally in a debate. Not forced. That is B1.2 vocabulary integration."

GT Flashcard Wall — 2 Rounds · S2A + S2B Key Language
Ronda 1: maestro da el prompt, alumno produce la oración PPC antes de hacer clic. Ronda 2: alumno conecta con su situación real.
Round 1 — Word → PPC Sentence
trust
tap to flip
Firm belief in reliability — being eroded over time.
"Trust has been deteriorating since the merger announcement."
spread
tap to flip
To move progressively from person to person.
"The rumor has been spreading across three departments since Friday."
backfire
tap to flip
To have the opposite of the intended effect.
"The strategy has been backfiring — it damaged her credibility, not his."
address
tap to flip
To deal with a problem directly.
"HR has been reluctant to address it — the rumor has been growing."
boundary
tap to flip
A line defining limits of acceptable behavior.
"The team hasn't been clear about the boundary between feedback and gossip."
Round 2 — Connect to YOUR workplace
"In my team, trust has been…"
your answer
Complete honestly:
"…deteriorating / strengthening / recovering since [point]. Because [specific reason]."
"A rumor has been spreading about…"
your answer
Don't name names — describe the dynamic:
"…a decision / change / person. It has been affecting [what]."
"Management has been / hasn't been…"
your answer
Be specific about what has (or hasn't) been happening:
"…addressing / communicating / acknowledging. The effect has been…"
"The gossip in my team has been…"
your answer
Name it, don't minimize it:
"…harmless / damaging / spreading / backfiring. The consequence has been…"
