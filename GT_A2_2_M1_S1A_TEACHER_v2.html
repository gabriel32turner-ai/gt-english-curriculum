§ 01
Warm-Up — Scenario
5–8 min
💼 Recruiter Call — 10 AM

Imagine: A recruiter calls you today. She says: "We have a great opportunity for you. Before we talk about the new role, can you tell me about your background? Where did you study? What did you do before your current job? What kind of work did you use to do?"

Tell me: What do you say? How do you describe your career history in English?

📌 Teacher Follow-Up Questions
"Where did you study? What did you use to study?"
"Did you use to work in the same industry? When did that change?"
"What is one thing you used to do at work that you don't do anymore?"
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This is A2.2's entry point for "used to." The recruiter scenario forces students to narrate career history before any grammar is taught — this exposes whether they already attempt "used to" or fall back to simple past for everything.
  • Listen for: attempts at "I used to work…" (great — reward it), use of simple past throughout without "used to" (normal — note it for §4 contrast), "I use to work" (missing -d — very common, noted for §4 error block).
  • Accept all responses. This is diagnostic, not evaluative. The discomfort with "how do I say what I USED to do?" is exactly the gap §4 fills.
  • Timing: let the student speak for 90 seconds uninterrupted. Then ask one follow-up. Move to §2 without overcorrecting.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "I'll ask you the same question at the end of class. Compare your two answers — that's your progress in 60 minutes."
  • "Every recruiter, every LinkedIn profile, every job interview asks this same thing. Today you get the exact language for it."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "By S1B, you'll tell your full career story for 90 seconds — without notes, without stopping. That's a professional skill people pay for."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Drill each verb in two ways: with "used to" (habitual past) and in simple past (specific event). "I used to intern at…" vs. "I interned there for 6 months."
  • Key pronunciation trap: "graduate" — the noun is /ˈɡrædʒuɪt/, the verb is /ˈɡrædʒueɪt/. Drill both. Most students use only the noun form.
  • "Hire" vs. "apply": students often confuse the perspective. "I applied" (candidate's action) vs. "They hired me" (company's action). Drill both sides.
  • Career nouns: ask student to make a sentence with "promotion" using simple past. Then with "internship" using "used to." This sets up the contrast for §4.

§ 04
Grammar — Used To vs. Simple Past
15–18 min
Structure — Used To
FormStructureCareer example
AffirmativeSubject + used to + base verbI used to work in quality control.
NegativeSubject + didn't use to + base verbShe didn't use to travel for work.
QuestionDid + subject + use to + base verb?Did you use to study engineering?
Short answerYes, I did. / No, I didn't."Did you use to manage a team?" — "Yes, I did."
Key meaning: "Used to" describes a habit or state that was true in the past but is NOT true now.
✅ "I used to wake up at 5 AM." → I don't anymore.
✅ "There used to be only 3 people on my team." → Now there are more.
✅ "I used to think engineering was my path." → Now I think differently.
When to Use Each — The Key Contrast
Used To — for habits & ongoing statesSimple Past — for specific events
Repeated action or habit: "I used to commute 2 hours every day." One specific event: "I left that company in 2018."
State that changed: "There used to be 5 people on my team." Specific moment: "They hired 10 more people in 2020."
Belief or feeling that changed: "I used to think I didn't like sales." Decision at a specific time: "I applied for a sales role in 2019."
❌ Cannot say: "I used to graduate in 2015." (one event) ✅ "I graduated in 2015." (specific moment)
Tip: If you can add "every day / every week / always / back then" and it still makes sense → use "used to."
If you can add "in 2015 / last year / on Monday / once" → use simple past.
⚠ Common Errors — A2.2
✗ I use to work there.→✓ I used to work there. (don't forget the -d)
✗ Did you used to travel for work?→✓ Did you use to travel? (Did already marks the past — no -d)
✗ I used to graduated in 2015.→✓ I graduated in 2015. (one event = simple past)
✗ I used to work in there.→✓ I used to work there. (no "in" before "there")
✗ I am used to work here since 2015.→✓ "Be used to" + -ing = different structure (B1 topic). Today: "I used to work here."

      § 05
      Reading — A Career Story
      8–10 min
      A2.2 · Used To + Simple Past · Professional Narrative
      From Engineering to Sales — Miguel Torres, Regional Sales Manager
      Watch for: used to (orange · habit/state) · simple past (purple · specific event) · time expressions (gold)

      My name is Miguel Torres, and I am now a Regional Sales Manager at an automotive company in Guanajuato.

      I used to study Industrial Engineering at the Universidad de Guanajuato. I graduated in 2012 and immediately applied for an internship at a manufacturing company in Celaya.

      Back then, I used to work in quality control — I used to inspect parts and write reports every day. I didn't use to think about sales at all. Then, in 2015, my mentor recommended me for a sales position.

      I applied for the role and the company hired me immediately. They promoted me to Regional Coordinator in 2018, and I transferred to the Guanajuato office the same year.

      There used to be only 5 people in our sales team. Now there are 22. The industry changed a lot in the last 10 years, but the fundamentals are the same: relationships and trust.

      Comprehension
      Answer the questions.
      What did Miguel study at university?
      When did Miguel get his first sales position?
      Miguel used to work in quality control before his sales career.
      Miguel graduated in 2015.
      The company promoted Miguel and he transferred to Guanajuato in 2018.
      There used to be 22 people in Miguel's sales team.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Orange = "used to" structures. Purple = simple past. Gold = time expressions. Ask student to count each color: how many "used to" (5), how many simple past (7+).
      • R2 trap: "I graduated in 2012 AND applied for an internship" — students sometimes confuse 2012 (graduation) with 2015 (sales position). Teach scanning for specific time markers.
      • R4 trap: "There used to be only 5 people." The text says 5 used to be the number — now there are 22. Trap question tests careful reading of "used to."
      • After reading: "Replace 'Miguel' with your own name. Change the sentences to describe YOUR career. Use at least 2 'used to' and 3 simple past." Oral production transfer.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Miguel sounds completely natural — and he's using the exact two structures you learned today. Read his last sentence again: 'The industry changed a lot… but the fundamentals are the same.' That's real professional voice."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "In S1B you'll write and deliver YOUR version of Miguel's paragraph — live, to the teacher, without notes. That's your speaking milestone for this module."

      Welcome to Career Corner. Today I'm talking with Valentina Cruz, a Project Manager at a technology company in Querétaro. Valentina, tell us about your career.

      Listening Activities
      Listen, then answer.
      What did Valentina study at university?
      What happened in 2017?
      Valentina used to work long hours — sometimes 12 hours a day — as an intern.

      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Listen 1: global — "What is Valentina's current job? What industry is she in now?" Listen 2: focused — answer comprehension questions. Listen 3 (optional): shadow the career narrative format.

      • Count challenge on 3rd listen: student counts every "used to" form (used to study, used to work, didn't use to travel, used to think = 4 instances). This builds metalinguistic awareness.

      Today I Learned
      • "Used to" describes past habits / states that changed
      • Affirmative: I used to work in sales
      • Negative: I didn't use to travel (no -d!)
      • Question: Did you use to work there? (no -d!)
      • Simple past for specific events: I graduated · They hired me
      • Can NOT say "used to" for one-time events
      • Career vocabulary: graduate, intern, promote, transfer
      Key Summary
      Used To — Habit / State
      I used to work in quality control.
      There used to be only 5 people on my team.
      Negative / Question
      I didn't use to travel. · Did you use to manage a team?
      Simple Past — Specific Event
      I graduated in 2012. · They hired me in 2015.
      The Rule
      "Every day / back then" → used to
      "In 2015 / once" → simple past
