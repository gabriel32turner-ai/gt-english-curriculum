🔥 Hot Take
Should Companies Pay for Ergonomics?
Timed opinion · 90 seconds · Should + Simple Present in production
⏱ 8 MIN
⚡ Teacher Note
Goal: production of "should/shouldn't" + Simple Present habits in an opinion context. Monitor for "should to" — every time you hear it, stop and drill. Cold call 3 students before the vote reveal. The goal is not the opinion — it is the grammar in the argument.
🔥 Today's Hot Take
"Companies should pay for ergonomic equipment — chairs, monitors, wrist rests — for every desk worker."
90 seconds to form your opinion. Vote: Agree or Disagree. Then give one reason using should/shouldn't and a Simple Present habit sentence. Example: "I agree because desk workers always sit for 8 hours. Companies should protect their employees' health."

⚡ Debate
Workplace Wellness Policy — 3 Rounds
Argue your position · Should + habits + body vocabulary in full production
⏱ 18 MIN
⚡ Teacher Note — Engagement
Award 1 point per round for: using "should/shouldn't" correctly, using a Simple Present frequency adverb, and naming a body part in context. Assign sides randomly. Track on leaderboard below. The strongest argument wins — not the strongest opinion.
Round 1 — Opening Statement (60 sec each)
Team A: "Companies should provide ergonomic equipment because desk workers always sit for 8+ hours. Their backs hurt. Their wrists ache. The company should protect their health." — Team B: Argue the opposite. You have 60 seconds.
💡 Must use: "should/shouldn't" + frequency adverb (always/never/usually) + 1 body part. 3 points max.
Round 2 — Rebuttal (45 sec each)
Respond directly to the other team. Start with: "I understand your point, but..." Use at least one "shouldn't" and one Simple Present habit to challenge their argument. Reference a specific body part or health habit.
💡 "I understand, but employees shouldn't rely on the company for everything. They should stretch and take breaks — that costs nothing."
Round 3 — One-Sentence Closer (20 sec)
Your team's strongest sentence — make it memorable. Must include "should" or "shouldn't." End with a body part or habit for impact. Class votes: which closing argument was the most convincing?
💡 "Companies should invest in health because a worker whose back always hurts cannot do their best work — and that costs the company more."

🔍 Speed
Speed Round — Should · Body Hurts · Habits
8 rapid-fire questions · 3 seconds max per answer
⏱ 8 MIN
⚡ Speed Round Rules
Teacher reads each prompt. Students answer immediately — no thinking time. 1 point per correct answer. Reveal after 2–3 responses. Fast pace builds automaticity. This is not about accuracy at full speed — it's about training automatic recall.
8:00
Speed Round — 8 minutes total
8 questions · 1 point each · No hesitation
1. Fix the error: "My back is hurt after sitting all day." — What is wrong?
✅ "My back hurts" — Simple Present, not passive. "Is hurt" is passive voice — wrong here.
2. Your colleague says "I should to drink more water." — Correct it fast!
✅ "I should drink more water." — should + base verb, never "to" after should.
3. Describe this habit in English: "Nunca me levanto de mi silla en todo el día."
✅ "I never stand up from my chair all day." — Simple Present + never + all day.
4. Give one "shouldn't" advice for someone with terrible eye strain. Go!
✅ "You shouldn't look at your screen for more than 90 minutes without a break." / "You shouldn't skip the 20-20-20 rule."
5. What body part aches from bad posture at a low monitor? (English word!)
✅ "Neck" — your neck aches when you look down at a low monitor all day.
6. "I sit at my desk all day because I always have meetings." — Is the grammar correct?
✅ Yes — correct. Simple Present habit + "because" + Simple Present cause. The frequency adverb "always" is between subject and verb. Perfect A1.2 sentence.
7. What is "sedentary"? Give a real sentence using it about office work.
✅ Sedentary = spending most of your time sitting. "Office work is very sedentary. I should walk more during the day."
8. Describe the 20-20-20 rule in one sentence — without looking at your notes.
✅ "Every 20 minutes, you should look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds — it helps with eye strain."
🏆 Solo
Solo Challenge — Your Personal Wellness Report
Individual production · All 3 grammar tools · Real personal information · No notes
⏱ 12 MIN
⚡ Solo Rules
No partners, no notes, no looking at the script from §6. Students write using their own real information. Peer-check after: 3 targets — (1) "my body part hurts/aches", (2) frequency adverb placed correctly, (3) "should + base verb" with no "to". Teacher circulates silently — mark errors without interrupting.
Zone 1 — Pain Report
Write 2 sentences about real pain or discomfort you experience at work. Use "My [body part] hurts/aches because..." for each. Be specific about the cause.
Check: (1) correct body part used? (2) "hurts" or "aches" — not "is hurt"? (3) "because" + Simple Present cause? All 3 correct = full marks.
Zone 2 — Habit Description
Write 3 sentences describing your current work habits using always, usually, sometimes, or never. Use a different frequency adverb each time. Include at least one negative habit and one positive habit.
Check: (1) frequency adverb between subject and verb — not at the end? (2) 3 different adverbs used? (3) Simple Present form correct — third person "s" if needed? Award 1 point per correct sentence.
Zone 3 — Recommendations to Yourself
Write 3 recommendations for yourself using should and shouldn't. One for back/posture, one for eyes/screen, one for hydration or breaks. No "to" after should.
Check: (1) should/shouldn't + BASE verb — no "to" anywhere? (2) 3 recommendations present? (3) topics match — posture, eyes, breaks/hydration? This is the full integration of S3A + S3B grammar.

§7
Wrap-Up — Certificate & My Win Today
3 takeaways · Checklist · Certificate · Next session preview
⏱ 8 MIN
01
Should is a modal — never followed by "to." "I should stretch" is correct. "I should to stretch" is an A1.2 fossil error. Fix it every time, immediately. By S4, it should be gone.
02
Frequency adverbs have one position. Between subject and verb: "I always sit. She never stretches." Never at the end: "I sit always" is wrong. This rule is non-negotiable at A1.2.
03
Pain sentences are Simple Present. "My back hurts" — not "is hurt" (passive), not "has hurt" (perfect). The simple form is the right form for current, ongoing physical pain at this level.
