Game 2 — 5–6 min · Finance Buzz

B1.2 standard: full sentence answers. Reported Speech bonus: add RS to any answer for maximum points.

DEFINE + USE
volatility
Price instability — dramatic ups and downs. "Crypto volatility is why I'd never put my savings there." → Now use it in a sentence about your own risk tolerance.
RS DRILL
"I will never put my savings in crypto."
→ "He told me he would never put his savings in crypto." / "She said she would never invest."
COLLOQUIAL
Someone tells you a 40% monthly return investment. Your reaction?
"That's sketchy." — "Any promise of 40% returns? That's sketchy. It's almost certainly fraud."
DEFINE + USE
speculate
To invest in high-risk, potentially high-reward opportunities. "He told me he was speculating — not investing — with his crypto."
RS DRILL
"I am thinking about diversifying my portfolio."
→ "She said she was thinking about diversifying." / "She mentioned she was thinking about her portfolio."
COLLOQUIAL
End of month, no money left. One phrase?
"I'm broke." — "Don't talk to me about investing right now — I'm broke until next Friday."
DEFINE + USE
portfolio
The collection of all your financial assets — stocks, savings, property, crypto. "My advisor told me my portfolio was too concentrated in one asset."
RS DRILL
"Are you happy with your return on investment?"
→ "She asked me if I was happy with my return on investment." (reported question → if + statement order)
COLLOQUIAL
Small daily expenses that accumulate. One phrase?
"It adds up." — "Three coffees a day, subscription services, lunch out — it adds up to $500 a month without noticing."
DEFINE + USE
blockchain
The distributed digital ledger technology behind crypto. "He mentioned that blockchain itself might be more valuable than any individual cryptocurrency."
COLLOQUIAL
Someone asks if you'll invest your bonus in crypto this year.
"Not gonna happen." — Full sentence: "Me putting my Christmas bonus in crypto? Not gonna happen — not after what I saw in 2022."
CHALLENGE
Use "told me" + "volatility" + a colloquial phrase in one sentence.
E.g.: "She told me the volatility was what made her nervous — not gonna happen for her savings." Accept any natural combination.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • B1.2 Buzz: full sentence answers + RS bonus. The challenge card (last one) is the B1.2 integration moment — vocabulary + grammar + colloquial in one sentence. Celebrate if achieved.
  • Wordwall 5 min, Buzz 5-6 min. If "speculate vs invest" distinction is still unclear → spend 60 extra seconds there before moving to §5.

Speaker A: Ana García (pro-regulation) · Speaker B: Marcos López (anti-regulation) · ~110 seconds · Natural fast B1.2 pace · Reported Speech in use

ElevenLabs: Ana → voz Bella · Marcos → voz Adam · Podcast debate · Fast natural pace · Strong opposing opinions · No music
Archivo: GT_B1.2_M2_S1B_Listening_v1.mp3

▶ Show ElevenLabs Script
PODCAST DEBATE — 2 SPEAKERS — FAST NATURAL B1.2 PACE

Ana: "Look, I think this is obvious. Governments need to regulate crypto. My neighbour told me last year that she had invested her entire emergency savings in a scheme someone promoted on Instagram. She said she had lost everything in a month. That's sketchy and it's dangerous. There's no accountability, no protection."

Marcos: "I completely disagree. A friend of mine mentioned recently that the best thing about crypto is precisely that governments can't touch it. He said he didn't trust any institution with his assets. The whole point of the blockchain was decentralisation. If you regulate it, you kill it."

Ana: "But decentralisation creates volatility and risk for ordinary people. My advisor asked me if I had ever looked at the statistics on retail losses in crypto markets. I hadn't. They're shocking. No way can we leave people exposed like that."

Marcos: "Regulation doesn't prevent stupidity. It just creates bureaucracy. People told us the stock market was safe too. We've seen what happens. Let people take their own risks and diversify. If you lose, you learn. It adds up — the experience, the knowledge. Government protection is an illusion."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical

  • RS hunt: count instances together. Ana: "told me / said / asked me" (3). Marcos: "mentioned / said / told us" (3). Total = 6 RS uses in 110 seconds. Show this is natural density at B1.2.
§ 05
Role Play — Financial Advisor Session
12–14 min
Role Play 1 — Guided · 5 min
💼 You Are the Financial Advisor
Escenario: el maestro es un cliente (33 años, buen salario, sin experiencia en inversiones) que llega al alumno (financial advisor) con una pregunta: "I've been hearing a lot about crypto — should I invest?" El alumno da consejos profesionales usando el vocabulario de S1A. El maestro reporta lo que amigos/conocidos le han dicho sobre crypto.
🎧 Maestro — Client (nervous, curious)
"I've been hearing a lot about crypto lately. A friend told me she had made a lot of money — should I do it?"
"But my brother said I could easily double my money in a year. Is that possible?"
"How much should I invest? I have about $5,000 saved up."
"My colleague mentioned he had put 50% of his savings in Bitcoin. Was that smart?"
"What about the volatility? I've heard the market crashes a lot."
"So what would YOU do if you were me?"
🗣 Alumno — Financial Advisor
"I understand — a lot of people say that. But let me ask: did your friend tell you what percentage of her savings she invested? Risk matters."
"When someone tells you you can double your money in a year, that's speculation, not investment. That's sketchy if they guarantee it."
"My advice? Diversify. Never put more than 5–10% of your savings in any single asset, especially one with high volatility."
"Your colleague may have made a mistake — a 50% concentration in one volatile asset is a serious risk to his financial security."
"Yes, markets crash. They also recover. The question is: can you afford to wait for the recovery without needing that money?"
"Honestly? I'd put most of it in a diversified portfolio. Crypto — maximum 5%. And only money you can afford to lose completely."
Role Play 2 — Free · 7 min · Sin scaffold
📰 Report a Real News Story with RS
No script. El alumno elige una de las tres situaciones y la relata como si fuera una historia que le contaron — usando Reported Speech natural. El maestro hace preguntas de seguimiento y el alumno responde en tiempo real. Mínimo 3 RS instances.
🗞 Choose a Situation — Relay it as a story you heard
Situation A: "A colleague invested $2,000 in crypto in 2021 — and lost 80%"
Your colleague called you after the crash. They were devastated. They had invested their vacation savings. The portfolio had crashed 80% in six weeks. They said they had thought it was safe because the market was going up. They told you they would never speculate again. They asked you if you thought it would recover.
Your task: Tell this story using RS. "She called me and told me she had invested… She said she was devastated because… She asked me if…"
Situation B: "Your boss mentioned crypto is the future at a team meeting"
In last week's meeting, your CEO said the company was going to invest part of its reserves in cryptocurrency. Half the team was excited. The CFO mentioned it was a serious risk and said they needed to diversify properly first. The debate was intense. Two people said they thought it was a terrible idea. One said it was brilliant.
Your task: Report the meeting using RS. "My CEO said the company was going to… The CFO mentioned it was a… Several colleagues told me they…"
Situation C: "You read a news article about a crypto exchange collapse"
An analyst quoted in the article said that thousands of users had lost their assets when the exchange crashed. Experts mentioned the platform had shown signs of volatility for months. A spokesperson said the company was working to recover users' funds. One investor told the journalist they had put their entire portfolio on the platform.
Your task: Report the article. "The article said that… An analyst mentioned… A spokesperson told journalists they…"
🎯 Free Role Play Rules — B1.2
Mínimo: 3 RS instances (said / told me / mentioned / asked) · 5 vocabulary words de S1A · 1 colloquial phrase · Reported question bonus: "She asked me if I thought it would recover." Nota: el tense shift debe ser correcto — presente → pasado, will → would.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • RP1 (Financial Advisor): the student is in a position of authority. This is key — they have to use vocabulary precisely, not vaguely. "That's speculative, not an investment" vs "that's risky." The precision is the B1.2 standard.
  • RP2 (News Story): this is the most powerful Reported Speech activity. The student has to produce RS continuously, not just occasionally. Monitor: is the tense shift consistent? Are they using said/told/mentioned naturally?
  • B1.2 correction: 1 RS error (tense shift usually: "will" not shifting to "would") + 1 vocabulary precision note + 1 strong celebration.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You just reported a news story about finance — in English, using Reported Speech naturally. That's real journalism-level language. That's B1.2."
Capa 3 · Retención & Upsell
  • "S2 is salary transparency — arguably more taboo than crypto in Mexico. Same grammar, same vocabulary precision, but even more personal stakes."
  • Upsell: "M2 covers crypto, salaries, side hustles, luxury brands, financial freedom — all in the same grammar frame of Reported Speech. You'll use 'he said / she told me / they mentioned' in every session. By S5 it'll be completely automatic."
§ 06
Debate — Should Young People Invest in Crypto?
10–12 min
💬 Opinion Debate · B1.2 Level — Real Stakes
Should 25-year-olds in Mexico invest in crypto with their savings?
"A 25-year-old earning $15,000 MXN a month just got their first job. A friend told them to invest 20% of their savings in crypto. An advisor told them to save in a traditional fund. Their parents told them crypto was a scam. Who's right?"
✅ Yes — young people should invest in crypto
"When you're 25, you can take the risk — time is your biggest asset."
"Experts said traditional savings accounts don't even beat inflation in Mexico."
"The blockchain technology has already proven it won't disappear — it's not just speculation."
"Every financial advisor told a previous generation NOT to invest in tech stocks. They were wrong."
⚠️ No — it's too risky
"Studies showed most retail investors lost money in crypto markets. Not gonna happen wisely."
"Economists mentioned volatility is a psychological trap — people sell low and buy high."
"That 25-year-old might need that money for rent, emergencies, health. You can't hold long-term."
"The sketchy platforms — scams, hacks, collapses — have already wiped out millions of investors."
💡 Maestro: toma la posición contraria. Usa RS en tus argumentos: "Economists have said…" / "Multiple studies mentioned…" / "My financial advisor told me…" — modela la gramática mientras debatey el alumno tiene que responder con la misma precisión.
Escalation (if time allows): "If you were a financial regulator and you had to decide: regulate crypto like a bank, ban it entirely, or leave it unregulated — which would you choose? Report what different experts have said about each option."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • B1.2 debate with RS integration: both you and the student should be using RS in your arguments. This models natural usage — "Studies have shown / Experts say / My advisor mentioned" — and the student mirrors it.
  • B1.2 fluency check: if the student argues for 3+ minutes using RS naturally + vocabulary + colloquial phrases → that's mastery. If they're still translating from Spanish → focus S2A on fluency over grammar.
  • Listen for: "He said that" (no tense shift) vs "He said he thought" (correct shift). The former is the most common B1.2 RS error. Note it but don't interrupt — correct at end of debate.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You just debated financial regulation using Reported Speech as an argument tool — 'Experts have said / Studies mentioned.' That's how real English speakers argue. That's B1.2."
Capa 3 · Retención & Upsell
  • Testimonial trigger — MANDATORIO: "What was the moment today when you felt most confident in English? Was it the financial advisor role play, the debate, or when you reported the news story?" — Deja que el alumno responda completamente.
  • "S2 is salary transparency — genuinely taboo in Mexico, and also the most personally relevant session of M2 for most professionals. Same vocabulary precision, same RS, but the stakes feel more personal."
  • Upsell: "The language of money — investments, reports, negotiations — is exactly the English that separates mid-level professionals from senior ones in international companies. You're building that language right now."
