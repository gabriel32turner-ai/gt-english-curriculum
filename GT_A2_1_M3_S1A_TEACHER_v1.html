§4

💬 Colloquial — Meeting Phrases

5 min
The phrases that make you sound like you belong in the meeting
Let's get started.
When: opening any meeting
"Everyone's here? Great. Let's get started. The agenda for today is…"
Good point.
When: someone says something smart
"Good point. Let's add that to the action items."
Any questions so far?
When: checking understanding mid-presentation
"That's slide three. Any questions so far?"
To wrap up…
When: closing a meeting or section
"To wrap up, we have three action items and the next meeting is Friday."
Práctica rápida: Contexto en español → alumno produce la frase. "Tu jefe dice algo inteligente. ¿Qué le dices?" → "Good point." / "Ya llevas 20 minutos de presentación. ¿Qué dices?" → "Any questions so far?"
Gramática integrada: "was/were" aparece naturalmente: "That was a good point" / "There were three action items."

🎯 Grammar — Was / Were

10 min
Was or were? The past of "to be" — essential for describing meetings
REGLA DE ORO §5: REFERENCIAL. Lee la tabla, da un ejemplo personal, activa el Poll. Nunca pidas conjugaciones aisladas.
Error #1: "The meeting were good" → was (singular). "The attendees was late" → were (plural). Siempre corrige con el sujeto visible.
SubjectAffirmativeNegativeMeeting Example
I / he / she / itwaswasn'tThe meeting was productive. / It wasn't long.
you / we / theywereweren'tThere were 10 attendees. / We weren't late.
Question (I/he/she/it)Was + subject…?Was the presentation good? — Yes, it was.
Question (you/we/they)Were + subject…?Were you at the meeting? — No, I wasn't.
Short answersYes, it was. / No, they weren't.Was it on time? — Yes, it was.
⚠️ #1 ERROR
✗ "The meeting were good" → ✅ The meeting was good (singular!)
✗ "The people was there" → ✅ The people were there (plural!)

🎧 Listening — Valeria's Monday Standup

8 min
📍 Solaris Manufacturing, Celaya — Monday 9 AM standup
🎙️
ElevenLabs — "Valeria's Monday Standup"
Voice: Sarah (American) · Speed: 0.82 · A2.1 M3 S1A
Good morning, team. Let's get started. I want to review last week's meetings.

The most important one was the Q3 budget review on Wednesday. It was three hours long — from 9 AM to noon. There were eight attendees, including two directors. The agenda was clear — three topics — and we made two important decisions. The meeting was productive, but it wasn't short.

Thursday's client call wasn't as good. The client wasn't happy with the delivery delay. There were some difficult questions about the timeline. We didn't have all the answers, so Daniel asked me to follow up with the warehouse team. That's my action item for today.

The Friday standup was quick — only 20 minutes. There were four people. We discussed the action items from Wednesday and confirmed the schedule for this week.

One thing I want to improve: our meetings need a clear agenda every time. Last week, two meetings didn't have one, and they were too long. This week, let's make sure every meeting has an agenda. Any questions?
