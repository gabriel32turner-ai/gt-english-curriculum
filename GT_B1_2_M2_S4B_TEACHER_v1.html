§ 02
⚔️ Pitch Battle — Defend Your Side
12 min
⚔️
Pitch Battle
Teacher assigns sides · 2 min per pitch · Debate meter tracks it
12 MIN
📋 Debate topic: "Luxury brands create more harm than good — they fuel inequality, waste and shallow values." Teacher assigns Side A or B. You argue your assigned position using RS throughout: "People have told me…" / "Brands have said…"
✅ Side A — Luxury is worth it
Craftsmanship matters. People who own luxury items have told me the quality difference is real — they said they could feel it.
Economic engine. Luxury brands have mentioned they employ millions globally. Someone told me Hermès has a 5-year waitlist — that's demand, not waste.
Aspiration is human. A friend mentioned that wanting beautiful things was natural — she said desire wasn't shallow.
Investment value. Collectors have said certain luxury items appreciate 10-15% per year. That's better than most stocks.
❌ Side B — Luxury does more harm
Inequality amplifier. Research has mentioned that visible luxury consumption increases social resentment in developing economies.
Environmental cost. Brands have said they're sustainable — but investigators told journalists they'd found massive waste and unsold stock destruction.
Status anxiety. Psychologists have said that the pressure to display brands created real mental health costs — especially for younger consumers.
The logo trap. Former industry insiders mentioned that production costs for luxury items were often 5-10% of retail price. Someone told me it was pure markup.

⏱ 2 Min · Each Pitch
Press START per side
  • All debate points are pre-written in RS to model the target language. The student must argue BEYOND these points — they can reference them but must add their own RS content: "Someone at work told me…" / "I read that experts have said…"
  • Side B (harm) is harder to argue for students who love luxury — that productive discomfort forces creative RS under pressure. Assign the uncomfortable side deliberately.
  • Move the Debate Meter live. When a student uses RS with correct backshift: give a visible point. That links accuracy to winning.
§ 03
🔄 RS Relay — Transform & Report
10 min
🔄
RS Relay
6 direct quotes · Transform to RS · Click done when correct · Badge at 6+
10 MIN
Teacher reads the direct speech. Student transforms it to RS immediately. Click ✓ when correct. Badge at 6+ completed. Relay Count: 0/6
Card 1 · Present → Past
"I buy luxury brands because they last longer."
→ He said he _____ luxury brands because they _____ longer.
Card 2 · PP → Past Perfect
"I've already spent too much on designer clothes this year."
→ She mentioned she _____ already _____ too much on designer clothes that year.
Card 3 · will → would
"I'll never waste money on a logo again."
→ He told me he _____ never _____ money on a logo again.
Card 4 · can → could
"I can't justify spending $3,000 on a handbag."
→ She said she _____ justify spending $3,000 on a handbag.
Card 5 · Cont. → Past Cont.
"I'm saving for a designer watch right now."
→ He mentioned he _____ saving for a designer watch.
Card 6 · ⭐ Free Production
"The worst purchase I ever made was a $900 pair of shoes I wore once."
→ Report this in YOUR OWN words — full RS with proper backshift + your reaction.
  • Each relay card has a specific backshift type. Card 6 is free production — the student must report AND react. That's the B1.2 stretch. Cards 1-5 are mechanical. Card 6 is mastery.
  • Badge 🔄 RS Relay awards at 6/6 — all cards done. The badge trigger is in checkRelay().
  • Common error watch: "She said she can't justify" → "couldn't." Push for it. This is the drill — the pressure makes the backshift automatic.

Speed Report

RS Transform
"I love designer shoes." → Report it.
She said she loved designer shoes.
Colloquial + RS
Your friend said a $5K watch was "sketchy." Report it.
He told me it sounded sketchy. He said he couldn't trust the seller.
RS + Opinion
Someone said "luxury is just marketing." Report + react.
She mentioned luxury was just marketing. I told her I disagreed — quality matters too.
Backshift
"I've never bought anything over $500." → RS.
He said he had never bought anything over $500.
Vocab + RS
Report what someone told you about brand perception at work.
She told me perception mattered more than she wanted to admit in client meetings.
RS Transform
"I'll invest in quality next time." → Report it.
He said he would invest in quality next time.
Coloquial
Use "it adds up" in a sentence about luxury spending.
She mentioned small luxury purchases added up — she said she'd spent $4K last year without realizing.
RS + Vocab
Report what a luxury sceptic told you about exclusivity.
He told me exclusivity was manufactured. He said brands created artificial scarcity to justify prices.
Debate
One sentence: why do people display luxury logos?
People have said they display logos to signal status — but research has mentioned it was really about identity.
RS Error Fix
"She told she couldn't afford it." Fix it.
She told me she couldn't afford it. ("Told" always needs an object!)
RS Transform
"I'm broke after that sale." → Report it.
She said she was broke after the sale.
