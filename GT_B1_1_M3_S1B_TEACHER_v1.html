🔥 Hot Take — Cold Start

5 min
No prep. Talk about YOUR money — 60 seconds NOW.
🔥 Hot Take — 60 sec
You just received a $5,000 bonus. RIGHT NOW — tell me exactly what you will do with it. Save? Spend? Invest? Be specific. If you save, how much? If you buy something, what? GO!

🎯 Pitch — Assigned Sides

10 min
Defend your side — even if you disagree
Topic: "Saving money is more important than enjoying life." Asigna lados. Push for First Conditional: "If you save too much, you won't enjoy life."
🟣 Side A — SAVING IS #1
"Saving is always more important. Defend this."
Starters:"If you save now, you'll have security later…""If you don't save, you'll have problems when you're older…""If you invest your savings, they'll grow over time…"
🔵 Side B — ENJOY LIFE NOW
"You should enjoy your money. Counter the other side."
Starters:"If you only save and never spend, you won't enjoy life…""If you wait too long, you'll be too old to enjoy it…""It's easy to lose track of what matters if you only think about money…"

🔄 Relay — Transform It

8 min
No grammar table. Transform the conditional.
Transform 1 — Positive → Negative result
"If I save $500 a month, I'll have $6,000 by December."
→ Change the result to negative.
"If I don't save $500 a month, I won't have $6,000 by December."
Transform 2 — Swap clause order
"If prices go up, I won't buy the apartment."
→ Put the result first.
"I won't buy the apartment if prices go up." (no comma needed)
Transform 3 — Statement → Question
"She will invest if the market improves."
→ Ask a question.
"Will she invest if the market improves?" or "What will she do if…?"
Transform 4 — Fix the error
"If I will get a raise, I'll save more."
→ Find and fix the error.
"If I get a raise…" — no "will" in the if-clause!
Transform 5 — Your reality
"Rodrigo will buy an apartment if prices stay stable."
→ What will YOU do if you get a raise? Make it real.
Open — "If I get a raise, I'll [your real plan]."

🔍 Source Analysis — Financial Advice

8 min
Read. Analyze. What would YOU do?
📰 Financial blog
"If you save 20% of your income every month, you'll have a solid emergency fund in one year. If you don't start now, you'll regret it when an unexpected expense appears."
❓ What does the blog recommend?
💬 Reddit comment
"I used to waste so much money on coffee and Uber Eats. If I cook at home, I save about $300 a month. That's $3,600 a year. It adds up fast."
❓ How much does this person save by cooking?
📧 Email from advisor
"If the market stays stable, your investments will grow about 7% this year. If it drops, don't panic — if you wait, the market will recover. The worst thing is to sell when prices are low."
❓ What should you do if the market drops?
🎙️ Podcast transcript
"Money isn't about being rich. It's about freedom. If you control your spending, you'll have choices. If you don't, your money controls you. That's the real difference between a saver and a spender."
❓ What does money give you, according to the podcast?
