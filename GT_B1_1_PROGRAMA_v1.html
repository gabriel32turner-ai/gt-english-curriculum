Who is this program for?

  • Students who completed A2.2 at Gabriel Turner English Training
  • Professionals who understand English but struggle to express opinions and ideas
  • Adults who want to move from functional English to real fluency
  • Corporate employees ready to participate actively in international meetings and conversations

By the end of B1.1, students will be able to:

  • Speak about personality, work, money, technology and society with confidence
  • Use Present Perfect, First Conditional, Passive and Reported Speech in context
  • Express opinions, give reasons and respond to different viewpoints
  • Use topic-specific vocabulary with precision across 30 real-world topics
  • Understand and produce natural colloquial English phrases
  • Listen to and respond to more challenging, natural-pace audio

Grammar — Referential
Present Perfect — just / already / yet / never / ever
"We've just implemented a new policy."
"Have you ever worked remotely?"
"They haven't updated the system yet."
"I've already sent the report."
These adverbs add precision: just = very recently · already = before expected · yet = until now (? and neg.) · never/ever = in your whole life/experience.

Grammar — Referential
First Conditional + Present Perfect — Consolidation
"If we don't act now, the situation will get worse."
"Travel has changed the way I think."
"Education hasn't kept up with the real world."
Both structures are used naturally in opinion-giving. Present Perfect: what has happened up to now. First Conditional: what will happen if a condition is met. Focus: using both confidently in discussion.
Opinion Language — B1.1 Range
"I strongly believe that…"
"In my opinion… / From my perspective…"
"I see both sides, but…"
"That's a good point, however…"
