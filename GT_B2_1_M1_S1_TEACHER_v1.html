Harvard Business Review · Leadership & Organizational Behavior · Study of 300 senior executives across Fortune 500 companies
🎯 The Question — Launch directly, no setup
"Is the loudest person in your company the most dangerous one?"
⚙️ Mechanics Project or read the stats. Read the question once — slowly. Point to 2 or 3 people. Max 20 seconds each. You're not looking for the right answer. You're activating the nervous system before vocabulary.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • If silence happens, wait 5 full seconds before redirecting. Silence is productive here — they're processing. If nobody moves, say: "You — first reaction. Go."
  • Don't validate or invalidate answers. Your job here is to listen and ask: "Why do you think that?" — then move to vocab.
  • The stat asymmetry (65% vs +24%) is the pedagogical hook. Let it land without explanation.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • Do not ask the question to the full group. Point directly. The surprise is the activation.
  • If someone gives a long answer: "I'll stop you there — hold that for §4. That's exactly where this goes."
  • The best hook responses are usually the most honest ones. Mark the person — they'll lead the debate later.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • After the session: "You just had a conversation a business school would charge $200 for. This is what B2 sounds like."
  • The stat is shareable. If students mention the session to colleagues, they'll remember "the Harvard thing." That's a referral trigger.

💡 10 Nouns — Power, Presence & Organizational Reality
🔵 10 Verbs — How Leaders Actually Operate
⚡ Production Challenge — No preparation
Each person picks 3 words. One sentence each, about your real professional life right now. No writing. No thinking time. Just English.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Pairs mechanic: Person A reads the definition (without the word), Person B produces the word. Switch. Then both attempt the production challenge simultaneously — no waiting.
  • Challenge Mode: Hit the button to hide definitions. Use this for the second pass if the group is strong — forces retrieval practice, which is 3× more effective than re-reading.
  • The production challenge is non-negotiable. If a student says "I can't think of one" — redirect: "You work at a company. You have a boss. One of these 20 words describes something happening there right now. Which one?"
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • These are not academic words — they're Monday morning words. Say it before starting: "These are the words your colleagues in New York are using right now."
  • When a student produces a great sentence: "That's a B2 sentence. That's what we're building toward."
  • Best production challenge trigger: "Which 3 words describe your manager? Go."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "These 20 words will come back in M2 and M3. You'll see how they evolve across the modules."
  • Homework trigger: "Before next session — use 2 of these words in a real conversation at work. Screenshot it if it's in writing. Bring it."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Read Sofía's profile last. Her context (Mexican corporate culture, performing extroversion) is the most resonant for this market. It lands harder after the international voices have framed the global problem.
  • The processing question never changes — this is intentional. It builds a conversational habit and prevents students from needing to decode a new question each round.

Capa 2 · Engagement
  • After Sofía's profile: pause before asking the processing question. Let it sit 3 seconds. The resonance is the point.
  • If a student says "I feel like Sofía" — that's your retention moment. Say: "Tell me more. When did that start?" Then redirect to §4.

Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • These profiles are fictional composites — but the situations are real. If a student asks, confirm: "The situations are real. The names aren't."
  • Testimonial trigger: "By the end of today you'll have had the most sophisticated conversation about leadership you've had in English. That's the B2 difference."
§ 04 · The Conversation
8 Questions · 4 Phases
Progressive depth · Pairs → Group → Debate · Do not skip questions · Do not reorder phases
04
25–28 min
⚠️
Flow rule: Questions 1–2 in pairs (activation). Q3–4 in pairs then open. Q5–6 full group. Q7–8 as 3v3 debate or full group. If running short on time, cut Q3 or Q5 — never cut Q7 or Q8.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Pair mechanic: Both pairs speak simultaneously. You monitor — don't interrupt unless there's a lexical block. After 90 seconds, open to group sharing with: "What did your partner say that surprised you?"
  • 3v3 debate (Q7–Q8): Assign sides. Students who naturally agree with a position argue the opposite. This forces higher-order language production. Allow 60 seconds per side, then open.
  • B2 target: sentences with subordinate clauses, hedging language, discourse markers. If a student produces: "I think that, although his approach works in a stable environment, it doesn't account for..." — that's B2. Name it.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • If a student goes quiet in Phase 3 or 4, don't call them out. Ask the group: "Is there a perspective we haven't heard yet?" — they'll include the quiet person organically.
  • Q4 ("performing a behavior not because it's natural") often unlocks the most honest language of the session. Let it breathe.
  • Energy typically peaks at Q7. If the debate is alive — let it run 5 minutes. That's a good sign. Pull it back with: "Last word, one sentence each."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • After Q8: "That's a conversation you couldn't have had in A2. That's the distance you've traveled."
  • Preview: "M2 goes deeper — we'll get into the language of conflict and difficult conversations at work. Same format, harder topic."
§ 05 · Exit Shot
One Sentence. No Preparation.
2 words from today · 1 truth about yourself · Spoken, not written
05
2–3 min
EXIT
🎯 The Prompt
"One sentence. At least two words from today.
Tell me: what kind of leader are you becoming —
and what is the one thing you still need to work on?"
No writing. No thinking time. You speak, then the next person speaks. If someone uses a word another person already used — redirect: "Use a word nobody has said yet."
✅ Model Answer — Teacher Reference Only (Never Read Aloud)
"I think I speak up when I need to, but I still hold back when the pressure comes from above — and I know that's the gap I need to own."
⚙️ Mechanics Start with yourself if the group is hesitant. Model the sentence using today's vocabulary. Once one person goes, the rest follow. The energy should be fast — no pauses between speakers.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This is retrieval + production + self-awareness in 10–15 seconds. The constraint (2 words from today) forces active vocabulary use, not filler.
  • If a student uses a word incorrectly in their exit sentence — note it, but don't correct in the moment. Address it in the next session warm-up.
  • Listen for the quality of the subordinate clause. B2 students should be producing: "I know that..." / "...even though..." / "...which is why..."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • After the last person finishes: "Write down that sentence. That's your professional English for this week."
  • The best exit sentences become session testimonials. If someone says something genuinely insightful — "That's the kind of English I want you to bring to every session."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Upsell trigger: "The quality of that sentence — that self-awareness in English — is something most professionals at your level never develop. That's what we're building."
  • Preview: "Next session we go into conflict — same format, much harder conversations. Come ready to disagree."
  • WhatsApp follow-up: send the session topic + one exit sentence (anonymized) to the group after class. Retention trigger.
