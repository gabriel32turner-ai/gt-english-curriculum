Health Data at Work — Who Owns It?
Timed opinion + vote · 90 seconds to form your position
⏱ 8 MIN
⚡ Teacher Note
No right or wrong answer here — the goal is to produce the grammar from S2A in a real opinion context. Monitor for: "I am allergic to" used in debate, imperatives for advice, possessive pronouns. Cold call 3 students to share their position BEFORE the reveal. This warms the room for the debate rounds.

"Companies should share employee health and allergy information with all team members — for safety reasons."
You have 90 seconds to form your opinion. Then vote: Agree or Disagree. You must give one reason — in English — using today's grammar: imperatives, possessive pronouns, or allergy language.

⚡ Debate
HR Health Policy — Argue Your Side
3 structured rounds · Imperatives + possessive pronouns in production
⏱ 18 MIN
⚡ Teacher Note — Engagement
Assign sides randomly — don't let students pick. This forces them to argue a position they may not believe, which produces richer language. Award 1 point per round for: using an imperative, using a possessive pronoun, and using allergy/health vocabulary. Track on the leaderboard below.
Round 1 — Position Statement (60 sec each)
Team A: "Companies SHOULD share health data — especially allergy information. It's not theirs alone — it's ours as a team. Don't hide information that could save someone's life." — Team B: Argue the opposite. You have 60 seconds.
💡 Must include: 1 imperative + "theirs/ours/mine" + 1 health/allergy word. Score: 3 points max per team.
Round 2 — Rebuttal (45 sec each)
Respond to the other team's argument directly. Start with: "I understand your point, but..." or "Don't forget that..." Then give your counter-argument using at least one possessive pronoun and one imperative.
💡 Starters: "Don't forget that this information is private — it's ours, not the company's." / "Share it, but protect it. Don't give it to everyone."
Round 3 — Final Pitch (30 sec each)
One sentence only — your team's strongest argument. Make it memorable. Use an imperative to end strong: "So remember: [imperative]. Because [reason using possessive pronoun]."
💡 "So remember: protect employee health data. Because it is theirs, not yours to share."

Speed Round — Imperatives & Possessive Pronouns
8 rapid-fire questions · No hesitation · 3 seconds per answer
⏱ 8 MIN
⚡ Speed Round Rules
Teacher reads each prompt out loud. Students answer immediately — no thinking time. Tap finger = answer or pass. Award 1 point per correct answer. Reveal each answer after 2–3 student responses. Fast pace is the goal — this builds automaticity, not accuracy at full speed.

Speed Round — 8 minutes total
8 questions · ~60 sec each · 1 point per correct answer
1. Your colleague has eye strain. Give one imperative instruction. (3 seconds!)
✅ "Look away from the screen." / "Take a break." / "Blink often."
2. HR asks: "Whose certificate is this?" — It belongs to Maria. Answer fast!
✅ "It's hers."
3. Fix this error: "I am allergic to the penicillin." — What's wrong?
✅ Remove "the" — "I am allergic to penicillin." (no article with substances)
4. Your employee is sick. Tell them one thing NOT to do today. Use a negative imperative.
✅ "Don't come in today." / "Don't push too hard." / "Don't skip the doctor."
5. "Whose laptop is this?" — It's ours. (HR team's laptop.) — Answer!
✅ "It's ours." (not "it's our" — the pronoun stands alone)
6. Fix this error: "Don't to come in today." What's wrong?
✅ Remove "to" — "Don't come in today." (Don't + base verb, no infinitive "to")
7. Give a 3-word imperative for someone who has a terrible headache. Go!
✅ "Take a painkiller." / "Drink some water." / "Rest right now." / "See a doctor."
8. Your colleague says: "This report — it's my." Is that correct? Fix it.
✅ "It's mine." — possessive pronoun stands alone, replaces the noun completely
🏆 Solo
Solo Challenge — Your HR Health Profile
Individual production · All 4 grammar tools from S2A · No partner support
⏱ 12 MIN
⚡ Solo Rules
No help from partners. No looking at notes. Students write directly in each box. After completion, pairs swap and peer-check for the 3 target errors. Teacher circulates and marks accuracy silently — don't correct out loud until all are done.
Zone 1 — Allergy Statement
Write your HR allergy statement. Use: "I am allergic to ___." + one imperative instruction for the company. If no allergies, write: "I am not allergic to anything, but [one health note]."
Check for: no article after "allergic to" · imperative has no "to" after "Don't" · sentence makes sense for HR context. ✅ Well done if all three are correct!
Zone 2 — Possessive Pronoun Practice
Write 3 sentences using different possessive pronouns: one about your own health document, one about a colleague's, one about a shared team resource. Use: mine / his / hers / ours / theirs.
Check for: pronoun used alone (not "it's my form" but "it's mine") · 3 different pronouns used · context makes sense. Award 1 point per correct pronoun.
Zone 3 — Return-to-Work Statement
You are back after 3 sick days. Write 4 sentences for HR: (1) how you feel now, (2) your allergy or health restriction this week, (3) one imperative instruction for yourself ("I will..."), (4) whose documents they already have.
Check for: 4 sentences present · allergy structure correct · possessive pronoun used standalone · imperative formed correctly. This is the full integration of S2A + S2B grammar.

Wrap-Up — Certificate & My Win Today
3 takeaways · Checklist · Certificate generator · Next session preview
⏱ 8 MIN
01
Imperatives are direct and professional. "Take a break. Don't come in." — no "to," no "you should." Direct commands sound confident in English, not rude. Use them with colleagues freely.
02
Sick day emails follow a formula. Opening + symptoms + allergy note + certificate + closing. You now have all the pieces. Write one this week — even if you're not sick. Practice makes the formula automatic.
03
Possessive pronouns in writing = fluency signal. "It's mine" instead of "It's my form" — small change, huge impact on how professional your English sounds to native speakers and HR teams.
