Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • ACCIÓN: Señala la foto y di "Go!" — 10 segundos para la primera oración. No des tiempo de pensar. Si el alumno duda → di "Is there any stock?" y señala la foto. La pregunta lo desbloquea.

  • Si el alumno usa "there isn't some" → recast inmediato: "Yes — there isn't ANY stock — correcto." Sin parar la conversación.
  • Diagnóstico rápido: ¿El alumno distingue "How much" vs "How many" espontáneamente? Si sí → S3B fluirá bien. Si no → el relay game en §3 lo consolida.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Hoy no hay grammar nueva. Solo usas lo que ya tienes — some, any, How much, How many — en conversaciones reales. Más difícil y más valioso que estudiar algo nuevo."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Flash drill: máximo 4 rondas, ritmo rápido. Si el alumno duda en "any" negativo → di "Remember: no → any." Una sola corrección. Sigue adelante. La velocidad es el punto — automatismo, no reflexión.
  • Si el alumno todavía dice "How many money?" → flash drill específico: "money — much or many?" → "How much money." Una vez. Sin explicación adicional. El §3 relay game lo consolida con más práctica.
  • 5 minutos duros. El rol play de §5 necesita tiempo real.

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Grammar Relay: 12 cartas, 5–6 minutos. Ritmo rápido — no más de 30 segundos por carta. Las cartas de error (FIND THE ERROR) son las más valiosas: el alumno tiene que identificar Y corregir, no solo elegir.
  • Carta Challenge al final: si el alumno produce 3 oraciones correctas usando some/any/How many naturalmente → CELEBRA con especificidad: "You used 'some', 'any', and 'How many' all correctly in 3 seconds. That's A1.2 mastery."
  • Si "How many money?" aparece aquí todavía → corrección directa una sola vez. Si aparece de nuevo → marcar para §6 speaking follow-up.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Las cartas de error son las más importantes — porque esos son exactamente los errores que un proveedor americano va a notar en tu inglés. Hoy los eliminas."

§ 05
Role Play — Emergency Purchase Request
12–14 min
Role Play · A1.2 · Scaffolding visible
The supplier calls — your stock is critically low.
It is Tuesday morning at Solaris Manufacturing. The office supplies are critically low: no ink cartridges, only a few folders, and 50 reams of paper left. The purchasing manager (teacher) calls the warehouse manager (student) to make an urgent order. The student needs to answer all questions about stock and agree on quantities and price.
YOUR ROLE — Warehouse Manager
Situation: You checked the storeroom this morning. You know the stock levels.
Stock facts: Paper: 50 reams ✓ · Ink: 0 units ✗ · Folders: 8 packs ⚠ · Pens: 12 boxes ✓ · Tape: 0 rolls ✗
Your sentences: "There is some paper — about 50 reams." / "There isn't any ink." / "There are some folders, but not many."
You can ask: "How many cartridges do you need?" / "How much is the budget for this order?"
TEACHER ROLE — Purchasing Manager
Open: "Good morning. I need a stock report — is there any paper left?"
Questions to ask:
→ "Is there any ink in the storeroom?"
→ "How many folders do we have?"
→ "How much money can we spend on this order?"
→ "Are there any tape rolls left?"
After stock check: "OK — please send a purchase request for 20 cartridges and 20 packs of folders."
📋 Sentence Starters — Use these in the role play
✅"There is some [noun] — about [number]." / "There are some [nouns] — about [number]."
❌"There isn't any [noun]." / "There aren't any [nouns] — we're out of stock."
❓"Is there any [noun]?" / "Are there any [nouns]?" / "How many [nouns] do we need?" / "How much [uncountable] do we have?"
📦"We need some [noun]." / "We need to order [number] [nouns]." / "I'll send the purchase request today."
🎯 A1.2 Challenge — After the Role Play
Without notes, tell the teacher: "What stock did you have? What didn't you have? What did you need to order? How many / how much?" — 4–5 sentences. This is the full recap of the role play using some, any, How much, How many spontaneously. A1.2 success = 4 correct uses of the grammar in 5 sentences.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • ACCIÓN: Las sentence starters son visibles — esto es A1.2, no B1.2. El alumno PUEDE mirarlas mientras habla. El objetivo no es producción libre total, sino producción CORRECTA con scaffolding. La corrección consolida el patrón.
  • Haz las preguntas del role play a ritmo real pero claro: "Is there any ink?" — Pausa. "How many folders do we have?" — Pausa. No corras. A1.2 necesita tiempo de procesamiento.
  • Corrección en el role play: recast silencioso. Si el alumno dice "There isn't some ink" → tú dices "Yes — there isn't ANY ink, OK. And folders?" — No pares el rol play. Corrige y sigue.
  • El challenge post-RP es el momento más valioso: el alumno produce sin apoyo. 4 correctos de 5 = A1.2 mastery. Celebra con especificidad: "You said 'there isn't any ink' and 'some folders' — both correct."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Acabas de hacer una llamada de compras en inglés. Verificaste el stock, reportaste lo que hay y lo que no hay, y acordaste cantidades. Eso es inglés de trabajo real, no de libro."
Capa 3 · Retención & Upsell
  • "S4A y S4B siguen con el siguiente bloque de M2: describir tu empresa y sus productos completos en inglés — qué produce, cuántos empleados, qué presupuesto. Todo lo que practicaste hoy es el vocabulario de esa presentación."
§ 06
Speaking — Your Company's Stock Situation
8–10 min
🗣
Speaking
Real company · Real answers · some / any / How much / How many
8–10 MIN
Speaking Practice — A1.2 Free Production
Your real company. Your real stock situation.
Answer each question in full sentences using some, any, How much, or How many. The teacher marks: ✓ correct / ✗ error. Goal: 4+ correct uses in 5 questions.
Question 1 — Office Supplies Check
Is there any paper in your office right now? Are there any pens? What about folders or markers?
💡 "Yes, there is some paper — about…" / "No, there isn't any paper." / "There are some pens but not many."
Question 2 — Equipment
Is there any equipment in your department that is old or broken? How many computers or screens are there in your area?
💡 Remember: "equipment" = uncountable. "How much equipment?" / "There is some equipment but…" / "There are [number] computers."
Question 3 — Budget
How much money does your department spend on supplies per month — roughly? Is there any budget left right now?
💡 "We spend about…" / "There is some budget — about $___." / "There isn't any budget left this month."
Question 4 — Suppliers
How many suppliers does your company work with? Are there any suppliers that have delivery problems?
💡 "We have some suppliers — about [number]." / "There aren't any problems with most of them, but…"
Question 5 — A Real Stock Problem
Describe a real situation at work when there wasn't any stock or something was out of stock. What happened? What did you need to order?
💡 Challenge: use 3 or more different structures — some / any / How much or How many — in your answer.
⭐ Question 6 — 60-Second Stock Report (No Notes)
Give a 60-second verbal stock report about your real company or department. Include: what you have, what you don't have, quantities, and what you need to order. Your "manager" (teacher) can ask follow-up questions.
💡 Model: "Good morning. I have some information about our stock. There is some… There isn't any… How many… do we need? We need some…"
🎯 A1.2 Speaking Standard — S3 Complete
"Give your full stock report for your real company. Use some, any, How much, and How many at least once each. 60 seconds maximum. No notes."
A1.2 success: student uses the 4 structures (some/any/How much/How many) correctly at least 4 times total, produces a coherent report without switching to Spanish, and avoids "equipments" and "How many money." That is 100% of S3 achievement.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • ACCIÓN: Para Q6, tú eres el manager. Haz PREGUNTAS mientras el alumno da su reporte: "Is there any paper?" / "How many cartridges?" — Esto convierte el monólogo en diálogo y genera más producción de some/any espontáneamente.
  • Corrección §6: UN error de some/any → recast. DOS → corrección directa: "Remember: questions use 'any'. Try again." TRES → parar y hacer el flash drill del §2 de nuevo con ese error específico.
  • Estándar de éxito A1.2: 4+ usos correctos de some/any/How much/How many en 60 segundos, sin cambiar a español, sin "equipments" ni "How many money." Si el alumno alcanza esto → S3 completo.
  • Celebración con especificidad: "You said 'there isn't any ink' — correct. 'There is some paper' — correct. 'How many suppliers' — perfect. That's three out of four structures in 30 seconds. Excellent A1.2."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Acabas de describir el stock de tu empresa real — en inglés. Con gramática correcta. Sin notas. Eso ya es inglés funcional para tu trabajo."
Capa 3 · Retención & Upsell
  • Trigger testimonial — MANDATORIO: "¿Qué fue lo más difícil de hoy — some vs any, How much vs How many, o el vocabulario?" — La respuesta te dice exactamente qué reforzar en S4A y cuál fue el achievement real de la sesión.
  • "M2 tiene 5 sesiones. En S5 presentas tu empresa completa en inglés: productos, empleados, presupuesto, inventario, proveedores. Hoy instalaste la mitad de esa presentación."
