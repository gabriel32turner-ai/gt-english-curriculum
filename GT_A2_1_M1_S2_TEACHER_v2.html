§ 01
Warm-Up — Picture Prompt
5–8 min
📸 Look at the office — answer these questions
1
What do you think these people did yesterday at work?
Try: "They probably attended a meeting / organized files / presented results…"
2
What did YOU do at work yesterday? Name 3 things.
Accept any answer — listen for missing -ed, correct after §4.
3
Did you finish everything on your to-do list? Why or why not?
Try: "No, I didn't. I didn't complete the ___ because…"
4
Did anyone cancel or delay something at your work last week?
Try: "Yes — the client delayed / cancelled the ___."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • S1 review checkpoint: Q2 and Q3 directly test S1 grammar. Note: does student use -ed consistently? Does "didn't" get the base verb?
  • Q4 introduces "delay" and "cancel" in context before the vocabulary section — natural pre-teaching.
  • Picture prompt: choose specific people in the photo and ask "What did she do yesterday?" — forces 3rd person awareness (S3 grammar preview).
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "I want you to describe that woman in the photo. What did she do yesterday? Use at least 3 past verbs." — concrete visual anchor.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Today's session adds 8 more verbs. By S5, you'll have 24 past verbs — enough to describe any full work week."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • "notify → notified" is the KEY new spelling rule today (consonant+y → ied). Point this out immediately: "Did you notice anything different about 'notified'?" — bridge to §3.
  • "attend" /əˈtendɪd/ — 3 syllables. Students often say "at-TEND-ed" with stress on 2nd syllable but compress to 2. Drill: at-TEND-id.
  • "present" (verb) vs "present" (noun/adjective): stress shift. Verb = /prɪˈzent/ · Noun = /ˈprezənt/. Both appear in corporate English.

Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "'organized' and 'prepared' are the two most used verbs in professional email sign-offs: 'I organized / prepared X for Y.' Memorize them."

§ 04
Grammar — Did / Didn't + Full Q&A
12–15 min
Structure — Yes/No Questions + Full Answers
FormStructureExample
QuestionDid + subject + base verb?Did you attend the training last week?
Short ✔Yes, + subject + did.Yes, I did.
Short ✗No, + subject + didn't.No, I didn't.
Full ✔Yes, I did. + full sentence.Yes, I did. I attended the Q3 training on Tuesday.
Full ✗No, I didn't. + full sentence.No, I didn't. I didn't attend because it was rescheduled.
Q&A Pairs — Read and practice with your teacher
Q
Did you attend the project meeting last week?
A+
Yes, I did. I attended the meeting on Wednesday morning.
A−
No, I didn't. I didn't attend — it was cancelled.
Q
Did the manager approve the budget?
A+
Yes, she did. She approved it last Friday afternoon.
A−
No, she didn't. She didn't approve it — she needed more information.
Q
Did you complete all your tasks yesterday?
A+
Yes, I did. I completed 6 out of 6 tasks by 5 PM.
A−
No, I didn't. I didn't complete the report — I needed two more hours.
Q
Did the supplier delay the shipment?
A+
Yes, they did. They delayed it by three days due to production issues.
A−
No, they didn't. The shipment arrived on schedule last Tuesday.
⚠ New Errors at S2 Level
❌ "Did she approved the budget?"→✅ Did she approve the budget? (base verb after Did)
❌ "Yes, I did attend." (with stress only)→✅ Yes, I did. — then add the full sentence separately.
❌ "She notifyed the team."→✅ She notified the team. (consonant+y → ied, not -yed)
❌ "I organized AND I prepared AND I completed…" (list without context)→✅ I organized the files, prepared the agenda, and completed the report. (compound sentence — natural & professional)

Drag the pieces to form a correct past sentence.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Q&A pairs are for oral drilling. Teacher asks Q, student gives short + full answer. Then swap: student asks, teacher answers. Speed up after 2 rounds.
      • Activity 3 drag: "She organized AND presented" — compound past with two verbs. Key A2.1 progression: single past verb → compound past sentences. Explicitly teach: "In one sentence, you can have 2 or 3 past verbs with AND."
      • T4: "studyed" is a very common error. The rule: consonant+y → ied. y+y is never correct.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Speed round: I ask, you answer — short answer only. As fast as possible." — 6 questions in 30 seconds. Creates positive pressure.
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "Compound past sentences are the key to sounding professional: 'I organized, prepared, and delivered.' That's 3 actions in one. That's A2+ English."
      § 05
      Reading — Daily Report
      8–10 min
      FROM
      Laura Vega <l.vega@vertexsol.mx>
      TO
      Roberto Salinas — Operations Manager
      📋 Daily Report — Wednesday
      Watch for: new past verbs from S2 · compound past sentences · negatives with didn't · reason + because

      Hi Roberto,

      Here is my update for today.

      This morning, I attended the weekly team meeting and organized the agenda for the distribution team. After the meeting, I contacted our supplier — they approved the new delivery timeline for next Monday.

      In the afternoon, I prepared and presented the Q1 summary to the finance team. I also notified the sales department about the new pricing — they approved it immediately.

      Unfortunately, I didn't complete the product catalogue update. The supplier call lasted two hours longer than planned. I will deliver it tomorrow morning before 9 AM.

      Overall, today was a productive day. I completed 4 out of 5 tasks on my list.

      Best regards,

      Laura Vega · Operations Coordinator · Vertex Solutions · Ext. 207
      Comprehension
      Answer the questions.
      What did Laura attend this morning?
      Why didn't Laura complete the product catalogue update?
      Laura submitted the Q1 report to the finance team.
      The supplier approved the new delivery timeline.
      Laura completed all 5 tasks on her list.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Compound past: "I prepared AND presented" — point this out explicitly. Ask student to find all compound structures in the email.
      • R1 trap: "submitted" — Laura presented, not submitted. Both words sound professional; students confuse them. "Present" = show/explain verbally. "Submit" = send formally.
      • Grammar hunt: find all past verbs (attended, organized, contacted, approved x2, prepared, presented, notified, completed) + the 1 negative (didn't complete).
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Can you write a 3-sentence version of Laura's email about YOUR day? Just 3 sentences — prepared, presented, and didn't complete."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "Notice: Laura always explains WHY she didn't complete something. That's professionalism. Always add a reason: 'I didn't finish because…'"

      S3 — Irregular Verbs + Wh- Questions  ·  go/went · give/gave · meet/met · make/made · write/wrote
      GT Originals
      📋 The 3-part work update formula: "I [action] + [object] + [outcome/reason]" — never just "I attended a meeting."
      ⭐ "notify → notified" — the -ied rule applies to: study, reply, apply, carry, supply, notify. All very common in business English.
      💼 "I organized, prepared, and presented" — compound past is B2 fluency at A2 level. Use it every session from now.
      ⚠️ "present" (verb) = to show/explain. "present" (adjective) = actualmente presente. Different stress and meaning. Don't confuse.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Info Gap: teacher card is hidden in Student Mode. Push for compound sentences and full answers. A "Yes, I did" without elaboration = incomplete.
      • Evaluate: -ied spelling correct? Compound structures attempted? Reasons given for incomplete tasks?
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "You just answered a manager's check-in call in English. Marcos did it in the audio — now you did it in real life."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "S3 is a big one — irregular verbs. These are the most common verbs in English: went, gave, had, made, met. You'll use them every single day."
      • Upsell: "By S5, you describe your full work week in English. That's 5 sessions away. You're already 2 in."
