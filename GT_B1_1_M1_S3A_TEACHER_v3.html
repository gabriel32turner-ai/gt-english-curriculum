§ 01
Warm-Up — Rapid Fire · S1 + S2 Review
5–8 min
🎤 Interview Room — 9:00 AM

Imagine: You're in a job interview in English. The interviewer says: "Tell me — what do you see as your biggest professional strength? And what's one area you've been actively working to improve?"

You have 45 seconds. What do you say? No clichés — real answer, real evidence, Present Perfect.

📌 Teacher Follow-Up — S1 + S2 Quick Review
S1 review: "Give me one PP sentence about an achievement this year." (I've achieved / I've exceeded / I've led)
S2 review: "Name your top professional value and give me one PP conviction sentence." (I've always believed…)
S3A preview: "What's one thing you've improved professionally in the last year? Be specific."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This is S3A — student should activate PP from S1 and conviction PP from S2. Listen for: does the student give a label only ("I'm reliable") or a label + PP evidence ("My biggest strength is reliability — I've delivered every project on time for 3 years")? The gap is exactly what this session closes.
  • Common warm-up error: "My strength is that I work hard." → no PP, no evidence. Recast: "Can you add evidence? 'I've worked hard — specifically, I've…'"
  • Do NOT correct in the warm-up. The goal is to surface the gap between label and evidence. That gap is exactly what §4 resolves.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "I'll ask you this exact question again at the end of class. The difference in your answer will be significant — and you'll hear it yourself."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "'Tell me your strengths and weaknesses' is in every English job interview, every 360-degree review, every leadership coaching session. Today you get the exact language for it."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • "overcome" — irregular: overcome → overcame → overcome. Participle = base form. Very common error: "I've overcame." Drill explicitly: overcome → overcame → overcome.
  • "perfectionism" — the classic interview trap weakness. Point out: "I'm a perfectionist" is a red flag in interviews. The correct approach: acknowledge it AS a weakness AND show what you've done about it.
  • Production task: "Pick 3 verbs that describe your real professional development this year. Make one PP sentence with each." Target: "I've improved / I've developed / I've had to acknowledge."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Which of the 8 verbs best describes something you've actually worked on this year? Tell me — one sentence, Present Perfect, specific."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "These verbs are the vocabulary of professional development plans (PDPs) in international companies. HR uses exactly these words. Knowing them positions you as someone who understands growth, not just tasks."

GT Originals — The Label vs. Evidence Gap
At B1.1, students can name their strengths but struggle to evidence them in English. "I'm reliable" is A2. "I've delivered every project on time for three years" is B1.1. The Generator forces the jump from label to evidence — this is the pedagogical core of S3A. Score ✓ only when the student produces a complete PP sentence with REAL content and specific detail. Capa 1 · Pedagogical

§ 04
Grammar — PP for Professional Development
10–12 min
S3A Grammar Focus — 4 PP Patterns of Professional Development
📈
Development — I've improved
"I've improved my delegation skills over the last year."
"I've developed a more collaborative approach since taking this role."
"I've worked on this consistently."
✅
Achievement — I've delivered
"I've delivered every project on time this year."
"I've built a reliable track record in this department."
"I've consistently met deadlines under pressure."
🔁
Progress — I've been working on
"I've been working on my public speaking — it's improved significantly."
"I've been developing my leadership style since 2022."
"This is something I've been actively addressing."
⚠️
Weakness + Action — I've had to / I've overcome
"I've had to work on delegating — I've made real progress."
"I've overcome my resistance to asking for help."
"I've accepted feedback as a development tool, not criticism."
💡 The B1.1 strength-weakness formula:
Strength: "My biggest strength is [label]. I've [evidence with PP] — [impact]."
Weakness: "One area I've had to work on is [honest label]. I've [specific action]. I've [result or progress]."

Both patterns end with evidence in Present Perfect. Never just the label alone.
Present Perfect vs. Simple Past — Development Contexts
Present Perfect — use when…
Track record still relevant now: "I have delivered every project on time."
Ongoing development: "I have been working on delegating."
Lesson still applies: "I have learned that good enough is sometimes enough."
Achievement up to now: "I have overcome my fear of public speaking."
Simple Past — use when…
Specific moment: "I presented to the board in March for the first time."
Finished period: "I worked on the delegation issue last year."
One-time event: "I asked for feedback after the Q3 review."
Sequence of past actions: "I identified the gap, set a goal, and practiced weekly."
Golden rule for development contexts: If you're describing a strength or improvement that is still part of who you are now → Present Perfect. If you're describing a specific past action that is finished → Simple Past. In interviews: your strengths are ALWAYS PP (ongoing). Your specific examples may use Simple Past for the action steps.
⚠ Most Common Errors — S3A
✗ "I have overcame my fear."→✓ I've overcome my fear. (irregular: overcome → overcome, not overcame)
✗ "I've improved myself."→✓ I've improved / I've improved my skills. (say what improved, not "myself" alone)
✗ "My strength is that I delivered consistently."→✓ My strength is that I've delivered consistently. (ongoing track record = PP)
✗ "I've been worked on delegating."→✓ I've been working on delegating. (PP continuous: been + -ing)
Activity 1 — Choose the Correct PP Form
Select the correct answer for each strength/weakness context.
"My biggest strength is reliability — I ___ every deadline this year."
"One weakness I've acknowledged is perfectionism — I ___ when good enough is good enough."
"I ___ my communication style significantly since joining this team."
Which sentence uses Present Perfect INCORRECTLY?
Activity 2 — True or False: Development Language
Are these statements about professional English correct?
"I've delivered every project on time" uses PP correctly — it describes an ongoing track record, not a specific past moment.
"I've been working on my delegation" is stronger than "I've worked on it" for weaknesses because the continuous form shows the effort is still ongoing.
"My strength is reliability. I delivered projects on time last year." — This is the ideal interview answer structure.
"I'm a perfectionist" is a strong weakness answer in English job interviews.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Contrast table: pide al alumno que encuentre ambos tipos en la lectura (§5). PP dominará (~14 instancias). Simple Past aparece para los pasos de acción específicos. Esa proporción es el target para §7.
  • Q4: "overcame" = Simple Past; "overcome" = past participle. Error muy común — "I've overcame" suena mal de inmediato para un nativo. Drill: overcome → overcame → overcome.
  • T2: "I've been working on" vs "I've worked on" — ambas son PP, pero la forma continua enfatiza proceso en curso. Para debilidades en entrevistas, la continua es más poderosa porque muestra esfuerzo activo.
  • Después de las actividades: el alumno produce su respuesta completa de fortaleza+debilidad (estructura de §7) — 2-3 oraciones cada una. El maestro evalúa el uso de PP.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "¿Cuál de los 4 patrones (development / achievement / progress / weakness+action) es más difícil de producir naturalmente? Ese es el que practicamos ahora."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In S3B, you'll use all four PP development patterns spontaneously — as the interviewer AND the interviewee. No grammar tables, just the language."
§ 05
Reading — The Honest Manager
8–10 min
Claudia Vega
Regional Manager · Nortek Industrial · Houston
Performance Interview
Watch for: PP of development (green) · Simple Past actions with specific time (violet) · duration FOR/SINCE (gold) · vocabulary from S3A (highlighted)

I have been asked to describe my strengths and weaknesses many times in my career. The first few times, I gave the usual answers — "I'm detail-oriented," "I'm a perfectionist." But over time, I have learned that honest, specific answers are far more useful — both in interviews and in my own development.

My clearest strength is reliability. I have delivered every major project on time for the past five years. My team has come to depend on that — and I have never let them down. I have built this track record by over-communicating about risks, not by working longer hours.

A second strength is commitment. I have stayed with difficult projects when others left. I have set clear personal goals every year since 2018 and reviewed them quarterly. That review process began after a mentor challenged me to be more deliberate about growth.

My most honest weakness? I have had difficulty delegating. For years, I held on to tasks others could have handled. I have acknowledged this and have been working on it actively for the last eighteen months. I have assigned progressively more complex work to my team — and the results have surprised me.

The second area: I haven't always found it easy to accept critical feedback. I have improved — but it's taken time and deliberate practice. I have overcome the instinct to defend myself and learned to listen first. That's been one of the most valuable things I have developed professionally.

Comprehension — Strengths & Weaknesses PP
Answer using information from Claudia's reflection.
What is Claudia's primary strength — and what PP evidence does she give for it?
How long has Claudia been actively working on her delegation weakness?
"I have been working on it actively" — this PP continuous form shows the process is STILL ongoing at the time of speaking.
"reviewed them quarterly" is in Simple Past (violet) because it describes the specific action Claudia did after setting the goals each year — a completed step.
"I've overcome the instinct to defend myself" — past participle of "overcome" is "overcome" (same as base form).
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Color coding: verde = PP (desarrollo, track record, progreso). Violeta = Simple Past (acciones específicas con tiempo: "held on," "began," "challenged," "taken," "reviewed quarterly"). Pide al alumno que cuente ambos antes de las preguntas de comprensión. PP: ~14 instancias. SP: ~5 instancias.
  • R2 test de precisión: "since 2018" (cuando empezó a establecer metas) vs "for the last eighteen months" (cuando empezó a abordar activamente la debilidad de delegación). Ambas usan PP — con diferentes puntos de referencia.
  • Después de la comprensión: "Tell me YOUR version of Claudia's structure. 1 strength + evidence. 1 weakness + what you've done about it. Use PP throughout. 2 minutes."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Claudia says: 'Over time, I've learned that honest, specific answers are far more useful.' Is your approach to weaknesses the same? What have you been working on?"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Claudia's structure: strength (label + PP evidence) → strength 2 (label + PP evidence) → weakness 1 (honest label + what I've done) → weakness 2 (honest label + how I've improved). This is the template for §7."

▶ Show ElevenLabs Script

Manager (Michael) + Employee (Sarah) · Speed: 0.84 · ~90 sec

Manager: So, let's talk about this year. What do you see as your biggest strength — based on what you've delivered?

Employee: I'd say reliability. I've met every deadline this year — even during the product launch when we had three simultaneous deliverables. I've always made sure the team knows what to expect from me.

Manager: That's consistent with what I've observed. What about a second strength?

Employee: Problem-solving, I think. I've handled three major client escalations this year and found solutions each time. I've developed a framework I've now shared with the whole team.

Manager: Good. Now — development areas. What have you been working on?

Employee: Honestly, I've had to work on giving difficult feedback. I've avoided some conversations I should have had earlier. I've acknowledged this — and I've started having those conversations earlier, even when they're uncomfortable.

Manager: Have you seen any improvement?

Employee: Yes. I've had two difficult conversations in the last month that I would have avoided six months ago. I've accepted that it's part of the role — and I've improved because of it.

⏱ Target: 85–95 sec · PP density: I've met / I've always made / I've observed / I've handled / I've developed / I've shared / I've had to / I've avoided / I've acknowledged / I've started / I've had / I've accepted / I've improved — 13 distinct PP uses

Listening Activities
Listen and answer. Identify Present Perfect forms, not just content.
What does the employee give as evidence for her reliability?
What has the employee been working on — and what evidence does she give of improvement?
"I've accepted that it's part of the role" — this PP shows a shift in mindset that started in the past and remains true now.
The employee says she has NEVER avoided difficult conversations — she deals with them immediately.
"I've had two difficult conversations in the last month" is PP because it's recent experience with present relevance — she's showing improvement NOW.

  • Después de las actividades: el alumno juega el papel del empleado. El maestro juega el gerente. Performance review real sobre el trabajo actual del alumno. 5 preguntas — completamente en PP.

Today I Learned
  • 8 development nouns: reliability, commitment, improvement, feedback, deadline, detail, potential, perfectionism
  • 8 growth verbs: improve, overcome, develop, acknowledge, accept, deliver, push, set goals
  • Strength formula: Label + "I've [evidence]" + impact
  • Weakness formula: Honest label + "I've been working on" + result

  • PP vs Simple Past: ongoing track record (PP) vs specific past action (SP)
