Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This warm-up activates S1A grammar in free production mode. Do NOT correct errors yet — this is a diagnostic baseline. Note: which past verbs did they attempt? Which did they get wrong?
  • Common errors to expect: "I was review the report" (omitting -ed), "I didn't went" (base verb after didn't), "I goed" (irregular attempt — tell them: great, we use 'went' for 'go', but today's verbs are all regular).
  • Accept all attempts. If the student goes quiet after 30 seconds, prompt: "What verb from S1A can you use for that?" Then give them the word.
  • Time: keep this tight at 4–5 minutes. If the student only manages 3–4 sentences, that's enough. The session will carry them much further.
  • Key: the picture prompt format (new in S1B) produces different language than the scenario prompt in S1A. Students tend to speak more freely with an image anchor.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Pick the image that feels most like YOUR week. There's no wrong choice — this is about your story."
  • "I'm going to ask you the same question at the end of class. Let's compare how much more you can say."
  • If the student says "I don't know what happened there" — redirect: "OK, what DID happen at your desk last week? Use that."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Every professional meeting starts with a past summary. Today you're going to do exactly that — in English, about YOUR real week."
  • This is the session where the student transitions from 'learner' to 'user.' Frame it that way from minute one.

▶ Show ElevenLabs Script
SPEAKER 1 — Mark (Project Lead) · Voice: Adam · Rate: 0.85

"Good morning, everyone. Thanks for joining the Monday standup. Let me start with last week's highlights. On Monday and Tuesday, I finished the Q3 planning document and submitted it to the board. The board reviewed it on Wednesday and approved the budget we proposed. On Thursday I scheduled three client calls for this week. I didn't complete the risk analysis — I needed more data from the operations team. I planned to finish it today. Any questions before we move on?"

SPEAKER 2 — Jessica (Operations Manager) · Voice: Sarah · Rate: 0.82

"Thanks Mark. Quick update from operations. Last Tuesday we delivered the prototype to the testing team. They tested it Wednesday and Thursday and confirmed the specs match the client requirements. We updated the documentation and emailed it to the engineering team on Friday. One issue: the supplier in Dallas canceled our material order, so I rescheduled it for next week. That's all from my side."

⏱ Target: 100–110 sec total · Past verbs highlighted in script (cyan = affirmative, red = negative) · No city names from Mexico · American English throughout

Comprehension — Listen & Answer
Listen to both speakers. Then answer the questions.
1. What did Mark do on Monday and Tuesday?
2. Why didn't Mark complete the risk analysis?
3. What happened with the supplier's material order?
The board reviewed and approved the budget Mark proposed.
The testing team confirmed the specs did NOT match the client requirements.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Two-listen approach: Listen 1 = global — "Who are the two speakers? What are their roles? What's the general topic?" Listen 2 = focused — answer the comprehension questions.
  • This standup is more complex than S1A's: richer vocabulary (approved, prototype, specs, documentation, rescheduled), 2 speakers, and different types of information from each. This is deliberate — the student needs to track who said what.
  • LQ2 is a listening trap: the student might confuse "needed more data" with "didn't have time" — these are related but different. Ask: "What are Mark's exact words?" Teach specific comprehension, not inference.
  • LTF2 is the most important trap: "confirmed the specs DO match" — the negative in the test statement inverts the meaning. This tests precise listening, not global understanding.
  • After questions: count past verbs from Mark's section (finished, submitted, reviewed, approved, scheduled, didn't complete = 6 verbs). Ask student: "What made Jessica's update feel more natural than just listing facts?"
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Notice how both speakers cover: what they DID · what they DIDN'T finish · a problem + solution. That's the 3-part formula. Your standup in §5 will follow the same structure."
  • "Jessica's 'One issue:' opener is a great professional phrase. It's honest and direct. We'll see it again in the preparation section."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "This is exactly what your Monday morning will sound like at your company — in English. Notice that both speakers sound confident, professional, and clear. That's what we're building toward in §5."
  • Upsell moment: "By M1 S5, you'll give a 2-minute review covering an entire work week. Jessica's style today is what that sounds like."
§ 04
Speaking Preparation — Your Own Standup
15 min
Your Mission
You are going to give a 90-second Monday morning standup about your real last week at work. Use at least 5 different past simple verbs. Include at least 1 negative (I didn't…) and mention at least 2 time expressions.
Standup Template — Use this as a guide (adapt it to YOUR week)
On Monday
I ___ [verb+ed] ___ . It was ___ .
Tue & Wed
I ___ [verb+ed] ___ and ___ [verb+ed] ___ .
On Thursday
I ___ [verb+ed] ___ . The result was ___ .
Negative
I didn't ___ [base verb] ___ because ___ .
Overall
Overall, last week was ___ . This week I plan to ___ .
🗣 Helpful Phrases for Your Standup — 8 Expansion Phrases
I managed to…
"I managed to finish the report before the deadline."
The main challenge was…
"The main challenge was the delay from the supplier."
I focused on…
"Last week I focused on the Q3 budget review."
I made progress on…
"I made progress on the new client proposal."
I wrapped up…
"On Friday I wrapped up the testing phase."
I kicked off…
"On Monday I kicked off the new project with the team."
I circled back to…
"I circled back to the supplier issue on Thursday."
I ran into an issue with…
"I ran into an issue with the delivery schedule."
⏱
Take 3 minutes to mentally organize your standup. In your head (or on a small notepad): write down 5 verbs you will use and 2 time expressions. Do NOT write full sentences — just keywords. The teacher will give you the signal when 3 minutes are up.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Your role in this section: coach, not teacher. Don't give the student their standup content. Help them find it. Ask: "What was the biggest thing you worked on last week?" → wait → "OK. Which verb from S1A fits that?" → wait.
  • If the student says "I don't know what to say" — they mean they don't know how to say it. Ask: "What did you actually do last Monday?" Usually this unlocks the content immediately.
  • The scaffold template is a guide, not a script. A student who goes slightly off-template but produces natural language is doing better than one who reads mechanically from the scaffold.
  • The 8 helpful phrases are A2.1-appropriate upgrades. "I managed to" and "The main challenge was" are the two highest-value ones — push the student to use at least one of them in their standup.
  • Monitor the 3-minute prep window. At 2:30, give a 30-second warning. Do NOT let this slide to 5 minutes — prep overload reduces spoken fluency.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "This is harder than a practice exercise — that's exactly why it matters. You're not producing a textbook answer. You're producing your week."
  • "If you can't remember the exact word, describe around it. That's a real communication skill too."
  • For confident students: push them to use 2 of the 8 expansion phrases. "Can you add 'I managed to' anywhere? 'The main challenge was'?"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • This preparation moment is where the student feels the gap between passive knowledge (I understand the grammar) and active skill (I can use it about my own real work). That gap is what closes in §5.
  • When the student gives their standup in §5, they will feel the payoff of this preparation. That emotional arc — challenge → production → success — is the retention hook for this session.

15 min
Your turn. Give your 90-second standup now. The teacher will act as your team lead or colleague and ask 3–5 follow-up questions. Respond naturally — even if you make mistakes, keep going. Don't stop to correct yourself. Just communicate.
Teacher Follow-Up Prompts — Use 3–5 based on what the student says
"Why did you decide to do that first?"
"Did you have any problems last week?"
"What did you learn from that meeting?"
"How did your manager react?"
"Who did you work with most last week?"
"What did you postpone for this week?"
"Which day was the most productive?"

"What would you do differently next time?"
"What did you finish that you're most proud of?"
Self-Evaluation — After Your Standup, Check What You Did
  • I used at least 5 different past simple verbs
  • I included at least 1 negative (I didn't…)
  • I mentioned at least 2 time expressions
  • I answered the teacher's follow-up questions in simple past
  • I spoke for at least 60 seconds without long pauses
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Teacher role: team lead or peer colleague — NOT a language teacher during the standup. Ask follow-up questions naturally, as a colleague would. Only comment on language AFTER the standup is complete.
  • Evaluation targets during the standup: (1) at least 5 different past verbs — count silently, (2) at least 1 negative used correctly (didn't + base verb), (3) at least 2 time expressions, (4) intelligible follow-up answers in past tense.
  • If the student freezes mid-standup: give them a lifeline — "So what happened after that?" or "And on Wednesday?" — a contextual prompt, not a grammar correction.
  • Do not correct during the standup. Take a few mental notes. After the standup is complete, do a brief 2-minute "language debrief": 1 thing they did very well (specific), 1 thing to improve (specific form, not a general comment).
  • After the self-eval checklist: ask "How many did you check? Was anything harder than you expected?" This surfaces the student's own learning awareness.
  • If student unchecks a box: that's honest metacognition — celebrate it: "Good — you noticed. That awareness is the first step to fixing it."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • Celebration phrases for after the standup: "That was a real Monday morning standup — in English. You did it." / "You described your entire work week in 90 seconds. That's a professional skill." / "I heard [specific verb they used well] — that's exactly right."
  • If the student made mistakes: "You communicated clearly — your meaning was 100% understood. Now let's make the form match the quality of your ideas."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Testimonial trigger: After the standup, ask — "How did that feel? Could you have done that an hour ago? What changed?" Let the student articulate their own progress. This is the highest-value moment of the session for retention.
  • Upsell: "In S2A, you'll add 8 more verbs and learn how to ask and answer work questions. Your standup gets richer every session."
  • If this session was their best English performance to date: "This is the moment where it clicks. Remember this feeling."

§ SP
Speaking Practice
10–12 min
🗣 Guided Speaking — Standup Mastery
Your Real Work Week — No Notes
You already gave your standup in §5. Now push further: answer follow-up questions, react to your partner's update, and handle unexpected questions — all in simple past. No preparation. No script.
Question 1
"From your standup — what was the MOST important thing you did last week? Tell me more about it."
Hint: The most important thing was… / I spent… hours on… / I submitted it to… / My manager reviewed…
Question 2
"You mentioned you didn't finish something. Why not — and what happened next?"
Hint: I didn't finish because… / The deadline changed… / I had to… first / Then I…
Question 3
"Did you work with anyone else on a task last week? What did THEY do?"
Hint: She reviewed… / He submitted… / We confirmed… / My team completed… / They attended…
Question 4
"If your manager asked you right now — 'What was your biggest win last week?' — what would you say?"
Hint: My biggest win was… / I successfully… / We finally… / The client approved…
Question 5
"Now YOU ask me a question about my work week using simple past."
Hint: Did you…? / What did you…? / Did you finish…? / Who did you meet with…?
⏱ 10–12 min total 📋 5 questions 🎯 Min. 6 past verbs 👥 Pair or teacher-led
⚡ Speaking Challenge — Closing
"Give me the SAME standup you gave in §5 — but this time add one negative and one question. 90 seconds. From memory. Go."
Teacher evaluates: past verb accuracy · at least 1 negative (didn't + BASE) · at least 1 past question formed · 2+ time expressions · fluency improvement vs §5 standup. Compare explicitly: "Was that easier than §5? Which verb came out faster?" That comparison is the confidence anchor.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This §SP is designed as a mastery loop: student already produced the standup in §5 — this section pushes them to handle unexpected follow-up questions, exactly what happens in real Monday standups.
  • Q3 is critical: it forces 3rd person (-s form). If student says "she attend," recast: "She attended — and what did she submit?" Keep the correction in context, never out of it.
  • Q5 (student asks you): if they struggle to form a question, prompt: "Start with Did you… or What did you…" Never give them the full question — make them build it.
  • Target: student should produce the Speaking Challenge standup faster and with fewer hesitations than in §5. If they do, that's the evidence of mastery.
  • If time is tight: skip Q3 and Q4, go straight to Q5 + Speaking Challenge. The question-formation and free output are non-negotiable.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Notice how the second standup came out faster? That's not luck — that's your brain building the pattern. Every time you repeat it, it gets 30% faster."
  • When student successfully forms a question (Q5): "You just asked a past question in English — unprompted. That is the hardest thing in this module. You did it."
  • After Speaking Challenge: "Compare §5 to now. Which verbs came out automatically? Those are yours now — no one can take them away."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In S2A you'll add a new set of past verbs to your standup: attended, notified, completed, resolved. You'll do the same standup — but richer."
  • Upsell: "By S5 you'll give a 2-minute spoken report of your whole work week — with irregular verbs, negatives, and Wh- questions. Today's standup is the foundation."
  • Testimonial trigger: "If you gave this standup in your next team meeting in English — how would your colleagues react? What would change?"

GT Originals — Mastery Tips
📋 A great standup = 3 parts: what I did · what I didn't finish · what I plan next. Cover all three, every time.
⚡ "I managed to finish" is stronger than "I finished." Use it when something was difficult or took effort.
🎯 After "The main challenge was," use a noun phrase: "the deadline" / "the supplier." NOT a full verb clause.
🔊 "I ran into an issue" — not "I found a problem." This is the phrase professionals actually use in standups.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • GT Flashcard Wall — Round 1: say the base verb, student says the past form + one real sentence from their own standup (not a generic one). This connects vocabulary to their own production from §5.
  • Round 2 phrases: drill as starters — teacher says the beginning ("The main challenge was..."), student completes it with a real example from their week. If they can't, give them 5 seconds, then help. Do not skip any card.
  • Speaking Challenge: this is free production — no scaffold, no template. Evaluate: fluency (did they stop less than in the warm-up?), grammar accuracy (how many correct past forms?), lexical range (did they use any of the 8 expansion phrases?). A student who uses 1–2 expansion phrases in the Storytelling challenge has internalized them.
  • Come back to §1 warm-up: "Earlier you picked a picture and said a few things. Can you tell the same story now — better?" This bookending is a powerful fluency demonstration.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "In S1A you learned it. In S1B you used it for real — about your actual week, with follow-up questions on the spot. That gap is where fluency lives."
  • Specific celebration: "I heard you use [specific phrase from §4]. That's not a textbook phrase — that's your English now."
  • If the student struggled in §5 but improved in the Speaking Challenge: "You got better between §5 and right now. That's 10 minutes of real improvement."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Testimonial trigger — Mandatory: Before ending the session, ask: "How did this session compare to S1A? Which felt harder? Which felt more real?" Let them answer fully. This self-reflection is the anchor for retention between sessions.
