§1
Hot Take — Should You Work When You're Sick?
Timed opinion · No preparation · Use S1A vocabulary immediately
⏱ 5 MIN
⚡ Teacher Note — Hot Take Setup
No preparation time. Students speak immediately. Timer starts the moment you read the prompt. The goal is FLUENCY under pressure — not perfection. Correct major errors (she/he have) after, never during. After 2–3 students share, use badge as reward for anyone who attempts the full 60 seconds.
🔥 THE PROMPT — Read aloud to students
"Your colleague has a bad headache and a slight fever — but tomorrow is a big presentation. Should they go to work?
Give your opinion. Use: I think… / You should… / You shouldn't… / I have a [symptom] and I…"
Badge rules: 🔥 = complete 60 seconds · Use at least 2 health phrases from S1A · Include 1 "should/shouldn't"
60
🔥 Hot Take Timer — 60 seconds
Speak without stopping. Use health vocabulary. No notes. Go!
💡 After Hot Take
Ask 2–3 students to share one sentence. Write any "she/he have" errors on the board. Leave them there — students will self-correct after the Relay section. This creates a productive correction loop.
§2
Debate — Strict Sick Day Policies: For or Against?
Two sides · Timed · Grammar in use: should/shouldn't + have/has · Realistic HR scenario
⏱ 10 MIN
⚡ Teacher Note — Running the Debate
Assign sides randomly — students do NOT choose their opinion. This forces language production over personal belief. 4 minutes per side, then 2 minutes open debate. The teacher referees: anyone who uses incorrect "she have" or "should to" gets flagged immediately. Earn badge ⚡ for using "should/shouldn't" at least 3 times correctly.
Context: The company HR department wants a new policy: employees must provide a medical certificate for ANY sick day — even one day. Some managers agree. Others think it's too strict. You will debate this.
Side A — PRO: Support the Policy
"Yes, employees should provide a certificate for every sick day."
  • "I think companies should have strict policies because…"
  • "Employees shouldn't take sick days without a certificate because…"
  • "If someone has a fever, they should see a doctor and…"
  • "The company has a lot of absent employees and…"
Side B — AGAINST: The Policy is Too Strict
"No, employees shouldn't need a certificate for one day."
  • "I think this policy is too strict because employees…"
  • "When someone has a headache, they should rest, but they shouldn't…"
  • "My back hurts sometimes. I shouldn't need a doctor to confirm…"
  • "Companies should trust their employees when they say they have a…"
4:00
⚡ Debate Timer — 4 minutes per side
Earn badge ⚡ for using "should/shouldn't" at least 3 times correctly

Relay — Document Ownership at HR
Transform sentences using possessive pronouns · New visual: HR Document Desk · 5 rounds
⏱ 8 MIN
⚡ Teacher Note — The Relay
This is the NEW visual for S1B: an HR document desk scenario. The context: Mónica's HR department has a pile of documents and needs to identify who each one belongs to. Students transform "possessive adjective + noun" into "possessive pronoun" sentences. This bridges the grammar gap from S1A (have/has) to the new structure (mine/yours/his/hers). Relay format: Student 1 writes answer → passes to Student 2 who checks and writes next. Badge 🎤 for completing all 5 correctly as a team.

⚡ Common Error to Pre-empt
Students often say "It's my" → ✅ "It's mine." / "Is your?" → ✅ "Is it yours?" Drill this pattern 3 times with the widget before starting the relay cards.
Round 1
HR coordinator holds a medical certificate: "Is this your certificate, Mónica?"
Transform: Use a possessive pronoun → "Yes, it's ___."
Round 2
Manager points to a report: "Whose report is this? Is it Carlos's?"
Transform: Confirm with possessive pronoun → "Yes, it's ___."
Round 3
Two reports are on the desk. "These are our team's absence reports."
Transform: "These reports are ___."
Round 4
HR finds a folder: "Is this Ana's sick leave form?"
Confirm: "Yes, it's ___." (she = ?)
Round 5 — Challenge ⭐
Mix of documents on the desk. HR asks: "Whose are these documents? Are they the operations team's?"
Confirm: "Yes, they are ___." (they = ?)

§5
Solo Challenge — The Sick Day Call
120 seconds · No notes · Full integration of S1A + S1B · Character: Mónica Herrera
⏱ 8 MIN
📋 The Scenario
You are Mónica Herrera. It's Monday morning. You wake up feeling terrible. You call your manager Daniel Ochoa.

In your call you MUST include:
✅ How you feel (I'm not feeling well / I have…)
✅ At least 3 symptoms with intensity (mild / bad / terrible)
✅ At least 2 body part sentences (My [part] hurts/aches)
✅ What you will send to HR and when
✅ One thing that belongs to you using a possessive pronoun (mine / hers / his etc.)
✅ Closing phrase (Take care / Get well soon etc.)
💡 Model opening: "Hi Daniel, good morning. I'm sorry to call so early. I'm not feeling well today. I have a terrible headache and…"
2:00
⭐ Solo Challenge — 2 minutes
Speak without stopping. Hit all 6 checkpoints. This is your full A1.2 M4 S1 integration test.
💡 Evaluation Note
Listen specifically for: (1) have/has agreement, (2) article before symptom, (3) possessive pronoun — not "it's my" but "it's mine." Note errors silently. Debrief after ALL students finish, not individually. This preserves confidence and fluency.

Source Analysis — Who Does It Belong To?
4 real workplace documents · Choose the correct possessive pronoun · HR context
⏱ 8 MIN
⚡ Teacher Note — Source Analysis
These 4 cards present common HR workplace documents. Students identify the owner and choose the correct possessive pronoun. The metacognitive question at the end of each card pushes students to explain WHY they chose the pronoun — this is the real language learning moment.
📱 WhatsApp — Team Group Chat
[Daniel]: "Hey team — I found a medical certificate on the printer. It says 'Solaris Manufacturing' and the date is today. Whose is it?"
[Mónica]: "Oh! That's ___. I printed it this morning."
Which possessive pronoun does Mónica use?
💭 Why? Because Mónica is speaking about herself → I → mine
📧 Work Email from Manager
From: Daniel Ochoa
To: HR Department
"Please find Carlos Vega's sick leave form attached. This form is ___ — he sent it to me this morning."
Which possessive pronoun goes in the blank?
💭 Why? Carlos = he → possessive pronoun = his
🗣️ Office Dialogue — HR Coordinator
HR: "These absence reports — are they the Operations team's?"
Manager: "Yes, all of these are ___. The team submitted them last Friday."
Which possessive pronoun is correct?
💭 Why? The Operations team = they → possessive pronoun = theirs
📋 HR Internal Note — Return to Work
HR Note: "Ana García returned today. The medical certificate on file — the coordinator confirmed it's ___. Please process her absence."
What possessive pronoun confirms Ana's certificate?
💭 Why? Ana = she → possessive pronoun = hers
