Model Answer

"I used to be a spender — I never had a budget. But a few years ago, I started tracking my expenses, and it changed everything. Now I save about 20% of my income every month. I'm more careful with money now than I was five years ago."

💬 Colloquial — Talking About Money

5 min
How real people actually talk about money in English
I'm more careful with money now.
When: describing how your habits have changed
"I used to spend a lot on clothes, but I'm more careful with money now."
It's easy to lose track.
When: talking about overspending
"With subscriptions and small purchases, it's easy to lose track of how much you spend."
I try to save every month.
When: describing your financial routine
"I try to save every month, even if it's just a small amount."
That's a waste of money.
When: criticizing an unnecessary purchase
"A $200 phone case? That's a waste of money."
Práctica rápida: "Tu colega gasta $500 al mes en Uber Eats. ¿Qué opinas?" → "That's a waste of money. If you cook at home, you'll save a lot."
Gramática integrada: Las frases se combinan naturalmente con First Conditional — "If you track your expenses, you won't lose track."

🎯 Grammar — First Conditional

10 min
If + Present Simple, will + infinitive — real possibilities, real consequences
REFERENCIAL — NOT drilling. B1.1 grammar is a reference tool, not a conjugation exercise. Show the table, give a personal example, activate the Poll. The activities test understanding, not memorization.
Error #1 globally: "If I will save more" → ✗. Present Simple in the if-clause. ALWAYS.
StructureFormMoney Example
If-clauseIf + Present SimpleIf I save $500 a month…
Result clausewill + infinitive…I'll have $6,000 by December.
Negative resultwon't + infinitiveIf prices go up, I won't be able to afford it.
If-clause secondResult, if + conditionI'll invest more if the market improves.
QuestionWhat will you do if…?What will you do if you get a bonus?
⚠️ #1 GLOBAL ERROR
✗ "If I will save more, I'll buy a house."
✅ "If I save more, I'll buy a house." — No "will" in the if-clause!

🎧 Listening — Rodrigo's Money Story

8 min
📍 Rodrigo talks about how his money habits changed over time
🎙️
ElevenLabs — "My Money Story"
Voice: James (American) · Speed: 0.85 · B1.1 M3 S1A
When I was 22, I was a total spender. I got my first real salary and I spent everything — restaurants, clothes, gadgets. I didn't save anything. I didn't even have a budget.

Then I got into debt. Credit card debt. About $3,000. It wasn't a lot, but it scared me. That was my wake-up call. I started tracking my expenses — just in a simple spreadsheet at first. And I realized I was wasting money on things I didn't even enjoy.

Now I'm 30, and my relationship with money is completely different. I save about 25% of my income. I have an emergency fund. I've started investing in index funds — nothing complicated.

If I get a bonus this year, I'll put half into savings and use the other half for a trip. If the economy stays stable, I'll probably buy an apartment next year. But if prices go up too much, I'll wait.

My advice? Start small. If you save even $50 a month, it adds up. And track everything — if you don't know where your money goes, you'll never control it.
