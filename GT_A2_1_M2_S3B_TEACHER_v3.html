Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • S3B arranca con producción de preguntas — no de respuestas. "You are the director now. Ask all 6 Wh- questions." En S3A el alumno respondía preguntas. En S3B las hace. Eso es el mastery test.
  • Diagnóstico en 60 segundos: ¿produce "Who did you meet?" o dice "Who you met?"? ¿Usa base verb después de did? ¿Forma "How long did it take?" correctamente? Lo que no puede hacer aquí = foco de §5 Live Interview.
  • Recast silencioso obligatorio. Si dice "Where you went?" → tú respondes "Good question! I went to the main conference room." — modelo correcto en respuesta sin señalar el error.
  • 5–7 minutos. El corazón de S3B es §5 Live Interview y §6 Storytelling.
Capa 2 · Engagement

Capa 3 · Retención

Recap Speedrun — S3A Wh- Questions
5 min
⚡
Speedrun
5 cards · Say the question BEFORE you flip · 2 rounds
5 MIN

⚡ See the Wh- word → say a COMPLETE question in Simple Past → flip to check. Round 2: faster. No hesitation.

1
WHO question
produce it now
Who did you meet at the conference?
Structure: WHO + did + subject + meet (base!) + ?
2
HOW LONG question
produce it now
How long did the meeting take?
Structure: HOW LONG + did + subject + take (base!) + ? ❌ "took"
3
WHY question
produce it now
Why did the deal get delayed?
Structure: WHY + did + subject + get (base!) + ? Answer starts with "Because…"
4
WHAT happened?
exception — why no did?
What happened at the trade show?
Exception: "what" is the subject → NO did. "What happened?" not "What did happen?"
5
WHERE question + go
the most common error
Where did you go after the presentation?
❌ "Where you went?" — always: WHERE + did + subject + go (base!) + ?
Speed Drill (60 seconds): Teacher calls out: "WHO / WHAT / WHERE / WHEN / WHY / HOW LONG" — student produces a complete Wh- question in Simple Past immediately. Round 1 slow, Round 2 no pause between questions. If any question fails → note it for §5 Live Interview target.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Speed Drill diagnóstico: el error que aparece en ronda 2 bajo presión es el error real del alumno. "Where did you went?" o "How long did it took?" bajo velocidad → eso consolida §5.
  • Si "What happened?" confunde al alumno (tiende a añadir did) → di: "This one is the exception. 'What' is the subject — like asking 'Who came?' — no did needed." Una vez, claramente, luego sigue.
  • 5 minutos duros. §5 Live Interview es el corazón de S3B.

Live Game

Game 1 — 5 min · Wordwall
Wh- Questions in Past — Match the Formula
[Wordwall — Match Up]

Pairs: WHO + did + S + base?→person question / WHAT + did + S + base?→thing or action / WHERE + did + S + go?→place / WHEN + did + S + base?→time / WHY + did + S + base?→reason / HOW LONG + did + S + take?→duration / What happened?→no did — subject question

Round 1: match question type · Round 2: beat your time
Teacher shares screen. Student matches Wh- word to its question type and structure. Round 2 challenge: beat your time.
Game 2 — 5–6 min · Wh- Buzz

Tap the card → produce a FULL correct question before reading the answer. A2.1 standard: correct Wh- + did + subject + base verb + ?

BUILD IT
WHO · meet · client · trade show
✅ "Who did the client meet at the trade show?" — WHO + did + S + meet (base!)
ERROR HUNT
"Where you went after the meeting?"
❌ Missing did! ✅ "Where did you go after the meeting?" — go not went after did
BUILD IT
HOW LONG · negotiation · take
✅ "How long did the negotiation take?" — take not took after did!
ERROR HUNT
"How long did the trip took?"
❌ "took" is past — never after did! ✅ "How long did the trip take?" — base form always
BUILD IT
WHY · project · delay
✅ "Why did the project get delayed?" or "Why did they delay the project?" — reason question
EXCEPTION
WHAT + happened + plant · no did?
✅ "What happened at the plant visit?" — no did because WHAT is the subject, not the object
BUILD IT
WHAT · client · decide
✅ "What did the client decide?" — decide not decided after did. Answer: "They decided to…"
ERROR HUNT
"Who you met at the conference?"
❌ Missing did + subject! ✅ "Who did you meet at the conference?" — full structure required
BUILD IT
WHEN · client · send · proposal
✅ "When did the client send the proposal?" — send not sent after did. Answer: "They sent it on…"
ERROR HUNT
"What did happen at the meeting?"
❌ Unusual — "What happened at the meeting?" is the standard form. No did needed when WHAT = subject.
BUILD IT
WHERE · team · go · after · lunch
✅ "Where did the team go after lunch?" — go not went after did. Answer: "They went to…"
⭐ CHALLENGE
Use all 6 Wh- words in 6 questions about ONE event.
Who did they meet? / What did they decide? / Where did they go? / When did it start? / Why did it take long? / How long did it last? — all 6 about the same event!
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Buzz: 12 cartas, 5–6 minutos. ERROR HUNT cards son las más valiosas — el alumno identifica Y produce la versión correcta. Si no puede producir la corrección → eso es exactamente el foco de §5.
  • Challenge card: "6 questions about ONE event" — si el alumno lo produce espontáneamente y correctamente → eso es el mastery A2.1 de S3. Celébra con especificidad: "You used all 6 Wh- words correctly in one sequence. That's the debrief skill."

EXTENDED DIALOGUE — TRADE SHOW DEBRIEF — FASTER PACE — MORE WH- QUESTIONS

Daniel: Roberto — good to have you back. What happened at the Mexico City trade show? Give me the full picture.

Roberto: It went better than expected. First, we set up the booth on Wednesday morning. Then we spoke to about forty potential clients over two days.

Daniel: Forty? Who did you think was the most promising contact?

Roberto: A company called TekNord. I met their procurement director on Thursday afternoon. She took our whole catalogue and asked us to send a formal proposal.

Daniel: Excellent. What did she say about pricing?

Roberto: She said it looked competitive. She didn't give a specific budget, but I could tell they were serious.

Daniel: Where did you have the main conversation with TekNord? At the booth?

Roberto: Actually no — we found a quiet area near the conference rooms. We spoke for about thirty minutes without interruptions.

Daniel: Smart. When did they say they would get back to us?

Roberto: She told me their team meets every Monday. She thought we would hear something by next Wednesday at the latest.

Daniel: Good. Why did you focus on TekNord specifically? There must have been other options.

Roberto: Because I knew they were expanding. I read about it before the show. It made sense to go to them first.

Daniel: Good preparation. How long did the whole show take — including setup and breakdown?

Roberto: Three days total. We left Celaya on Tuesday evening and got back Friday morning.

Daniel: Perfect. What happened with the competitor — TechMax? Did you see them there?

Roberto: Yes — they had a bigger booth but I didn't see many people visiting it. I think we won the visibility battle. Their pricing is higher — that became obvious when I talked to shared contacts.

Daniel: Great work, Roberto. Let's prepare that TekNord proposal today.

§ 05
Live Interview — You Are the Director
12–14 min
🎙
Live Interview
You are the Director — conduct the full debrief · All 6 Wh- questions
12–14 MIN
The Full Debrief — Role Inversion
The teacher just returned from a business event (client visit, trade show, negotiation — you decide which). You are the Director. Your job: conduct a complete professional debrief using all 6 Wh- question types. Push for detail. Don't accept one-word answers. Use follow-up questions.
YOUR ROLE — The Director (Student)
Conduct a complete professional debrief. You must use all 6 Wh- question types — at least once each. Push for detail. If the answer is too short, follow up immediately.
  • Start: "Welcome back. Can you give me a quick update on…"
  • WHO → Who did you meet / speak to / present to?
  • WHAT → What happened? / What did they decide / say?
  • WHERE → Where did you go / meet / have the conversation?
  • WHEN → When did they call / decide / contact you?
  • WHY → Why did it take so long / go well / get delayed?
  • HOW LONG → How long did the whole trip / meeting take?
TEACHER ROLE — The Returning Employee
Answer every question with full sentences. Use irregular verbs and sequence connectors naturally. Give enough detail to allow follow-up questions. Don't make it too easy.
  • Give answers that require follow-up: "It was interesting…"
  • Use irregular verbs: went / met / spoke / took / told / said
  • Use sequence: "First… Then… After that… Finally…"
  • Include an unexpected event or complication
  • End with a clear outcome: "In the end, we…"

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • ACCIÓN: Antes de empezar di: "You are the director now. I just got back from a client visit in Chicago. You have 5 minutes to find out everything. Go." — Sin más instrucciones. El Live Tracker marca el progreso en tiempo real.
  • Como "returning employee": responde de forma que requiera follow-up. Si el alumno pregunta "Who did you meet?" → di "Oh, there were several interesting people there." — Eso obliga un follow-up WHO question o WHAT question.
  • Errores en el Live Interview: recast en respuesta, nunca corrección directa. Si pregunta "Who you met?" → tú respondes "Good question! I met the regional VP and three managers." — El modelo correcto está en tu respuesta.
  • Si el alumno agota sus preguntas antes de los 5 minutos → di: "You still haven't asked me HOW LONG it took — or WHY it took so long." — Señala los Wh- words faltantes sin dar la pregunta.
  • Switch: después del Live Interview, el alumno responde y el maestro hace las preguntas sobre el evento real del alumno. Inversión de roles = consolidación completa.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Acabas de hacer una reunión de debrief en inglés como si fueras el director. Con las 6 preguntas. Con respuestas en irregular verbs. Sin notas. Eso es A2.1 mastery — y es exactamente lo que se espera en una empresa internacional."
Capa 3 · Retención & Upsell
  • "S4A y S4B son la recta final de M2 — narrar un evento completo pasado, incluyendo problemas, decisiones y resultados. Para entonces ya dominas las preguntas, los verbos irregulares y los conectores. Estás listo."
  • Trigger testimonial MANDATORIO: "¿Qué fue más difícil — hacer las preguntas o responderlas? ¿Cuál fue la Wh- question más natural para ti?" — La respuesta revela el perfil de aprendizaje del alumno.

Story Photo Wall. Each photo represents a moment from a business event. For each photo: (1) tell what happened in that moment, (2) ask a Wh- question about it. The Wh- word for each photo is shown.

Your Story — With Wh- Questions Built In
1
First, I [ irregular verb ] to [ place ] and [ met / spoke to / gave… ] [ WHO? ].
2
Then, we [ WHAT happened? ]. They [ decided / told me / asked… ].
3
After that, we [ WHERE did you go? ] and [ what happened there ].
4
There was a complication: [ WHY did it happen? ]. I had to [ solution ].
5
Finally, [ outcome ]. The whole event [ HOW LONG? ].
6
In the end, it was [ positive/challenging/unexpected ] because [ reason ].
🗣️ After telling the story: Partner asks at least 3 Wh- follow-up questions. The storyteller answers without looking at the scaffold. That's A2.1 free narrative production.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Story Photo Wall: cada foto activa un Wh- word diferente. El alumno cuenta la historia fotoísmo a fotismo, produciendo espontáneamente el tipo de pregunta correcto para cada escena. Esto integra narración + interrogación en una sola actividad.
  • El scaffold de storytelling tiene 6 líneas con blanks. A2.1 B-session: el alumno puede ver el scaffold MIENTRAS habla (a diferencia de B1 donde no hay red). Lo que importa es la producción oral correcta, no la producción sin apoyo.
  • Follow-up questions: el partner hace 3 Wh- questions sin preparación sobre lo que acaba de escuchar. Esta es la producción espontánea más auténtica de la sesión — las preguntas salen de lo que realmente dijeron.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Fíjate en lo que acabas de hacer: contaste una historia con todos los verbos irregulares, los conectores, y después respondiste preguntas Wh- espontáneas sobre ella. En S3A no podías hacer eso. Ahora sí."

A2.1 Mastery Challenge
6 Wh- questions · Real event · No prep · No notes · 3 minutes
MASTERY TEST
🎯 The Final Challenge — S3 Mastery Standard
Think of a real business event from your professional life — a trip, client visit, negotiation, trade show, or meeting. Tell your partner (or teacher) the story. Your partner then conducts a full 3-minute debrief using all 6 Wh- question types. Switch roles.
STORYTELLER must use:
✅ Minimum 5 irregular verbs
✅ First / Then / After that / Finally
✅ At least 1 negative (didn't + base)
✅ A clear outcome statement
✅ Duration: "It took / lasted…"
INTERVIEWER must use:
✅ All 6 Wh- question types
✅ Correct structure: Wh- + did + S + base
✅ At least 2 follow-up questions
✅ "How long did it take?" mandatory
✅ "What happened?" as opener or closer
A2.1 S3 Mastery confirmed when: Interviewer produces all 6 Wh- question types correctly · Storyteller uses 5+ irregular verbs with correct past forms · The conversation flows for 3+ minutes without switching to Spanish or needing to look at notes. That's the A2.1 debrief at professional standard.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Esta es la evaluación de mastery de S3. Si el alumno hace los 6 Wh- correctamente y el conversación fluye 3 minutos → S3 completo. Si no → nota exactamente cuál Wh- falló para trabajar en la próxima sesión.
  • Corrección post-challenge: UN error de Wh- (estructura) + UN error de irregular verb. Solo dos correcciones al final, nunca durante. La fluidez de producción libre es el objetivo — la precisión se consolida en S4.
  • Si el alumno usa un evento real de su trabajo → oro puro. La emoción y el conocimiento real del tema elimina el procesamiento cognitivo de inventar contenido → toda la energía va a la gramática.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "¿Recuerdas cuando en S3A no sabías cómo preguntar 'How long did it take?' correctamente? Ahora la usas espontáneamente en una conversación real. Eso es exactamente el progreso de dos sesiones."
Capa 3 · Retención & Upsell
  • "S4A y S4B son el cierre de M2 — la historia completa de un evento de negocios, desde el planning hasta el outcome, con todas las preguntas y respuestas. Es el exit task del módulo."
  • Trigger testimonial MANDATORIO: "¿Cuándo vas a usar este debrief en inglés en tu trabajo? ¿La próxima reunión? ¿El próximo viaje?" — Ancla la sesión en aplicación real inmediata.
