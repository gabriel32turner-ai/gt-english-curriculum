Transform
Turn it into a question: "You should ask your manager."
"Should I ask my manager about this?"
Add Reason
"You shouldn't send that email." — Add WHY.
"You shouldn't send that email because it sounds too aggressive and it might damage the relationship."
Soften It
Make this more indirect: "You should talk to HR."
"Why don't you talk to HR directly? They can help with this."
Balanced
Give advice FOR and AGAINST taking a new job.
"I think you should apply — it's a great step. But I don't think you should resign before you have a signed contract."
Fix the Error
"Why you don't call him first?"
"Why don't you call him first?" — Inversion: Why don't + subject, never subject first.
We Form
Ask the team for a group decision about a delayed project.
"Should we tell the client about the delay now, or wait until we have a solution?"
Fix the Error
"I don't think you shouldn't resign."
"I don't think you should resign." — Only one negative. "Don't think" already carries the negative.
Real Situation
Your colleague is always late to meetings. One indirect suggestion.
"Why don't you set a phone alarm 10 minutes before each meeting? It might help."
Transform
Make this personal: "Accepting is risky." → advice sentence.
"I don't think you should accept immediately — the risk is real. You shouldn't decide without researching the company first."
Register
Which is more formal/collaborative: "You should ask." or "Why don't you ask?"
"Why don't you ask" is more collaborative and indirect — better in meetings and when the relationship matters. "You should" is more direct.

§3 — Pitch & Debate
Sides Assigned — No Choice
12–14 MIN
⚖️
Pitch Debate
Andrea's startup dilemma — assigned sides, no choice
12–14 MIN
📋 Scenario: Andrea Soto has a final decision to make. The startup Operations Director role — or stay in her stable, boring job. Teacher assigns sides. You argue your position with should/shouldn't/why don't. No choosing your own side.
✅ SIDE A — TAKE THE JOB
She should accept the startup role
Argue: "I think she should accept because the title and salary are strong signals of growth."
Argue: "She shouldn't stay just because it's comfortable — that's not a career strategy."
Suggest: "Why don't we consider the 90-day trial? It reduces the risk significantly."
Counter: "Should she wait another year in a role that doesn't challenge her?"
⚠️ SIDE B — STAY AND WAIT
She shouldn't take the startup risk
Argue: "I don't think she should resign from a stable job for an 18-month company."
Argue: "She shouldn't rush this decision because startups fail at a high rate."
Suggest: "Why don't we ask: what happens to her career if the startup closes in 6 months?"
Counter: "Should she really bet her career stability on a company with no track record?"

§4 — Relay
Transform It — No Support
8–10 MIN
🔄
Relay · Conversion
Transform the sentence — say it first, then reveal
8–10 MIN
Rule: Read the input. Transform it into the target structure — say it out loud. Then click REVEAL to compare. No support material. This is pure conversion under pressure.
Relay 1 — Direct → Indirect (Why don't you)
"You should speak to your manager about the workload."
Target: make it a gentle indirect suggestion
"Why don't you speak to your manager about the workload? It might be easier than you think." — Same message, softer register, more collaborative.
Relay 2 — Statement → Question + Reason
"Accepting immediately without negotiating is a mistake."
Target: "Shouldn't + reason" AND "Should we…?" in two sentences
"You shouldn't accept immediately without negotiating, because companies almost always have more flexibility than they show. Should we draft a counter-offer first?" — Both structures in one natural sequence.
Relay 3 — One side → Both sides
"She should change jobs."
Target: "I think you should" + "I don't think you should" — both in one balanced response
"I think you should explore the opportunity — it's genuinely exciting. But I don't think you should resign until you have a signed contract in your hand, because startups can change plans overnight." — This is A2.2 mastery: balanced, nuanced, professional.
