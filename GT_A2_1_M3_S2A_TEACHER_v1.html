📍 Your next presentation — do you know how to open it in English?
⚡ Quick Win — 20 sec
Say this out loud right now: "I'd like to present our Q3 results. As you can see in this slide…" That's your first presentation line in English!

Nota: Si dicen "we exceed the target" → nota el error. Simple Past en §5 lo cubre.

The Q3 Results — What Happened?

Valeria needs to present last quarter's results to her team. Positive, negative, and why. Can you do the same for your area?

Round 1 · 60 seg
T→S "Present 3 results from your last quarter. Did you achieve / miss / improve anything?"

S→S "Partner A presents results. Partner B asks: 'Why was that?' / 'Didn't you hit the target?'"

Model Answer

"Good morning, everyone. I'd like to present our Q3 results. As you can see in this slide, the results were mostly positive. We exceeded our delivery target by 8%. We also improved our supplier response time. However, we didn't reach our cost-reduction goal — it was 12% below target. The main reason was the raw material delay in August. Any questions so far?"

💬 Colloquial — Presentation Phrases

5 min
The phrases that separate good presenters from great ones
The floor is yours.
When: inviting someone to start speaking or presenting
"Valeria, the presentation is ready. The floor is yours."
Let's move on to the next point.
When: transitioning between presentation sections
"OK, that covers the Q3 numbers. Let's move on to the next point — Q4 forecast."
Any questions so far?
When: checking understanding mid-presentation
"That's the first section. Any questions so far? No? Great, let's continue."
I'll follow up on that.
When: you don't have the answer now but will find it
"I don't have those numbers right now, but I'll follow up on that after the meeting."
Drill: Teacher → Student. "Tu colega te da la presentación." → "The floor is yours!" / "Ya terminaste la primera sección. ¿Qué dices?" → "Any questions so far?" / "No sabes el dato que te preguntan. ¿Qué dices?" → "I'll follow up on that."

🎯 Grammar — Simple Past & Negative Questions

10 min
Past results need Simple Past — and negative questions make you sound professional
REFERENCIAL. S2 expands on S1 (was/were) — now full Simple Past review in presentation/results context + the negative question forms that are natural in meetings.
Error #1: "We exceed the target" → exceeded. Simple Past, not base form.
Error #2: "Didn't you reached it?" → Didn't you reach it? — base verb after didn't.
FormStructurePresentation / Results Example
Simple Past ✅Subject + verb-ed / irregularWe achieved the target. / The results were positive.
Negative ❌Subject + didn't + base verbWe didn't reach the cost goal. / The delivery wasn't on time.
Question ❓Did + subject + base verb?Did you exceed the sales target? / Was the outcome good?
Neg. question ⚠️Didn't + subject + base verb?Didn't you send the report? / Wasn't it on Monday?
Short answerYes, we did. / No, we didn't."Did you reach the target?" — "Yes, we did — by 8%."
⚠️ TOP ERRORS — Correct immediately
✗ "We exceed the target" → ✅ We exceeded — Simple Past always!
✗ "Didn't you reached it?" → ✅ Didn't you reach it? — base verb after didn't

🎧 Listening — Valeria's Q3 Presentation

8 min
📍 Solaris Manufacturing, Celaya — Valeria presents Q3 results to the operations team
🎙️
ElevenLabs — "Valeria's Q3 Results Presentation"
Voice: Sarah · Speed: 0.82 · A2.1 M3 S2A
Good morning, everyone. I'd like to present our Q3 operations results. I'll cover three areas: production, delivery, and costs. As you can see in the first slide, it was a mixed quarter.

First, production. We exceeded our output target by 8% — that was excellent. The team improved efficiency significantly after the July training. We gained two new production lines from the Chicago project.

Next, delivery. This section wasn't as positive. We didn't reach our on-time delivery target. Only 82% of orders were delivered on time — our target was 90%. The main reason was the supplier delay in August. Didn't I mention this in the last meeting? Yes — and unfortunately the problem continued.

Finally, costs. We reduced operational costs by 4% overall. We lost some savings because of the raw material price increase, but we gained efficiency in other areas.

To summarize: production was very good, delivery wasn't great, and costs improved. Any questions so far? Daniel — the floor is yours.
