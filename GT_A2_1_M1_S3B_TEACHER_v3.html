Warm-Up — Irregular Speed Round
5 min

Sin modelos. El maestro dice el BASE. El alumno dice el PAST INSTANTÁNEAMENTE + una oración real de su trabajo. Haz clic en la tarjeta cuando hayas respondido. ⚡

Critical check: ¿Dijo "didn't go" o "didn't went"? Si dice "didn't went" → corrección inmediata. La regla: didn't + BASE siempre. Que el alumno la diga en voz alta antes de continuar.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • V8 "get/got" es el que más se olvida. Si titubea: "In the audio, what did Carlos say when Ana asked about the clients?" → "We GOT excellent feedback."
  • Exige que las oraciones usen datos REALES del trabajo del alumno. "Last week I went to…" debe completarse con un lugar real.
  • Threshold para role play: 8/8 correctos + al menos 2 oraciones con Wh- question espontánea.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "8 verbos, 8 oraciones de tu trabajo real. Eso es lo que le dirías a tu director en inglés mañana por la mañana."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In S4 you'll add: 'I didn't go because…' + 'I worked on it for 3 hours.' These 8 verbs are the foundation of everything."

Recap — Battle R2 + Wh- Generation
7–8 min
⚡ Verb Battle — Round 2: Speed Mode (3 segundos máx)
Solo el PAST form — sin pensar. Di el pasado ANTES de que el maestro haga clic en "Flip". ¡Campeón si logras 8/8!
Battle Score R2
0/ 8
¡3 segundos por tarjeta!
Wh- Generation Drill — Say the correct Wh- question

El maestro hace una afirmación. El alumno produce la Wh- question correspondiente INSTANTÁNEAMENTE usando el verbo correcto.

"Carlos went somewhere last week."
→ "Where did he go?"
"She met someone important at the conference."
→ "Who did she meet?"
"They made some kind of agreement."
→ "What did they make?"
"I wrote the report at some point."
→ "When did you write it?"
"He went there for some reason."
→ "Why did he go?"
"The presentation went well/badly."
→ "How did it go?"
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Wh- Generation: las respuestas del maestro están ocultas en Student Mode — el alumno produce la pregunta SIN ver el modelo. Si se equivoca en el Wh- word: pausa, corrige, repite.
  • El error más probable: "What did she go?" (qué → dónde). Refuerza: Where = lugar, Who = persona, What = cosa/acción, When = tiempo, How = manera, Why = razón.
  • Threshold para role play: 8/8 battle + 5/6 Wh- questions correctas. Si no → drill adicional de 3 min.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Battle R2 es la versión de velocidad. Si puedes hacer 8/8 en 3 segundos, los verbos ya están en tu memoria automática."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "La generación de Wh- questions es el nivel más alto — no solo responder preguntas, sino CREARLAS. Eso es lo que hace un hablante fluido."
§ 03
Listening — The Full Debrief
10 min
🎧
Team Debrief — Carlos, Ana + Director
ElevenLabs · 3 speakers · Sarah + Adam + Mike · Rate: 0.82 · ~95 sec · A2.1

📎 Pega tu URL de Google Drive aquí. Convierte: /view?usp=sharing → /preview

▶ Show ElevenLabs Script

Ana (Sarah) + Carlos (Adam) + Director Roberto (Mike) · Rate 0.82 · ~95 sec


Ana: Good morning everyone. Carlos, the director is here — he wants to hear about Dallas directly. Roberto, Carlos just got back.


Roberto: Welcome back, Carlos. Where did you go first when you arrived in Dallas?


Carlos: I went straight to the hotel and met Jim Carter — our regional contact. We had lunch and talked about the Q4 plan.


Roberto: Good. Who did you meet from the client side?


Carlos: On Tuesday I met the Director of Operations. We gave our main presentation — 40 people attended. We got excellent feedback from three companies.


Roberto: Three companies interested! What agreement did you make with them?


Carlos: We made an agreement to schedule a follow-up call next month. They want a formal proposal.


Roberto: Perfect. How did you feel the presentation went overall?


Carlos: It went really well. I took notes on every conversation and wrote the full report on Wednesday evening.


Roberto: Excellent work. Ana, when do we have the follow-up call scheduled?


Ana: I didn't have that information yet — Carlos, can you send the details today?


Carlos: Of course. I'll send it before noon.

ElevenLabs: Ana = Sarah (femenina), Carlos = Adam, Roberto = Mike (o voz masculina diferente a Adam). Rate 0.82, stability 0.65. Graba por separado y combina. Sube a Google Drive con iframe /preview.
"Count every irregular past verb you hear in this audio. How many total?" (Target: 11+ — went, met, had, gave, attended, got, made, took, wrote, went, didn't have)
First listen: count only. Don't answer questions yet.
"How many Wh- questions did the Director ask? Which Wh- word did he use for each?" (Answer: 5 — Where, Who, What, How + Ana's: When)
Second listen: focus only on question words.
"Now YOU are Carlos. The Director asks the same 5 Wh- questions. Answer from memory — no notes. Use the 8 irregular verbs."
Minimum 4 irregular verbs in your answers.
"What did Ana say she didn't have? Was the negative form correct?" (Answer: "I didn't have that information" — YES, correctly BASE after didn't)
Grammar check: confirm the negative rule in context.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Task 3 es el diagnóstico principal: ¿puede el alumno responder como Carlos con 4+ verbos irregulares sin notas? Si sí → listo para role play completo. Si no → 3 min de repaso con las tarjetas de §2.
  • Task 4 refuerza el negativo en contexto real: Ana dice "I didn't have" — el alumno ya escuchó la forma correcta antes de producirla.
  • El Director hace 4 Wh- questions + Ana hace 1 (When). Escucha separada por tipo de pregunta.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "El Director pregunta 5 Wh- questions en 90 segundos. Ese es el ritmo de inglés profesional real. ¿Pudiste seguir las 5?"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "En S4 agregas 'I didn't go because…' y expresiones de duración. Tu debrief pasará de bueno a excelente."

Game — Wh- Situation Challenge
10–12 min
🎮 Wordwall — External Game
Irregular Past Match — 8 Base → Past + Context

Sugerencia: "Match-Up" — Base verb ↔ Past form + business context sentence
Pares: go ↔ went / have ↔ had / make ↔ made / give ↔ gave / meet ↔ met / take ↔ took / write ↔ wrote / get ↔ got
Tiempo: 60 segundos · Enfatiza: "Remember: didn't + BASE, not the irregular form"
Instrucción: "Match the base form with its irregular past. 60 seconds. Go!"
🎯 GT Oral Game — Situation → Wh- Question → Full Answer
El maestro da una situación. El alumno forma la Wh- question correcta Y responde.
Round 1 — Wh- word selection
Situation
"Carlos went somewhere for the conference." — ¿Cuál Wh- word corresponde a "somewhere"?
Round 2 — Full question
Situation
"She met someone important at the presentation." — Form the complete Wh- question.
Round 3 — Negative + Wh- combined
Situation
"They didn't ___ a decision at the meeting." — ¿Cuál es el verbo correcto en esta forma negativa?
Round 4 — Tu semana real · Oral Production
El alumno produce un mini-debrief oral de 60 segundos sobre algo real que hizo la semana pasada — una reunión, un viaje, una presentación. El maestro escucha y hace 3 Wh- questions al terminar. El alumno responde instantáneamente.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Round 2: "Who did she met?" usa la forma pasada después de "did" — error muy común. La respuesta correcta "Who did she meet?" refuerza la regla BASE.
  • Round 4 es el diagnóstico definitivo antes del role play. ¿El alumno usa went/had/made/gave espontáneamente en contexto real?
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Round 4: 60 segundos, tu semana real, sin apuntes. Eso es exactamente lo que le dirías a tu director en inglés."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Wh- questions + irregular past = el 70% de cualquier debrief de negocios en inglés. Domina esto y dominas las conversaciones más importantes."
§ 05
Role Play — Trip Debrief Scenarios
12–15 min
Key Phrases — Click to Practise
Trip Opening Summary
"I went to Dallas, met Jim Carter, and gave a presentation."
Uso: abrir un debrief en segundos
Outcome Report
"We made an agreement — three companies want a proposal."
Uso: reportar resultados concretos
Negative + Reason
"I didn't have time — I had an early flight Thursday."
Uso: explicar lo que no hiciste
How did it go?
"How did it go? — It went really well. We got excellent feedback."
Uso: la pregunta más útil post-evento
🎭 Role Play 1 — Manager Debrief (Carlos + Ana)
Tú eres Carlos. Acabas de regresar del Dallas Sales Conference. Ana (el maestro) quiere escuchar el resumen completo — sin leer el trip report. Usa los 8 verbos irregulares en contexto real.
Carlos (Student)
Reporta espontáneamente: dónde fuiste, a quién conociste, qué hiciste cada día, qué acuerdo hiciste, qué no pudiste hacer y por qué. Mínimo 5 verbos irregulares en pasado. Sin notas.
Ana (Teacher) — Wh- Questions
Deja hablar al alumno 30 segundos, luego interrumpe con 5 Wh- questions: Where / Who / What / How / When. Fuerza respuestas completas, no solo "yes/no".
[Ana]: "Welcome back Carlos! How did the conference go?"
Objetivo: el alumno usa went/met/had/gave/got/made espontáneamente + responde 5 Wh- questions con formas irregulares correctas (BASE después de did/didn't). Si comete el error "didn't went" → corrección inmediata, el alumno repite correctamente antes de continuar.
🎭 Role Play 2 — Director Review (Carlos + Roberto)
El Director Roberto (el maestro) quiere más detalle sobre los 3 clientes potenciales. Carlos (tú) debe responder con exactitud usando el vocabulario del audio de §3.
Carlos (Student)
Usa estos hechos: gave presentation to 40 people · got feedback from 3 companies · made agreement for follow-up call · wrote report on Wednesday · took notes on all conversations. Inventar un detalle adicional que no estaba en el audio.
Roberto (Teacher)
"What did the companies say exactly?" / "Who did you give the presentation with?" / "How did the Director react when you made the agreement?" / "When did you take notes — during or after?"
[Roberto]: "Carlos, I read your report. I want to understand the client feedback better. What exactly did they say after your presentation?"
Objetivo: el alumno responde Wh- questions con formas irregulares sin hesitación. Evalúa especialmente: "gave" (no gived), "took" (no taked), "wrote" (no writed).
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • RP1: si el alumno se queda sin verbos para reportar → pídele que use el email de §5 de S3A como guion mental (lo leyó en la sesión anterior). No usar el documento físicamente.
  • RP2: el "detalle adicional inventado" activa producción verdaderamente libre con irregulares. Si inventa algo incorrecto gramaticalmente → corrección después de que termine, no durante.
  • Error más probable en ambos RPs: "When did you went?" / "I met with him and we maked…" → registra, corrige, repite.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Acabas de dar un debrief completo en inglés sobre un viaje de negocios real. Carlos lo hizo en el audio — tú lo hiciste con tu trabajo real. Eso es más difícil y más valioso."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Upsell: "En S4 agregas 'I didn't go because…' + 'for 3 hours' — time expressions. Tu debrief pasará de bueno a ejecutivo."
§ 06
Debate — Business Trips vs Video Calls
8–10 min
🗣 Free Speaking — Debate with Irregular Past
"Carlos went to Dallas, gave a presentation, and made a deal in person. Was the trip necessary — or could he have done the same with a video call?"
✈️ Business trips are necessary
"I went to a meeting last year and made a deal that we couldn't make by email."
"When I met the client in person, we got better results."
"The feedback we got was only possible because we were there."
"You can't make real agreements through a screen."
💻 Video calls are enough
"Last month I had 5 client calls online and made 3 agreements — no travel."
"We gave our best presentation ever via Zoom last quarter."
"The time I spent traveling, I could have met 10 more clients."
"We got the deal without going anywhere."
Last time I went to… We made a deal when… I met the client and… We got better results because… I didn't have to go because…
🎤 Optional Extension — Trip Story · 90 seconds
"Tell me about a real business trip or important meeting you had. Where did you go? Who did you meet? What did you make or get? How did it go?"
Mínimo 6 oraciones. Al menos 6 verbos irregulares diferentes. Al menos 2 respuestas a Wh- questions espontáneas del maestro. Mínimo 1 negativo.
Teacher Follow-Up — After Debate
"When did you last go on a business trip? What happened exactly — who did you meet?"
"What was the best agreement you ever made in a meeting? How did you make it?"
"Have you ever gave a presentation that didn't go well? What happened?" (Catch error: "gave" vs "given" — focus on past)
"What did your team get from the last important client call — positive or negative feedback?"
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • El debate activa verbos irregulares en discurso argumentativo — el nivel más alto de producción. Regla: el alumno DEBE usar al menos 4 verbos irregulares durante el debate. Si no aparecen naturalmente: "Ahora di eso mismo con un ejemplo en pasado."
  • Las frases en las "debate-phrase" cards son modelos — el alumno debe adaptarlas a su experiencia real, no repetirlas textuales.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Acabas de debatir viajes de negocios vs video calls en inglés usando went, met, made, got. Eso es inglés ejecutivo A2."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Testimonial: "Si tu director te preguntara en inglés 'Carlos, how did the Dallas trip go?' — ¿podrías responderle en inglés con went/met/made/got? Sí. Eso es lo que aprendiste hoy."
