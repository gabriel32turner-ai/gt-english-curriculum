Hot Take — Guide Me Without Looking
Directions under pressure · Full M4 integration · 60 seconds · No notes
⏱ 5 MIN
Scenario — Solaris Manufacturing, Celaya
A new supplier from Monterrey calls your mobile while driving to your plant. They say: "I'm at the main gate. I have an appointment with Engineering. Where do I go?" You have 60 seconds on the phone — no screens, no maps.
Give complete directions from the main gate to the Engineering department. Include: how to enter, where to go, which floor, and what they'll see when they arrive. No notes!
🔥Badge earned: complete guidance (start → destination) in under 60 seconds

Pitch — GPS vs Human Directions
Argue your side using direction language · Grammar integrated into real opinion
⏱ 10 MIN
Team A — GPS is better
GPS never gets it wrong!
  • "There is always a signal — GPS says 'turn left' and you turn left. Simple."
  • "With GPS, there aren't any mistakes — it shows you exactly where to go."
  • "Are there any roads? GPS knows them all. It says 'go straight for 2 km then turn right'…"
  • "Human directions are confusing — 'go to the corner where there used to be a pharmacy'…"
Team B — Human directions are better
A real person always knows more!
  • "There is a shortcut behind the market — GPS doesn't know it. A person does!"
  • "Are there any roadworks? A human says 'don't go that way, turn left instead'…"
  • "GPS says 'go straight' but there isn't a road — it's a wall! A person says…"
  • "In Mexican cities like Celaya, there are alleys and markets GPS can't find but people know…"
⚡ Badge ⚡ — Pitch Note

Badge ⚡ when a student uses "there is/are" + a direction word + a preposition in a single argument turn. Write it on the board and read it back — "That was a perfect M4 sentence." Class vote at end. Note: this debate is culturally rich for Mexican learners — GPS reliability in industrial zones of Celaya/Silao is a real topic.

Solo Challenge — The Complete M4 Brief
2 minutes · there is/are + prepositions + directions · Full Module 4 integration · No notes
⏱ 8 MIN
Solo Challenge — 2 minutes
You are the host. Give a complete welcome brief to an international visitor who just arrived at your plant entrance.
  • Use there is/are at least 4 times to describe spaces in the building
  • Use at least 3 prepositions of place (next to / in front of / between / behind / on / in / under)
  • Give directions to at least 2 destinations using go / turn / take
  • Include "Are there any…?" or "There aren't any…" at least once
  • End with: "If you need anything, just ask me — I'm in the office next to / in front of / at…"
  • No notes. Partner tallies: there is/are uses, prepositions, direction verbs. Class competes for highest tally.

Source Analysis — Directions in the Wild
4 real-world sources · Find directions + there is/are + any · Metacognitive Q each
⏱ 8 MIN
📱 Google Maps Voice Direction
"Go straight on Avenida Tecnológico for 500 metres. Turn right at the traffic lights. There is a Solaris Manufacturing sign on the left. Turn left into the main entrance. There are security guards at the gate."
This is a GPS giving directions. Rewrite it as a human giving the same directions — add "you can't miss it" and "there is a big sign."
📱 WhatsApp — Colleague to Visitor
"Hi! When you arrive, go straight to reception. There aren't any visitor badges there — pick yours up at the security desk. It's opposite the main elevator, next to the restrooms. Are there any questions?"
Find: 1 negative with "any", 1 question with "any", and 2 prepositions. Write them below with their purpose.
🏢 Building Notice — New Layout
"IMPORTANT: As of Monday, the HR office is on Floor 3, not Floor 2. There is a new meeting room between HR and IT. There aren't any changes to parking. To get to HR: take the elevator, turn left, go straight to the end of the corridor."
A colleague asks: "Where is HR now?" Write their answer using the notice — include the floor, directions, and something "there is" in the building.
🪧 Reception Welcome Card
"Welcome to Solaris Manufacturing, Celaya. There is free Wi-Fi — ask at reception. There are restrooms on every floor. There isn't a café in the building but there is a vending machine next to the elevator on each floor. For directions, ask any employee."
The visitor asks: "Is there a café?" Write the complete answer using "there isn't… but there is…" — then add directions to the nearest vending machine.
📌 Source Analysis Note

Sources 1 and 3 are production tasks — students rewrite or answer. Sources 2 and 4 are comprehension + production — spot the structure first, then produce. Source 4 (the welcome card) is the module capstone: "there isn't… but there is…" + directions. Slow down here. Elicit 1 complete answer per source from different students. Celebrate those who combine all three M4 elements (there is/are + preposition + direction) in one answer.

3 Phrases — Use These at Work This Week
M4 complete — these are your go-to phrases from now on
Phrase 1
"Go straight, turn left — there is a sign on the door. You can't miss it."
The complete M4 direction sentence. Everything in one: go, turn, there is, you can't miss it. Use it the next time any visitor — international or local — asks where to go.
Phrase 2
"There isn't a [X] here, but there is a [Y] — it's just down the hall."
The "negative + positive + direction" combo. Use when someone asks for something you don't have — redirect them in English with confidence. Works for café, parking, restrooms, any space.
Phrase 3
"Are there any questions? — If not, I'll take you there."
Close every visitor welcome with this. "Any" in a question + the warmest possible offer — to walk them. It's grammatically complete, culturally warm, and shows your English is real.
