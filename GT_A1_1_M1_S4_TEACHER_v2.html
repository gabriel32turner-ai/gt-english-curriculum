§ 01
Warm-Up — Rapid Fire
5–8 min

Rules: Teacher reads each question out loud. Student answers immediately — no thinking time! Use everything from S1, S2, and S3. Click a card when you've answered it. ⚡

Q1
What is your name?
Full sentence: "My name is ___."
✓
Q2
What is your job?
Use A or AN: "I am a/an ___."
✓
Q3
Where are you from?
"I am from ___, Mexico."
✓
Q4
What is your nationality?
"I am ___." — nationality word
✓
Q5
Who is your manager?
"My manager is ___. His/Her job is ___."
✓
Q6
Is your company big or small?
"My company is ___. It is in ___."
✓
⏱ 30 sec per question
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Rapid Fire reviews all of S1–S3 in under 8 minutes. Speed matters — don't allow time to translate internally.
  • Acceptable answers: any grammatically close response. Full sentences preferred but not required here.
  • Common hesitations: Q2 (a/an still uncertain) and Q5 (his/her). Note these — address in §4 review if needed.
  • Use the timer button to add energy. If student is slow, do 2 rounds — speed improves dramatically.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Round 2 — faster! This time you don't even think. Your brain already knows the answers."
  • Celebrate fluency: "That took you 4 seconds. In S1 it took 30. That's what progress looks like."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Today you'll add Wh- questions so you can ask these same things — not just answer them."
  • Upsell: "In S5 you'll do this same rapid fire with the whole module. Perfect retention checkpoint."

§ 04
Grammar — Wh- Questions
12–15 min
Structure — Wh- word + to be + subject
Wh- WordQuestionNatural Answer
WhatWhat is your name?My name is Ana. / I'm Ana.
WhatWhat is your email?My email is ana@gruponorte.mx
WhatWhat is your phone number?My number is 461 147 2119.
WhereWhere is your office?My office is on the 3rd floor.
WhereWhere are you from?I'm from Celaya.
WhoWho is your manager?My manager is Carlos.
HowHow are you?I'm fine, thanks! / Very well, thank you.
Short Answers — Yes / No
QuestionYESNO
Are you from Mexico?Yes, I am.No, I'm not.
Is she a manager?Yes, she is.No, she isn't.
Are they in the meeting?Yes, they are.No, they aren't.
⚠️ Important: Never use contractions in positive short answers.
✅ "Yes, I am."    ❌ "Yes, I'm." — always incorrect in short answers.
Activity 1 — Choose the correct Wh- word
Click the correct option.

___ is your office? — It's on the 2nd floor.
___ is your manager? — His name is Roberto.
___ are you? — I'm fine, thank you!
Is the meeting at 9 AM? — Yes, it ___.
Activity 2 — Choose the correct question
Read the answer. Click the question that matches.
Answer: "I'm a coordinator at Grupo Norte."
Answer: "My office is in Monterrey."
Answer: "No, she isn't. She's a coordinator."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Common error: "Yes, I'm." in short answers — reinforce that positive short answers never use contractions.
  • Activity 2 is reverse-format (answer → question) — this is harder and builds active production. Expect slower pace.
  • After both activities: oral drill. Teacher gives an answer, student must form the question instantly. 3 rounds.
  • Connect to Rapid Fire from §1: "Now you can ask all those questions too — not just answer them."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You just learned the 4 most important questions in professional English: What, Where, Who, How."
  • When student gets Activity 2 correct: "You're thinking in English now — not translating."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In A1.2 you'll add 'When' and 'How many' — your question toolkit will be complete."
  • Upsell: "Imagine opening a meeting in English: 'Good morning! Where are you from? What is your role here?' — that's you in Module 2."
§ 05
Reading
8–10 min
Read · ~90 words · A1.1 level

Hello! My name is David Chen. I am from Vancouver, Canada. I am visiting the Grupo Norte office in Celaya this week.

My contact at Grupo Norte is Ana García. Her office is on the third floor. Her email is ana@gruponorte.mx. Her phone number is 461 147 2119.

Ana is a great colleague. She introduces me to her team. "This is David. He is an engineer from Canada. Nice to meet you, David!" — "Nice to meet you too! Pleased to be here!"

The meeting is tomorrow at 9 AM. I am ready!

Comprehension
Answer the questions.
1. David is from Mexico.
2. Ana's office is on the 3rd floor.
3. The meeting is in the afternoon.
What is David's job?
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This text integrates all S4 vocabulary naturally — ask student to find and underline: email, phone, office, floor, contact, colleague, introduce, meeting, pleased.
  • Q3 is a detail trap: "meeting is tomorrow at 9 AM" = morning, not afternoon. Students who skim miss this.
  • Read aloud the dialogue section: model David's and Ana's voices. Then student repeats both roles.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "David is a Canadian engineer visiting Celaya — that's a real situation in your industry. This is your world."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In your next international visit or video call, you'll use every sentence in this text."

GT Originals
🤝 "Pleased to meet you" sounds more formal than "Nice to meet you" — use it with senior executives
🔊 "colleague" /ˈkɒliːɡ/ — the GUE is silent. Common mispronunciation: "co-LEA-gue"
📱 In professional English: say "email address" not just "email" when asking for contact
🇨🇦 Canadian English sounds similar to American — your ear is already calibrated
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Presentation: give student 2 min to prepare mentally (no writing). Then deliver. Then repeat without any preparation — the second delivery is always significantly better.
  • Evaluate: Did they use A/AN correctly? Did they use his/her? Did they ask at least one Wh- question? Note for S5 review.
  • If student gets stuck in step 4 or 5: acceptable to drop one step. Core = steps 1–3.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You just gave a professional presentation in English. Four sessions ago you couldn't say hello. Look at where you are now."
  • Celebrate with specifics: "Your a/an was perfect. Your greeting was natural. Next time — more eye contact, less hesitation."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "S5 is your consolidation session — everything from S1 to S4 comes together. Come prepared."
  • Testimonial trigger: "How would you describe your English level today versus 4 sessions ago?"
  • Upsell: "In A1.2 you start talking about your daily routine, your schedule, your company — with the same structure you built here."
