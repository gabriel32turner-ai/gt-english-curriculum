§ 01
Warm-Up — Picture Prompt
5–8 min
📸 Mira la foto — describe lo que ves en inglés. Sin gramática perfecta todavía.
1
What are the people doing? What is their job?
Try: "She is a manager." / "They are engineers." / "I think he is a designer."
2
What is your job? What does your team do every day?
Try: "I am a ___. My team ___."
3
Who is your manager? What is his / her name?
Try: "My manager is ___. His/Her job is ___."
4
What jobs do people in your family have?
Example: "My father is a doctor. My mother is a teacher."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Vocabulario esperado: cualquier título de trabajo + "to be". Acepta todos los intentos — solo activación, sin corrección todavía.
  • Q3 apunta a posesivos (his/her) — nota cuál forma usa. Si está mal, no corrijas ahora: "Great — we'll perfect that in §4."
  • Si el alumno no sabe nombrar un trabajo: apunta a las vocab cards de §2. El preview está bien aquí.
  • Si usa "a engineer" — sonríe y anota: "Almost! We'll nail that in grammar today."
  • Tiempo: máx 7 min. Mantén el ritmo conversacional y ligero.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You already know how to describe jobs — today we make the grammar perfect."
  • "Tu título de trabajo en inglés es lo primero que un contacto internacional lee en tu firma de correo. Hoy lo dejamos exacto."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Your job title in English is the first thing an international contact reads in your email signature. Today we get it exactly right."

§ 04
Grammar — A/An + Possessive Adjectives
12–15 min
Rule 1 — A / AN Before Job Titles
Use A before a consonant sound: a manager · a doctor · a teacher · a lawyer · a designer · a coordinator.
Use AN before a vowel sound: an engineer · an accountant · an assistant.
⚠ Common Mistakes — A/AN
❌ I am a engineer. → ✅ I am an engineer. "engineer" empieza con vocal (E) → usa AN.
❌ She is an manager. → ✅ She is a manager. "manager" empieza con consonante (M) → usa A.
❌ I'm coordinator. (sin artículo) → ✅ I'm a coordinator. En inglés, siempre necesitas A o AN antes de un trabajo singular. Este es el error #1 en México.
Rule 2 — Possessive Adjectives
My
I → my
"My name is Ana."
"My job is coordinator."
Your
You → your
"What is your job?"
"Your company is big."
His
He → his
"His name is Carlos."
"His company is Toyota."
Her
She → her
"Her name is Ana."
"Her job is director."
⚠ Common Mistake — His / Her Confusion
❌ Ana is a director. His job is in Celaya. → ✅ Her job is in Celaya. Sigue a la PERSONA, no al objeto. Ana = she → her. Carlos = he → his. Siempre.
Activity — Choose the Correct Word
Haz clic en la opción correcta para completar cada oración.
She is ___ engineer in our company.
What is ___ job? Are you a manager? (asking the student)
Carlos is ___ assistant. (Carlos = he)
___ name is Ana. She is a coordinator.
I work at Grupo Norte. ___ company is in Celaya.
Robert is the manager. ___ department is Sales. (Robert = he)
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Error más común: confusión his/her. Los alumnos aplican lógica del español (género del objeto, no de la persona). Refuerza: "his = he, her = she. Always follow the person."
  • Después de la actividad: el alumno hace 2 oraciones sobre colegas reales usando his/her. La personalización sella la regla.
  • A/AN: si el alumno aún falla, regresa a §3 y voltea 2 tarjetas más juntos.
  • Cuando logre los 6 correctos: "You just mastered two grammar points in one session. That's A2 territory."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Every professional LinkedIn profile uses these exact words. You're writing real business English right now."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "A/AN and possessives stay forever — every tense, every level. One rule, unlimited application."
  • Upsell: "In A1.2 you'll use these to talk about your daily routine: 'My morning starts at 7. Her team meeting is at 9.'"
§ 05
Reading — My Team at Grupo Norte
8–10 min
Ana García
Coordinator · Grupo Norte · Celaya, GTO
Read · ~90 words · A1.1 level

My name is Ana García. I am a coordinator at Grupo Norte. My company is in Celaya. It is a manufacturing company.

My manager is Carlos Ramírez. He is a director. His office is on the third floor. He is not from Celaya — his city is Monterrey.

My colleague is Mariana. She is an accountant. Her department is Finance. Tom is also in our team. He is an engineer from Canada. His Spanish is very good!

We are a great team at Grupo Norte. What is your job?

Comprehension
Lee el texto. Responde las preguntas.
1. Ana is a director.
2. Carlos's office is on the third floor.
3. Mariana is an engineer.
What is Ana's job?
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Pide al alumno que encuentre TODOS los posesivos en el texto: "his office", "his city", "her department", "his Spanish". Cuéntenlos juntos (4).
  • Pide que encuentre todos los usos de A/AN: "a coordinator", "a director", "an accountant", "an engineer". Cuéntenlos (4).
  • Lectura en voz alta: el alumno lee un párrafo a la vez. Corrige solo mispronunciaciones de A/AN.
  • Q1 es trampa: Ana es coordinator, no director (Carlos es el director). Muchos alumnos fallan aquí.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "¿El equipo de Ana se parece al tuyo? Dime una diferencia y una similitud."
  • "Tom es canadiense pero su español es muy bueno — igual que tú hablarás inglés muy bien pronto."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Imagina enviar un email así — profesional, claro, y todo en inglés. Eso es tu S3B speaking challenge."
