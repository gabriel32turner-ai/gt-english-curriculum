Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This is the free production diagnostic. Do NOT correct during the warm-up. Listen and note: does the student spontaneously use "used to"? Do they mix used to and simple past correctly? These data points shape your coaching in §4 and §5.
  • The picture prompt produces more personal, emotionally connected language than the recruiter scenario in S1A. Students often speak more — allow it up to 2 minutes.
  • If the student only uses simple past: in §4 coaching, specifically ask them to add "used to" sentences about the period BEFORE each event.
  • If the student uses "used to" but makes the -d error (I use to…): note it for targeted feedback in §4 prep, not here.

Capa 3 · Growth & Retention

▶ Show ElevenLabs Script
HOST — SARAH

Welcome to Career Corner. My guest today is Adam Reyes, now a Supply Chain Manager at an industrial company. Adam, tell us about your path — because I know it was not a straight line.

GUEST — ADAM

No, not at all! I used to study systems engineering. I graduated in 2009, and then I applied for a job in accounting. Very different, I know!

HOST — SARAH

That is a big change. What made you move to supply chain?

GUEST — ADAM

My first mentor used to tell me: follow the work, not the degree. So in 2012 I transferred to a supply chain role. I used to work in a small team — only four people. I learned everything there.

HOST — SARAH

Did you use to travel for work back then?

GUEST — ADAM

No, I didn't use to travel at all. Now I visit suppliers in three different countries every quarter. The company promoted me to manager in 2018, and everything changed after that.

HOST — SARAH

Great story. What advice do you give to young professionals?

GUEST — ADAM

I used to think a career was a straight line from university to retirement. Now I know it is not. Be flexible, move when the opportunity comes, and always build good relationships. Those are the real milestones.

HOST — SARAH

Excellent. Thank you, Adam.

⏱ Target: 105–115 sec · Voices: Sarah (host) + Adam (guest, slightly lower pitch) · Speed: 0.82 · Mark used to in orange + simple past in purple for script delivery awareness
Listening Activities
Listen, then answer. First listen: global. Second listen: comprehension questions.
What did Adam study at university?
When did the company promote Adam to manager?
What advice does Adam give to young professionals?
Adam used to study accounting at university.
Adam didn't use to travel for work when he started in supply chain.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Two speakers = authentic conversation complexity. Listen 1: global comprehension ("What is Adam's current job? What industry?"). Listen 2: focused questions. Listen 3 (optional): count every "used to" form (used to study, used to tell, used to work, used to travel, used to think = 5 instances).
  • L1 trap: Adam studied SYSTEMS ENGINEERING, not accounting. He applied for an accounting JOB after graduating. Students confuse "studied" with "applied for." Teach scanning for the correct action.
  • The two-speaker format introduces podcast English. After §3: "Notice how the host asks 'Did you use to travel…?' — that is the question form in real conversation."
  • After §3: "Can you do a 30-second version of Adam's story — in third person? 'Adam used to study…'"
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Adam's story is not perfect — he changed direction twice. But he sounds completely professional. The grammar helps him sound confident."
  • "The host asked 'Did you use to travel?' in a natural conversation. You just heard the grammar in real use."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In §5, you are the guest and I am the host. Same format, your story. That is the full transfer goal of this session."
§ 04
Speaking Preparation — Your Career Story
15 min
Your Mission for §5
Tell your professional story in English for 90 seconds.
Use "used to" at least 3 times correctly.
Use simple past at least 5 times.
Include at least one negative: "I didn't use to…"
No notes in §5 — this preparation is your only time to organize your ideas.
Story Scaffold — Fill this in mentally before §5
Background
My name is [name]. I used to study [subject] at [university/city]. I graduated in [year].
Early Career
After university, I [applied for / started working at] [company/role]. Back then, I used to [describe a habit from that time].
What Changed
I didn't use to [something that is different now]. Then in [year], [specific event — simple past].
Key Milestone
[Company/person] [action — simple past] me in [year]. That was a real milestone because…
Today
Now I work as [current role]. My career used to look very different, but today I [result / feeling].
8 Helpful Phrases — Click to hear and practice
"I used to think…"
"I used to think management was not for me."
"Back then I…"
"Back then I worked 12-hour days and loved it."
"When I started…"
"When I started, there were only 3 people on the team."
"Eventually I decided to…"
"Eventually I decided to change industries."
"That experience taught me…"
"That experience taught me a lot about people."
"Looking back, I…"
"Looking back, I made the right decision."
"What changed was…"
"What changed was the opportunity in Monterrey."
"The best part was…"
"The best part was working with my first mentor."
⏱
You have 5 minutes to prepare your story mentally. Read the scaffold. Choose your 3 "used to" sentences. Choose your 5 simple past events. Pick 2 helpful phrases you will use. Then close the scaffold — in §5, you speak without it.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • During the 5-minute prep, the teacher is NOT silent. Walk through the scaffold with the student: "Tell me your Background line. Good. Now your Early Career line." This oral rehearsal is the preparation — not writing.
  • Watch for: student choosing "used to + event" (e.g. "I used to graduate in 2015"). Redirect immediately: "That's one specific moment — use simple past: I graduated."
  • The 8 helpful phrases are powerful connectors. Ask the student to commit to 2 before §5. These phrases make A2.2 speech sound markedly more fluent.
  • Coaching note for §5: evaluate the story against the 3+5 minimums (3 used to, 5 simple past). Do not stop the student mid-story for corrections — hold feedback until the 90 seconds are complete.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You're not reading a script in §5 — you're telling YOUR story. The scaffold is just a mental map. The language is already yours from S1A."
  • "The 8 phrases make the difference between 'I worked there and then I left' and 'Looking back, that experience taught me everything.'"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "This scaffold structure — Background, Early Career, Change, Milestone, Today — is the exact structure of every LinkedIn 'About' section written by a professional in English."
§ 05
Speaking Free — Your Real Career Story
15 min
You are the guest. I am the host. Tell me your professional story — 90 seconds, no notes, no scaffold. Use "used to" at least 3 times. Use simple past at least 5 times. Start with: "My name is [name], and…"
Headhunter / Host Follow-Up Prompts
"What did you use to do in your first job that you don't do anymore?"
"When did you realize you wanted to change direction?"
"Did you use to work with international teams? How about now?"
"Who promoted you to your current role? How did that happen?"
"What was the biggest challenge in your transition?"
"Tell me about a milestone that changed your career."
"Did you always plan to work in this industry?"
"What is one thing you wish someone told you at the beginning?"
"Where do you see your career in the next 5 years?"
"What advice do you give someone starting in your field today?"
Self-Evaluation — After Your Story
  • I used "used to" at least 3 times correctly
  • I used simple past at least 5 times for specific events
  • I used at least one "I didn't use to…"
  • I spoke for 60+ seconds without stopping
  • I used at least 2 helpful phrases from §4
  • My story had a clear beginning, middle, and ending
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Do NOT interrupt during the 90-second story. Take notes on errors — address them in one batch after the story ends. This builds fluency tolerance, which is a real professional skill.
  • Minimum evaluation standard: 3 used to (any form) + 5 simple past + 1 negative + 60 continuous seconds. If the student achieves all 4: full success. Celebrate explicitly.
  • If the student stops before 60 seconds: use a follow-up prompt from the box. "Tell me about the first moment when you realized your career was going in a new direction." This extends the story naturally.
  • After the story: give feedback in this order — (1) celebrate what went well, (2) one error to fix, (3) ask them to repeat ONE sentence with the correction. No more than 3 minutes of feedback.
  • Optional second round: student delivers the story again for 60 seconds — faster, with the feedback incorporated. This second delivery is always noticeably stronger.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You just told your professional story in English for 90 seconds. That is not a small thing. Recruiters, networking events, LinkedIn introductions — you are now ready for all of them."
  • If the student nails it: "That story would work in any English-speaking professional context. You sound like someone who belongs in international business."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In S2A you'll add wh-questions to interview someone else using 'used to.' You'll be the recruiter — and your questions will be in English."

Live Game — Wordwall
5 min
Practice Game
Career Journey — Used To vs. Simple Past

Replace with Wordwall iframe · Recommended template: Airplane or Match-Up
15 items: used to vs. simple past in career sentences
Teacher-led. 5 minutes. Suggested Wordwall activity: Airplane game (students choose "used to" or "simple past" for each sentence). 15 context-based items. First player to finish wins. Review any missed items together.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This is the energy reset after the intensive speaking in §5. Keep it fast and fun. 5 minutes maximum.
  • After the Wordwall: review any item the student missed. Ask them to say the correct sentence out loud with the correct structure. 30 seconds per item.
  • If Wordwall is not available: quick verbal game — teacher reads a sentence with a blank ("She _____ work at 6 AM every day back then") and student calls out "used to" or simple past plus the full sentence. 10 items, 30 seconds each.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Let's make this competitive — you vs. the clock. Every correct answer = 1 point."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • The gamification element creates a positive emotional anchor for the grammar. Students who play Wordwall during class report higher vocabulary retention in follow-up surveys.
