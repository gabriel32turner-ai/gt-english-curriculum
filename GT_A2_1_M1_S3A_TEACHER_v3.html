⚠ No -ed Rule — Must Be Memorized
go → went  ·  have → had  ·  make → made  ·  give → gave  ·  meet → met  ·  take → took  ·  write → wrote  ·  get → got
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Memory patterns: meet/met · get/got (cambio de vocal corta); make/made · give/gave · write/wrote (vocal+d); go/went (completamente diferente); have/had · take/took (cambio interno).
  • Drill: maestro dice BASE → alumno dice PAST inmediatamente. Luego al revés. Luego maestro dice PAST → alumno dice BASE.
  • "had" es el más importante — "I had a meeting / a call / a problem" cubre el 30% del reporteo diario.

§ 04
Grammar — Irregulars + Wh- Questions
15 min
Irregular Past — All 4 Forms
FormStructureExample
AffirmativeSubject + irregular pastShe went to Dallas. / We had a meeting. / They made a deal.
NegativeSubject + didn't + BASEShe didn't go. / We didn't have time. / They didn't make a decision.
Yes/No QDid + subject + BASE?Did she go? / Did you have a meeting? / Did they make a deal?
Wh- QWh- + did + subject + BASE?Where did she go? / Who did you meet? / What did they make?
Critical rule: Didn't and Did always take the BASE verb — never the irregular past.
❌ "She didn't went" → ✅ She didn't go  |  ❌ "Where did she went?" → ✅ Where did she go?

⚠ Most Common Errors — S3
❌ "I didn't went to the meeting."→✅ I didn't go. (base after didn't)
❌ "Where did she went?"→✅ Where did she go? (base after did)
❌ "He maked a decision yesterday."→✅ He made a decision. (irregular — must memorize)
❌ "They didn't had time for the report."→✅ They didn't have time. (base verb after didn't)
Activity 1 — Choose the Correct Past Form
Haz clic en la opción correcta.
We ___ a very productive meeting with the director last week.
She ___ a presentation to the board last Friday.
Where ___ you ___ for the client visit last month?
They ___ a decision about the contract yesterday.
___ did Carlos meet at the conference?
___ did the presentation go? Was it successful?
Activity 2 — True or False
¿Esta oración es gramaticalmente correcta?
"She didn't make a decision" — this is grammatically correct.
"Where did she went?" — this question is correctly formed.
"Wh- questions use Did + subject + BASE verb — same as Yes/No questions."
"I didn't had time" is the correct negative form of "I had time".
Activity 3 — Wh- Question Order
Arrastra las piezas para formar una Wh- question correcta en simple past.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Q3 es el diagnóstico Wh-: "did you go" vs "did you went". Si elige "went you" — aplica orden de palabras del español (¿fuiste tú?).
      • Después: drilling oral Wh-. Maestro describe un escenario. Alumno hace una Wh- question. "Carlos went somewhere last week." → "Where did he go?" Rondas rápidas.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "I'll make a statement. You ask me a Wh- question. 'I went somewhere interesting last week.' — What's your question?"
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "Wh- questions are what your international manager asks every Monday. 'What did you do? Who did you meet? How did it go?' — memorize the pattern."

      FROM
      Carlos Mendoza <[email protected]>
      TO
      Ana Rivera — VP Sales
      ✈️ Trip Report — Dallas Sales Conference
      Carlos Mendoza
      Sales Manager · Grupo Industrial Nortek
      Watch for: irregular past verbs (went/had/made/gave/met/took/wrote/got) · Wh- question forms · negatives · outcomes

      Hi Ana,

      Here is my summary of the Dallas Sales Conference last week.

      On Monday, I went to the airport at 5 AM and took the early flight to Dallas. I met our regional contact, Jim Carter, at the hotel. We had lunch and discussed the Q4 strategy.

      On Tuesday, I gave our main presentation to 40 participants. The response was very positive — we got excellent feedback from three potential clients. After the presentation, I met with the Director of Operations and we made an agreement to schedule a follow-up call next month.

      On Wednesday, I wrote a summary of all the conversations and took notes on the action items. I didn't have time for the networking dinner — I had an early flight the next morning.

      Overall, the trip was very productive. I can answer your questions in our Monday call. What did you want to know about the presentation?

      Carlos Mendoza · Sales Manager · Ext. 108
      Comprehension — Wh- Questions
      Responde usando información del trip report.
      Where did Carlos go last week?
      Who did Carlos meet at the hotel?
      What did Carlos make with the Director of Operations?
      Carlos gave a presentation to 40 participants on Tuesday.
      Carlos attended the networking dinner on Wednesday evening.
      They got positive feedback from three potential clients.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Comprehension questions escritas como Wh- questions — refleja la gramática de §4. Alumno lee y responde Wh- questions naturalmente.
      • Irregular verb count: went, took, met, had x2, gave, got, met, made, wrote, took, didn't have, had. Cuenta con el alumno: 12+ usos en un email.
      • Trampa R2: "didn't have time" — claro negativo. Los que no leen con cuidado responden TRUE.
      • Después: "Can you ask me 3 Wh- questions about Carlos's trip?" — producción inversa.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Have you ever written a trip report in English? After today you have the grammar for it."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention

      § SP
      Speaking Practice
      10–12 min
      🗣 Guided Speaking — Pair / Group Discussion
      Irregular Verbs — Your Real Stories
      Answer each question using today's irregular verbs: went · had · made · gave · met. Answer the Wh- questions in full sentences. Your teacher will ask follow-up questions — stay in English.
      Question 1
      "Where did you go for work last month — or last year? What happened there?"
      Hint: I went to… / We went to the client's office… / I went to a conference in…
      Question 2
      "What meetings did you have last week? Who did you meet with?"
      Hint: I had a meeting with… / I met with… / We had a call about… / I met the new…
      Question 3
      "What decisions did you make at work recently? How did you make them?"
      Hint: I made a decision to… / We made a plan for… / I made changes to… / My manager made…
      Question 4
      "Did you give a presentation, a report, or feedback to anyone last week?"
      Hint: I gave a presentation to… / I gave feedback on… / I gave the update to… / No, I didn't give…
      Question 5
      "Ask ME a Wh- question about my last work trip or meeting — use went, had, met, or gave."
      Hint: Where did you go…? / Who did you meet…? / What did you give…? / How many meetings did you have…?
      ⏱ 10–12 min total 📋 5 questions 🎯 Min. 5 irregular verbs 👥 Pair or teacher-led
      ⚡ Speaking Challenge — Closing
      "Tell me about a real work trip, conference, or important meeting you had — 60 seconds, no notes. Use at least 4 of today's irregular verbs: went, had, made, gave, met."
      Teacher evaluates: correct irregular past forms (went — NOT goed, had — NOT haved, made — NOT maked) · at least 1 Wh- question structure produced · at least 4 of the 5 target verbs used · natural time expressions (last week / last month / in March). Key error to watch: "I didn't went" — recast immediately: "I didn't go — didn't always takes the base form."
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • This is the first time students use irregular verbs in free production. Errors are expected and welcome — they reveal which forms aren't automatized yet. Don't suppress them; use them as data.
      • The most common errors at this stage: "I goed," "I maked," "I didn't went." Recast every one naturally in your follow-up question. Never correct mid-sentence — wait for the full thought to finish.
      • Q2 and Q4 are the highest-yield questions — met and gave generate the most practice repetition. Prioritize them if time is short.
      • Q5 (student asks a Wh- question): many students will produce "Where you went?" instead of "Where did you go?" — this is the inversion error. Recast: "Where did I go? I went to…" and ask them to repeat the question correctly once.
      • The Speaking Challenge is a story, not a list. Push for narrative: "And then what happened?" "Why did you go?" "Who made that decision?"
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "You just told me a real story from your work — in English, with verbs that 90% of English learners get wrong. You got them right. That's not beginner level anymore."
      • After Speaking Challenge: "Count the irregular verbs you used. Each one you said correctly is now in your active vocabulary — not passive, active."
      • When a student self-corrects (says "goed" then fixes it): "You caught that yourself. That's the sign that the correct form is already in your head — it just needs a little more speed."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "In S3B you'll use these same verbs in a trip debrief role play — your manager will ask you Wh- questions and you'll answer without preparation. Same verbs, higher speed."
      • Upsell: "By S4 you'll add duration — 'I worked on the proposal for 3 hours / from Monday to Wednesday.' That makes your stories sound complete and professional."
      • Testimonial trigger: "Which of today's irregular verbs will you use first in a real work conversation? Which one do you need to practice more?"
