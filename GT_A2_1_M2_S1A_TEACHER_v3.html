Warm-Up — Last Time You…
5–7 min
Maestro hace las preguntas. El alumno responde con lo que puede. No hay respuesta incorrecta — es activación diagnóstica. El foco de la sesión emerge de estas respuestas.

02 · Last big event
🎤 Conference

03 · Last meeting result
✅ Success
it went really well
OR
⚠️ Problem
something didn't go as planned
What was the outcome? What decision did you make?
04 · Last report or presentation
📊 Presented
spoke in front of others
OR
📝 Wrote
sent a written report
Who did you present to? What did you say?
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • ACCIÓN: Abre con la imagen del evento. Pregunta "What is this?" antes de mostrar las tarjetas. Activa el campo semántico visual primero.
  • M2 arranca con irregular verbs — el alumno en M1 aprendió verbos regulares. Aquí verás exactamente qué irregulares ya sabe. Los que no sabe son los de §2.
  • Si el alumno dice "I goed to…" o "I maked" — nota mental, no corrijas. Esos son los errores que la sesión va a resolver. Úsalos como diagnóstico.
  • Timing: 5–7 minutos. No te quedes aquí. La densidad de la sesión está en §2, §3 y §4.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Piensa en el último viaje de trabajo o la última visita de cliente. Esa es exactamente la historia que vas a poder contar en inglés al final de esta sesión."
  • Conecta con su empresa: "¿Tu empresa tiene clientes en otras ciudades o países? ¿Van a ferias o congresos? Ese inglés es el que construimos hoy."
Capa 3 · Retención & Upsell
  • "Al final de M2 vas a poder contar cualquier historia de trabajo en inglés — con verbos irregulares, preguntas Wh- y la estructura de storytelling profesional angloamericana."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • ACCIÓN: Presenta verbos con sus colocaciones — NUNCA solo el verbo aislado. "had A MEETING", "made A DECISION" — el chunk completo es la unidad de aprendizaje.
  • Pronunciación crítica: "met" /met/ vs "meet" /miːt/ — confusión pasado/presente muy común en hablantes. Drill directo: "I meet every day. Yesterday I MET." 3 repeticiones.
  • Sistema de patrones: no memorices 100 verbos irregulares — hay patrones. Este es el puente a §3. "meet/met, get/got — ¿ves el patrón? La vocal cambia de /iː/ a /e/."
  • "went" es la irregularidad más extrema — completamente diferente a "go". Menciónalo explícitamente. "Hay solo 1 verbo en inglés que cambia completamente en pasado. Es 'go'. Ahora ya lo sabes."

§ 04
Grammar — Irregular Past · Wh- Questions · Duration
12–15 min
Negative with Irregular Verbs — The Golden Rule
Critical rule: In negatives and questions, use didn't + BASE FORM — NEVER the irregular past form.
✅ "She didn't go" — NOT "She didn't went" ✗
✅ "He didn't have a meeting" — NOT "He didn't had" ✗
✅ "We didn't make a decision" — NOT "We didn't made" ✗
FormStructureExample
AffirmativeSubject + irregular pastI went to the conference. / She made a decision.
NegativeSubject + didn't + BASE formI didn't go. / She didn't make a decision.
QuestionDid + subject + BASE form?Did you go? / Did she make a decision?
Short answerYes, I did. / No, I didn't."Did you meet the director?" — "Yes, I did."
Wh- Questions in Simple Past
What
asks about things / events
"What did you discuss at the meeting?"
"What happened at the conference?"
"What decision did they make?"
Who
asks about people
"Who did you speak to?"
"Who gave the presentation?"
"Who did you meet at the trade show?"
Where
asks about places
"Where did the meeting take place?"
"Where did you go for the site visit?"
"Where did they send the proposal?"
When
asks about time
"When did you get the feedback?"
"When did the negotiation take place?"
"When did she send the report?"
Why
asks about reasons
"Why did the meeting go well?"
"Why didn't he come to the conference?"
"Why did they change the supplier?"
How long
asks about duration
"How long did the conference take?"
"How long did the trip take?"
"It took two days. / It took about 3 hours."
Structure: Wh- word + did + subject + BASE verb?
"What did you find?" · "Who did she speak to?" · "How long did it take?"
Exception — when "who" is the subject: "Who spoke first?" — no "did", the verb is already past.
⚠ Most Common Errors — A2.1 Students
❌ "I didn't went to the meeting."→✅ I didn't go to the meeting.
the #1 A2 error globally — didn't + BASE form always
❌ "We didn't had a solution."→✅ We didn't have a solution.
same rule — base form after didn't
❌ "What you spoke about?"→✅ What did you speak about?
Wh- questions always need "did" for past
❌ "She gived a presentation."→✅ She gave a presentation.
give is irregular — not give+d
❌ "How long it took?"→✅ How long did it take?
questions need auxiliary — did + base form
⚡ Micro-Speaking — 90 seconds
Maestro hace las preguntas → alumno responde con verbos irregulares correctos:
"What did you do last week at work?" — Student uses: had / made / went / met / sent
"Who did you speak to about it?" — Student answers with a real person from their work
"How long did the meeting take?" — Student answers: "It took about [time]."
"Did you write a report after? What did you find?" — Student uses wrote / found in context

Activity 2 — True or False: Grammar Rules
Is this rule correct?
After "didn't," we always use the BASE FORM of the verb — even if the verb is irregular.
"She didn't went to the meeting" is the correct negative form.
"What did you find?" is a correct Wh- question in Simple Past.
"How long did the conference took?" is grammatically correct.
"I goed to Guadalajara last week" is the correct past form of "go".

      • La regla más importante de M2: didn't + BASE FORM. Repítela 3 veces en la explicación. Escríbela. Haz que el alumno la diga. Es el error #1 en A2 a nivel mundial.

      Hi team,

      I'm back from Monterrey. Here's a quick summary of what happened.

      On Tuesday, I went to the industry conference at Expo Monterrey. I met three potential suppliers and spoke to two distributors from Guadalajara. The conference took two full days. I also gave a short presentation about our Q3 results — it went really well. The audience gave excellent feedback.

      One challenge: I spoke to the logistics team and they said there was a delay with the last shipment. They didn't give a clear reason. I wrote a summary and left it with the operations manager.

      Long story short — it was a productive trip. I'll share the full report tomorrow.

      Operations Director — IndusMex
      Celaya, Guanajuato · +52 461 147 2119

      MONOLOGUE SCRIPT — STORYTELLING STYLE

      "So, let me tell you what happened at the Guadalajara trade show last week.

      I went there on Monday morning — took an early flight. I met our distributor at the entrance and we went straight to our booth. The event was massive — over 400 companies took part.

      On the first day, I gave two product demos and spoke to about 30 potential clients. I wrote everything down — I didn't want to forget anything. I got a lot of business cards. Some people sent me follow-up messages the same evening.

      The best part? I met a buyer from a company in León. We had a great conversation and they made a verbal commitment to order 200 units. I sent them a formal proposal on Wednesday. We didn't sign anything yet — but the outcome was excellent.

      How long did the trip take? Three days total. Honestly, better than expected."

      § 07
      Production — Tell Your Story
      8–10 min
      Speaking Practice
      What Happened at Your Last Business Event?
      El maestro hace las preguntas. El alumno responde usando los verbos irregulares y Wh- questions de hoy. Mínimo 5 verbos irregulares en las respuestas.
      Question 01
      When did you last go on a business trip or attend a work event? Where did you go?
      Target: "I went to [place] [time expression]. I met [person/people]."
      Question 02
      What happened there? Who did you speak to? What did you discuss?
      Target: "We had a meeting about [topic]. I spoke to [person]. We made a decision about [X]."
      Question 03
      How long did the trip or event take? Was it worth it?
      Target: "It took [time]. The outcome was [positive / mixed / challenging]."
      Question 04
      Did you write a report? Did you send any follow-up messages? What did you find?
      Target: "I wrote / sent / found…" or "I didn't write a formal report, but I left notes."
      Question 05
      Did anything go wrong? What didn't happen as planned?
      Target: "We didn't [base verb]…" — practice the negative with base form.
      🎯 Speaking Challenge — 90 seconds, no notes
      "Tell me the story of a real or imaginary business event. Use: So, what happened was… First… Then… In the end… Use at least 5 irregular past verbs."
      Target: "So, what happened was — I went to [city] last [time expression]. I met [people], had [event], gave [presentation/demo]… In the end, we made a decision about [X]. It took [time]. The outcome was [result]."
