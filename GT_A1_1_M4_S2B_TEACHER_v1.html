§2
Pitch — Organized vs Messy Desk
Argue your side using prepositions of place · Grammar embedded in real opinion
⏱ 10 MIN
Team A — Everything in its place
A tidy desk = a tidy mind!
  • "In a tidy office, everything is in the right place — the folders are in the cabinet, the…"
  • "When the keyboard is in front of the monitor and nothing is under the desk, you feel…"
  • "There is a place for everything: pens are next to the notebook, the…"
  • "Behind a messy desk, there is always a stressed person — but behind a tidy desk…"
Team B — Creative chaos works!
A messy desk = a creative mind!
  • "I know where everything is — the report is under the blue folder, between the…"
  • "Creative people have things on the floor, behind the monitor, next to the…"
  • "In front of a messy desk, you see ideas — everything in the drawer means…"
  • "The best inventors had messy desks! Einstein's desk had papers on…"
⚡ Badge ⚡ — Pitch Note

Badge ⚡ when a student uses 3 different prepositions in a single turn. Write their sentence on the board — celebrate it publicly. Class votes at the end: which team was more convincing? Tally the vote on the board.

§3
Relay — Fix the Preposition!
5 error-correction cards · Each student fixes one · Class builds full correction chain
⏱ 8 MIN
🔄 Preposition Swap NEW VISUAL Click each card → reveal the correct sentence + why
Each card has a sentence with the WRONG preposition. Student corrects it first — then click to reveal the answer.
Card 1 — Fix the preposition
❌ "My laptop is in front my desk."   →   What's wrong?
✅ "My laptop is on my desk." — OR — "My laptop is in front of the monitor."
Two errors here: "in front" must always be "in front OF" (never without "of"). And a laptop is ON a desk, not "in front" of it. "In front of" describes facing position, not placement on a surface.
👆 Click to reveal the answer
Card 2 — Fix the preposition
❌ "The contracts are on the cabinet."   →   What's wrong?
✅ "The contracts are in the cabinet." (inside the drawers)
"On" = on top of the surface. "In" = inside the space. Documents go IN a cabinet (in the drawers), not on top of it. If they're stacked on top, then "on" is correct — but usually documents are stored inside.
👆 Click to reveal the answer
Card 3 — Fix the preposition
❌ "The monitor is between of the two speakers."   →   What's wrong?
✅ "The monitor is between the two speakers." (no "of"!)
"Between" never takes "of" after it. It's "between A and B" — not "between of". This is a very common error — students confuse it with "in front of". Remember: only "in front of" needs the "of".
👆 Click to reveal the answer
Card 4 — Fix the preposition
❌ "The bin is next to of my desk."   →   What's wrong?
✅ "The bin is next to my desk." (never "next to of")
"Next to" never needs "of" — it's complete on its own. Only "in front of" requires "of" in this set of 7 prepositions. If students are unsure: "in front OF" = the only one with "of". All others: no "of".
👆 Click to reveal the answer
Card 5 — Fix the preposition
❌ "My desk is on front of the window."   →   What's wrong?
✅ "My desk is in front of the window." (never "on front of")
Students often say "on front of" by analogy with "on the left/right". But this is wrong — it's always "in front of". Think of it as a fixed 3-word phrase: IN · FRONT · OF. Always three words, always starts with "in".
👆 Click to reveal the answer
💡 Relay — How to run it

Student 1 reads Card 1 aloud, corrects the sentence, then clicks to verify. If correct, they earn the "right to pass" to Student 2. The corrections chain — each student must remember what the previous one corrected. Badge 🎤 unlocks after all 5 cards are corrected. The 3 errors involving "of" (cards 3, 4, 5) are the most common at A1.1 — linger on those.

Solo Challenge — The Perfect Office Brief
2 minutes · All 7 prepositions · this/that/these/those · No notes
⏱ 8 MIN
Solo Challenge — 2 minutes
You're the office manager. Brief a new employee on where everything is in the office.
  • Use ALL 7 prepositions at least once: in · on · under · next to · in front of · behind · between
  • Use this/that/these/those at least 3 times (mix of singular and plural)
  • Mention at least 5 different office objects from today's vocabulary
  • Describe at least 2 different spaces or floors
  • End with: "And the most important rule in this office is…" (teacher's choice — any rule using a preposition)
  • No notes — just speak! Partner tracks prepositions on a tally sheet.

🏢 New Employee Welcome Card
"Welcome to Solaris! Your desk is on Floor 2, between the windows and the printer. Your laptop is in the drawer under your desk. Your access badge is in the red envelope next to your monitor. That folder in front of your keyboard has your contracts."
A new employee asks: "Where is my badge?" and "Where is my laptop?" Answer both using prepositions from the text.
🏗️ New Office Layout — Manager's Brief
"In the new layout: meeting rooms are behind the reception, not in front. The manager's office is between the IT room and the break room. These desks near the windows are for the senior team. Those chairs against the wall are for visitors."
Find "this/that/these/those" in the text. What are they pointing to? Are they close or far from the speaker?

The Source 4 metacognitive question is the highlight — slow down here.

3 Phrases — Use These at Work This Week
Starting tomorrow — say them out loud in a real situation
Phrase 1
"It's in the drawer, under the laptop, on the left."
Three prepositions in one sentence — this is the gold standard for describing locations precisely. Use this the next time someone asks where something is. It will impress immediately.
Phrase 2
"This is my report — that is the client's copy over there."
Use this/that contrast naturally when showing two documents or objects. In meetings, on Zoom calls, or with visitors — it instantly signals you know what you're talking about in English.
Phrase 3
"The [object] is between the [A] and the [B], in front of the [C]."
The most complete location sentence at A1.1 — two prepositions working together. Say this once on a Teams call and your international colleagues will know your English is real, not just basic.
