Live Game — Wordwall & Vocabulary Buzz
10–12 min
Game 1 — 5 min · Wordwall
Work Environment — Vocabulary Match
[Wordwall iframe — Match Up template]

Noun pairs: atmosphere→general mood or feeling / morale→team confidence & motivation / vibe→informal energy / layout→arrangement of the office / culture→values and practices of a company
Verb pairs: collaborate→work together / motivate→encourage performance / design→create intentionally / improve→make better / build→develop gradually

Round 1: normal · Round 2: fastest time
Maestro comparte pantalla. Para 1 a 1: alumno intenta batir su tiempo de ronda 1. Para grupos: primer equipo en completar gana.
Game 2 — 5 min · Vocabulary Buzz

Student responds BEFORE clicking. All answers must be in full sentences, not single words.

DEFINE IT
morale
Team confidence and motivation. "High morale means people want to come to work." Use it: your company's morale right now?
PP ADVERB
"Has your company ___ improved the office?" (recently, small change)
just — "Has your company just improved the office?" / "They've just moved to a new space."
COLLOQUIAL
Things at your company are getting better but aren't perfect. One phrase?
"We're getting there." — "The culture has improved a lot. We're getting there."
DEFINE IT
vibe
(informal) the energy or feeling of a place. "The vibe at that startup is really creative and intense." Your office vibe?
PP ADVERB
"We ___ created quiet zones." (not done, but implies we should)
haven't…yet — "We haven't created quiet zones yet." (yet = end of sentence!)
COLLOQUIAL
You want to say something you'd change about your office. One opener?
"If I could change one thing…" — diplomatic, thoughtful, natural. Use it now about your real office.
DEFINE IT
layout
The arrangement of furniture and areas. "An open-plan layout encourages collaboration but reduces privacy." Your office layout?
PP ADVERB
"Have you ___ worked in a toxic environment?" (in your whole experience)
ever — "Have you ever worked in a truly toxic environment?" ever = in your whole life/experience.
USE IT
Use "collaborate" in a sentence about your team
Any sentence using the verb: "We collaborate well across departments." / "It's easier to collaborate in an open office."
PP ADVERB
"She ___ sent the performance review." (before expected)
already — "She has already sent it." already = before expected, often implies positive surprise.
DEFINE IT
culture (workplace)
The values, behaviours and practices shared by a company. "Culture is what people do when no one is watching." Your company culture in one word?
USE IT
"The atmosphere at my workplace is…"
Student completes with a real, specific description. Not: "good." Full sentence with detail.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • B1.1 Buzz: full sentences, not single words. If student gives a one-word answer → "Put it in a sentence about your real workplace." That's the B1.1 standard.
  • PP adverb cards: student must say the COMPLETE sentence, not just the adverb. Position matters — if they place "yet" wrong, correct it immediately and move on.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Notice you're answering faster than last session. Your brain is building the vocabulary system. That's fluency starting to happen."

Listening — Podcast Conversation: Remote vs Office
8–10 min
🎙
Conversation: "Remote, Office, or Hybrid? — What Actually Works?"
2 speakers: Marco (pro-office) + Elena (pro-remote) · Duration: ~100 seconds · B1.1 natural-fast pace · Opinion language + PP adverbs

Genera con ElevenLabs voces Adam (Marco) + Bella (Elena) · Podcast debate style · Conversational but professional · No music · Natural interruptions
Archivo: GT_B1.1_M2_S1B_Listening_v1.mp3

▶ Show ElevenLabs Script
PODCAST SCRIPT — 2 SPEAKERS — FASTER THAN S1A

Marco: "I'll say something unpopular: I think the office is better. Full stop. I've never been as productive at home as I am in the office. The atmosphere changes everything."

Elena: "I hear you, but I completely disagree. I've already saved over 10 hours a week just by not commuting. That time goes back into my work. And honestly, the vibe at home is better for me — more focused, less distraction."

Marco: "But what about morale? Teams that haven't met in person haven't built real relationships yet. You can't collaborate deeply on a screen. I'm sorry — I've tried. It doesn't work the same."

Elena: "That's a fair point. But the best companies have already figured out hybrid — two or three days in the office, the rest at home. Have you ever tried a proper hybrid structure? Not half-half, but intentional?"

Marco: "Actually, we've just started a hybrid pilot at my company. Three weeks in. So far — honestly? We're getting there."
🔧 ElevenLabs: Multilingual v2 · Marco: Stability 65% · Style 40% · Rate 1.0. Elena: Stability 60% · Style 45% · Rate 1.0. Tono podcast — opiniones directas, interrupciones naturales, energía contrastante entre los dos hablantes.
Listening Task — Opinions & Vocabulary in Context
Listen once. What is each speaker's position? Listen again to answer.
According to Marco, what does "atmosphere" have to do with productivity?
What has Elena already saved by working remotely?
Marco's argument about morale — what does he say about remote teams?
"We're getting there" — what is Marco referring to?
🎙 Post-Listening — Your take: which side is closer to yours? (60 seconds)
1
I think [Marco / Elena] makes the stronger point, because [your reason].
2
I've already experienced [remote / office / hybrid] work and I found that the atmosphere [was / wasn't] good for me because [reason].
3
My company hasn't fully solved the [morale / communication / layout] question yet, but we're getting there.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • B1.1 listening is a debate model — student listens to native-level opinions and then produces their OWN version. Not comprehension for its own sake. Listening as debate preparation.
  • Post-listening scaffold: 60 seconds max — then the REAL debate at §6. Don't let §4 steal time from §6.
  • Count PP adverbs in the audio: just(1) already(2) yet(1) never(1) ever(1) = 6 uses in ~100 seconds. That's the B1.1 natural density.
§ 05
Role Play — Describe Your Company to a Visitor
12–15 min
Role Play 1 — Guided · 5 min
🌍 Foreign Visitor — Tell Me About Your Company
Escenario: un visitante internacional (el maestro) acaba de llegar a la empresa del alumno. Quiere entender la cultura y el ambiente de trabajo. El alumno hace de host / employee y describe la empresa usando el vocabulario de S1A.
🎧 Maestro — Foreign Visitor
"Thanks for having me. What's the atmosphere like here?"
"And the layout? Is it mostly open-plan or do people have their own spaces?"
"Has the culture changed a lot recently? What's already different from before?"
"What's the morale like right now? Are people happy here?"
"What hasn't changed yet that you think should?"
"If you could design this office from scratch — what would you change?"
"One word to describe the vibe of this place. Go."
🗣 Alumno — Employee / Host
"The atmosphere is actually quite [adjective]. People here [collaborate / support / motivate] each other well."
"We have an open-plan layout, which [encourages collaboration / makes concentration harder]."
"The culture has already changed a lot. We've just [started / implemented / created]…"
"Morale is [high / recovering / mixed] — we've had some changes but we're getting there."
"We haven't created [quiet zones / flexible hours / a new policy] yet."
"If I could change one thing, it would be the [layout / communication / morale]."
"The vibe? I'd say it's [creative / professional / intense / relaxed]."
Role Play 2 — Free · 7 min · Sin scaffold
📊 The Honest Exit Interview
Escenario: el alumno acaba de recibir una oferta de trabajo mejor y está haciendo su exit interview con HR. El maestro hace de HR manager. El alumno da su opinión honesta sobre la cultura, el ambiente y lo que la empresa debería mejorar — usando toda la vocabulary de S1A sin notas.
🎯 Free Role Play Rules — B1.1 Standard
El alumno debe usar durante el role play: 5 vocabulary words de S1A de forma natural · 3 PP adverbs distintos (just / already / yet o never / ever) · 1 colloquial phrase ("We're getting there" / "If I could change one thing" / "The atmosphere is really good") · Opiniones genuinas, no solo descripciones neutras — B1.1 is about expressing points of view.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • RP2 — Exit Interview: this is high-engagement at B1.1 because it requires genuine opinions, not just descriptions. Students who are confident speak freely. Students who struggle have the vocabulary scaffold from RP1 still fresh.
  • B1.1 correction protocol: 1 vocabulary note (precision — "you used 'environment' 4 times — what alternatives did we learn?") + 1 PP adverb note (position or double negative) + 1 strong celebration.
  • If the student says something genuinely interesting about their real workplace — build on it. The role play becomes a real conversation. That's B1.1 mastery.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You just described your actual company to someone — in English, without notes, with opinions. That's real professional English. That's the level we're building."
Capa 3 · Retención & Upsell
  • "In S2 we go deeper — remote work, pros and cons, what research says. The same vocabulary, higher-level opinions. That's how B1.1 builds."
§ 06
Debate — Remote vs Office: The Real Answer
10–12 min
💬 Opinion Debate · B1.1 Level
Remote work has already changed everything. Should offices even exist anymore?
"Since COVID, the best companies have already moved to hybrid or fully remote. Offices are expensive, commuting is exhausting, and the research shows remote workers are often MORE productive. Why are companies bringing people back? Is it about control, not performance?"
✅ Offices still matter
"Companies haven't fully solved remote collaboration yet — some things need to be in person."
"The atmosphere in an office motivates people in ways that Zoom never has."
"Junior employees haven't built their skills yet — they learn by being in the room."
"Team morale has already suffered in many fully remote teams — isolation is real."
⚠️ Offices are outdated
"Companies have already proven that productivity doesn't drop when people work from home."
"The best talent expects flexibility — companies that haven't adapted yet are losing people."
"We've just seen the biggest work experiment in history — and remote WON."
"Office culture often just means more gossip, more interruptions and worse morale."
💡 Maestro: toma la posición CONTRARIA a la del alumno. Push hard. Mínimo 5 turnos de debate real. Objetivo: alumno usa PP adverbs ARGUMENTATIVAMENTE (just / already / yet / never / ever) — no como ejercicios, sino como parte de sus argumentos.
Escalation option: After main debate — "Has AI already changed your workplace? What hasn't happened yet that will happen in 5 years?" This bridges to S5 (Future of Work) and generates advanced PP use with predictions.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • B1.1 debate standard: the student expresses opinions AND responds to counter-arguments. That's the jump from A-level discussions (describe) to B1.1 (argue). Push the counter-argument hard.
  • PP adverbs in debate: listen for "already" and "yet" appearing in arguments. "Companies have already proven…" / "We haven't solved…yet" — this is the naturalised use target.
  • If the student uses all three colloquial phrases from S1A in the debate: that's the mastery signal. Note it and celebrate it explicitly at §7.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You just debated the future of work in English. With real opinions. Using vocabulary and grammar you learned today. That's B1.1."
Capa 3 · Retención & Upsell
  • Testimonial trigger — MANDATORIO: "Look at the difference between your first response at §1 and what you just said in the debate. Same topic. Completely different level of English. What changed?" — Deja que el alumno responda. Esta reflexión ancla el progreso percibido.
  • "S2 goes deeper into remote work — pros and cons, the research, what different types of professionals actually prefer. Same vocabulary, richer discussion."
  • "In M2, by S5 you'll be discussing the future of work and AI. You'll use today's vocabulary again — but with predictions and Present Perfect perfect naturally woven in."
