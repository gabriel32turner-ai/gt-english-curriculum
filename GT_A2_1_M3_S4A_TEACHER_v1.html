§1 — Warm-Up
What were your results?
Activate past tense for results context · Connect to real presentations · Simple Past + was/were integrated
5–8 MIN
📊 Opening question: Think about the last time someone asked you “How did it go?” at work — after a meeting, a project, or a delivery. What did you say? Did you give numbers, a summary, or just “bien”? In English, professionals always answer with data + an opinion. Today we practice exactly that.
💬 Partner activity: Tell your partner about one recent work result in 2 sentences. Example: “We completed the delivery on time. The client was satisfied.” Don’t worry about grammar yet. Teacher: collect sentences and identify who uses was/were naturally.
🧠 Language focus: Notice the difference: “We improve our numbers.” vs “We improved our numbers.” vs “The numbers were good.” When do you use each? What’s the difference between an action verb and a state verb in the past?
⚡ Teacher note: S4 targets the integration of Simple Past action verbs (improved, exceeded, completed) with past states (was/were) in a presentation context. This is the most important grammar combination for the S5 exit task. Collect student errors from this warm-up — use them in §5 as live correction examples.
⚡ Quick Win — 30 seconds
Say this out loud: “In my last project, we [action verb-ed] [result]. The outcome was [positive/challenging/successful].” Real situation. Say it now!

§4 — Colloquial
Phrases That Sound Professional
5 presentation phrases used by real professionals in English · Context + example + when to use
8 MIN
PHRASE 1
“I’ll start with the good news.”
Opens a results presentation on a positive note. Sets the tone and keeps your audience engaged before sharing problems.
“I’ll start with the good news: we exceeded our target by 8%.”
PHRASE 2
“As you can see from this slide…”
Directs the audience to data on a visual. Essential for any slide deck presentation. Sounds natural and professional.
“As you can see from this slide, delivery times improved by 20% last quarter.”
PHRASE 3
“The key takeaway here is…”
Signals the most important conclusion from your data. Managers love this because it saves time. Use it after each slide or section.
“The key takeaway here is that the new process worked — defects were reduced by 30%.”
PHRASE 4
“That’s a good question — I’ll come back to that.”
Handles unexpected questions professionally. Buys you time to think without losing face. Critical for A2.1 learners in real meetings.
“That’s a good question about the costs — I’ll come back to that in slide 5.”
PHRASE 5
“In summary, the data showed that…”
The professional way to close any results presentation. Signals you’re wrapping up and connecting the data to a clear conclusion.
“In summary, the data showed that Q3 was our best quarter in 18 months.”
🎯
PRACTICE CHALLENGE
Use all 5 phrases in order to give a 90-second presentation about any result from your work. Your partner marks each phrase as it appears.
⭐ Upsell note: These 5 phrases are the foundation of B1.1 presentation English. Students who master them at A2.1 will immediately stand out in meetings. Mention that the next level (A2.2) adds modals like “could” and “should” to recommendations — making presentations even more powerful.
§5 — Grammar
Presenting Results in the Past
Action verbs + state verbs · Simple Past integration · NEW: Presentation Builder visual
15 MIN
🚨 The Real Challenge
Your director asks you to present last quarter’s results in 3 minutes. In Spanish you say: “Mejoramos la entrega. Los números fueron buenos. Redujimos defectos. El cliente estuvo satisfecho.”

You know these words exist in English. But which are action verbs? Which are states? How do you combine them in a professional presentation? That’s what we practice today.
✅ The Tool: Two Verb Types, One Presentation
Action verbs = things that happened: We improved / We reduced / The team achieved / I presented
State verbs (was/were) = how things were: The result was positive / The numbers were strong / The client was satisfied

Professional pattern: Action verb + result → State (was/were) + evaluation
“We improved delivery by 15%. The result was excellent.”
🏗 NEW VISUAL — Presentation Builder
Build Valeria’s Q3 results presentation step by step. Each step has an instruction, a model sentence, and a student template. Click Complete Step when you’ve said your own version out loud. Finish all 4 to earn the badge.
✅
STEP 1 — INTRO
Open with context
Introduce WHAT you are presenting and WHEN. Use Simple Past + was/were for the opening context.
“Good morning. Today I’m going to present the Q3 results for Solaris Manufacturing. The quarter ran from July to September. There were 12 weeks of production.”
✅
STEP 2 — DATA
Present the numbers
Use action verbs + numbers. Then evaluate with was/were. Say what your team ACHIEVED and what the result WAS.
“We achieved 97% on-time delivery. We reduced defects by 22%. The overall production volume was 8% above the Q2 target.”
✅
STEP 3 — PROBLEM
Report what went wrong
Use negative Simple Past + wasn’t/weren’t. Be honest. Professionals always report challenges with past negatives.
“We didn’t complete the supplier audit in Q3. There were two delays in October. The cost reduction target wasn’t met — we reached 14%, not 20%.”
✅
STEP 4 — CLOSE
Conclude + recommend
Summarize the quarter with “In summary, the data showed...” and make one recommendation. Use Simple Past for the summary.
“In summary, the data showed that Q3 was our strongest quarter this year. I recommend we continue with the new supplier process and set a 25% reduction target for Q4.”

State (positive)Subject + was/were + adjectiveThe client was satisfied. The results were above target.
State (negative)Subject + wasn’t/weren’t + adjectiveThe numbers weren’t final. The report wasn’t complete.
Combined (target)Action verb result + state evaluationWe reduced defects by 30%. The result was excellent.
Neg. questionWasn’t/Weren’t + subject?Wasn’t the target already confirmed? — Yes, it was.
⚠️ 4 errors to correct immediately — every time:
✗ “The results were improve” → ✅ “The results improved” (action verb, not state)
✗ “We was completed the project” → ✅ “We completed the project” (action verb = no was)
✗ “The numbers were exceed” → ✅ “We exceeded the numbers” OR “The numbers were above target”
✗ “We didn’t achieved” → ✅ “We didn’t achieve” (base form after didn’t)

§6 — Reading
Valeria’s Q3 Results Report
Valeria Torres presents results to the board · Action verbs + state verbs highlighted · Professional structure
10 MIN
Valeria Torres
Operations Coordinator · Solaris Manufacturing · Celaya
Audience: Board of Directors
Subject: Q3 Results Presentation
📌 Watch for: action verbs · state verbs (was/were) · negatives · numbers
Good morning. Thank you for this opportunity. I’ll start with the good news.

In Q3, we achieved 97% on-time delivery — our best result this year. We reduced defects by 22% after we implemented the new quality protocol in August. The supplier partnership was more effective than in Q2, and the operations team was very motivated throughout the quarter.

As you can see from this slide, production volume improved by 8% compared to Q2. There were three major milestones: we launched the new logistics system, we completed the ISO audit, and we signed two new supplier contracts.

I also need to be honest about the challenges. We didn’t complete the cost reduction target. The goal was 20%, but the final result was 14%. There were two unexpected equipment repairs in September that weren’t in the budget.

The key takeaway here is that Q3 was a strong quarter overall. The results were above expectations in three of four categories. I recommend we maintain the quality protocol and increase the maintenance budget for Q4.

In summary, the data showed that our team performed well under difficult conditions. Thank you.

§7 — Listening
Q3 Board Presentation — Full Audio
Valeria presents results live to the board · Action verbs + states throughout · ElevenLabs
10 MIN
Q3 Results Presentation — Valeria Torres, Solaris Manufacturing
A2.1 · M3 · S4A · Voice: Sarah (American English) · Speed: 0.82
Good morning, everyone. Thank you for having me. I’ll present the Q3 results for Solaris Manufacturing. I’ll start with the good news.

First, the highlights. In Q3, we achieved 97% on-time delivery. That was our best result in 18 months. We also reduced defects by 22% — this was after we implemented the new quality protocol in August. The operations team was very engaged and the result was excellent.

As you can see from this slide, production volume improved by 8% compared to Q2. There were three major milestones this quarter. We launched the new logistics system in July. We completed the ISO 9001 audit in August. And we signed two new supplier contracts in September. All three were on time and under budget.

Now for the challenges. We didn’t complete the cost reduction target. The goal was 20%, but we achieved 14%. There were two unexpected equipment failures in September — they weren’t in the maintenance plan, and they cost us 6 weeks of the savings we had accumulated.

The key takeaway here is that Q3 was strong overall. The results were above expectations in three of four categories. Only the cost reduction target wasn’t met.

I recommend we maintain the quality protocol for Q4. I also recommend we increase the maintenance budget by 15% to avoid the same problem next quarter.

In summary, the data showed that the team performed very well under difficult conditions. Q3 was a success. Thank you. Any questions?

§SP — Speaking Practice
Your Turn to Present
6 guided questions · Results presentations in past tense · Exit task rehearsal
10 MIN
📚 How to run this: Students answer using action verbs + was/were in the past. Partner marks: (1) every correct action verb, (2) every correct state verb. Correct errors immediately. This is direct S5 exit task rehearsal — simulate real presentation conditions.
Question 1 — Overview
Tell your partner about one successful result from your work this year. What did your team achieve? Use at least one action verb and one was/were state.
💡 Use: “We [verb-ed] [result]. The outcome was [positive/significant/important].”
Question 2 — Numbers
Describe a result with a specific number or percentage from your work. How much did something improve, decline, or change? What was the impact?
💡 Use: “We improved/reduced/exceeded [something] by [X%]. The result was [evaluation].”
Question 3 — Challenge
Report something that didn’t go as planned at work. What was the target? What was the actual result? Use didn’t + base verb AND wasn’t/weren’t.
💡 Use: “We didn’t [achieve/complete] [goal]. The [result/problem] wasn’t [expected/planned] because…”
Question 4 — Structure
Give a 60-second results summary using the 4-step Presentation Builder structure: (1) Context, (2) Data, (3) Problem, (4) Conclusion. You can use a real or imaginary project.
💡 Open with: “I’ll start with the good news.” Close with: “In summary, the data showed that…”
Question 5 — Recommendation
Based on a real or imaginary Q3 result, make one professional recommendation for Q4. Use Simple Past for the context + present for the recommendation.
💡 Use: “In Q3, we [verb-ed]. The [result] was [evaluation]. I recommend we [action] for Q4.”
Question 6 — Full Mini-Presentation ⭐ Exit Task Rehearsal
Give a 2-minute spoken presentation about your team’s last quarter results. Use: 2+ action verbs, 3+ was/were states, 1 negative, 2 colloquial phrases from §4. No notes. Partner times and scores.
💡 This is the S5 exit task format. Treat it like a real board meeting. Stand up if you want!

3 Phrases to Use in Your Next Presentation
PHRASE 1
“We improved [X] by [Y%]. The result was [evaluation].”
The core results sentence. Action verb + state verb together. Use this in your next standup or update meeting. It instantly sounds professional.
PHRASE 2
“As you can see from this slide, [past result].”
Every time you share a chart or table in a meeting, use this. It tells your audience exactly what to look at. Simple, professional, and natural.
PHRASE 3
“In summary, the data showed that [conclusion].”
End every presentation, report, or meeting update with this. Your manager will remember you as someone who knows how to close. That’s a career skill.

Action verbs tell what happened. improved / exceeded / reduced / achieved / implemented — these describe specific actions. Never add “was” before them: “We was improved” is wrong.
