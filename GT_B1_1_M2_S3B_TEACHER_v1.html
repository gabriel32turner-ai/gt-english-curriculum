Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This replaces the S3A diagnostic photo-grid. No support. Mastery = production without scaffolding from second one.
  • What to listen for: Do they use vocabulary from S3A (compensation, benefits, retain, attract, negotiate)? Do they use PP adverbs naturally (already, never, just, yet)? If yes — they retained. If no — this session builds it back through pressure.

Capa 2 · Motivation
  • Script: "S3A gave you the tools. S3B is where you use them for real. No lists, no tables — just you, the topic, and English. Let's find out what you've actually retained." The slight pressure is intentional.
§ 02
⚔️ Debate Round — Salary vs. Benefits
Two positions · Defend whichever side you're assigned · No notes
02
15 min

Teacher assigns a side. Student must defend it — even if they personally disagree. This forces active use of compensation vocabulary and PP adverbs from both perspectives.

✅ Side A — Salary First
Cash is real and immediate. You can invest, save, and control it yourself.
Benefits can disappear. Companies restructure packages. A higher salary stays yours.
Flexibility matters. With more money, you buy your own healthcare, pension, or perks.
Market value. Your next job negotiation starts from your current salary — not your benefits.
❌ Side B — Benefits First
Total compensation beats salary. Benefits often represent 20–40% of real value.
Tax advantages. Benefits like healthcare or pension are pre-tax — more efficient than raw salary.
Quality of life. Flexibility, health cover, and food allowances affect daily wellbeing.
Retention signal. Companies that invest in benefits treat employees as long-term assets, not costs.
🎙 How to run the Debate:
(1) Assign Side A or B — do NOT let the student choose their preferred side. (2) Student opens with 90 seconds defending their position. (3) Teacher challenges with 1 counter-argument. (4) Student responds — 60 seconds. (5) SWITCH sides. Repeat. Full round = 15 min.
1:30
⏱ 90 sec · Opening position
Defend your assigned side — no notes, no stopping
Press START when student begins their opening
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • The debate forces the student to produce vocabulary they'd normally avoid. Defending a position they disagree with is a B1.1+ skill — it requires more active construction.
  • Target structures to listen for: "I've already seen…" / "Companies have never…" / "The benefits package has just been updated…" — any natural PP adverb in the context = win.
  • Teacher challenge prompts (use these to extend): "But what about employees who've never had healthcare before?" / "Has your company already offered that?" / "Haven't they tried that yet?"
Capa 3 · Bajío Context
  • In automotive companies (Toyota, Mazda, Honda in Guanajuato/Celaya), benefits packages are highly structured and transparent. Students in these companies often know exactly what "total comp" means. Use that knowledge — it generates richer debate.
§ 03
🤝 Negotiation Simulations — Make the Deal
3 real scenarios · Role-play · No scripts · Use every tool from S3A
03
15 min
1
The Job Offer That's Not Quite Right
You want the job — but the package needs work
Candidate vs HR Manager 4 min B1.1
"You've just received an offer from a company you want to join. The salary is 8% below your current one, but the benefits package includes private healthcare, a pension plan, and flexible Fridays. You haven't made a final decision yet — you want to negotiate the base salary up while keeping all the benefits."
🧑‍💼 Student — Candidate
Express interest. Reference the package positively. Negotiate the salary — use specific reasons. Don't accept immediately. Use PP adverbs throughout.
👩‍💼 Teacher — HR Manager
The budget is fixed but flexible on one point. Push back once. Then offer a small compromise (signing bonus or extra vacation). See how the student responds.
🎙 Language to push for (without prompting)
"I've already reviewed the package in detail…"
"I haven't made a final decision yet, but I'm very interested…"
"I've never received a below-market offer at this stage, so I'd like to discuss…"
"The total compensation is attractive — I just need the base salary to reflect my current market value."
2
The Annual Review — It's Time
You've been waiting for this conversation all year
Employee vs Manager 4 min B1.1 High Pressure
"It's your annual review. You've had a strong year — you delivered a major project on time and the team has grown under your coordination. You want to negotiate both a salary increase AND an upgrade to your benefits. The company has already improved the package for senior roles — but not for yours yet."
🧑‍💼 Student — Employee
Open by referencing your results. Ask for a salary increase first. Then negotiate benefits (healthcare upgrade, extra allowance). Use data: "The market rate for my role has already increased this year."
👩‍💼 Teacher — Manager
Acknowledge performance. Budget is tight — you can offer one of the two things (salary OR benefits upgrade, not both). See how the student handles the constraint. Push: "What's your priority?"
🎙 Language to push for
"I've already delivered [result] — and I think that justifies a conversation about compensation."
"The team has grown, but my package hasn't been updated yet."
"I've never asked for a raise before — but the market has changed significantly."
"If I had to choose one — I'd prioritize the salary, because I've already invested in my own pension plan."
3
The Counter-Offer — Stay or Go?
Another company has made you an offer. Your current employer wants you to stay.
Employee vs Director 5 min B1.1 ⭐ Advanced
"You've received an offer from a competitor — 20% salary increase, full benefits, remote work. Your current company has just found out and your director wants to meet urgently. They haven't made a counter-offer yet, but they're about to. You haven't accepted the other offer yet either. What do you say?"
🧑‍💼 Student — Employee
Be honest about the offer without giving all the details. Listen to the counter-offer. Evaluate it out loud: "I've already considered the culture here…" Make a decision by end of role-play.
👩‍💼 Teacher — Director
React with genuine interest in keeping them. Make a counter-offer: match salary partially + improve benefits. Ask: "What would it take for you to stay?" Really listen to the answer.
🎙 Language to push for
"I've already started evaluating both options, and honestly it's not just about the salary."
"The other company has offered something I haven't had here yet — remote flexibility."
"I've never felt unvalued here — but I have felt undercompensated."
"If you can match the benefits package, I've already decided I want to stay."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • These scenarios are graded in difficulty: Sim 1 (low stakes, clear goals) → Sim 2 (real performance context) → Sim 3 (emotional + strategic). Choose based on the student's level and energy.
  • You don't need all 3. One well-executed simulation (5–7 min) is worth more than three rushed ones. Choose Sim 2 or 3 for stronger students.
  • Award Badge 2 (🤝 Negotiator) after any simulation where the student: (a) opens professionally, (b) uses at least 2 PP adverbs naturally, (c) reaches a conclusion without the teacher rescuing them.
Capa 2 · Error Focus
  • Most common mastery-level error: reverting to present simple under pressure. "I receive a low salary" → "I've never received market-rate compensation." Correct after the simulation, not during.
  • Second most common: forgetting adverb position. "I already have evaluated" → "I've already evaluated." Address position errors systematically in the debrief.

§ SP
🗣 Extended Speaking — Your Real Position
4 questions · Progressive depth · No notes · Click to reveal model answers
SP
12 min
How this works: Student speaks first — full response, no notes. Then reveal model answer. Student self-evaluates: did they cover the same ideas? Did they use the same structures? This is the B1.1 benchmark, not the script.
1
If your company offered you a choice between a 10% salary increase or a full private healthcare package — which would you take, and why? Has your answer changed since S3A?
This should be different from your S3A answer. Richer, more justified, more natural.
Personal Opinion 90 sec B1.1
Model B1.1 Answer
"I've thought about this more since S3A, and my answer has actually changed. I initially said salary — but when I calculate the real value of private healthcare, it's significant. I've never had it at my current company, and I've already spent a lot on medical bills this year out of pocket. So now I'd take the healthcare. The 10% salary increase sounds big — but healthcare for my family is worth more than that number. It also says something about how the company sees its people."
Note how the answer explicitly references S3A — this models metacognitive awareness. Push the student to do the same.
2
Describe the best and worst compensation package you've ever had. What made the difference — and what have you learned from both?
Use: I've had / I've never had / I had (for a specific past experience) / I've learned
Real Experience 2 min B1.1
Model B1.1 Answer
"The best package I've ever had was at a multinational — they provided food vouchers, private health cover, a savings fund, and flexible hours. On paper, the salary wasn't the highest I've seen, but the total compensation was excellent. The worst was a startup — great salary, no benefits at all. I've never felt less financially secure. I was paying for everything myself. What I've learned is that the number on your payslip doesn't tell the whole story. I look at the full package now — I always ask about healthcare, pension, and flexibility in the first interview."
This answer moves between PP and simple past correctly — "was", "provided" (specific past job) vs "I've learned", "I always ask" (habits now). That tense shift is B1.1 mastery.
3
You are an HR Director. What changes to your company's benefits package have you just implemented — and what haven't you done yet? Defend your decisions.
Role-play + reporting. Use: we've just implemented · we haven't updated yet · we've already invested
Role-Play 90 sec B1.1 ⭐
Model B1.1 Answer
"We've just implemented a new food allowance program for all employees — it already covers 40% of a standard lunch. We've also updated the remote work policy — two days from home per week for non-production roles. What we haven't done yet is improve the pension plan — that's the next priority. We've never offered private healthcare as a company — it's on the table for next year's budget. The goal is to attract better candidates and retain the people we have. We've already seen turnover decrease 12% since we started."
This is the most sophisticated answer — it uses all four adverbs (just, already, yet, never) in a natural professional context. If the student produces something close to this, they are operating at B1.1+ level.
