🔥 Hot Take — Cold Start

5 min
No prep. No notes. Describe a meeting — 60 seconds starts NOW.
Cold start: El alumno describe una reunión real sin preparación. Si se detiene: "Was it long? Were there many people? What was the outcome?"
🔥 Hot Take — 60 seconds
Describe the WORST meeting you ever had. What was it about? How long was it? Were there too many people? What was wrong with it? GO!

§2

🎯 Pitch — Assigned Sides

10 min
You don't choose your side — defend what you're given
Topic: "Meetings should ALWAYS have a written agenda." Teacher asigna los lados — el alumno NO elige.
🟣 Side A — AGREE
"Yes — every meeting needs a written agenda. Defend this."
Starters:"The agenda was clear, so the meeting was productive…""Without an agenda, the meeting wasn't organized…""Last week there were two meetings without an agenda — they were too long…"
🔵 Side B — DISAGREE
"No — some meetings are fine without an agenda. Counter."
Starters:"Quick standups weren't long — they didn't need an agenda…""The Friday meeting was only 10 minutes — an agenda wasn't necessary…""Sometimes the best ideas weren't on the agenda…"
§3

🔄 Relay — Transform It

8 min
No grammar table. No support. Just transform the sentence.
Instrucción: Teacher lee → alumno transforma en voz alta. Sin notas. Was/were bajo presión.
Transform 1 — Affirmative → Negative
"The meeting was productive."
→ Say the opposite.
"The meeting wasn't productive."
Transform 2 — Statement → Question
"There were 12 attendees."
→ Ask a question.
"Were there 12 attendees?" or "How many people were there?"
Transform 3 — Singular → Plural
"The slide was very clear."
→ Change to plural.
"The slides were very clear."
Transform 4 — Fix the error
"The results was better than expected."
→ Find and fix the error.
"The results were better" — plural subject!
Transform 5 — Your reality
"Valeria's meeting was three hours long."
→ How long was YOUR last meeting? Answer with was/were.
Open answer — "My last meeting was [time] long. There were [X] people."

🎤 Solo Challenge — 2 Minutes

8 min
Two minutes. No notes. Chair a meeting from start to finish.
Solo: Alumno dirige una mini-reunión completa. Open → agenda → discussion → action items → close. 2 min. Badge 🎤 al completar.
🎤 SOLO — Chair a Complete Meeting
Scenario: You are the meeting chair. Lead a full meeting with your team. Include:
Open: "Let's get started"State 3 agenda itemsDescribe last week's results (was/were)Assign 2 action itemsClose: "To wrap up…"

🔍 Source Analysis — Meeting Minutes

8 min
Read real meeting excerpts. Analyze. Answer.
Dinámica: Alumno lee → responde en voz alta → click opción. Preguntas abiertas → discusión. T→S
📋 Meeting minutes
"The Q3 review was on Wednesday. There were 8 attendees. The meeting was 3 hours long. Two decisions were made about the new supplier."
❓ Was the meeting short?
💬 Slack message
"The Friday standup wasn't great — only 3 people were there and the agenda wasn't ready. We need to improve next week."
❓ Was the standup productive?
📧 Email from Valeria
"Hi Daniel, the client presentation was excellent. The slides were clear, and the client was very impressed. The next step is to follow up with a proposal."
❓ How was the presentation?
📞 Voicemail
"Hi, it's Carlos. I wasn't at the meeting this morning because I was at the plant. Were there any important decisions? Can you send me the minutes?"
❓ Why wasn't Carlos at the meeting?
