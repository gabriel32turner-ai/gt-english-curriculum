🗣️ Speaking Topic — Receiving a Client

8 min
Your client just arrived. What do YOU say?

Welcome to Our Office

A client from Chicago just arrived at reception. You need to welcome them, show them the way, and help them.

Round 1 · 60 seg
T→S "A client arrives. Welcome them and ask for their name."
Round 2 · 60 seg
S→S "You are the host. Give directions to the meeting room."
Round 3 · 90 seg
Class "What do you say if the client needs water, Wi-Fi, or help with their luggage?"
Model Answer

"Welcome! Good morning. My name is Mónica. I'm your host today. This way, please. The meeting room is on the third floor. Can I help you with your luggage? Can I get you some water or coffee? The Wi-Fi password is on the badge. Please call me if you need anything."
■ Vocabulary ■ Object pronouns ■ Colloquial
Coaching tip: En Round 1, si el alumno solo dice "Hello, welcome" → prompt: "Good! Now ask their name: 'Can I have your name?'" Nunca corrijas mid-utterance. Recicla al final.

§4

💬 Colloquial Phrases — Welcoming Visitors

5 min
The phrases that make visitors feel comfortable — learn them now
Welcome! This way, please.
When: a visitor arrives at reception
"Hi, Mr. Davis! Welcome! This way, please. The meeting room is on the second floor."
Can I help you with that?
When: offering help with luggage or bags
"You have a lot of bags. Can I help you with that?"
Right this way.
When: guiding someone to a room
"The conference room is ready. Right this way."
Do you need anything?
When: checking if the visitor is comfortable
"Here is some water. Do you need anything before we start?"
Práctica rápida: Di el contexto en español → el alumno produce la frase en inglés. Ejemplo: "Llegó un cliente a tu oficina y tiene muchas maletas. ¿Qué le dices?" → "Can I help you with that?"
Gramática integrada: Las frases usan object pronouns (help you, help me) — target de §5. El alumno lo practica sin darse cuenta.
§5

🎯 Grammar — Object Pronouns

10 min
Grammar is a tool — use it to help people, give things, and communicate at work
REGLA DE ORO §5: Esta sección es REFERENCIAL, no drilling. Lee la tabla con el alumno, da un ejemplo personal, luego activa el Poll. Nunca pidas que conjuguen fuera de contexto.
Error #1 de mexicanos: "Call to me" → Call me. "Send to her the file" → Send her the file o Send it to her. Drill con escenarios reales.
SubjectObject PronounWork Example
ImeCan you help me? — Call me when you arrive.
youyouI can help you. — Can I call you later?
hehimCall him at the hotel. — Send him the address.
sheherSend her the badge. — Tell her the Wi-Fi password.
ititSend it to the client. — I need it for the meeting.
weusSend us the files. — Can you help us?
theythemCall them before 3 PM. — Give them a badge.
⚠️ Common Error
✗ "Call to me" → ✅ Call me
✗ "Send to her the file" → ✅ Send her the file / Send it to her

🎧 Listening — Client Arrives at the Office

8 min
📍 Lobby de la planta — Mónica recibe a un cliente de EE.UU.
🎙️
ElevenLabs Audio — "Welcome to Solaris"
Voice: Sarah (American) · Speed: 0.80 · A1.2 M3 S1A
Good morning, Mr. Davis. Welcome to ConnectPro International. My name is Mónica. I am your host today.

Please sign in here. This is your visitor badge. Please wear it all day.

Can I help you with your luggage? — Oh, thank you. Please put it here.

The meeting room is on the second floor. This way, please. The elevator is next to the reception desk.

Can I get you some water or coffee? — Coffee, please.

The Wi-Fi password is on the back of your badge. If you need anything, please call me. My extension is 205.

The meeting starts at ten. Mr. Garza is on his way — he will be here in five minutes. Please make yourself comfortable.
📎 ElevenLabs: Voice — Sarah (American English) · Speed 0.80 · Pitch 1.0
✏️ Listening — Multiple Choice
1. What does Mónica give Mr. Davis?
2. Where is the meeting room?
✏️ Listening — True or False
Mónica asks Mr. Davis to sign in at reception.
Mr. Davis asks for water.
The Wi-Fi password is on the visitor badge.
Después del listening: "Close the script. Tell me THREE things Mónica did for Mr. Davis." Esto refuerza object pronouns sin que el alumno lo note: "She helped him with his luggage."
