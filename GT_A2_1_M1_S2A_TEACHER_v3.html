§ 01
Warm-Up — Picture Prompt
5–8 min
📸 Mira la oficina — responde estas preguntas
1
What do you think these people did yesterday at work?
Try: "They probably attended a meeting / organized files / presented results…"
2
What did YOU do at work yesterday? Name 3 things.
Acepta cualquier respuesta — escucha si usa -ed. Corrige después de §4.
3
Did you finish everything on your to-do list? Why or why not?
Try: "No, I didn't. I didn't complete the ___ because…"
4
Did anyone cancel or delay something at your work last week?
Try: "Yes — the client delayed / cancelled the ___."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • S1 review checkpoint: Q2 y Q3 testean directamente la gramática de S1. Nota: ¿usa el alumno -ed consistentemente? ¿"didn't" lleva verbo base?
  • Q4 introduce "delay" y "cancel" en contexto antes del vocab — pre-enseñanza natural.
  • Picture prompt: elige personas específicas en la foto y pregunta "What did she do yesterday?" — fuerza conciencia de 3ª persona.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "I want you to describe that woman in the photo. What did she do yesterday? Use at least 3 past verbs." — ancla visual concreta.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Today's session adds 8 more verbs. By S5, you'll have 24 past verbs — enough to describe any full work week."

§ 04
Grammar — Did / Didn't + Full Q&A
12–15 min
Structure — Yes/No Questions + Full Answers
FormStructureExample
QuestionDid + subject + base verb?Did you attend the training last week?
Short ✔Yes, + subject + did.Yes, I did.
Short ✗No, + subject + didn't.No, I didn't.
Full ✔Yes, I did. + full sentence.Yes, I did. I attended the Q3 training on Tuesday.
Full ✗No, I didn't. + full sentence.No, I didn't. I didn't attend because it was rescheduled.
Key rule: After "Did", always use the base verb — not the past form.
✅ "Did she approve?" — ✗ "Did she approved?" (Never past form after Did)
Q&A Pairs — Lee y practica con tu maestro
Q
Did you attend the project meeting last week?
A+
Yes, I did. I attended the meeting on Wednesday morning.
A−
No, I didn't. I didn't attend — it was cancelled.
Q
Did the manager approve the budget?
A+
Yes, she did. She approved it last Friday afternoon.
A−
No, she didn't. She didn't approve it — she needed more information.
Q
Did you complete all your tasks yesterday?
A+
Yes, I did. I completed 6 out of 6 tasks by 5 PM.
A−
No, I didn't. I didn't complete the report — I needed two more hours.
Q
Did the supplier delay the shipment?
A+
Yes, they did. They delayed it by three days due to production issues.
A−
No, they didn't. The shipment arrived on schedule last Tuesday.
⚠ New Errors at S2 Level
❌ "Did she approved the budget?"→✅ Did she approve the budget? (base verb after Did)
❌ "Yes, I did attend." (con énfasis solo)→✅ Yes, I did. — luego agrega la oración completa por separado.
❌ "She notifyed the team."→✅ She notified the team. (consonant+y → ied, not -yed)
❌ "I organized AND I prepared AND I completed…" (lista sin contexto)→✅ I organized the files, prepared the agenda, and completed the report. (oración compuesta — natural y profesional)
Activity 1 — Choose the Correct Form
Haz clic en la opción correcta.
We ___ the presentation to the client last Friday.
She ___ the contract yesterday — she was out of the office.
___ you ___ the budget report last week?
"Did she notify the team?" — "___"
The verb "reply" in the simple past is ___.
Activity 2 — True or False: M1 Rules
¿Esta regla gramatical es correcta?
"Did the team attended the training?" — This question is correct.
"She notified the team yesterday." — This is correctly spelled.
"I organized files, prepared the agenda, and completed the report." — Compound past sentences like this are correct and professional.
"I study → I studyed" follows the correct -y spelling rule.
Activity 3 — Sentence Order
Arrastra las piezas para formar una oración correcta en pasado.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Q&A pairs para drilling oral. El maestro pregunta Q, el alumno da respuesta corta + completa. Luego swap: el alumno pregunta, el maestro responde. Acelera después de 2 rondas.
      • Activity 3 drag: "She organized AND presented" — pasado compuesto con dos verbos. Enseña explícitamente: "In one sentence, you can have 2 or 3 past verbs with AND."
      • T4: "studyed" es un error muy común. La regla: consonante+y → ied. La terminación -yed nunca es correcta.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Speed round: yo pregunto, tú respondes — solo short answer. Lo más rápido posible." — 6 preguntas en 30 segundos. Crea presión positiva.
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "Compound past sentences are the key to sounding professional: 'I organized, prepared, and delivered.' That's 3 actions in one. That's A2+ English."
      § 05
      Reading — Daily Report
      8–10 min
      FROM
      Laura Vega <l.vega@vertexsolutions.com>
      TO
      Roberto Salinas — Operations Manager
      📋 Daily Report — Wednesday
      Watch for: new past verbs from S2 · compound past sentences · negatives with didn't · reason + because

      Hi Roberto,

      Here is my update for today.

      This morning, I attended the weekly team meeting and organized the agenda for the distribution team. After the meeting, I contacted our supplier — they approved the new delivery timeline for next Monday.

      In the afternoon, I prepared and presented the Q1 summary to the finance team. I also notified the sales department about the new pricing — they approved it immediately.

      Unfortunately, I didn't complete the product catalogue update. The supplier call lasted two hours longer than planned. I will deliver it tomorrow morning before 9 AM.

      Overall, today was a productive day. I completed 4 out of 5 tasks on my list.

      Best regards,

      Laura Vega · Operations Coordinator · Vertex Solutions · Ext. 207
      Comprehension
      Responde las preguntas.
      What did Laura attend this morning?
      Why didn't Laura complete the product catalogue update?
      Laura submitted the Q1 report to the finance team.
      The supplier approved the new delivery timeline.
      Laura completed all 5 tasks on her list.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Pasado compuesto: "I prepared AND presented" — señálalo explícitamente. Pide al alumno que encuentre todas las estructuras compuestas en el email.
      • Trampa R1: "submitted" — Laura presentó, no envió. Ambas palabras suenan profesionales; los alumnos las confunden. "Present" = mostrar/explicar verbalmente. "Submit" = enviar formalmente.
      • Grammar hunt: encuentra todos los verbos en pasado (attended, organized, contacted, approved x2, prepared, presented, notified, completed) + el único negativo (didn't complete).
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Can you write a 3-sentence version of Laura's email about YOUR day? Just 3 sentences — prepared, presented, and didn't complete."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "Notice: Laura always explains WHY she didn't complete something. That's professionalism. Always add a reason: 'I didn't finish because…'"

      David: Excellent. And the quarterly summary — did you complete it?


      Marcos: Not yet. I started it, but I didn't finish it. I'm going to complete it tonight and send it to you before eight tomorrow morning.


      David: Perfect. Thanks, Marcos. Talk tomorrow.

      GT Originals
      📋 The 3-part work update formula: "I [action] + [object] + [outcome/reason]" — never just "I attended a meeting."
      ⭐ "notify → notified" — the -ied rule: study, reply, apply, carry, supply, notify. All very common in business English.
      💼 "I organized, prepared, and presented" — compound past is B2 fluency at A2 level. Use it every session from now.
      ⚠️ "present" (verb) = to show/explain. "present" (adjective) = actualmente presente. Different stress and meaning.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Info Gap: teacher card está oculta en Student Mode. Presiona por oraciones compuestas y respuestas completas. Un "Yes, I did" sin elaboración = incompleto.
      • Evalúa: ¿spelling -ied correcto? ¿Estructuras compuestas intentadas? ¿Razones dadas para tareas incompletas?
