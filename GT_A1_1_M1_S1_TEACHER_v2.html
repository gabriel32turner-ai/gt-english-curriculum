§ 01
Warm-Up — Scenario
5–8 min
📍 The Scenario
You are at a corporate event in Monterrey. A person from the USA walks up to you and says: "Hi! I'm David. Nice to meet you." — What do you say?
1
What do you say first? Try to say hello in English.
Hint: "Hello!" / "Hi!" / "Good morning!"
2
How do you introduce your name? Try: "My name is ___."
Or the short form: "I'm ___."
3
How do you say your job? "I am a ___."
Example: manager, engineer, coordinator, director…
4
Where are you from? "I'm from ___, Mexico."
Say your city and country in English.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Expected responses: "Hello / Hi / Good morning", "My name is ___", "I'm a ___", "I'm from Celaya/Mexico"
  • If student freezes: point to the scenario box. Read it aloud together first.
  • Accept Spanish words for jobs — note them and give the English equivalent immediately.
  • Do not correct pronunciation here — this is activation, not evaluation. Timing: max 7 min.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "¡Eso es! That's exactly what you'd say to David." — celebrate the first English sentence.
  • If they attempt a full sentence: "You just spoke English in a real work situation. That's the goal!"
  • Keep energy high — this first moment sets the tone for the whole module.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "By the end of today, you'll be able to do exactly what we just tried — but in full English."
  • Gentle preview: "In session 4 you'll practice this in a real networking format."

Grammar — Verb to be
12–15 min
Rule 1 — Subject + to be (affirmative)
SUBJECT+ AM / IS / ARE+ COMPLEMENT
PronounTo beShort formExample
IamI'mI'm Carlos. I'm a manager.
YouareYou'reYou're from Mexico.
HeisHe'sHe's an engineer.
SheisShe'sShe's a director.
WeareWe'reWe're from Celaya.
TheyareThey'reThey're in the company.
❌ I am manager.
→
✅ I am a manager.
Use a before a job title!
Rule 2 — Real context: job + company + city
I'm Carlos. I'm a manager.
Soy Carlos. Soy gerente.
She's Ana. She's a director.
Ella es Ana. Es directora.
We're from Celaya, Mexico.
Somos de Celaya, México.
I'm at Toyota. It's a big company.
Estoy en Toyota. Es grande.
He's Miguel. He's an engineer.
Él es Miguel. Es ingeniero.
Nice to meet you. I'm from Mexico.
Mucho gusto. Soy de México.
✦ Activity — Choose the correct form of "to be"
1.I ___ Carlos. I work at Toyota.
2.She ___ a director at the company.
3.We ___ from Celaya, Mexico.
4.He ___ an engineer. His name is Miguel.
5.You ___ a manager. Nice to meet you!
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Most common errors at A1.1: "I am manager" (missing article), "She are" (wrong conjugation).
  • Let student attempt the activity first — don't correct preemptively. Error = learning moment.
  • After activity: review wrong answers together. Ask "Why did you choose that?" — metacognition.
  • Grammar exposure is brief at A1.1 — keep explanation under 3 min, activity under 8 min.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • When student gets 4/5 or 5/5: "Look! You're already using English grammar correctly. That's not easy."
  • Link to real life: "You'll use this structure in your first email to an international client."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Preview: "In Module 2, you'll use 'to be' to describe your entire team — managers, directors, engineers."
  • Upsell moment: "Imagine writing your LinkedIn profile in English using exactly this structure."
§ 05
Reading
8–10 min
Read · ~80 words · A1.1 level

Hello! My name is Carlos Reyes. I'm from Celaya, Mexico. I'm a manager at Toyota. Toyota is a big company. It's in Celaya.


My colleague is Ana. She is a director. She is from Guadalajara. Ana and I are at Toyota.


This is Miguel. He is an engineer. He is from Mexico City. We are a good team.


Nice to meet you! I'm happy at my job. Toyota is a great company.

1. Carlos is a ___.
2. True or False: Ana is from Celaya.
3. Miguel is an ___.
4. True or False: Toyota is a small company.
5. Where is Carlos from?
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • First pass: teacher reads aloud while student follows. Second pass: student reads silently. Third: student reads aloud.
  • Watch for "colleague" /ˈkɒliːɡ/ mispronunciation — very common.
  • After reading: close the text. Answer questions from memory = better retention.
  • Q2 and Q4 are T/F — most students get Q4 wrong (they skim "big" as positive and miss it). Let them discover the error themselves.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You just read a full text in English. Your brain processed every sentence — that's real progress."
  • Ask: "Is Carlos's situation similar to yours?" — personal connection to the content.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Retention trigger: "Imagine reading an email like this from a new client. You already can."
  • Moodle: this text appears in their autonomous practice with audio support.

5. How many people are in the team?
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Listen 1: global comprehension — no questions. Listen 2: answer questions. Listen 3 (if needed): verify.
  • Pause after each name is mentioned if student struggles (Carlos → pause, Ana → pause).
  • For Q5 (how many people): students often say "two" — they're listening for names but miss Miguel. Play from "And this is Miguel…" again.
  • Script is for teacher/audio production use only — don't show in student mode.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "The first listen is always the hardest. Your ear is training right now — it gets faster every session."
  • If 4/5 or 5/5: "You just understood a native speaker. That's your new baseline."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Homework bridge: "Listen to this audio again at home before S2. Try to repeat each sentence."
  • Testimonial trigger: "How would you describe this experience to a colleague who hasn't started yet?"
