§ 03
M1 Grammar Review & Challenge
10–12 min
M1 Grammar — Complete Reference
S1 — Used To
Past habit or state — no longer true. All subjects. Aff: used to + base. Neg: didn't use to + base. Q: Did + subject + use to + base?
I used to commute 2 hours. / I didn't use to manage anyone. / Did you use to travel? — Yes, I did.
S2 — Used To vs Simple Past
Used to = repeated habit, no longer true. Simple Past = specific event with date or clear time marker. Change events always Simple Past.
I used to work in sales (habit). / I got a promotion in 2021 (specific event). / She switched companies (change event).
S3 — Would
Formal alternative to "used to" for past REPEATED ACTIONS only. Never for states (be/have/feel/know). More literary register.
Managers would give feedback once a year ✅ / The HQ would be small ✗ → used to be ✅
S4 — Collocations
Fixed verb+noun pairs — must be learned as units. Not interchangeable with "make" or "do".
give feedback · take initiative · celebrate a milestone · implement a policy · negotiate a deal · adapt to change

8–10 min
Andrea Soto
Operations Director · TechFlow Solutions · CDMX
M1 Final Reading
This is M1's most complex text. All 4 grammar areas + 18+ M1 vocabulary words · Find every grammar form and every collocation.

I joined my first company at 24. Back then, I used to commute almost two hours each way. The workplace used to have a rigid hierarchy with six management levels. There didn't use to be any flexibility — I used to report to a manager who reviewed every single document before it moved forward.

The culture used to feel very traditional. Managers would give feedback only at the annual review — one meeting a year. Employees didn't use to take initiative; everything needed approval from the leadership. The company would implement new policies without consulting anyone. The strategy used to focus exclusively on the local market — no international ambition, no plans to expand.

I used to deal with the same clients every week. We would negotiate every contract through the director — never directly. I used to manage a team of four people and didn't use to have any budget responsibility.

Then things changed. The company merged with a US group in 2018. The headquarters moved. The leadership restructured. I switched to the new corporate team. It was the biggest milestone of my career.

Nowadays, I manage a team of 35 people across three countries. I negotiate international contracts directly. Our team celebrates every milestone together. The company has expanded to twelve markets. There is full flexibility — I work from home two days a week and travel only when necessary. Everyone takes initiative, gives and receives feedback constantly, and adapts quickly.

The biggest change? Back then, I used to feel invisible in a rigid system. These days, I feel like I grew up professionally — and I'm exactly where I should be.

Comprehension — All M1 Grammar
Answer using information from Andrea's text.
Find ONE correct use of "would" in the text. Why is "would" correct — and not just "used to" — in that sentence?
Which paragraph uses Simple Past for change events? Name 3 Simple Past verbs from that paragraph.
Andrea used to feel visible and powerful in her first company.
The text uses "would implement" correctly because "implement" is an action verb, not a state.
The final line "I grew up professionally" uses Simple Past — it refers to a completed process of professional growth.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Grammar hunt: used to (aff) = commute, have, feel, report to, deal with, manage, focus, used to feel x2. Used to neg = didn't use to be, didn't use to, didn't use to have, didn't use to take. Would = would give, would implement, would negotiate. Simple Past = joined, changed, merged, moved, restructured, switched, grew up.
  • Collocation count: 18 M1 vocabulary words highlighted in the text. Student can find them without highlighting in Student Mode — it's a collocation/vocab hunt activity.
  • RQ1 answer logic: "would give feedback" = action verb → would is correct. "The company would have a rigid hierarchy" would be WRONG because "have" here is a state — the text uses "used to have" correctly.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Andrea started this module in S1 — now she closes it in S5. Her story has 4 grammar forms, 18 vocabulary words, and 6 collocations. Can you tell her full story from memory — without reading?"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Your §7 Exit Task will follow the same structure as Andrea's text — but about YOUR life and YOUR company. The grammar is the same. The vocabulary is the same. Only the content changes."

GT Originals — M1 Exit
M1 in one sentence: "I used to [habit] — then I [Simple Past event] — now I [present reality]." This 3-part structure is the A2.2 exit competency and the foundation for all B1 narrative production.

The M1 → M2 bridge: "I used to [past habit]" connects naturally to "I'm going to [future plan]" — the same personal narrative structure, oriented forward instead of backward. Students who master M1 are significantly more prepared for M2 production.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Teacher marks each skill IN REAL TIME during the presentation — not after. Click the skill the moment it appears in the student's speech. The student can see the bar filling if the screen is visible to them (or keep it teacher-facing only).
  • If the student finishes under 3 minutes: don't stop. Ask "What used to be the biggest challenge in your COMPANY (not just your role)?" — forces them to continue and use company transformation vocabulary from S3.
  • If all 7 skills are reached before 3 minutes: let the confetti fire and then ask "One more thing — what would you like your career to look like in 5 years?" This bridges M1 (past) to M2 (future).
