S2 — Pitch Round
Defend Your Results
Two positions · past tense argumentation · Data-driven persuasion · 90 seconds each side
10 MIN
📚 Setup: Divide students into two groups. 3 minutes to prepare. Each side presents 90 seconds using past tense throughout. The audience (or teacher) tracks: how many action verbs? How many was/were? Who used more colloquial phrases from S4A?
SIDE A — Q3 Was a Strong Quarter
“Even though we missed one target, Q3 was overall our best quarter this year.”
  • “We achieved 97% on-time delivery — that was exceptional.”
  • “We improved defects by 22%. The result was significant.”
  • “We completed the ISO audit and signed two new contracts.”
  • “The team was motivated and the outcome was professional.”
SIDE B — Q3 Wasn’t Complete
“We didn’t achieve the most important target. Cost reduction wasn’t met — that was the main goal.”
  • “The cost reduction target wasn’t met. We only achieved 14%, not 20%.”
  • “The equipment failures weren’t planned — that was a management problem.”
  • “We didn’t complete the supplier audit. That was a key commitment.”
  • “The savings we built weren’t protected — the final number wasn’t enough.”

S3 — Relay
Tense Repair Workshop
New Visual · 5 broken presentation sentences · Identify the error · Fix and say the correct version
8 MIN
🔧 NEW VISUAL — Tense Repair Workshop
Each card shows a sentence from a real-sounding results presentation — with one or more tense errors. The underlined part is broken. Step 1: Say the correct version out loud. Step 2: Explain WHY it’s wrong. Step 3: Reveal to confirm.
Repair 1 — Action Verb Error
❌ “The team was improve delivery times by 15% in Q3.”
💬 What’s the error? Fix it and say the correct sentence. Then explain: why can’t we use “was” here?
✅ “The team improved delivery times by 15% in Q3.”
Why: “improve” is an action verb — use Simple Past directly. Never “was + base verb” for actions.
Repair 2 — Was / Were Agreement
❌ “There was many suppliers at the meeting, but the outcome were positive.”
💬 Two errors in one sentence. Find both, fix both, say the correct version.
✅ “There were many suppliers... the outcome was positive.”
Why: “many suppliers” = plural → were. “outcome” = singular → was.
Repair 3 — Negative Form Error
❌ “We didn’t achieved the cost reduction target. The result wasn’t positive.”
💬 One error, one correct form. Which is which? Fix only what’s wrong and explain why.
✅ “We didn’t achieve the target. The result wasn’t positive.”
Why: didn’t + BASE verb always. “wasn’t positive” is already correct — don’t touch it!
Repair 4 — Negative Question Error
❌ “Wasn’t the suppliers prepared for the audit? — No, they wasn’t.”
💬 Two errors: question form + short answer. Fix both. (Hint: think plural!)
✅ “Weren’t the suppliers prepared? — No, they weren’t.”
Why: “suppliers” = plural → weren’t in question AND in short answer.
Repair 5 — Triple Challenge ⭐
❌ “The data was showed that we didn’t reduced costs. The results was below target.”
💬 Three errors in one sentence. Find all three, fix all three, and say the corrected version perfectly. This is the exit task standard.
✅ “The data showed that we didn’t reduce costs. The results were below target.”
Fixes: showed (not “was showed”) · didn’t reduce (not “reduced”) · results were (plural).
⭐ Scoring: 2 points per repair completed correctly (find error + say correction). Bonus: +1 for each “why” explanation. Maximum 15 points. Add to leaderboard in S7.
S4 — Speed Round
Action or State? Speed Cards
10 cards · Action verb or was/were? · First correct answer wins the point
6 MIN
0
points
Card 1 of 10
“The team _____ its production target in Q3.” (action)
Action verb or was/were?
✅ achieved / exceeded / met — action verb (Simple Past), NOT “was achieved”
Card 2 of 10
“The client _____ very satisfied with the final delivery.” (state)
Action verb or was/were?
✅ was — state verb. “satisfied” = adjective describing a state, NOT an action that happened.
Card 3 of 10
“Sales _____ by 12% during the supplier strike.” (action)
declined or were declined?
✅ declined — action verb. “were declined” is passive (B1 level) — not used here.
Card 4 of 10
“The cost reduction results _____ below the 20% target.” (state)
was or were?
✅ were — “results” = plural noun → were. (If “the result” = singular → was.)
Card 5 of 10
“I _____ the Q3 findings to the board last Friday.” (action)
presented or was presented?
✅ presented — action verb. Subject “I” did the action directly.
Card 6 of 10
“The three milestones _____ all completed on time.” (state)
was or were?
✅ were — “three milestones” = plural → were. “Completed on time” is the state they were in.
Card 7 of 10
“We _____ the root cause of the equipment failure in September.” (action)
identified or was identify?
✅ identified — action verb (to identify → identified). Never “was + identify”.
Card 8 of 10
“_____ the delivery numbers above the Q2 target?” (question form)
Was or Were?
✅ Were — “the delivery numbers” = plural → Were. (Were the numbers above target? — Yes, they were.)
Card 9 of 10
“The data _____ that the team performed very well under difficult conditions.” (action)
showed or was showing?
✅ showed — Simple Past action verb. “was showing” would be Past Continuous (A2.2 level).
Card 10 of 10 ⭐ Challenge
“In summary, Q3 _____ strong. The team _____ its targets in three categories and _____ that results _____ possible even under difficult conditions.”
Fill all 4 blanks: was / achieved / showed / were
✅ Q3 was strong / achieved its targets / showed that results / were possible. Action + state combined!
S5 — Solo Challenge
Your Board Presentation
2-minute solo · Real or imaginary Q3 results · 4-step structure · Exit task rehearsal
8 MIN
Solo Challenge — 2 Minutes, No Notes
You are presenting Q3 results to your director. Use the 4-step structure from S4A: (1) Open with good news, (2) Present data, (3) Report a challenge, (4) Close with summary + recommendation. Real or imaginary numbers. No notes allowed.
  • Use at least 3 different action verbs (achieved, improved, reduced, implemented, identified...)
  • Use was/were at least 4 times — both positive states and negatives
  • Use at least 1 negative: didn’t + base verb AND 1 wasn’t/weren’t
  • Use 2 colloquial phrases: “I’ll start with the good news” / “As you can see” / “The key takeaway is” / “In summary”
  • Close with: “In summary, the data showed that…” and one recommendation
2m
Solo Challenge Timer — 2 minutes
Partner scores: 1pt each action verb + 1pt each correct was/were + 1pt each colloquial phrase. Target: 10+ points.
📚 Debrief: Partner gives exactly 3 pieces of feedback: (1) Count of action verbs, (2) Count of correct was/were, (3) Were both verb types mixed naturally? Then switch. This IS the S5 exit task format — treat it as a real rehearsal.

Real Presentation Language
4 authentic result communications · Spot action verbs + state verbs · Compare registers

💌 Executive Email Summary
“Q3 results: We achieved 97% OTD and reduced defects by 22%. The supplier partnership was effective. The cost reduction target wasn’t met (14% vs 20% goal). Overall Q3 was strong.”
❓ Underline action verbs and circle state verbs. How many of each? Is the balance natural? Could you write this email right now?
💬 WhatsApp Team Group
“Hey team! Q3 closed strong 🔥 We hit 97% OTD!!! Defects improved by 22% — best result this year. Costs were below target but overall it was a GREAT quarter. You were amazing!”
❓ Same data, different register. Find the action verbs and state verbs. How does informal writing change the balance? What would you change for a formal email?
📊 Slide Deck — Slide Title + Bullet Points
“Q3 Key Highlights: • Achieved 97% on-time delivery (best in 18 months) • Reduced defects by 22% after new protocol • Production volume was 8% above Q2 • ISO audit was completed on schedule”
❓ Bullet points mix action verbs and state verbs. Which are which? Why does professional slide language use both? What’s the rhythm?
👤 LinkedIn Post — Professional Achievement
“Proud of our Q3 results at Solaris. The team achieved exceptional delivery rates and reduced defects to a new record. The challenge was real, the effort was extraordinary, and the results were worth it.”
❓ This uses action verbs for achievements and state verbs for evaluation. Find each pair. How does the pattern “action verb → state verb” create professional rhythm in writing?
💡 Discussion question: “Which of these 4 formats do you use at work? Which would you like to improve in English first?” This is a natural upsell into A2.2 writing skills — emails, reports, LinkedIn.

3 Phrases to Use Before Your Next Meeting
PHRASE 1
“We achieved [X]. The result was [evaluation].”
The core professional results sentence. Action verb + state verb together. In WhatsApp, in a meeting, in an email — this is always correct and always professional.
PHRASE 2
“The key takeaway here is that [past result].”
Every presentation needs this sentence. It signals the most important point and uses past tense for the result. Your director will remember you said it — because it sounds like someone who prepared.
PHRASE 3
“We didn’t achieve [X], but the data showed that [Y] was possible.”
The professional way to report a missed target without sounding defensive. Honest + forward-looking + grammatically perfect. This is the phrase that gets you promoted.
