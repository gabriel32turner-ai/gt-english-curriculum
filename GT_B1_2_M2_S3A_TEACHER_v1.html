Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • S3 side hustles is the most emotionally loaded topic of M2 for corporate students. It touches real salary frustration, real ambition, and real fear. Both the ambition and the fear generate authentic RS context.
  • Note the student's initial position: necessity-driven (I need extra money) vs ambition-driven (I want to build something). This distinction feeds into §5 RS: "She told me she had always wanted to build something of her own" vs "He said he was doing it because his salary wasn't enough."

§ 02
Speaking Topic — Side Hustles
8–10 min
M2 · S3 — Money, Power & Choices
"Do you have a side hustle — or would you want one?
Necessity or ambition?"
First answer: no preparation, no vocabulary. Honest position right now. This topic generates real emotion — both the desire and the fear of going beyond a 9-to-5.
Push the conversation — 3 rounds
Round 1 — Your real situation: Do you have any income outside your main job? Have you ever tried? What happened — or what has stopped you? Be honest.
Round 2 — Reported Speech bridge: Think of someone you know with a side hustle — friend, colleague, family. What did they tell you about it? What did they say about the challenges? What did they mention about the money? Report the real conversation.
Round 3 — The bigger picture: Is a side hustle a sign of ambition — or a symptom of a system where one salary is never enough? Report what you've heard people say about financial freedom in México. What do people around you believe?
B1.2 Model Answer — "Side Hustles" · 2.5–3 min · Natural & Real
"I don't have a side hustle right now, but I think about it constantly. My income covers everything — but there's nothing left at the end of the month. It adds up: the kids, the car, the rent. Not gonna happen on one salary forever.

A friend of mine told me she had been working on a freelance design platform for two years alongside her corporate job. She mentioned she had been waking up at 5 AM every day to work on it before her main job started. She said it wasn't about the money at first — it was about the opportunity to build something of her own. But then the revenue started growing and she told me she had been thinking about quitting her 9-to-5. She didn't. She said she was scared of losing stability.

That's the tension, right? The burnout is real — managing a job AND a passion project at the same time is exhausting. But I've also heard people say they've never felt more alive than when they started something for themselves. No way that feeling comes from a monthly paycheck alone.

I think a side hustle is both — necessity AND ambition. One salary isn't enough, and that frustration becomes fuel. The people who succeed aren't always the most talented — they're the ones who didn't wait for permission."
🏷 Colour key: teal = S3 vocab · cyan = Reported Speech tense shift · gold = colloquial phrases · Count: 11 vocab words, 7 RS uses (all with tense shift), 3 colloquial phrases
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Student speaks FIRST — no vocabulary. The goal is to extract raw RS material. "Someone told me they…" / "A friend said they wanted to…" — these are pre-loaded tense-shift opportunities. Don't formally correct yet; bank the sentences.
  • Round 2 is critical. "What did they tell you?" is a direct invitation to RS. Write the student's answer on the board in direct speech, then ask them to convert. "She said 'I want to launch something' → she said she wanted to launch something." Real conversion, real sentence.
  • The model answer uses 7 RS sentences — all with tense shift. Point these out after revealing: "told me she had been working / mentioned she had been waking up / said it wasn't / told me she had been thinking / said she was". These are the target structures for §5.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • If the student has a real side hustle: "Perfect. You're going to report on your own experience using RS. That's advanced B1.2 territory — your own story, reported from a neutral distance."
  • If the student has no side hustle: "Even better — the tension between wanting one and not having one is exactly the emotional material that generates the best language."

§ 04
Colloquial — M2 Phrases in Context
5–7 min
"No way"
Expressing shock, disbelief, or strong refusal
"She mentioned she had left her director role to sell handmade jewellery. No way — and yet she was making more money."
"That's sketchy"
Something feels dishonest, unethical, or suspicious
"He told me about a ‘passive income’ scheme online. That's sketchy — it sounded like a pyramid."
"I'm broke"
Informal: having no money right now
"She said she was starting the hustle because she was basically broke after paying rent every month."
"It adds up"
Small amounts accumulate into something significant
"He mentioned he had been charging just $500 per client — but it adds up: 10 clients a month is a second salary."
"Not gonna happen"
Something is very unlikely or impossible — emphatic refusal
"She told me she had asked HR for a 30% raise. They said: ‘Not gonna happen this quarter.’ That's when she decided to launch her own thing."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • "Not gonna happen" is the most powerful colloquial phrase for S3 — it captures the frustration that drives most side hustles. Ask the student to use it in a real RS sentence: "She told me her manager had said her raise was not gonna happen this year. That's why she launched the hustle."
  • "It adds up" generates excellent RS practice when combined with numbers: "He mentioned that $200 per client sounded small, but it adds up." Encourage students to use real numbers from their industry.
§ 05
Grammar — Reported Speech: Tense Shift
10–12 min
Reported Speech — Tense Shift (said / told / mentioned / asked)
Core rule: When reporting what someone said in the past, tenses shift one step back.
Present → Past · Past → Past Perfect · Will → Would · Am/Is/Are → Was/Were · Have done → Had done
Direct Speech
"I'm building something."
→
he said he was building something.
"I will quit soon."
→
she said she would quit soon.
"I've launched my hustle."
→
he told me he had launched his hustle.
"I've been working on it."
→
she mentioned she had been working on it.
Key Reporting Verbs
said
→
no indirect object needed · she said (that)
told
→
ALWAYS needs object · she told ME (that)
mentioned
→
casual, no urgency · he mentioned (that)
asked
→
questions only · she asked if/whether
⚠️ Top Errors — Correct Every Time
✗ "She said me she was launching"→✅ She TOLD me she was launching (told + object)
said ≠ told. "Said me" does not exist in English.
✗ "He told me he is building"→✅ He told me he WAS building (tense shift)
Present → Past when the reporting verb is past tense.
✗ "She mentioned that she will quit"→✅ She mentioned she WOULD quit
Will → Would in reported speech.

Two Colleagues — "Are You Still Doing That Thing on the Side?"
B1.2 · M2 · S3A · ElevenLabs · Voice: Emma (British) + James (American) · Speed 0.88 · Natural dialogue

📎 ElevenLabs Audio Placeholder
Script available below · Voice: Emma + James · Speed 0.88 · Conversational · B1.2 M2 S3A

▶ Show ElevenLabs Script
JAMES: "Hey Emma, are you still doing that thing on the side? The design work?"

EMMA: "Yeah, actually — it's grown a lot. I told my partner last month that I'd scaled to six clients. I didn't expect that."

JAMES: "No way. Are you thinking about quitting here?"

EMMA: "I mentioned it to my partner — I said I was seriously considering it. But then there's the stability thing. He asked me if I was sure I could replace the full income. I said I wasn't sure yet."

JAMES: "I get it. I tried something a while back — a platform for freelance translations. I told everyone I was going to launch it. Then the burnout hit. Managing both was — not gonna happen long-term."

EMMA: "That's the thing. Someone told me once — they said the side hustle doesn't fail because of the idea. It fails because people don't manage their energy. And they're right. The schedule is brutal."

JAMES: "So what's keeping you going?"

EMMA: "The revenue. I mean — it adds up. And honestly, it's the first time work has felt like mine. That's something a salary can't give you."
📎 ElevenLabs: Emma = British English, calm · James = American English, casual · Speed 0.88 · 90-second dialogue · No background music · B1.2 M2 S3A

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This dialogue is dense with RS — count the reporting instances with the student after listening: "I told my partner that / I mentioned it / I said I was considering / He asked me if / I told everyone / Someone told me — they said." At least 7 RS uses in 90 seconds. That's the B1.2 RS density target.
  • After comprehension: "Replay only the Emma-partner exchange. Stop after 'He asked me if I was sure I could replace the full income.' Now — what did she say? Report it back in your own words." Live RS production from listening material.

Talk About It — Now With All the Tools
Use vocabulary from §3, RS from §5, colloquial from §4. Answer each question in full, natural sentences. Minimum 60 seconds per question.
Q1 — Personal RS
Think of someone you know who has — or had — a side hustle. Report what they told you about it. What did they say? What did they mention about the challenges?
Use: told me / mentioned / said + tense shift · vocab: burnout / revenue / manage / scale
Q2 — Hypothetical RS
Imagine you told a friend: "I just launched my side hustle and my first client paid me $5,000 pesos." How would they react? Report their imagined response using RS.
Use: they would say / they would ask if / they would mention · colloquial: No way / It adds up
Q3 — The Big Picture
Is the rise of side hustles a symptom of a broken economic system — or proof of people's ambition? Report what experts, friends, or colleagues have said about this. Add your own position.
Use: mentioned / said / told + full tense shift · passion project / opportunity / platform
