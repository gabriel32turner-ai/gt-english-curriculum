§1

⚡ Warm-Up — Quick Fire

5 min
📍 Another Monday morning… ¿Y tú? What does YOUR morning look like?
🎯 Objetivo del Warm-Up: Activar vocabulario de rutinas y tiempo. Sin corrección de errores aquí — fluidez y confianza primero.
Dinámica: Teacher→Student (ronda 1), luego Student→Student (ronda 2 si hay tiempo).
Nota: Apunta respuestas interesantes en el board para recycling en §7.
Q1 T→S
What time do you start work?
Q2 T→S
What do you do first in the morning at work?
Q3 S→S
Do you have meetings every day?
Q4 S→S
What time do you finish work?
Layer 1 — Escucha activa: ¿Usan "at" antes de la hora? ("at 8", "at noon")
Layer 2 — Error frecuente: "I start at the 8" → no article. Corrección gentil, no inmediata.
Layer 3 — Extension: Si la respuesta es corta, pregunta: "And what day is your BUSIEST day?"
§2

🗣️ Speaking Topic

8 min
Talking about YOUR schedule — the most useful thing you can do in English at work

My Work Schedule

Talk about your typical workday: times, activities, and responsibilities.

Round 1 · 60 seg
T→S "Tell me about your typical workday. What time do you start?"
Round 2 · 60 seg
S→S "Ask your partner: what do you do on Mondays?"
Round 3 · 90 seg
Class "Who has the BUSIEST schedule? Let's compare."
Model Answer

"My workday starts at seven in the morning. First, I check my emails — that usually takes about 30 minutes. Then I have a team meeting at nine. In the afternoon, I work on reports and talk to our suppliers. My day is pretty full, but I take a break at noon. I finish at five — usually!"
■ Vocabulary ■ Simple Present ■ Colloquial
Coaching tip: En Round 1, si el alumno para antes de 30 seg → prompt: "And then? What's next in your day?" Nunca corrijas mid-utterance. Espera al final y recicla 1 frase con la forma correcta.

§4

💬 Colloquial Phrases

5 min
Real phrases — the ones native speakers actually say at work
I'm pretty swamped.
When: you have too much work
"I can't have lunch today — I'm pretty swamped. I have three deadlines this week."
Let me check my schedule.
When: someone asks for a meeting
"Thursday? Let me check my schedule. I usually have a team meeting at nine."
I work the morning shift.
When: explaining your schedule
"I work the morning shift, so I start at six and finish at two."
It's pretty full-on.
When: your day is intense
"Mondays are crazy — meetings, reports, calls. It's pretty full-on."
Práctica rápida: Di el contexto en español → el alumno produce la frase en inglés. Ejemplo: "Tu colega te invita a comer el martes pero estás saturado. ¿Qué dices?" → "I'm pretty swamped."
Gramática integrada: Las frases usan Simple Present — mismo target que §5. El alumno lo practica sin notarlo.
§5

🎯 Grammar — Simple Present + Time Preps

10 min
Grammar is a tool — use it to describe YOUR schedule, not to memorize rules
REGLA DE ORO §5: Esta sección es REFERENCIAL, no drilling. Lee la tabla con el alumno, da un ejemplo personal tuyo, luego activa el Poll. Nunca pidas que conjuguen verbos fuera de contexto.
Structure Form Work Example
I / You / We / They I start / we have I start work at 7. We have a meeting on Fridays.
He / She / It she starts / he checks She starts at 8. He checks his email first.
Negative I don't / she doesn't I don't work on Sundays. She doesn't have breaks.
Question Do you…? / Does she…? Do you have overtime? Does he start early?
⏰ Prepositions of Time
AT at 7, at noon, at night I start at seven in the morning.
ON on Monday, on Fridays I have meetings on Tuesdays.
IN in the morning/afternoon I send reports in the afternoon.

🎧 Listening — A Day at the Plant

8 min
📍 Planta en Celaya — Rodrigo habla con su equipo sobre los turnos
🎙️
ElevenLabs Audio — "Rodrigo's Workday"
Duration: ~1:10 · Industrial context · Bajío setting
NARRATOR

Hi. My name is Rodrigo. I work at a manufacturing plant in Celaya. Every day, I start work at six in the morning. That's early, I know!


RODRIGO

First, I check the production report — that takes about 20 minutes. Then, at eight, I have a team meeting with my operators. We talk about the shift goals.


RODRIGO (cont.)

At noon, I take a lunch break — only 30 minutes. In the afternoon, I send my report to the plant manager. I usually finish at three. But on Fridays? I don't finish until five. It's pretty full-on!

Pre-listening: "You're going to hear about Rodrigo. He works in a plant in Celaya — like many of you. Listen for: what time does he start? What does he do first?"
Post-listening: Pide al estudiante que reconstruya la rutina de Rodrigo de memoria, sin ver el script.
Multiple Choice
1. What time does Rodrigo start work?
2. What does he do FIRST in the morning?
True / False
3. Rodrigo has a 1-hour lunch break.
4. He usually finishes at 3 PM, but not on Fridays.
5. His team meeting is at 7 AM.
