⚡ Warm-Up — Quick Fire

5 min
📍 Getting from A to B in English — directions are a survival skill
🎯 Objetivo: Activar experiencias reales de travel y directions. Muchos alumnos del Bajío viajan a CDMX, Monterrey o al aeropuerto de Guanajuato (BJX). Usar ese contexto.
Quick Win activado: Antes de las preguntas, el alumno dice una dirección en inglés. No importa si es perfecta — que hable.
⚡ Quick Win — 20 sec
Say this out loud right now: "Turn right, then go straight." That's your first direction in English!
Q1 T→S
How do you get to work? By car? By taxi? By Uber?
Q2 T→S
Did you ever get lost in another city? What happened?
Q3 S→S
How do you get from your house to the airport? How long does it take?
Q4 S→S
A client calls you from the airport. They need directions to your office. What is the first thing you say?
Layer 1: ¿Dicen "by taxi" o "in a taxi"? Ambos suenan a A1.2 — ambos están bien.
Layer 2: Si dicen "I go straight" → push: "How far? How many blocks?"
Layer 3: "What if the client doesn't speak Spanish? What do you do?"

🗣️ Speaking — Getting from the Airport

8 min
A client just landed at BJX Airport. How do they get to your office?

From the Airport to Our Office

A supplier from Chicago just arrived at Guanajuato Airport (BJX). They need directions to your office in Celaya. How do you help them?

Round 1 · 60 seg
T→S "How do you get from BJX Airport to your office? What transport do you use?"
Round 2 · 60 seg
S→S "Give your partner directions from the office reception to the meeting room floor by floor."
Round 3 · 90 seg
Class "Do you prefer to travel by plane or by car for business? How long does each take?"
Model Answer

"From BJX Airport, take a taxi to Celaya — it's about 45 minutes. It's not far. Go through the exit and take the highway. Turn right at the first traffic lights. Go straight for about 2 kilometres. Our office is on the left, past the Oxxo. Call me when you arrive — I'll come down to the lobby. Have a good trip!"
■ Vocabulary■ Prepositions/Directions■ Colloquial
Coaching: Si el alumno da solo "take a taxi" → "Great! Then what? Where does the client go? Turn left or right? How long?" Push for complete directions.

Every word you need at the airport — in English
terminal

delay

§4

💬 Colloquial — Travel Phrases

5 min
The phrases that make travel conversations feel natural
It's not far.
When: reassuring someone about distance
"Is it far from the airport? — It's not far. About 30 minutes by taxi."
Have a good trip!
When: saying goodbye before travel
"Your flight is at 8. Have a good trip! See you next Monday."
You can't miss it.
When: a place is very easy to find
"Turn right at the traffic lights. Our building is the tall blue one. You can't miss it."
I'm on my way.
When: you are already travelling to somewhere
"Are you here yet? — Not yet. I'm on my way. Five minutes."
Práctica rápida: Teacher da el contexto → alumno produce la frase. "Tu cliente dice que el edificio está lejos. ¿Qué le dices?" → "It's not far!" / "Tu cliente va al aeropuerto. ¿Qué le dices?" → "Have a good trip!"

🎯 Grammar — Directions & Movement

10 min
Turn left. Go straight. Go through the gate. — Essential for every business trip
REFERENCIAL. Las preposiciones de movimiento y los imperativos de dirección son vocabulario en contexto, no gramática abstracta. Muestra la tabla, da un ejemplo real del camino del aeropuerto a la oficina, activa el Poll.
Tip: Pide al alumno que dibuje el mapa mientras da las instrucciones — comprensión kinestésica.
🗺️ Prepositions of Movement
➡️
to
moving toward a destination
"Take a taxi to the office."
⬅️
from
the starting point of movement
"How long from the airport?"
🚶
through
entering and exiting a place
"Go through customs."
🏃
past
passing a landmark without stopping
"Go past the Oxxo."
🛣️
along
following a road or path
"Go along the main road."
🌉
across
from one side to the other
"Walk across the bridge."

Activity B — Correct the Direction 0 / 4

One word is wrong. Choose the correct replacement.

"Go right at the traffic lights."
"Go" isn't right here. What should it be?
"Take a taxi from the hotel."
You want to go TO the hotel, not leave it.
"Go along customs and show your passport."
You enter customs, not follow it.
"How far does it take by taxi? — About 40 minutes."
We're talking about time, not distance.

🎧 Listening — Mónica Gives Directions

8 min
📍 Mónica Herrera calls a supplier arriving at BJX Airport
🎙️
ElevenLabs — "From BJX to ConnectPro"
Voice: Sarah · Speed: 0.80 · A1.2 M3 S2A
Hi, Mr. Davis! Welcome to Mexico. Are you at the airport now?

Great. Listen — it's easy. From the airport, go through the arrivals exit and take a taxi. Tell him: ConnectPro International in Celaya. It's about 45 minutes from the airport — it's not far.

When you arrive in Celaya, the taxi will take the highway. Go along the highway and take the second exit. Turn right at the first traffic lights. Go straight for about two kilometres. Go past a big Oxxo on your left. Our building is right after it. You can't miss it.

Call me when you arrive at the lobby. I'll come down and meet you. My number is 461 555 0192. Send it to me in a WhatsApp message if you need it again.

Oh — and don't worry about baggage claim. I have someone from our office there now to help you. Call him if you have any problems — his name is Roberto.

OK? Have a good trip! See you soon.
📎 ElevenLabs: Voice — Sarah · Speed 0.80 · A1.2 M3 S2A
