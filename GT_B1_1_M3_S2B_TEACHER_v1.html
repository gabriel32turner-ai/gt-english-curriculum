🔥 Hot Take — Cold Start

5 min
Rodrigo opens his statement. The number is 3x what he expected. 60 seconds. GO.
Cold start: Alumno habla de Rodrigo — o de sí mismo. Escucha First Conditional natural: "If he keeps doing this, he'll…". Si se bloquea: "Should he cancel the apps? What will happen if he doesn't?"
🔥 Hot Take — 60 sec
Rodrigo just checked his credit card. He spent 3x his normal amount this month — all online shopping. He says "I don't know how it happened." You have 60 seconds. What are the reasons? What will happen if he doesn't change?

§2

🎯 Pitch — Assigned Sides

10 min
The data is clear — but is it the platforms' fault or ours?
Topic: "Online shopping platforms are designed to make us spend more — and it's not fair." Teacher asigna lados. Push para First Conditional en los argumentos: "If they remove countdown timers, people will spend less." / "If you don't control yourself, no app can help you."
🟣 Side A — PLATFORMS ARE GUILTY
"Online platforms manipulate us with dark design. It's their responsibility." Defend this.
Starters: "If they ban countdown timers, people will make better decisions…" "If the algorithm didn't target your insecurities, you wouldn't buy so much…" "If prices were always honest, we'd trust the platform more…"
🔵 Side B — PERSONAL RESPONSIBILITY
"We can't blame the apps — adults control their own choices." Counter the argument.
Starters: "If you set a monthly budget, you'll stick to it regardless of the design…" "If people take 24 hours before buying, they'll avoid most impulse purchases…" "If you delete the app, the algorithm can't reach you…"

🔄 Relay — The 24-Hour Rule Simulator

8 min
New feature: The product is real. The pressure is real. Buy or wait?
Nueva mecánica — 24-Hour Rule Simulator: El alumno ve un producto con precio, countdown timer, y badges de escasez. Teacher dice "10 seconds — buy or wait?" Alumno responde con First Conditional: "If I buy it now, I'll…" / "If I wait, I'll…". Luego cambia de escenario con los botones de abajo. El widget fuerza producción espontánea bajo presión simulada — idéntica a la que siente online.
Después del simulator: 4 transformaciones escritas de First Conditional.

🎙️ Now Transform — Teacher reads, student speaks
Transform 1 — Invert the clause
"If the price drops, I'll buy it."
→ Say the same idea with the result clause first.
"I'll buy it if the price drops." — Both are correct. Same meaning, different emphasis.
Transform 2 — Use UNLESS
"If it doesn't have good reviews, I won't buy it."
→ Replace "if not" with "unless". Say the new sentence.
"Unless it has good reviews, I won't buy it." — unless = if not. Use it to sound more natural at B1.
Transform 3 — Fix the error
"If Rodrigo will delete the apps, he'll save money."
→ There is a mistake. Say the correct version.
"If Rodrigo deletes the apps, he'll save money." — NO will in the if-clause. Present Simple only.
Transform 4 — Make it negative
"If I set a budget, I'll spend less."
→ Make both clauses negative. What does the reverse rule sound like?
"If I don't set a budget, I won't spend less." (= I'll spend more) — Double negative but fully correct.
Transform 5 — Your real rule
"Think about the last time you spent too much online."
→ Create 3 personal First Conditional rules that would have prevented it.
Open — "If I [had done X], I'll [do Y next time]." / "I won't buy [X] if [condition]." / "Unless [condition], I'll always [rule]."

🔍 Source Analysis — First Conditional in the Wild

8 min
Real texts. Real First Conditional. Find it, analyze it, improve it.
Dinámica: 4 fuentes reales: push notification de app, email de banco, review de producto, grammar detective. Cada una tiene una tarea diferente. Source 4 es metacognitiva — el alumno detecta cuántos First Conditionals hay y si son todos correctos.
📱 App Push Notification — MercadoLibre
"🔥 Last chance! If you complete your purchase in the next 30 minutes, you'll receive free same-day delivery. Don't miss this — if you wait until tomorrow, this offer won't be available."
❓ This notification uses First Conditional twice. What does the platform WANT you to feel?
📧 Email from Rodrigo's Bank — BBVA
"Hi Rodrigo, we noticed your spending increased 280% this month vs. last month. If this pattern continues, you'll exceed your monthly credit limit next week. If you'd like to review your spending categories, you can access your dashboard here. If you need to speak with an advisor, we'll connect you within 24 hours."
❓ The bank uses First Conditional three times. Which one is a WARNING, not an offer?
⭐ Product Review — Amazon MX
"★★★★☆ Great headphones BUT: If you use them more than 3 hours a day, the ear cushions will start to wear out quickly. If the price drops below $2,500 MXN, I'll buy a second pair. If you're looking for deep bass, these will disappoint you."
❓ One of these "if" sentences is NOT a First Conditional. Which one, and why?
🧠 Grammar Detective — Find the Error
"My new rule: A) If I will see a countdown timer, I'll close the app immediately. B) If the review score is below 4.0, I won't buy it. C) Unless I've used my monthly budget, I'll allow one impulse buy. D) If I wait 24 hours and I still want it, I will buy it."
❓ Only ONE of these four rules has a grammar error. Which one?
