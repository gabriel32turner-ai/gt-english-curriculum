Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • "recharge" appears as both noun and verb — point this out. "I need a recharge" (noun) vs "I need to recharge" (verb). Both are natural in professional English.
  • "speak up" — phrasal verb. Common error: "speak up yourself" → "speak up" (no reflexive). "I've learned to speak up" — note: this is PP + infinitive, not PP + gerund.
  • "drain" — powerful, specific word. At B1.1, students often say "it tires me" or "it's exhausting." Push: "Can you use 'drain'? 'That meeting drained me.' 'I've noticed that X drains me.'"
  • Production: "Pick 3 verbs — make a PP sentence about your WORK LIFE with each. All 3 must use 'I've + noticed/found/learned/always.'"

§ 04
Grammar — PP of Observation
"I've found that…" / "I've noticed…" / "I've had to adapt…" — patterns of self-awareness
04
10–12 min
🔭
S4 Grammar Focus — PP of Observation & Discovery
👁
I've found that… (discovery)
"I've found that I work better in quiet environments."
"I've found that back-to-back meetings drain me by afternoon."
"I've found that writing helps me clarify my thinking before speaking."
💡
I've noticed… (pattern recognition)
"I've noticed I contribute more in small groups."
"I've noticed that I need preparation time before speaking in meetings."
"I've noticed that social events leave me more tired than physical work."
🔄
I've had to… (growth)
"I've had to learn to speak up earlier in meetings."
"I've had to push myself to network at industry events."
"I've had to adapt my communication style for extroverted managers."
⏱
I've always… / I've never… (consistent pattern)
"I've always preferred written communication over spontaneous discussion."
"I've never felt truly comfortable in open-plan offices."
"I've always recharged better in quiet environments."
💡 The S4 grammar insight: These PP patterns express self-knowledge gained through experience — not just facts. "I'm an introvert" (present simple = label). "I've found that I work better alone" (PP = lesson from lived experience). The PP version is significantly stronger in professional contexts because it shows evidence-based self-awareness, not just self-description.
⚠️ Common Errors — S4
❌ "I noticed that I prefer small groups."→✅ I've noticed that I prefer small groups. (ongoing pattern = PP, not Simple Past)
❌ "I've found that I am preferring small groups."→✅ I've found that I prefer small groups. (after PP, use simple present for the discovery)
❌ "I've had to learned to network."→✅ I've had to learn to network. ("had to" + base infinitive, not past participle)
❌ "I have always preferred to work alone since always."→✅ I've always preferred to work alone. ("always" = no additional time reference needed)
✏️ Activity 1 — Choose the Correct Form
Use data-correct pattern — select the correct PP observation form.
"___ that I work better alone — group brainstorming sessions exhaust me."
I ___ to speak up in meetings — it doesn't come naturally to me."
I ___ preferred small groups — I've always worked better that way."
Which sentence expresses a PP observation CORRECTLY?
✏️ Activity 2 — True or False: PP of Observation
Is this statement about the grammar correct?
"I've found that I prefer small groups" is stronger than "I prefer small groups" because it shows a lesson from experience, not just a label.
"I've had to learned" is correct because "had to" is past and needs a past form after it.
"I noticed that I prefer small groups" is wrong in this context because the pattern is ongoing — it needs Present Perfect.
"I've always preferred" and "I've always been preferring" have the same meaning in English.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Q2: "have had to learn" is PP of obligation ("had to"). "Had to learn" is Simple Past. "Have to learn" is present simple. At B1.1, the distinction between "I had to" (Simple Past, finished obligation) vs "I've had to" (PP, experience that informs now) is important.
  • Q4 trap: "am preferring" after "I've noticed that" — "prefer" is a stative verb, so it doesn't take continuous form. "I've noticed that I prefer" (PP + simple present for the discovered state).
  • T4: "I've always preferred" = correct. "I've always been preferring" = unnatural (stative verb). Stative verbs (prefer, like, believe, want) don't use continuous forms.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Oral production: I say a situation from the Energy Map — you produce the PP observation sentence. 'Large group meetings → ' What have you found?' 10 seconds."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "'I've found that…' / 'I've noticed…' are high-value language patterns in English coaching, leadership development, and performance conversations. They signal someone who observes themselves professionally."
§ 05
Reading — The Quiet Professional
An introvert reflects on thriving in an extrovert-rewarding workplace
05
8–10 min
Daniel Torres
Senior Analyst · Global Strategy Partners · Denver
LinkedIn Article
Watch for: PP of observation (orange) · "I've found / noticed / had to" patterns · energy/recharge vocabulary (highlighted)

I've worked in consulting for eleven years. For most of that time, I didn't fully understand why certain situations left me depleted while my colleagues seemed energised by the same things. I've learned — slowly — that it's about energy.

I've found that large group meetings are my biggest challenge. I can perform well in them, but they drain me. I've noticed that after a full day of interaction, I need at least an hour of quiet before I can think creatively again. My extroverted colleagues have told me they feel the opposite — they're energised by exactly those interactions.

I've had to learn to network more deliberately. Networking events drain me if I approach them without a plan. So I have developed a system: I arrive with two or three specific people I want to speak to, I set a time limit, and I leave without guilt. I've built strong professional relationships this way — just more intentionally than my extroverted peers.

The hardest thing? I've had to learn to speak up more in meetings. As an introvert, I process internally — by the time I'm ready to contribute, the conversation has moved on. I have practised entering conversations earlier — even when my thoughts aren't fully formed. It's uncomfortable. But I've learned that being heard matters as much as being right.

What I've never done is apologise for my preference for quieter settings. I've found that my focus, my attention to detail, and my ability to listen deeply are valued in every team I've been part of — once people understand how I work best. Self-awareness, I've learned, is the introvert's superpower.

✏️ Comprehension — PP of Observation
Answer using information from Daniel's article.
What has Daniel found drains him the most at work?
What system has Daniel developed for networking events?
"I've had to learn to speak up more" — this shows Daniel has developed this skill through experience, not that it comes naturally.
Daniel says his introversion has always been a professional disadvantage that he has failed to overcome.
"I've never apologised for my preference for quieter settings" — this PP negative shows a consistent personal choice across his whole career.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Count PP observation patterns: "I've found" (3×), "I've noticed" (1×), "I've had to learn" (2×), "I've learned" (3×), "I've developed" (1×), "I've built" (1×), "I've practised" (1×). Total: ~13 PP instances. Ask student to find them before comprehension questions.
  • RQ2 tests reading precision: the networking system has 3 specific elements (target people + time limit + leave without guilt). All 3 must be in the answer for ✓.
  • After comprehension: "Tell me YOUR version of Daniel's article — replace his introvert examples with YOUR energy patterns from the Energy Map. 2 minutes."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Daniel's last line: 'Self-awareness is the introvert's superpower.' Do you agree? What's the extrovert's equivalent? Give me your opinion with 'I've found that…'"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Daniel's article is the pro-introvert argument for §7's debate. The anti-introvert argument is that professional success requires visibility — and visibility requires extroversion. You'll defend one side."

🗣 Speaking Challenge — 🔥 Debate
Format: Debate · 2+ minutes · Present Perfect throughout

"Companies tend to reward extroverts more than introverts in professional settings." Do you agree? Take a position and defend it — using "I've found…" and "I've noticed…" with real examples.

✅ Agree — extroverts win
Visibility = promotions — "I've noticed that the people who get promoted are usually the ones who speak most in meetings."
Networking is valued — "I've found that building relationships requires social energy — introverts have to work harder at it."
Leadership = presence — "I've always seen extroverted leaders be perceived as more credible, even when they're not always right."
🔄 Disagree — skills over style
Results matter more — "I've found that companies that reward only visibility lose their best deep thinkers."
Introverts lead differently — "I've noticed that the most effective leaders I've worked with are often the quietest ones."
Modern work = more introvert-friendly — "I've seen remote work shift the advantage — written communication is an introvert strength."
▶ Show B1.1 Model Answer (reveal AFTER student speaks)
"I agree that companies tend to reward extroverts more — but I've found that this is changing. I've noticed throughout my career that the people who get promoted fastest are usually the ones who are most visible — not necessarily the most effective. I've seen introverts do extraordinary work and receive less recognition simply because they didn't promote themselves loudly enough. However, I've also noticed a shift. Since remote work became normal, I've found that written communication — an introvert strength — has become more valued. I've never believed that extroversion equals leadership. The best managers I've worked with have listened more than they've spoken. So yes, the bias exists — but I've found it's not as absolute as it used to be."
