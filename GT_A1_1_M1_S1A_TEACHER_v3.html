§ 01
Warm-Up — Scenario
5–8 min
🎬 Scenario — Your Workplace, Day 1
Imagine: It is Monday morning. You are at your desk. A new person walks in. They smile and say:
"Hi! I am David. I am the new sales manager. Nice to meet you!"
👉 What do you say? — Try it. Any words. Any attempt. It is all correct right now.
Expected student output at A1.1: A smile, "Hi," one or two words. Maybe "My name... Carlos?" or just "Hello!" Accept everything. Zero correction at this moment. The goal is activation, not accuracy.
Teacher Follow-Up Questions (say these after student's first attempt)
"What is YOUR name? Tell David your name."
"Are you happy to meet him? Say it: 'Nice to meet you too!'"
"Where are you from? Try it: 'I am from…' — just the city."
"What is your job? Try: 'I am a…' — just the job title."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Accept all of these as valid responses: "Hello" / "Hi" / "My name Carlos" / "I am Carlos" / "Carlos" / silence with a smile / "Hola" (laugh and accept). All are valid starting points.
  • If student says "My name Carlos" → do a recast: "Oh! Your name IS Carlos. Very nice!" Do NOT say "that's wrong." Just model the correct form with enthusiasm.
  • If student says "I from Mexico" → recast: "Oh! You ARE from Mexico! Very nice!" Same technique.
  • DO NOT drill grammar yet. This is activation. Student will discover "to be" in §4. For now, just let them try.
  • Timing: keep this at 5 minutes max. Move to vocabulary quickly. Energy is everything at A1.1.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You just said your first sentence in English! That is amazing. In 55 minutes, you will say it perfectly."
  • Match their energy. If they are nervous, slow down and smile more. If they are excited, match that energy.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Plant this seed: "Everything you try to say today — you will say perfectly in your next class." No pressure. No upsell yet. Just a gentle promise.

§ 04
Grammar — Verb "to be"
15–18 min
The Tool: In English, verb "to be" = am / is / are. Use it to say WHO you are, WHERE you are from, and WHAT you are. It is the most important verb in English.
PronounAffirmative (+)Negative (−)Question (?)Short Answer
I I am / I'm
"I am Carlos."		 I am not / I'm not
"I'm not the manager."		 Am I late? Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.
You You are / You're
"You are the new analyst."		 You are not / You aren't
"You aren't from Mexico City."		 Are you new here? Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.
He / She / It He is / He's
"She is the HR director."		 He is not / He isn't
"He isn't from Japan."		 Is she Mexican? Yes, she is. / No, she isn't.
We We are / We're
"We are a great team."		 We are not / We aren't
"We aren't late today."		 Are we in the right room? Yes, we are. / No, we aren't.
They They are / They're
"They are from Canada."		 They are not / They aren't
"They aren't in the office."		 Are they new colleagues? Yes, they are. / No, they aren't.
⚠ Common Errors — Hispanohablantes & Verb "to be"
✗ "I am from Mexican."→✓ "I am Mexican." / "I am from Mexico."
Nationality = adjective only. "Mexican" not "from Mexican."
✗ "She is engineer."→✓ "She is AN engineer."
Article "a/an" is required before job titles.
✗ "I from Mexico."→✓ "I AM from Mexico."
Never drop "am/is/are" — the verb is always there in English.
✗ "Yes, I tired."→✓ "Yes, I AM tired."
Short answers need the verb: "Yes, I am." / "No, I'm not."
✗ "Are you tired? — Yes, I'm."→✓ "Yes, I am." (Never "I'm" alone in short answer.)
Contraction only in negatives: "No, I'm not." Never "Yes, I'm."

      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Before the activities, read the grammar table together. Pronounce each form: "I am / you are / he is / she is / it is / we are / they are." Student repeats each. Minimum 2 passes.
      • The "am/is/are" choice is the only grammar challenge. For MC: eliminate by pronoun. "My name ___" = "My name IS" because "name" = third person singular.
      • Activity 2 — common confusion: students think "You is" might be correct. It is not. Reinforce: "I = am, You/We/They = are, He/She/It = is." Write on the board if needed.
      • Activity 3 — drag & drop: help students by saying "Questions always start with the verb in English — Is / Are / Am come FIRST." This is a key discovery moment.
      • Timing flex: if student is fast, do a quick oral drill — you say a pronoun, student says the form: "I → am! You → are! She → is!" Repeat x5 with speed increasing.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Look at that table. You now KNOW the entire verb 'to be.' This took native speakers years. You just learned it in 5 minutes. That is the power of a live class."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "In S2, you will use 'to be' + more vocabulary. Everything in this table stays with you for life."

      § SP
      Speaking Practice
      10–12 min
      🗣 Guided Speaking — Pair / Teacher-Led Discussion
      Who Are You? — Introduction Practice
      Answer each question using the verb "to be" (am / is / are). Aim for 2–3 sentences per answer.
      Question 1
      "What is your name and where are you from?"
      Hint: My name is ___. I am from ___.
      Question 2
      "What is your job? What company are you from?"
      Hint: I am a/an ___. My company is ___.
      Question 3
      "Is your manager Mexican or American? Is your team big?"
      Hint: My manager is ___. My team is big / small.
      Question 4
      "Tell me about a colleague. What is their name and job?"
      Hint: My colleague is ___. He/She is a/an ___.
      Question 5
      "Are you happy at your company? Why?"
      Hint: Yes, I am happy because ___. / My company is very ___.
      ⏱ 10–12 min total 📋 5 questions 🎯 Min. 5 uses of am / is / are 👥 Teacher-led
      ⚡ Speaking Challenge — Closing
      "You have 60 seconds. Introduce yourself completely — name, city, job, company, one thing about your team. No template. Go."
      Teacher counts uses of "to be" silently. A1.1 success = 4+ correct uses in 60 seconds. After the challenge: name the count aloud and celebrate. One correction maximum — the most important one only.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • This is the production moment of S1A. Questions 1–2 are warm-up for speaking — student already knows this content. Questions 3–5 require slight creative effort, which is intentional.
      • Common errors at A1.1: dropping "is" ("My manager Mexican"), using "are" for 3rd person singular ("My company are"), saying "I'm" in a short answer ("Yes, I'm" → recast to "Yes, I am").
      • Correction protocol: NEVER stop mid-sentence. Wait for the sentence to end, then recast once. Celebrate immediately after the correction: "Exactly — IS. Say it again."
      • Q3 is the discovery question — most students haven't thought about their manager's nationality in English. Use this to generate genuine communication, not just pattern drilling.
      • Speaking Challenge: 60 seconds is attainable for A1.1. If student finishes in 30 seconds: "Great. Now add 2 more sentences about your team." Push for density, not speed.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "You just answered 5 questions in English. Real questions. Real answers. About YOUR real life. That is what this program is."
      • After the challenge: "I counted ___ uses of 'to be.' You needed 4. You got ___. That is today's score."
      • "In class 1 you said 'Hi.' Right now you answered 5 real questions. 60 minutes of English — and this is what it looks like."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "In S1B — your next class — you do this same thing with NO template and NO help. Free production. You are already closer than you think."
      • Gentle preview only at A1.1. No upsell, no testimonial trigger — student is still in the first confidence-building phase.
