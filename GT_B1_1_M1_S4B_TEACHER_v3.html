GT Originals — Energy Map → Debate Evidence
El Energy Map no tiene respuestas correctas — todas las respuestas son válidas en diferentes situaciones. El punto es la justificación PP: "I've always found that…" / "I've been in situations where…" / "I've learned that…". Marca solo la calidad del PP, no la elección de energía. Los resultados del Energy Map se convierten directamente en la evidencia del debate en §5. Nota qué situaciones marca el alumno como agotadoras — esas se convierten en sus ejemplos para "Companies reward extroverts."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Marca ✓ PP Correct solo cuando el alumno produce una oración PP completa. "I don't like meetings" = no PP. "I've found that back-to-back meetings drain my concentration by afternoon" = ✓.
  • Los alumnos que marcan 4-5 como "draining" en la mayoría de situaciones → argumento pro-introvert en §5. Los que marcan 4-5 como "energizing" → argumento pro-extrovert. Ambos son igualmente válidos y generan debate rico.
  • Si el alumno puntúa 5-6 correctos: está listo para el debate sin scaffold. Si puntúa 2-3: necesita drilling extra en §4 antes del debate libre.
Capa 2 · Engagement

§ 04
Debate Preparation — Build Your Argument
10 min
Your Mission for §5
Defend ONE position on the debate statement: "Companies reward extroverts more than introverts."
2 minutes. No notes. Every argument must be backed by a PP observation from your own career.
Use: "I've found that…" / "I've noticed that…" / "I've always seen…" — minimum 4 PP uses.
The teacher will argue the opposite position. Defend your view.
✅ Agree — Companies DO reward extroverts
"I've found that visibility matters more than effectiveness in my company — and extroverts are naturally more visible."
"I've noticed that the people who get promoted fastest are the ones who speak up most — not necessarily the most effective."
"I've had to work on my visibility because I've found that quiet, consistent performance doesn't get noticed enough."
"I've always seen that extroverted managers tend to build larger networks — and networks drive promotions."
🤔 Disagree — It's more nuanced than that
"I've found that this is changing — since remote work became normal, I've noticed that written communication — an introvert strength — has become more valued."
"I've noticed that the best managers I've worked with have listened more than they've spoken."
"I've had to learn to be visible — but I've found that strategic visibility (less but higher quality) works better than constant presence."
"I've never believed that extroversion equals leadership — I've seen too many counterexamples in my career."
▶ Show B1.1 Model Debate Answer (reveal AFTER student speaks)
"I agree that companies tend to reward extroverts more — but I've found that this is changing. I've noticed throughout my career that the people who get promoted fastest are usually the ones who are most visible — not necessarily the most effective. I've seen introverts do extraordinary work and receive less recognition simply because they didn't promote themselves loudly enough. However, I've also noticed a shift. Since remote work became normal, I've found that written communication — an introvert strength — has become more valued. I've never believed that extroversion equals leadership. The best managers I've worked with have listened more than they've spoken. So yes, the bias exists — but I've found it's not as absolute as it used to be."

📣 Debate Phrases — Haz clic para escuchar

"I've found that…"
"I've found that visibility matters more than effectiveness in my company."
"I've noticed that…"
"I've noticed that the people promoted fastest are the most visible."
"I've always seen…"
"I've always seen that extroverted managers build larger networks."
"I've had to adapt…"
"I've had to adapt my style to be more visible — even though it's not natural."
"I've never believed that…"
"I've never believed that extroversion equals leadership ability."
"That's really important to me."
"Fairness in how performance is recognized — that's really important to me."
"I've seen introverts…"
"I've seen introverts do extraordinary work with less recognition."
"For me, it's all about…"
"For me, it's all about the quality of your contribution — not how loud you are."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Debate cards: dale al alumno 3 minutos para elegir su posición y elegir 2-3 argumentos. El punto no es memorizar los argumentos — es internalizar la estructura: PP observation + specific example + implication.
  • Respuesta modelo: revela DESPUÉS del debate del alumno. Compara: "¿Qué patrones PP usó el modelo que tú no usaste? ¿Cuál argumentó más eficazmente?"
  • Mínimo B1.1 para §5: 4+ PP observations + argumento coherente + lenguaje de conexión (but / however / though). Si el alumno solo da labels ("extroverts win") → empuja: "Have you ever seen that? Tell me a specific situation — 'I've noticed that…'"
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Elige tu posición en 30 segundos. I'll argue the opposite — genuinely. Use your Energy Map results as evidence. Go."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "This debate structure — PP observation as argument → specific evidence → implication — is exactly how senior professionals defend positions in English. Not opinions alone: observations from experience."
§ 05
Debate Free — Your Position, Your Evidence
15 min
Take a position. 2 minutes. No notes. Defend: "Companies reward extroverts more than introverts — Agree or Disagree?" Back every argument with a PP observation from your own career. The teacher will challenge you. Start with: "I've found that…"
Teacher Challenge Prompts — Contra-argumentos para presionar al alumno
"Have you ever seen an introvert outperform an extrovert in a visible role? What happened?"
"I've noticed that remote work has changed everything — have you found the same?"
"What about deep work and focus? Haven't introverts found an advantage there?"
"Have you ever had to speak up in a meeting when you weren't ready — what did you notice?"
"I've always believed that visibility is a learnable skill — have you found that too?"
"What have you found about the relationship between confidence and effectiveness in your company?"
"Have you ever noticed a difference between how introverted and extroverted managers are perceived?"
"I've found that the best listeners are the most trusted — do you agree?"
Self-Evaluation — Después del debate
  • I used Present Perfect observation at least 4 times correctly
  • Every argument was backed by a PP observation (not just an opinion)
  • I used at least one "I've found that…" and one "I've noticed that…"
  • I defended my position for 60+ seconds without stopping
  • I responded to at least one challenge from the teacher with PP evidence
  • I used Energy Map results as specific evidence
  • I used at least one debate phrase from §4
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Toma la posición opuesta — genuinamente. Si dice "extroverts win" → desafía con el punto de remote work. Si dice "no, it's nuanced" → desafía con un ejemplo concreto de visibilidad. El objetivo es que el alumno use PP observations como respuesta, no solo opiniones.
  • Evalúa: ¿cada argumento tiene PP observation? ¿Es específico o genérico? ¿Usó la evidencia del Energy Map? ¿Respondió a los challenges con más PP?
  • Feedback post-debate: (1) nombra el argumento más fuerte con su PP específico, (2) señala un lugar donde podría haber usado PP observation en lugar de simple present, (3) pide que reformulen ese argumento.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You just defended a professional position in English — with evidence from your own career — for 2 minutes. That's a skill most native speakers struggle with. You did it."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In S5A you'll use ALL M1 vocabulary — personality, values, strengths, energy — to narrate a real conflict situation. Come ready with a real story."
