Preguntas de seguimiento para el maestro

Capa 1 · Pedagogical

  • Estándar mínimo §1: 4+ oraciones con irregular past sin ayuda. Si no llega → usa las follow-up questions para scaffoldear.

didn't + BASE FORM
✅ "I didn't go" ❌ "I didn't went"

PAST?

PAST?

gave — "She gave a presentation to the board."
ERROR?
"I didn't went."

COMPLETE

PAST?
write
wrote — "He wrote the follow-up report on Friday."
DURATION

took — "The trip TOOK two days." (How long did it TAKE?)
PAST?

"She gived feedback."
✅ "She GAVE feedback." — give is irregular!
WH-Q
"___ long did the negotiation take?"
How — "How long did it take? It took 3 hours."
CHUNK
"Long story ___…"
short — "Long story short, we made a deal."
PAST?
find
found — "We found a solution to the delay."
CHUNK
"In the ___, we…"
end — "In the end, we made an agreement." — storytelling chunk!

Listening — Client Visit Monologue
8–10 min
🎙
Monologue: "How the Client Visit Really Went"
Speaker: Alejandra Vega, Account Manager · Duration: ~90 seconds · A2.1 · Normal-fast pace

Genera con ElevenLabs voz Rachel o Nova · Conversational storytelling · Normal-fast pace · No music
Archivo: GT_A2.1_M2_S1B_Listening_v1.mp3

▶ Show ElevenLabs Script
MONOLOGUE — FASTER THAN S1A, STORYTELLING TONE

"OK so let me tell you about the client visit. It was last Thursday — I went to their office in León. I took the 7 AM bus and got there by 9.

I met the operations director, Mr. Fuentes. He gave me a tour of the plant — it was impressive. Then we had a meeting with his team. Four people. I gave a short product overview — about 20 minutes. They asked a lot of questions. Good sign.

After the meeting, we spoke about pricing. They didn't make a decision on the spot — they said they needed a week. But I felt positive. They found our product interesting.

I sent a follow-up email that same evening. I wrote a summary of everything we discussed and left the door open for a second meeting. How long did the whole visit take? About six hours, door to door. Better than expected."

§ 05
Role Play — Business Story Interview
12–15 min
Role Play 1 — Guided · 5 min
🎤 The Post-Event Interview
Maestro = manager o colega que pregunta sobre el evento. Alumno = profesional que acaba de volver de un viaje, conferencia o visita de cliente. El alumno usa el scaffold de su columna.
🎧 Maestro — Manager / Colleague
"So, how did it go? Tell me everything."
"Where did you go exactly? How long did it take to get there?"
"Who did you meet? What did they say?"
"Did you give a presentation? How did it go?"
"What decision did they make? Did you make a deal?"
"Did anything go wrong? What didn't happen as planned?"
"Long story short — was it worth the trip?"
🗣 Alumno — Professional returning from event
"So, what happened was… I went to [place] [time]."
"The trip took about [time]. I took a [transport]."
"I met [person]. We had a [type of meeting]."
"I gave a presentation. It went really well / didn't go as planned."
"We made / didn't make a decision. They sent a proposal."
"One challenge: they didn't [base verb] because [reason]."
"In the end, the outcome was [result]. Better than expected."
Role Play 2 — Free · 7 min · Sin scaffold
📋 Your Real Story — No Script, No Net
El alumno elige UN evento real de su historia laboral: un viaje, una conferencia, una visita de cliente, una negociación. El maestro hace el rol del manager o colega curioso. El alumno narra sin leer, usando solo los verbos que tiene internalizados.
🎯 Free Role Play Rules
El alumno debe usar en la narrativa: 5+ irregular verbs distintos · 1 negative con didn't + base form · 1 Wh- question (el maestro la hace, el alumno la contesta con estructura correcta) · Los chunks: "So, what happened was…" + "In the end, we…" · Una expresión de duración: "It took [time]."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • RP2: dale 2 minutos de silencio para que el alumno elija su historia. Luego "OK, I'm your manager. How did the trip go?" — sin aviso previo.
  • NO interrumpas durante la narrativa. Al final: 1 corrección de irregular verb (si hubo) + 1 celebración específica. Solo 1 de cada.
  • Estándar A2.1: 5+ irregular verbs correctos + 1 negative correcto + storytelling coherente = mastery de S1B §5.
  • Este RP es el momento de mayor valor de la sesión. Protege 12 minutos.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Acabas de contar una historia de trabajo real en inglés — con estructura, con verbos irregulares, con negativas correctas. Eso es lo que hace un profesional bilingüe."
  • Celebración específica: menciona UNO de los verbos que salió natural. "Escuché 'I gave' sin pensarlo. Eso ya es tuyo."
Capa 3 · Retención & Upsell
  • "En M3 aprendes a chairing meetings y dar presentaciones estructuradas — usando was/were para describir resultados. Todo lo que hiciste hoy es el fundamento."
§ 06
Debate — Was It Worth the Trip?
8–10 min
💬 Opinion Debate · A2.1 Level
In-person visits vs. virtual meetings — which is better for business?
"Carlos went to Monterrey for 3 days and came back with a potential deal — but no signature. Alejandra went to León for 6 hours and left the door open. Did they make the right decision to go in person? Was it worth it?"
✅ Yes — in-person was worth it
"They met the director in person — that built real trust."
"She gave a product demo in the plant — impossible online."
"He found 3 potential suppliers and spoke to 2 distributors."
"The client said yes after she went — not before."
⚠️ Maybe not — virtual could work
"They didn't sign anything — they went but didn't close the deal."
"The trip took 6 hours — they could have had 3 virtual calls."
"He didn't make a decision either — just got business cards."
"Companies sent proposals by email anyway — no need to travel."
💡 Maestro: toma la posición contraria. Mínimo 4 turnos de debate real. Objetivo: el alumno usa verbos irregulares EN CONTEXTO ARGUMENTATIVO — "They went / didn't sign / made / gave / spoke" — no solo en respuestas.
Preguntas adicionales para grupos o si sobra tiempo
"Think about your last business trip or client visit. Was it worth it? What did you get from it? Use: 'I went / I met / I found / In the end, we…'"
"Your company has budget for 1 business trip next month. Where do you go? Who do you meet? What do you want to achieve? Justify with irregular past AND future logic."
"What's the best business event you ever attended? What happened? Who did you speak to? What did you find there?"
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • El debate es la prueba de internalización. Si el alumno usa "went / gave / met / spoke" argumentativamente — sin buscarlos — los verbos están internalizados.
  • Sé un buen opponent — no te rindas fácil. El alumno tiene que esforzarse para sostener su posición.
  • Corrección post-debate: 1 error de irregular verb + 1 celebración. Nunca más de 1 de cada.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Acabas de debatir estrategia corporativa en inglés — usando past tense naturalmente en argumentos. Eso no es A2. Eso es la mentalidad de un hablante."
Capa 3 · Retención & Upsell
  • Testimonial trigger — MANDATORIO: "¿Cuál se sintió más difícil — S1A o S1B? ¿Cuál fue más real? ¿En cuál usaste más tu propio inglés?" — Deja que responda completamente. Esta reflexión es la que el alumno comparte con colegas.
  • "S2A de M2 trae más irregular verbs + narrativas más largas + Wh- questions en conversación con cliente. Cada sesión construye sobre la anterior."
  • Upsell: "Cada vez que vayas a una junta, visita o conferencia — tienes el inglés para reportarlo después. Eso es valor profesional inmediato."
