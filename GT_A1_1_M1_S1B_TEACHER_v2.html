Teacher Follow-Up — Push for More
"Is he happy? Is she tired? Is the office big?"
"Are they from Mexico? Are they new colleagues?"
"What is his name? Make one up! It is your story."
"Now describe Photo 2 also — 2 sentences only."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This is S1B — student should be warmer and more willing to attempt than in S1A. If hesitant: "You know this. You learned it last class. Any sentence is a win."
  • Minimum acceptable output: "He is the manager. She is from Mexico. They are a team." — 3 sentences with is/are = full warm-up success at A1.1.
  • Common error: "They is colleagues" → recast without stopping: "Yes! They ARE colleagues. Great idea!" Never interrupt the flow.
  • Strictly 5 minutes. The value of S1B is in §4 and §5 — get there without rushing but without losing time here.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Last time you needed the grammar table. Today you are doing it from memory. That IS progress. That IS your English."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In 50 minutes, you will introduce yourself — without any notes — to a new American colleague. Let's build up to that."

Comprehension — Multiple Choice
Listen carefully. Choose the correct answer for each question.
Where is David from?
What is Marta's job?
Is it David's first day?
Comprehension — True or False?
Is each statement True or False based on the dialogue?
Marta is from Monterrey.
David is the new operations coordinator.
Marta is American.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Two-voice dialogue is harder than one voice. Switching helps students adapt to real conversation rhythm — critical at A1.1.
  • Listen 1: global — "Who are these people?" (names, nationalities). Listen 2: detail — answer questions. Listen 3 optional: student shadows one character.
  • MC trap: David = sales manager, Marta = operations coordinator. Very common swap error. Confirm after: "David IS the sales manager. Marta IS the coordinator."
  • T/F 3: Marta is Mexican, not American. Easy but deliberate — builds confidence by catching the obvious one.
  • After comprehension: "You are David. I am Marta. Let's do this conversation. Start with 'Hi, I am David.'" Point to §4 scaffold if needed.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You just followed a real conversation between two people — in English. On your second class ever. Think about that."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention

Sounds like: sii yu tumóro

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Step 1: 2 minutes silent reading — student fills in blanks mentally, NOT in writing. Oral prep only.
  • Step 2: student reads template aloud once while you correct pronunciation only (not grammar here).
  • Step 3: student does it again with template covered. This is the bridge to §5 free production.
  • Phrases: listen to all 8, then ask: "Which 3 are most useful for YOUR job?" Student uses each in a sentence.
  • Most common error here: "I am a engineer" → correct to "an engineer" (a/an before vowel sounds). Address this now so it doesn't appear in §5.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Count the uses of 'to be' in your template: 6 times. Six times in 7 sentences. That is your first professional English introduction. It is real. It is you."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In S2A you will say MORE about yourself — your daily routine, your team, your schedule. Today: who you are. S2: what you do every day."
§ 05
Speaking Free — The Real Introduction
15 min
🗣 Role-Play — No Template Allowed
"You are at your company. A new American colleague named Alex walks up to you and says: 'Hi! I am Alex. I am from Austin, Texas. It is very nice to meet you!' — It is your turn. Go."
Introduce yourself completely. Use "to be" at least 6 times. No looking at the template. Teacher listens, counts uses of "to be" silently, and asks the follow-up questions below after the introduction.
10 Teacher Follow-Up Questions — Ask one at a time, after introduction
1."What is your name again? Is it [wrong name]?"
2."Where are you from exactly? Is it a big city?"
3."What is your job? Is it a management role?"
4."How old are you? Is that a secret?"
5."Are you happy at your company?"
6."Is your boss American or Mexican?"
7."Are you new at the company?"
8."Is your team big or small?"
9."Are we colleagues now?"
10."Is English important for your job?"
Self-Evaluation — After the Role-Play
  • I said my name correctly: "My name is ___."
  • I said where I am from: "I am from ___, Mexico."
  • I said my job: "I am a / an ___."
  • I answered at least 3 follow-up questions.
  • I used "to be" at least 6 times total.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This is the CORE section of S1B. Protect it — minimum 10 minutes of actual speaking time. Do NOT cut it short for any other section.
  • A1.1 mastery standard: 4–6 sentences with "am/is/are" correct in at least 4 of 6 uses. That is full success for day 2.
  • Follow-up questions: use questions 1–5 minimum. Add 6–10 if student is flowing. Stop if student is clearly maxed — do not push to frustration.
  • If student freezes on a follow-up: give two options: "Just say 'Yes, I am.' or 'No, I'm not.' — which one?" Scaffolded support is appropriate at A1.1.
  • Correction protocol: NO stopping the flow. Use recasts only during. After the full role-play: identify 1 main correction and 1 celebration. Keep the balance positive.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "That was a real English conversation. You just had it. With no notes. That is your real English level — and it is only class number two."
  • Count uses of "to be" aloud: "I counted 7. You only needed 6. You exceeded the goal on your second class."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Your 'to be' foundation is solid. Everything in A1.1 — every session — builds on exactly what you did in these last 15 minutes."
  • Gentle preview: "In S2A you add vocabulary for your daily work routine. Same grammar foundation, bigger vocabulary. That is the next step."

GT Originals — Mastery Tips
🏆 Mastery check: student introduces themselves in 30 seconds with no template and uses am/is/are correctly 5+ times. That IS A1.1 Module 1 mastery.
💡 Exit ticket: "Say your name, your job, your company — one sentence each. Go." Three sentences. That is the S1B exit standard at A1.1.
💡 Between sessions: encourage student to say their introduction out loud once a day — alone, in the car, in the shower. Not writing. Oral rehearsal only. Not homework. Mental activation.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Two-round Flashcard Wall: keep energy high. Round 1 = grammar. Round 2 = phrases. Student calls the answer before you flip each card.
  • Final challenge: this should feel noticeably more relaxed and confident than §5. If it does — celebrate it explicitly. If student still struggles — reassure: "You are better than 60 minutes ago. That is the only measure that matters."
  • Exit ticket: "Three sentences — your name, job, company. Go." This is the A1.1 M1 S1B exit standard. Confirm it before closing the session.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Two sessions. Zero to introducing yourself professionally in English — no notes, no translation. That is Gabriel Turner English Training."
  • "Remember S1A? You said 'Hi.' Today you said: 'My name is ___, I am from ___, I am a/an ___, my company is ___.' That is YOUR English."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "S2A adds new vocabulary for your daily work context — same 'to be' foundation, much bigger voice. That is the next step."
  • Gentle preview only. No upsell, no testimonial prompts at A1.1 — student is still building confidence. That moment comes at A2+.
