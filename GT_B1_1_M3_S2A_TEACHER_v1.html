⚡ Warm-Up — Quick Fire

5 min
📍 You open your phone at 11PM. The cart has 7 items. What happens next?
⚡ Quick Win — 20 sec
Say this out loud: "If I see free shipping, I'll add one more item to my cart." — Real. Specific. First Conditional.
🎯 Objetivo: Activar el tema de online shopping con experiencias reales. El First Conditional emerge naturalmente aquí: "If I find a discount, I'll buy it." Escucha — no corrijas.
Data point: El mexicano promedio hace 3-4 compras online por mes (Statista 2024). Hot Dot, Mercado Libre, Amazon MX — todos los alumnos los conocen.
Q1 T→S
How often do you buy things online? What was your last purchase?
Q2 T→S
Have you ever bought something online that disappointed you? Did you return it?
Q3 S→S
What makes you click "Buy now" — price? Reviews? Free shipping? Urgency?
Q4 S→S
If online shopping didn't exist, how would your spending habits change?
Layer 1: ¿Usan First Conditional naturalmente? ("If I get the email, I'll click") → nota para §5
Layer 2: Present Perfect: "I've ordered…", "I've never returned…" — conecta con M3 S1 grammar
Layer 3: "What's the most impulsive thing you've ever bought online?"

🗣️ Speaking — Online Shopping Behavior

8 min
Does online shopping make you spend more — or are you in control?

Does Online Shopping Make You Spend More?

Rodrigo says he spends 40% more since he started shopping online. Is that your experience? What triggers your spending?

Round 1 · 60 seg
T→S "Describe your online shopping behavior. Are you impulsive or careful?"
Round 2 · 90 seg
S→S "Partner A: describe a shopping regret. Partner B: give advice using First Conditional."
Round 3 · 90 seg
Class "If online stores removed countdown timers, would people spend less? Debate."
Model Answer

"I used to think I was careful, but I've realised that I browse a lot more than I actually need. I've returned quite a few things. The problem is the algorithm — if it shows me something I've looked at before, I'll click almost automatically. I bought it without thinking and then felt bad. Now I try to wait 24 hours. If I still want it the next day, I'll buy it. That's my rule."

12 words that describe how we really shop online
cart
/kɑːrt/
Your online shopping basket
"I have 6 things in my cart but I haven't paid yet."
checkout
/ˈtʃɛkaʊt/
The payment process at end of shopping
"If you enter the code at checkout, you'll get 15% off."
review
/rɪˈvjuː/
A customer's written opinion of a product
"I always read reviews before I buy anything expensive."
refund
/ˈriːfʌnd/
Money returned after cancelling a purchase
"If the product is damaged, I'll request a refund."
delivery
/dɪˈlɪvəri/
The process of receiving your order at home
"If I order before 2 PM, I'll get same-day delivery."
discount
/ˈdɪskaʊnt/
A reduction in the original price
"If there's no discount, I won't buy it."
algorithm
/ˈælɡərɪðəm/
Code that shows you products based on your behavior
"The algorithm knows what I want before I do."
subscription
/səbˈskrɪpʃən/
A recurring payment for a service or product
"If I cancel my subscription, I'll save $15 a month."
browse (v)
/braʊz/
To look at items without a specific goal
"I browsed for 30 minutes and bought nothing."
compare (v)
/kəmˈpɛər/

"If you compare prices, you'll always find a better deal."
regret (v)
/rɪˈɡrɛt/
To feel bad about something you did
"I regret buying it — it looked better online."
scroll (v)
/skroʊl/
To move through content on a screen

💬 Colloquial — How We Actually Talk About Shopping

5 min
The phrases that make you sound like a real English speaker — not a textbook
I bought it without thinking.
When: describing an impulsive purchase
"I bought it without thinking — I was tired and the ad was everywhere."
It's too easy to spend money.
When: talking about how online shopping removes friction
"One click and it's done. It's too easy to spend money this way."
I've returned a lot of things.
When: talking about the gap between expectation and reality
"The quality wasn't what I expected. I've returned a lot of things from that store."
I wait a day before buying.
When: describing your anti-impulse strategy
"I wait a day before buying — if I still want it tomorrow, I'll get it."
Drill: Teacher provides context → student produces phrase. "Compraste algo a las 2 AM y lo lamentaste." → "I bought it without thinking." / "Tu amigo dice que no puede ahorrar." → "It's too easy to spend money."

🎯 Grammar — First Conditional in Shopping Context

10 min
"If the sale ends in 10 minutes, I'll buy it now." — First Conditional in the wild
🧠 Shopping Psychology Triggers — First Conditional in Real Life
⏱ Countdown Timer
"Only 3 hours left!" — creates urgency
"If the timer reaches zero, I'll miss the deal."
📦 Scarcity Signal
"Only 2 left in stock" — triggers fear of missing out
"If I don't buy it now, it'll be gone tomorrow."
🚚 Free Shipping Threshold
"Add $5 more for free shipping" — makes you buy more
"If I add one more item, I'll get free shipping."
🤖 Algorithm Feed
"Customers also bought…" — you didn't plan to look
"If the algorithm shows me this, I'll probably click it."
REFERENCIAL. B1.1 grammar = reference + 2 activities. The triggers above are the "real-world problem". The table is the tool. Students have already seen First Conditional in S1 — this session goes deeper into the shopping context.
Error: "If I will see a discount" → If I see a discount. No will in the if-clause. Ever.
FormStructureShopping Example
Real condition → resultIf + Pres. Simple, will + inf.If I find a discount, I'll buy it immediately.
Negative resultIf + Pres. Simple, won't + inf.If there's no free shipping, I won't order it.
Inverted clauseWill + inf. + if + Pres. SimpleI'll wait until tomorrow if the price doesn't drop.
Yes/No questionWill + subject + inf. + if…?Will you buy it if it goes on sale?
⚠️ #1 ERROR — never will in the if-clause
✗ "If I will see a discount, I'll buy it."
✅ "If I see a discount, I'll buy it." — Present Simple in if-clause always.

🎧 Listening — Rodrigo's Shopping Problem

8 min
📍 Rodrigo realises his online spending has doubled — and he doesn't know why
🎙️
ElevenLabs — "Rodrigo's Shopping Problem"
Voice: James · Speed: 0.85 · B1.1 M3 S2A
I never used to have a problem with spending. I was actually quite disciplined. Then I started shopping online, and everything changed. Last month, I looked at my credit card statement and I nearly had a heart attack. I'd spent three times more than I usually do.

The problem is the apps. If I open any shopping app for more than five minutes, I'll almost certainly buy something. It's not even intentional. I'll just browse — and the next thing I know, I'm at checkout. The algorithm knows exactly what I want. If it shows me something I've looked at before, I'll click it automatically.

There was this pair of headphones. I looked at them twice. Then they started appearing everywhere — on Instagram, on the shopping apps, even in my email. And they had a countdown timer: "Sale ends in 3 hours." So I bought them. If the timer hadn't been there, I probably wouldn't have bought them at all. That's the thing — I know these tricks exist, but they still work on me.

My rule now is: if I want something, I'll wait 24 hours. If I still want it the next day, I'll buy it. And if I can't afford it without using my credit card, I won't buy it. It's simple, but it's the only thing that's actually worked.

Have I changed? A little. If I stick to the rule, I'll probably save a few hundred pesos a month. That's not nothing.
📎 ElevenLabs: Voice — James · Speed 0.85 · B1.1 M3 S2A
