§1

🔥 Hot Take — Cold Start

5 min
No warm-up. No notes. You speak — NOW. 🔥
🔥 Hot Take · 60 seconds · No prep
"My workday starts at ____. The first thing I do is ____. On Mondays I always ____. I don't like ____ because ____."
⏱ 60 sec — no stopping
🚫 No notes
✅ Use AT / ON / IN
🎯 Min. 5 sentences
Cold start — regla de oro: NO des contexto extra. El alumno habla directo al timer. Si para antes de 20 seg → no interrumpas, levanta la mano (gesto) para que continúe. Solo intervén al final.
Post Hot Take: Repite 1 frase del alumno con la corrección implícita: "Nice — so you start AT seven, right?" No corrijas explícitamente durante la producción.
§2

🎯 Pitch — Assigned Sides

10 min
You don't choose your side — you defend what you're given. That's real English.
Regla de sesión B: El alumno NO elige su lado — el teacher lo asigna. Esto rompe la zona de confort y obliga a producción real. Si el grupo tiene 2+ alumnos, asigna lados opuestos.
Topic: "It is better to work the morning shift than the night shift at a factory."
🟣 Side A — AGREE
"Morning shifts ARE better. Defend this position for 90 seconds."
Starters: "I think morning shifts are better because…" "You start early and finish early, so…" "In the morning, I don't have overtime…"
🔵 Side B — DISAGREE
"Night shifts are FINE. Counter the other side for 90 seconds."
Starters: "I don't think morning is always better because…" "At night, I have fewer meetings, so…" "My schedule is different — I start at…"
Después del debate: Pregunta al alumno: "What was ONE sentence you used that you're proud of?" → Refuerza positivamente antes de corregir. Luego señala UNA forma para mejorar.
§3

🔄 Relay — Transform It

8 min
No support. No grammar table. Just you and the language.
Instrucción al alumno: "I give you a sentence. You change it. No looking at notes. Speak out loud." Las transformaciones son las mismas estructuras de §5 de S4A — ahora en producción espontánea.
Dinámica: T→S — Teacher lee el prompt, alumno transforma. No hay opciones — producción libre.
Transform 1 — I → She
"I start work at 7 in the morning."
→ Say the same sentence about your female colleague.
"She starts work at 7 in the morning." (adds -s)
Transform 2 — Positive → Negative
"He checks his emails every morning."
→ Say what he does NOT do.
"He doesn't check his emails every morning." (doesn't + base verb)
Transform 3 — Statement → Question
"She has a meeting on Fridays."
→ Turn this into a yes/no question.
"Does she have a meeting on Fridays?" (Does + subject + base verb)
Transform 4 — Sentence → Your Reality
"Rodrigo takes a break at noon."
→ Say what YOU do at noon (real information).
Open answer — student uses "I take / I don't take / I have…" + real time expression.
Transform 5 — IN / ON / AT
"She works ___ the morning and has meetings ___ Mondays."
→ Complete with the correct preposition. Speak the full sentence.
"She works IN the morning and has meetings ON Mondays."

§5

🎤 Solo Challenge — 2 Minutes Straight

7 min
2 minutes. No stops. No help. This is YOUR English. 🎤
🎤 Solo Challenge · 2 minutes · Badge awarded
"You are training a new colleague. Explain YOUR work schedule to them — what you do, when you do it, and what they need to know about a typical week."
Use at least 3 different verbs (start, have, check, send, take…)
Use AT, ON, IN at least once each
Include one negative ("I don't work on…")
Include one question ("Do you have…?")
Use at least 1 colloquial phrase from Session A
Setup: "Pretend your new colleague is me. Go." — No dar más contexto.
Mientras habla: Toma nota de 2 cosas: (1) algo que dijo MUY bien para reforzar, (2) UN error recurrente para corregir después.
Badge: Se otorga automáticamente al completar el timer — no condicionado a perfección. El criterio es intentar los 2 minutos.
§6

🔍 Analyse — What Do They Mean?

8 min
Real workplace English — can you understand what they're actually saying?
Dinámica: El alumno lee cada cita, luego responde la pregunta de análisis en voz alta. No hay opciones correctas/incorrectas en las preguntas abiertas — en las cerradas sí. T→S para preguntas de análisis.
📧 Email from manager
"Please send the report before 3 PM. I have a meeting with the plant director at 4."
❓ What time does the manager need the report? What does she do at 4?
💬 WhatsApp from colleague
"I'm pretty swamped this week — 3 deadlines and overtime on Friday. Can we talk on Monday?"
❓ Is this person free this week? What do they want to do on Monday?
📋 HR notice
"The morning shift starts at 6 AM. The afternoon shift starts at 2 PM. Overtime is on Fridays only."
❓ How many shifts are there? When is overtime?
📞 Voicemail
"Hi, it's Lupita. I don't have the weekly report. Does Carlos send it on Mondays or Tuesdays?"
❓ What does Lupita need? What is she not sure about?
Extension para nivel alto: Después de cada cita → "Now YOU write a reply in English. 2 sentences." Si es clase grupal: debate sobre el significado antes de dar las opciones.
