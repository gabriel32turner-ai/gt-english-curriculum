Warm-Up — This or That
5–8 min

Think about your career 5–10 years ago. Choose your reality for each pair and explain. The goal: notice what has changed.

1 · Location
🏢 Fixed office — every day, same desk
Where was it?
OR
🏠 Flexible — remote or different locations
How often?
Where did you use to work before?
2 · Team
👤 Work alone — individual contributor
What type of work?
OR
👥 Manage a team — responsible for others
How many people?
Did you use to manage people before?
3 · Travel
✈️ Frequent travel — clients, meetings, trips
How many times a month?
OR
🖥️ Mostly online — everything virtual
What tools?
Did you use to travel a lot for work?
4 · Industry
🔄 Same industry as today
What's changed inside?
OR
🔀 Completely different sector
When did you switch?
Did you use to work in a different industry?
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Note how the student expresses the past — "before I work in…" / "I was work in…". These errors are exactly what "used to" fixes. Don't correct yet — save it for §4.
  • The warm-up questions already use "use to" in interrogative form. This is intentional pre-exposure. Don't explain the grammar here — just model it naturally.
  • If student says "I worked in sales for 3 years" — note it. You'll use it later to contrast with "I used to work in sales."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Pick the one that matches your past. Now tell me what changed — what do you do differently today? Don't worry about the form — just tell me the story."
  • Energy: this warm-up often opens personal stories. Let them run — it's authentic past language production.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "The 4 pairs above are the 4 most common career change topics in A2.2 speaking exams and real job interviews. You'll describe all 4 precisely by S3."

Vocabulary — Career Change Verbs
10–12 min
commute
/kəˈmjuːt/
To travel regularly between home and work
"I used to commute two hours every day — now I work from home."
manage
/ˈmænɪdʒ/
To be in charge of a team or project
"She didn't use to manage anyone — now she leads a team of 20."
travel
/ˈtrævəl/
To go to different places, often for work
"I used to travel every month for client visits."
work from home
/wɜːrk frəm hoʊm/
To do your job from your house, not the office
"We didn't use to work from home — now it's three days a week."
report to
/rɪˈpɔːrt tuː/
To be directly responsible to a specific manager
"I used to report to the VP of Finance — now I report to the CEO."
switch
/swɪtʃ/
To change from one thing to another
"She switched from marketing to operations in 2019."
deal with
/diːl wɪð/
To handle a situation, person or problem
"I used to deal with international clients every day."
grow up
/ɡroʊ ʌp/
To develop professionally; to take on more responsibility over time
"He grew up in a small startup — now he leads 200 people."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Each card shows "used to / didn't use to" in the example — natural pre-teaching. Ask student to read each example aloud and identify: is this affirmative or negative "used to"?
  • "report to" — phrasal verb with a person as object: "I used to report TO Daniel" (not "I used to report Daniel"). Common error at A2.2.
  • "grow up" at work — explain this is the professional version, not just for childhood. "She grew up in this company" = started as intern, now leads a department.
  • "switch" — always switch FROM something TO something. Drill: "I switched FROM ___ TO ___."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Which 3 of these 8 verbs best describe YOUR career so far? Use each one in a true sentence about yourself." — forces authentic personal production.
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "All 8 verbs appear in professional job interviews in English. 'What did you use to manage?' · 'Did you use to travel?' · 'How did you deal with X?' — These are real interview questions."

GT Originals — The False Cognate Trap
Spanish speakers say "I was used to work late" — translating "acostumbraba". This is wrong in English.
✅ "I used to work late" = habitual past action (ya no lo hago).
✅ "I'm used to working late" = I'm comfortable with it NOW (estoy acostumbrado a). Completely different structures. Teach them together so the student never confuses them.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Flip cards present the 3 contrast systems: back then/nowadays, not any more, used to/didn't use to. Each card ends with a ⚠ error note — read these aloud to the student.

  • For each BEFORE card: student must produce a "used to" sentence. Track: does the student use the correct form? (used to + BASE, not "I used to working")

§ 04
Grammar — Used To
12–15 min
Full System — Used To
FormStructureCareer example
AffirmativeSubject + used to + base verbI used to commute two hours every day.
NegativeSubject + didn't use to + base verbWe didn't use to work from home.
QuestionDid + subject + use to + base verb?Did you use to manage a team?
Short answerYes, I did. / No, I didn't."Did you use to travel?" — "Yes, I did — every month."
⏮ Then — used to
I used to commute 2 hours a day.
She used to manage 5 people.
I used to report to the CEO directly.
We didn't use to have video calls.
▶ Now — simple present
Now I work from home — no commute.
These days she manages a team of 30.
Nowadays I report to the regional director.
Now everything is online.
"Used to" vs Simple Past — the key difference:
"I worked in sales for 3 years." = past fact — could be a single period or job, no contrast implied.
"I used to work in sales." = repeated habit or state — and I DON'T any more. The contrast with the present is built in.
⚠ Most Common Errors at A2.2
❌ "I use to work in sales."→✅ I used to work in sales. (always "used" — past form)
❌ "I was used to work late."→✅ I used to work late. (not "was used to" — different meaning)
❌ "Did you used to travel?"→✅ Did you use to travel? (base form after "did")
❌ "I didn't used to commute."→✅ I didn't use to commute. (base form after "didn't")
Activity 1 — Choose the Correct Form
Click the correct option.
Before my promotion, I ___ in the accounting department.
We ___ have video calls — everything was face to face.
___ you ___ manage a bigger team before this role?
I don't travel for work ___ — I do everything online now.
"I used to work in sales" means I ___.
Activity 2 — True or False: Used To Rules
Is this statement about the grammar correct?
"I used to work in sales" implies that I don't work in sales now.
"Did you used to travel?" is the correct question form.
"I am used to working from home" and "I used to work from home" have the same meaning.
"Not any more" always goes at the END of the sentence, not the beginning.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • A2.2 grammar is referential — brief, then practice. Don't explain for more than 5 minutes. The table + error box is enough. The activities do the consolidation work.
  • T3 is the key distinction: "I am used to working" (familiar with, comfortable) vs "I used to work" (past habit). Write both on the board simultaneously. Drill in context.
  • Q5 tests conceptual understanding, not just form. This is A2.2 level — students need to understand WHAT "used to" communicates, not just produce the form.
  • After activities: oral drilling — teacher gives a present situation, student forms the "used to" contrast. "Now I work from home." → "I didn't use to work from home."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Speed round: I say a PRESENT sentence. You give me the PAST contrast with 'used to'. Ready? 'Now I manage a team of 20.'" → Student: "I didn't use to manage anyone."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "S2 expands the vocabulary of change: promotion, routine, deadline, colleague. Same 'used to' grammar — bigger, richer stories."
§ 05
Reading — My Career Journey
8–10 min
PLATFORM
LinkedIn Profile · Career Narrative
AUTHOR
Andrea Soto · Operations Director
Andrea Soto
Operations Director · TechFlow Solutions · CDMX
Watch for: used to · didn't use to · not any more · back then / nowadays · contrast with simple present

Ten years ago, my career looked very different.

I used to work as a junior analyst in a small accounting firm. Back then, I used to commute almost two hours every day. I used to report to a very strict manager who reviewed every single document. I didn't use to make any decisions independently — everything needed approval.

I also used to travel a lot for client visits — sometimes three times a month. I used to love it at first, but after two years, it was exhausting.

Things started to change when I switched to the tech industry in 2018. I left the accounting firm and joined TechFlow as a project coordinator. It was a completely different world.

Nowadays, I manage a team of 35 people across three countries. I work from home two days a week — something I didn't use to do at all. I don't commute any more, and I travel only twice a year.

The biggest change? Back then, I used to feel invisible. These days, I feel like I'm exactly where I should be.

Andrea Soto · Operations Director · TechFlow Solutions
Comprehension
Answer using information from the text.
How often did Andrea use to travel in her first job?
What changed in 2018?
Andrea used to make decisions independently in her first job.
Nowadays Andrea manages a team of 35 people across three countries.
Andrea still commutes two hours every day.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Color-coded text: orange = used to/didn't use to, red = didn't use to (negative), green = present contrast, gold = time expressions. Ask student to count: how many "used to" structures appear? (Answer: 7)
  • R1 trap: "twice a year" is Andrea's current travel frequency. Students who don't read carefully choose this. Teach: always locate WHEN — "in her first job" = past section.
  • After comprehension: "Tell me Andrea's story in your own words — without looking at the text. Use at least 3 'used to' sentences."
  • Then: "Now tell me YOUR story the same way." — direct transfer.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Andrea switched from accounting to tech. What's your 'switch' story? What changed — and when? Tell me with 'used to'."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "This LinkedIn narrative format is what international companies expect in an English interview when they say 'Tell me about yourself.' You'll practice it in §7 and master it by S3."

I started my career in manufacturing — a very different world from where I am now. Back then, I used to commute over an hour to a factory on the outskirts of Chicago. I used to work six days a week, and I didn't use to have any flexibility at all.

I didn't use to manage anyone for the first three years. I just worked on my own — analyzing data, writing reports, dealing with suppliers. And I used to love that, actually. It was very focused work.

  • After activities: student does a 60-second version of this audio about their own career. Same structure: THEN → CHANGE → NOW.

§ 07
Wrap-Up
5 min
Today I learned…
  • used to + base verb = past habit that no longer exists.
  • Negative: didn't use to (NOT "didn't used to").
  • Question: Did you use to…? (NOT "Did you used to").
  • "Not any more" goes at the end of the sentence.
  • I can contrast past habits with my current situation.
Key Summary
Affirmative
I used to commute · I used to manage · I used to travel
Negative
I didn't use to + BASE: work from home · manage anyone
Question
Did you use to + BASE? → Yes, I did / No, I didn't.
Contrast
I used to commute → I don't commute any more.
🗣 Speaking Challenge
Format: Debate

"Working from the office is better than working from home." Do you agree? Defend your position using "used to" to describe what work life was like before and after the change.

Teacher takes the opposite position and challenges. Each argument must include at least one "used to" structure. Minimum 2 minutes.

I used to love working in the office because… I didn't use to have any flexibility… Back then, I used to spend hours commuting… Nowadays I manage / deal with / travel… I don't [verb] any more, which means…
