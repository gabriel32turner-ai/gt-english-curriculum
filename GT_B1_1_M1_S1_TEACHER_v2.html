§ 01
Warm-Up — Scenario
5–8 min
The Situation
"It's your first week at a new international company. Your manager asks you to introduce yourself to the team in a group meeting — not just your name and role, but WHO you are as a professional."
Teacher: at B1.1 students need to move beyond describing facts — they need to interpret and reflect. Push for opinions + evidence. This session introduces Present Perfect for experience: "I've always been…" / "I've worked with…" Model the form naturally if students struggle.
1
In that meeting — one word. What word would you use to describe your work personality?
Just one word. Then: why that word? What have you done that shows it?
2
Leader, team player, or solo worker? Which one is most you — and has that changed over your career?
"I've always been…" / "I've found that I work better when…"
3
What kind of colleagues have you worked best with? What personality types complement yours?
"I've worked with people who…" / "The best team I've been part of…"
4
What's one thing your manager would say about you — good or not so good? Be honest.
B1.1 challenge: use evidence, not just labels. "He'd say I… because I've…"
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • This is diagnostic production. Note: does the student use Present Perfect spontaneously? ("I've worked with…" / "I've always been…") or do they default to Simple Past only? This determines how much time to spend in §4.
  • Q4 is the most complex: "my manager would say I… because I've…" — requires conditional + Present Perfect in one sentence. Very few A2.2 students do this naturally. Don't correct at this stage — observe and note.
  • At B1.1, the expectation is that students can produce opinions + evidence, not just state facts. If student only says "I am organized" — push: "How have you shown that at work? Have you ever led a project that required that?"
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "You're about to meet your team for the first time in English. 30 seconds. What do you say? Go — I'm timing you."
  • After the free attempt: "Now — same introduction but with at least 2 'I've' sentences. Ready?"
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In M1 you build the language to describe who you are professionally — in any context: job interviews, team introductions, performance reviews, networking events. By S5, your professional self-introduction will be fluent and compelling."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • All verb examples use Present Perfect deliberately — this is implicit grammar pre-teaching. Ask student to identify the PP form in each verb example before §4.

  • "influence" — B1.1 target question format: "Who has influenced your professional style the most?" — generates authentic discussion. Ask this after the verb card.
  • Production task: student makes 1 sentence per verb — must use Present Perfect. Target: 8 sentences in 4 minutes.

GT Originals — Self-Promotion in a Mexican Professional Context
Mexican corporate culture often rewards humility. In international English-speaking contexts, not describing your strengths reads as a lack of confidence, not modesty. The fix is framing: "I've always been good at…" / "People have told me that my strength is…" / "I've been recognized for…" — professional, factual, and not arrogant. This is the language of the Profile Builder above.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical

  • The 60-second profile reading at the end is the key production moment. Push student to use their actual words, not read from a script.

§ 04
Grammar — Present Perfect
12–15 min
Present Perfect — Three Core Uses at B1.1
UseStructureProfessional example
Experience
no specific time		 have/has + past participle I've led a team of 15. / She's worked in three countries. / I've never managed remotely.
Never / Ever
questions & negatives		 Have you ever + pp? / I've never + pp Have you ever led a cross-functional team? / I've never worked in finance.
Duration
started in past, still true		 have/has + pp + for / since I've worked here for three years. / She's been a manager since 2021.
Short answers Yes, I have. / No, I haven't. "Have you ever led a project?" — "Yes, I have — several times."
FOR vs SINCE — one rule:
FOR = a period of time → "for three years / for six months / for a long time"
SINCE = a specific point in time → "since 2021 / since January / since I joined / since I graduated"

Test: can you answer "How long?" → FOR. Can you answer "When did it start?" → SINCE.
Present Perfect vs Simple Past — the key contrast:
✅ "I've worked here for 3 years." (still working now → PP)
✅ "I worked there for 3 years." (not there now → Simple Past)
✅ "I've led a team." (experience, no time given → PP)
✅ "I led a team in 2019." (specific time → Simple Past)
⚠ Most Common Errors at B1.1
❌ "I have worked here since 3 years."→✅ I've worked here for 3 years. (period = for)
❌ "I never managed anyone."→✅ I've never managed anyone. (life experience = PP + never)
❌ "Have you ever led?" (no object)→✅ Have you ever led a team / a project? (verb needs object)
❌ "I have leaded the team."→✅ I've led the team. (irregular: lead → led → led)
❌ "I have never managed someone."→✅ I've never managed anyone. (negative = anyone, not someone)

Activity 2 — True or False: Present Perfect Rules
Is this statement about the grammar correct?
"I've worked here since three years" is wrong — it should be "for three years".
Present Perfect is used when the exact time is not mentioned or not important to the message.
"I worked here for 3 years" and "I've worked here for 3 years" have the same meaning.
"Have you ever led?" is incomplete — the verb needs an object: "led a team / a project / a department."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Q5 is the most important: "I've led this team in 2022" is wrong — specific year → Simple Past. "I've led this team for two years — and I still do" is correct. This distinction is the core of B1.1 PP teaching.
  • T3 is the key conceptual error: "worked" (not there now) vs "I've worked" (still there). These are NOT the same. Draw the timeline on paper if needed.
  • After activities: oral drilling — teacher gives a scenario ("You've been in your current role since 2020. Tell me using PP."), student produces the sentence. 5 scenarios, 20 seconds each.
  • B1.1 specifics: 2 activities max. Grammar is referential. If student is strong → move faster to §5 and spend more time on §7 role-play production.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Speed round: I give you a situation, you give me the PP sentence. 'You've been a manager for 4 years.' → 'I've been a manager for 4 years.' Go — one per 10 seconds."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "In M2, Present Perfect expands with 'just / already / yet / never' — the tools of precision. The foundation you build today makes that natural."
§ 05
Reading — Professional Profile
8–10 min
Rodrigo Serrano
Operations Manager · Vertex Industries · Monterrey
LinkedIn Profile
Watch for: Present Perfect (orange) · negative PP (red) · duration FOR/SINCE (gold) · S1 vocabulary (highlighted)

I'd describe myself as a steady, analytical character — the kind of professional who prefers to think before acting. I've always been more of a solo worker than a natural leader, but that has changed significantly over the years.

I've worked in operations for over twelve years, starting as a logistics analyst and eventually moving into management. I've been in my current role since 2020, when the company offered me the operations director position after a period of significant growth.

My main strength is analytical thinking — I 've built a reputation for solving complex problems methodically. I've never been the loudest voice in the room, but I've found that my style has earned the trust of my team over time. One of the things I've learned is that you don't need to be an extrovert to motivate people — you just need to be consistent.

My biggest weakness? I 've had to work on delegating. As someone who trusts process over people, I 've sometimes held on to tasks longer than I should. It's something I've been actively reflecting on — and I think I've improved significantly in the last two years.

The managers who have influenced me most have been patient, structured, and direct. I've never responded well to chaotic or impulsive leadership. I've learned that knowing your own mindset — and the mindsets of others — is one of the most valuable professional skills you can develop.

Comprehension — Present Perfect Focus
Answer using information from Rodrigo's profile.
How long has Rodrigo been in operations?
What has Rodrigo been working on improving?
Rodrigo describes himself as an extrovert who thrives in team meetings.
"I've been in my current role since 2020" uses SINCE correctly because 2020 is a specific point in time.
Rodrigo says you need to be an extrovert to motivate a team effectively.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Color-coded grammar: PP sentences (orange), negative PP (red), duration expressions (gold), vocabulary from S1 (yellow bg). Count PP instances: 18+ in this text. Point out the density — this is what B1.1 fluent English looks like.
  • RQ1 trap: "since 2020" is when he started his CURRENT role, not how long he's been in operations (12 years total). Students who skim will confuse these. Teach: always locate the specific question in the text.
  • After comprehension: "Tell me Rodrigo's story in your own words — without looking at the text. Use at least 4 Present Perfect sentences." Then: "Now tell me YOUR version."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Rodrigo says 'you don't need to be an extrovert to motivate people.' Do you agree? Give me your opinion + 2 present perfect examples from your own experience."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • "Rodrigo's profile is the model for §7's role-play. Your 'Tell me about yourself' answer should sound exactly like this — professional, honest, evidence-based."

Job Interview — Personality, Strengths & Experience
B1.1 · M1 · S1 · Dialogue · ~90 seconds · Natural interview pace · American English

🔈 Audio placeholder. Replace with Google Drive embed.
ElevenLabs voice: Sarah + Michael · Rate: 0.84 · Stability: 0.60

▶ Show ElevenLabs Script

Interviewer: So, tell me a bit about yourself — your personality, how you work, what you bring to a team.

Candidate: Sure. I'd describe myself as someone who is naturally collaborative — I'm definitely more of a team player than a solo worker. I've always been energised by working with people, exchanging ideas, and building towards something together. That's one of my core traits.

Interviewer: And what about your strengths?

Candidate: I've been told that my main strength is communication — particularly translating complex technical information into something non-technical people can understand. I've worked across several departments — engineering, marketing, and operations — and that's given me a very broad perspective. I've built relationships across the company that have been genuinely useful for cross-functional projects.

Interviewer: Any weaknesses?

Candidate: I'd say I've had to work on my ability to say no. As a team player, I've sometimes taken on more than I could realistically handle because I didn't want to let anyone down. I've been much better at setting boundaries in the last year or so, but it's something I'm still working on.

Interviewer: Have you ever led a team?

Candidate: Yes, I have. I've led a team of eight people for the last two years. It's been a challenging but incredibly rewarding experience. I've learned a lot about my own leadership style — and about myself.

⏱ Target: 85–95 sec · Two speakers or single narrator · Speed: 0.84 · Full of PP: I've been told · I've worked · I've built · I've had to · I've sometimes taken · I've been · I've led · I've learned

Listening Activities — Present Perfect Identification
Listen and identify. Focus on grammar, not just content.
What does the candidate identify as her main strength?
Which sentence does the candidate use? (Listen carefully to the verb form)
The candidate describes herself primarily as a solo worker.
"I've been much better at setting boundaries" — this uses Present Perfect correctly because the improvement started in the past and continues now.
The candidate says she has never worked in multiple departments.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Focus task: "Count the 'I've' sentences. How many do you hear?" Answer: I've always been · I've been told · I've worked · I've built · I've had to · I've sometimes taken · I've been · I've led · I've learned = 9 PP instances in 90 seconds.
  • LQ2 is critical for B1.1: student must identify the exact verb form — "I've led… for the last two years" (PP + for = still leading). Not Simple Past ("I led"), not continuous ("I'm leading since").
  • After activities: student plays the candidate. Teacher plays the interviewer. Same 5 questions — student answers about THEIR OWN career. Direct transfer.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "The candidate answers 'Tell me about yourself' in under 2 minutes — professionally, honestly, with evidence. Can you do the same? I'll be the interviewer."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
  • Homework: listen 3× and write every "I've" sentence. "9 PP sentences in 90 seconds — that's the density of professional English in a real interview. This is what B1.1 sounds like."
