Choose one photo. Build a 5-sentence story using sequence connectors + irregular verbs from both S2A and S1A. No preparation time — instinctive production reveals your real level.

👉 Tell the story of the photo in 5+ sentences. Use: "First… Then… After that… Finally…" — minimum 4 irregular verbs from yesterday or today. No looking at notes.
Follow-up questions for the teacher
"Who brought the proposal to the negotiation? What did they think about the offer?"
"What happened first? And then what? Use 'after that' — don't say 'and then' again."
"Did they win the contract? What was the outcome? How long did it take?"
"What didn't go as planned? Push for negative form: didn't know / didn't choose / didn't win."
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • S2B arranca con producción instintiva inmediata. "Choose one. Go." — sin pensar, sin preparar. La velocidad de la respuesta y el uso espontáneo de conectores es el diagnóstico real del nivel.
  • Standard mínimo §1: 5+ oraciones con conectores y 4+ irregular verbs sin ayuda. Si no llega → las follow-up questions scaffoldean. Si llega → sube el nivel en §5 y §6.
  • Recast silencioso: si dice "thinked" → tú respondes "Yes, they THOUGHT the deal was fair." Nunca corrección frontal — modelo correcto en contexto.
  • 5–7 minutos duros. El corazón de S2B está en §5, §6 y §7.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "En S2A necesitabas ver la tabla de conectores. Ahora los produces solos. Eso es lo que pasa entre sesión A y B — el conocimiento se convierte en hábito."

Recap Speedrun — S2A Key Points
5 min

⚡ Say the answer BEFORE you tap — recall, not recognition. Two rounds. Round 2 faster.

1
buy · bring · think
tap → past forms
bought · brought · thought
"We bought new equipment. She brought the proposal. I thought it was great."
2
know · tell · become
tap → past forms
knew · told · became
"I knew the client. She told me the news. It became a serious issue."
3
lose · win · choose · keep
tap → past forms
lost · won · chose · kept
"We lost the bid. They won. We chose a new supplier. I kept notes."
4
Sequence order
tap → connectors
First → Then → After that → Finally
"First, I went. Then, we had a meeting. After that, they chose us. Finally, we won."
5
Negative rule — new verbs
tap → rule + examples
didn't + BASE FORM
✅ "didn't know / didn't win / didn't choose" ❌ "didn't knew / didn't won"
Speed Drill: Teacher says base verb → Student gives past form + immediate sentence using a sequence connector.
"buy → ?" · "think → ?" · "lose → ?" · "tell → ?" · "win → ?" · 2 rounds, round 2 no hesitation allowed.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Speed Drill: mínimo 2 rondas. Si "bought/brought" se confunden → ronda extra solo con esos dos. El sonido /ɔːt/ es el mismo — el error es de visual, no de audio.
  • 5 minutos duros. Lo que no sabe aquí = foco adicional de §5 storytelling. Nota mentalmente.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "En S2A mirabas la tabla. En el Speed Drill de hoy no hay tabla. Los verbos ya son tuyos — ahora los automatizamos."

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Wordwall 5 min + Buzz 5 min. Wordwall ahora tiene los 24 verbos — si el alumno falla consistentemente en un grupo → nota para §5 (storytelling) donde ese verbo debe aparecer forzado.
  • Buzz card "After that" connector: si el alumno dice "And then" → acepta pero señala: "Both work — but 'after that' sounds more professional in a written report. Practice it."
  • Para grupos: Buzz por equipos. Marcador visible. Ronda extra con los 3 más difíciles si hay tiempo.

§ 04
Listening — Board Report: What Happened Last Quarter
8–10 min
🎙
Monologue: "Q3 Summary — What We Did, What We Won, What We Lost"
Speaker: Ana González, Operations Director · Duration: ~2 minutes · A2.1 · Faster pace — slightly above comfort zone

Generate with ElevenLabs voice Rachel or Nova · Confident, professional, board-room tone · Slightly faster than S2A · No pauses between connectors
File: GT_A2.1_M2_S2B_Listening_v1.mp3

▶ Show ElevenLabs Script
MONOLOGUE — BOARD REPORT — FASTER, PROFESSIONAL TONE — MARKERS FOR TEACHER

"Good morning everyone. I want to give you a quick summary of what happened in Q3 — what we won, what we lost, and what comes next.

First, the good news. We won three new contracts in the Bajío region. Our team went to the Guadalajara trade show and met eleven potential partners. We chose five to follow up with. We brought new pricing to those conversations and they thought our offer was competitive. Two of them sent proposals back within the week.

Then, the challenges. We lost the NovaTech account — they chose a competitor who gave them a better delivery timeline. I knew this was a risk. We didn't act fast enough. The situation became difficult when the client told us they needed an answer by Tuesday and we didn't give one.

After that, we had an internal review. I brought the team together. We spoke honestly about what went wrong and what we understood too late. We kept the lessons — we didn't just move on.

Finally, the outcome. We made a strategic decision to focus on three verticals. We wrote new account rules and I told each team lead directly. We won more than we lost this quarter — and now we know exactly why we lost the ones we did. That is valuable."
🔧 ElevenLabs: Multilingual v2 · Stability 60% · Similarity 80% · Style 45% · Rate = 1.05 (slightly faster than S2A listening). Board room confidence — not rushed, but no filler pauses. Ana is direct and composed.
Listening Task — Comprehension at Speed
Listen once. Answer from memory. Then verify on second listen if needed.
How many new contracts did the team win in Q3?
Why did they lose the NovaTech account?
What did Ana do "after that" — after the loss?
What was the key lesson Ana mentioned?
Which 4 sequence connectors did Ana use?
🎙 Post-Listening — Your Own Q3 Summary (90 seconds)
1
First, the good news. We [won / made / gave / sent…] [what? how many?].
2
Then, the challenge. We [lost / didn't win / became…] because [reason].
3
After that, we [had / spoke / brought / understood…] [what happened].
4
Finally, the outcome: [chose / made / told / kept…] [result or decision].
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Este listening es deliberadamente más rápido que S2A — es el primer input en "real native pace" de A2.1. El objetivo no es entender cada palabra — es extraer los 4 puntos clave (win / loss / review / outcome).
  • Post-listening scaffold: el alumno adapta la estructura de Ana a su propio Q3/trabajo real. Esto convierte el input en output personal inmediato — la transferencia más eficaz.
  • Si hay confusión en Q5 (sequence connectors): pausa y pide que los identifiquen en el script. Luego escucha de nuevo.
  • Tip de timing: §4 + §5 son el núcleo de S2B. No comprimas §5. Si §4 tarda más, acorta §6 debate a 5 min.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Ana habla más rápido que los audios de S1. La comprendiste. Eso es tu nivel real — por encima de lo que crees."
§ 05
Extended Storytelling — The Full Business Story
10–12 min
Your Story — Minimum 8 Sentences · 4 Connectors · 5 Irregular Verbs · 1 Negative
1
First, I [went / had / met / came…] to [place or event]. I [met / spoke / brought…] [who / what].
2
Then, we [had / gave / made / told…] [what happened next]. They [thought / chose / became…] [reaction].
3
After that, [something developed, changed or went wrong]. We didn't [know / choose / win / tell…] [what] because [reason].
4
Finally, we [won / lost / chose / sent / made…] [outcome]. The [result] was [better / worse / different] than expected.
Story Scenarios — Student picks one or teacher assigns
Scenario A
You went to an industry trade show last month.
You brought new material · met 5 potential clients · gave a product demo · thought the results were positive · chose 2 to follow up · sent proposals · won 1 contract · lost 1 because of pricing.
Scenario B
Your team had to find a new supplier last quarter.
You went to 3 supplier meetings · brought a requirements list · chose 2 candidates · kept the criteria objective · told the team the results · became more confident · won a good contract · didn't choose the cheapest option — chose quality.
Scenario C · Real
Tell the real story of something that happened at work recently.
Use any real event: project / meeting / client / trip / problem. Minimum: 8 sentences · 4 connectors · 5 irregular verbs · 1 negative. Teacher coaches and asks follow-up questions.
Scenario D · Challenge
Tell a story that ended badly — a loss or a mistake.
Focus on negative forms: didn't win / didn't know / didn't choose in time / lost / became a problem / told the team too late. End with: "But we understood the lesson and kept moving." Honest storytelling = advanced communication.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Este es el momento de evaluación de S2B. Criterios: 8+ oraciones · 4 conectores · 5 irregular verbs (incluyendo por lo menos 2 de S2A) · 1 negativa · mínimo 4 minutos de producción oral continua sin referencia a notas.
  • Scenario C (real) y D (loss story) producen el mejor output pedagógico porque conectan con la memoria emocional del alumno. Los eventos reales retienen más vocabulario.
  • No interrumpas durante la historia. Escucha completo. Feedback al final — corrección de los 2–3 errores más importantes. No más. Satura el input, no el feedback.
  • Si el alumno está muy cómodo → pide Scenario D (loss story). El discomfort narrativo activa más recursos lingüísticos.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Esta historia — con esta estructura — es exactamente lo que dices en inglés cuando tu director americano te pregunta '¿cómo fue el Q3?' Esto no es práctica. Es el inglés real."
Capa 3 · Retención & Upsell
  • "En S3A/B aprendes was/were — que te da el vocabulario para describir resultados con más precisión: 'The meeting WAS productive. The client WAS impressed.' Ese nivel de detalle narrativo viene en el siguiente módulo."
§ 06
Debate — What Was the Right Decision?
7–8 min
Debate Prompt
"We lost the NovaTech contract. Was it better to lose it — or should we have acted faster at any cost?"
Side A — It was the right outcome
  • "We didn't lose — we chose to be honest about the timeline."
  • "We told them the real delivery date. That built trust for the future."
  • "We kept our standards. We didn't promise what we couldn't deliver."
  • "We found out NovaTech chose based only on speed — not on quality."
  • "We understood the lesson and kept it. That made us stronger."
Side B — We should have acted faster
  • "We knew the client needed an answer on Tuesday. We didn't give one."
  • "We lost a deal we could have won if we became more flexible."
  • "The competitor gave a faster answer — and won. Speed matters."
  • "We didn't think creatively. We could have chosen a different delivery plan."
  • "In the end, a lost contract is a lost contract — lessons are not revenue."
Language support: "I think we made the right decision because…" · "I disagree — we should have…" · "On the other hand…" · "In the end, we…" · "We didn't [win/choose/act] because…" · "But the real problem was that we didn't [know/tell/bring]…"
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • El debate conecta directamente con el listening de §4 — el alumno ya procesó la historia de Ana. Ahora opina sobre ella con los mismos verbos. Esto es la transferencia de comprensión a producción evaluativa.
  • Si el alumno elige Side A: asegúrate de que defiende con verbos de S2A — "kept / knew / understood / chose." Si elige Side B: "didn't act / didn't think / didn't give / lost."
  • Para grupos: debate formal 3 min por lado + 2 min de conclusión. Para 1-on-1: el alumno elige un lado, tú juegas el otro.
  • Objetivo mínimo: 5 enunciados con irregular past en el debate. Si no llega → micro-prompts: "And why do you think they chose that option?"
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Este debate no es artificial — es exactamente la conversación que tienes en un comité de revisión de cuenta perdida. 'What should we have done differently?' — ahora lo puedes decir en inglés."
§ 07
Writing Output — Your Trip Report Email
8–10 min
Live Writing — Guided Template
Complete this email in real time. Use the vocabulary and connectors from S2A + S2B.
From
[Your name] <[your.email@company.com]>
To
[Manager or team]
Subject
Trip Report — [Event / City / Date]
Dear [name / team],

I'm back from [place]. Here is a quick summary of what happened.

First, I [went / met / gave / had…] [detail]. I also [brought / spoke / took / sent…] [detail]. They [thought / chose / told me…] [reaction or result].

Then, we [had / made / spoke / became…] [what happened next]. I [kept / understood / knew…] [what you learned or observed].

After that, [a challenge, development or final meeting]. We didn't [know / win / sign…] [what] — but [positive note or explanation].

Finally, [main outcome / decision / result]. The [outcome / experience / result] was [adjective].

Next steps: [one or two concrete actions + deadline].

Best regards,
[Your name]
Minimum requirements: 4 sequence connectors (First / Then / After that / Finally) · 5 irregular past verbs (at least 2 from S2A) · 1 negative form (didn't + base) · 1 "next steps" line · Professional tone throughout. This email should be 100% usable in a real work situation.
Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • El email de §7 es el output integrador de toda S2A + S2B. El alumno usa: vocabulary de §2 (email words: summary, outcome, highlight, next steps) + connectors de §3 + irregular verbs de ambas sesiones. Todo en un producto real.
  • Proceso sugerido: 1 min de planificación mental (qué evento usar: real o del scenario de §5) → 5 min de escritura → 2 min de revisión con el maestro.
  • Revisión: corrige solo los 2 errores de mayor impacto — no todo. El alumno necesita confianza, no perfeccionismo.
  • Si el alumno no tiene un viaje reciente → usa el scenario de §5 (A, B, C o D). El contenido es imaginado, la estructura es real.
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "Este email — con tu nombre, tu empresa, tu historia — lo puedes mandar esta semana. Eso es el método GT: lo que aprendes en clase, lo usas el lunes."
Capa 3 · Retención & Upsell
  • Testimonial trigger — MANDATORIO: "¿Cuándo fue la última vez que mandaste un email así en inglés? ¿Cómo te sentirías mandando este mismo email la próxima semana?" — Esta pregunta ancla la retención emocional y genera conversación de upsell natural.
  • "En S3A aprendes was/were — que te da adjetivos de resultado con más precisión: 'The meeting WAS productive. The negotiation WASN'T easy.' Esto completa tu toolkit de past tense."
