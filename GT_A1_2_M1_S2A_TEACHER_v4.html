What day is today? What month? Say the full date in English.

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Q4 es el más diagnóstico: ¿puede el alumno decir la fecha completa de hoy en inglés? Anota errores en: ordinal (22nd vs "22"), nombre del mes, preposición ("on the 22nd of April" — no "in the 22").
  • No corrijas todavía — solo observa. Todos los errores en Q4 se abordan directamente en §3 y §4.
  • Puente desde S1: "In S1 you said HOW OFTEN. Today we add WHEN — specific dates."
Capa 2 · Engagement
  • "¿Cuál es tu deadline más estresante ahora mismo? ¿Puedes decir la fecha en inglés? Al final de hoy puedes."
Capa 3 · Growth & Retention

Capa 1 · Pedagogical
  • Trampas de pronunciación: "February" /ˈfebrueri/ (1er R mudo), "Wednesday" /ˈwenzdeɪ/ (D muda), "budget" /ˈbʌdʒɪt/ (no "bood-JET").
  • Drill de meses: el maestro dice un número (1–12), el alumno dice el mes. Luego al revés. Luego: "¿Qué mes es Q1? ¿Q2? ¿Q3? ¿Q4?"
  • "Quarter" — aclara: Q1=Ene–Mar, Q2=Abr–Jun, Q3=Jul–Sep, Q4=Oct–Dic. Usa el contexto real de la empresa del alumno.
  • "Agenda" falso amigo: en español "agenda" = planificador. En inglés "agenda" = lista de temas para una reunión. Marca la diferencia.

GT Memory Rule — Think by size:
AT = smallest (exact moment: 9 AM)  →  ON = medium (a day: Monday / 20th)  →  IN = largest (a month or year: March / 2025)
⚠ Most Common Errors — Correct immediately
❌ "in Monday"→✅ ON Monday
❌ "on March"→✅ IN March
❌ "in the 15th"→✅ ON the 15th
❌ "the deadline is in Friday"→✅ The deadline is ON Friday
❌ "the meeting is on the morning"→✅ The meeting is IN the morning

      § 05
      Reading — Q2 Key Dates Email
      8–10 min
      FROM
      Sarah Mitchell <s.mitchell@celaya-corp.com>
      TO
      Team Celaya
      SUBJECT
      Q2 Key Dates — Please save to your calendar
      DATE
      Monday, 31 March 2025

      Hi team,

      I'm writing to share our updated agenda for Q2. Please save these key dates in your calendar.

      The first team review is on Monday, the 7th of April. The agenda is already in your email — please read it before the review starts.

      The client presentation is on April 22nd at 10 AM. This is a very important date. Please confirm your attendance before the 15th.

      Our project deadline is on May 30th. All reports must be ready before that due date. We have a budget review in the last week of May to check progress.

      The final delivery to the client is in June — specifically, on the 3rd. The contract for this project also ends in June.

      Remember: the office is closed on May 1st for Labour Day.

      Thank you — see you on Monday!

      Sarah Mitchell
      Project Manager · NovaTech Solutions · Ext. 204
      Comprehension
      Responde las preguntas.
      When is the first team review?
      What time is the client presentation?
      When is the project deadline?
      The office is closed on May 1st for Labour Day.
      The final delivery to the client is in May.
      The team must confirm attendance by April 15th.
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • El formato de email es deliberado — este es exactamente el tipo de email que los alumnos reciben de contactos internacionales.
      • Pide al alumno que resalte todas las preposiciones de tiempo (verde = ON, azul = IN, rojo = AT). Cuenta: 9+ instancias.
      • TR2 trampa: "final delivery is in June" — los alumnos dicen TRUE porque ven "June" pero pasan por alto que el texto dice "specifically on the 3rd." NO en mayo.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "¿El email de Sarah se parece a los que recibes en el trabajo? ¿Puedes leerlo más rápido que la lectura de S1? Sí puedes."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "In A2, you'll write emails like this. In A1.2, you read and understand them. That's your current target — and you just hit it."

      § SP
      Speaking Practice
      10–12 min
      🗣 Guided Speaking — Pair / Group
      Dates & Deadlines at Work
      Answer each question using ON/IN/AT + dates and times. Aim for 2–3 sentences per answer.
      Question 1
      "When is your next important deadline at work? Give the exact date."
      Hint: My deadline is on ___ (date). The project is due in ___ (month).
      Question 2
      "What meetings do you have this week? When are they — day, date, and time?"
      Hint: I have a meeting on ___ at ___. It is on the ___ (ordinal) of ___.
      Question 3
      "Tell me about a project due date coming up. Which quarter is it in?"
      Hint: The deadline is in Q___. It is on ___ (date). We deliver it in ___.
      Question 4
      "What time do your meetings usually start? Morning or afternoon?"
      Hint: My meetings start at ___. They are usually in the morning / afternoon. The team meets on ___.
      Question 5
      "What is the most important date in your work calendar this month?"
      Hint: The most important date is on ___. It is in the ___ week of ___.
      ⏱ 10–12 min📋 5 questions🎯 ON / IN / AT correct👥 Teacher-led
      ⚡ Speaking Challenge
      "Present your work agenda for next week — 5 sentences. Use real dates, ON/IN/AT, and at least one ordinal number. No notes."
      Evaluate: ON/IN/AT correct in every use, at least one ordinal number, at least 2 real dates from their actual calendar. Come back to §1 warm-up: "Can you say that better now?"
      Capa 1 · Pedagogical
      • Error más común: "at March" o "in Monday" — recast inmediato: "IN March — month. ON Monday — day. Try again."
      • Q2 y Q5 son las más valiosas — conectan con el calendario real del alumno. Si el alumno no tiene fechas reales, ofrece el escenario del email de §5.
      • Speaking Challenge: evalúa ON/IN/AT correcto + al menos 1 ordinal + 2 fechas reales. Si los 3: S2A completada.
      Capa 2 · Engagement
      • "Acabas de dar tu agenda de trabajo en inglés — con fechas reales. Eso es lo que hacen en las juntas internacionales."
      Capa 3 · Growth & Retention
      • "En S2B das estas mismas fechas en un role play real — sin scaffold, con follow-up questions. Hoy preparaste exactamente eso."
